# DM Notes

---

## Campaign Tone

**Urban heist fiction inside a classic high-fantasy world.** The dragons and archmages exist — they are distant problems for distant people. The crew lives in the city's working layers: industrial quarters, merchant districts, temple precincts, the rooftops and back rooms and garden walls of estates that hold more than they are owed.

Stakes are financial, structural, and personal. The vault. The payout. The crew's continued freedom. The people they are quietly funding with the proceeds. Not "save the world" — save the neighbourhood, empty the right vault, get out clean.

Morally grey in method, morally clear in target. The crew robs people who have accumulated wealth through corrupt or exploitative means. They do not consider this heroic. They consider it work. The campaign's ethical texture comes not from whether the targets deserve it — they do — but from the questions that arise in the margins: what counts as acceptable risk, who is a civilian, what happens when good intelligence turns out to be incomplete.

---

## DM Principles

1. **Roll only when it matters.** Call for a roll only when both success and failure are plausible and interesting. Auto-succeed the trivial. Auto-fail the impossible. Narrate everything else.
2. **DC before dice.** Set the Difficulty Class before invoking the roll. Keep rulings honest.
3. **Show the work.** When a roll happens, display the DC, the result, and the modifier transparently.
4. **Heroic Inspiration.** Bradkell starts each adventuring day with Heroic Inspiration (Human: Resourceful). Track usage explicitly.
5. **Track concentration.** Tanenni runs Concentration spells — Fog Cloud, Detect Magic, Guidance. Only one at a time. Check at the start of each encounter which is active, if any.
6. **Concentration saves on damage.** When Tanenni takes damage while concentrating, she must make a CON saving throw (DC = 10 or half damage, whichever is higher). **Never skip this.**
7. **Heist pacing.** Structure sessions in phases: intelligence, planning, approach, execution, extraction. Let the players spend time on each. The planning is the game — do not rush it.
8. **Consequences over punishment.** When a plan goes wrong, the consequence should be interesting rather than lethal. Guards raise an alarm; the crew's exit closes; a witness sees a face. Complications compound across jobs.

**The party's moral axis:** No strong Good anchor. Bradkell is the closest — Neutral Good, principled, but the principles encompass the work itself. The crew has no internal brake. When all four agree a target is valid, there is no one whose role is to challenge the premise. Ethical tension arrives from the margins: the unexpected civilian, the intelligence that was wrong, the complication that changes the calculus.

**The party's tactical shape:** Milores anchors the front (AC 15 unarmoured, Bonus Action unarmed strike). Darwhidan flanks off her for guaranteed Sneak Attack. Bradkell controls the opening with Sleep and covers the social perimeter. Tanenni provides intelligence (Speak with Animals), magical security detection (Detect Magic), and the only healing in the crew (Healing Word).

## Character Hooks & Threads

### Bradkell

- **The mutual aid network.** He funds three streets in the industrial quarter through intermediaries. The money comes from the crew's earnings. No one in those streets knows the source. If the network is threatened — by guild enforcement, by a new city tax, by someone tracing the funds — his operational detachment collapses.
- **The sorcerous origin.** The power arrived during a riot at twenty-two. He understands its theory but not its source. Something crackled in the air between him and a guard, and the guard was thrown back six feet. That event is unresolved. Develop it slowly — connect it to whatever his Sorcerous Origin becomes at Level 2.
- **The guild-arcanist's book.** He stole an arcane text from a guild-arcanist under false pretences. The arcanist may not yet know the book is missing. The arcanist may know exactly who took it. This is a thread to pull when external pressure on the crew is needed.
- **The theorist's flaw.** He trusts his read on people more than is always warranted. He reads desire well; he reads fear poorly. When a mark or a contact panics, his model breaks.

### Darwhidan

- **The family.** Aasimar lineage with genuine religious conviction. They expected a paladin or a cleric. He became a thief. Two unanswered letters from the order he never joined. This is not guilt — it is the specific discomfort of a man who has made a choice he is satisfied with and cannot explain to the people who expected a different one.
- **The Duke's correspondence.** He sent a corrupt duke's private letters to three parties who would find them actionable. Those parties now owe him, or fear him, or both. The letters are in circulation. The duke knows someone took them. The duke does not know who.
- **The vault debt.** He owes Tanenni half a vault from three years ago. Neither has mentioned it. The debt is not really about money — it is the visible surface of a question about what they are to each other, and whether the framework of debt applies at all. Let it surface naturally.
- **Feather Fall dependency.** One free cast per Long Rest. When it is spent, his preferred exit strategy — headfirst through a window — becomes a genuine risk. Track this resource explicitly.

### Milores

- **The cottage.** A specific property on a specific coast, north-facing window, good painting light. Four jobs from the deposit. Every payout brings it closer. This is not sentiment — it is a project plan with a definite completion point. The question the campaign must eventually ask: what happens when the number reaches zero?
- **The former company.** She beat her commanding officer and walked out. The company may still be looking for her. An NPC who recognises her, or a contract that crosses her old company's territory, reactivates this thread.
- **The painting.** Seven completed coastal landscapes, rolled in a leather tube at the bottom of her pack. The eighth is in progress. The crew has noticed. No one has commented. The paintings are the clearest window into who she is when she is not working — let them surface through circumstance, not through forced revelation.
- **The code's limits.** Civilians are out of scope. The operation is the operation. When an operation produces a complication that falls outside her code — a person in trouble, a moral claim on the crew's attention — she will not act. Others will. The gap between those positions is where the drama lives.

### Tanenni

- **The temple.** Twelve years as archivist. She discovered the corruption, did not expose it, and instead quietly rerouted funds. The temple thinks she is on administrative leave. Her access credentials are still valid. This is an ongoing operational asset and an ongoing risk — if the temple discovers the rerouted funds, or if someone inside the temple connects her to the crew's work, the consequences are institutional and personal.
- **The ecological framework.** She sees the city as a system with identifiable imbalances. This is not ideology — it is observation. Weight in the wrong places. Develop this through her Druidic connection: the natural world she communicates with is also a system in balance, and the city is a system out of balance, and the parallel is where her conviction lives.
- **The animal network.** Pigeons, rats, cats, sparrows — her informants. They are not pets or companions; they are intelligence sources. Populate the city with animals she can consult. Give them personality and limited perspective. A pigeon knows what it sees from the rooftop; a rat knows what it hears through the walls. The intelligence is real but partial, and the gap between animal perception and operational reality is where Tanenni's interpretive skill matters.
- **The exit question.** Milores has four jobs to the cottage. Tanenni has no stated exit. At some point the crew must reckon with whether she intends to do this indefinitely, and whether the answer to that question changes anything.

## Mechanical Reminders

### Heroic Inspiration (Bradkell)
Bradkell gains Heroic Inspiration at the end of every Long Rest (Human: Resourceful). He can also distribute it: during a Short or Long Rest where he plays music for 10+ minutes, up to 2 allies gain Heroic Inspiration. **Ask at every Short Rest whether he plays.** Ask at every critical moment whether a character with Heroic Inspiration wants to use it.

### Sneak Attack Trigger (Darwhidan)
Milores adjacent to the target satisfies the condition. No Advantage needed. Every round that Milores is in melee with Darwhidan's target, Sneak Attack is on. **Call the shared target at the start of each round.**

### Sleep (Bradkell)
WIS save DC 13 (DC 14 with Innate Sorcery). Two-stage: fail first save = Incapacitated; fail second save (end of target's next turn) = Unconscious. Unconscious targets grant auto-crit from melee within 5 ft. **Do not let Darwhidan attack before the second save resolves.** Elves and Exhaustion-immune creatures auto-succeed.

### Weapon Masteries
- **Vex (Darwhidan, Shortsword):** On hit, gain Advantage on next attack roll against that target before end of next turn.
- **Nick (Darwhidan, Dagger):** Light-property extra attack is part of the Attack action, not the Bonus Action. This frees Darwhidan's Bonus Action every round.

### Spells — Key Notes
- **Healing Word (Tanenni):** Bonus Action, 60 ft range, 2d4+3 HP. The only HP restoration in the crew. Guard these slots.
- **Fog Cloud (Tanenni):** Concentration, 20 ft sphere. Heavily Obscured. Darkvision does not pierce it. Position to block enemy line of sight on Bradkell (AC 13, no Darkvision).
- **Guidance (Tanenni):** Concentration, 1 minute. Choose skill at cast time. Stack with Darwhidan's Thieves' Tools (+7 + 1d4) or Bradkell's Persuasion (+5 + 1d4). **Conflicts with Fog Cloud — choose one.**
- **Command (Tanenni):** Free cast 1/LR. Also castable with spell slots. WIS save DC 13. Drop, Flee, Grovel, Halt.
- **Sleep (Bradkell):** 1 of 2 slots (Mage Armour takes the other). After both are spent, Bradkell is cantrips and social skills only.
- **Mage Hand (Darwhidan):** 30 ft range, 10 lb limit. Remote manipulation of mechanisms, retrieval of objects.
- **Minor Illusion (Darwhidan):** Sound or image, 5 ft cube, DC 12 Investigation to discern. Place sounds to redirect guard attention.
- **Feather Fall (Darwhidan):** Reaction, 1 free cast/LR. Up to 5 creatures. The crew's emergency vertical exit.
- **Fire Bolt (Milores):** 1d10 Fire, 120 ft. Ranged fallback when melee is not viable. Ignites unattended flammables.
- **Thaumaturgy (Milores + Tanenni):** Both know it. Milores uses it for Intimidation (Advantage). Tanenni uses it for credential displays (amber eyes, booming voice).

## The Crew's Operational Pattern

**Intelligence phase.** Tanenni gathers information: animal reports (Speak with Animals, 2 free casts or Ritual), ledger review (temple access), ward assessment (Detect Magic as Ritual). Bradkell collects social intelligence: tavern conversations, public records, contact networks. Duration: days to weeks before a job.

**Planning phase.** Bradkell builds the operational plan from Tanenni's intelligence. Floor plans, timing sequences, contingencies. Milores assesses physical security: patrol quality, guard count, structural weaknesses. Darwhidan does walk-throughs: enters the target space under cover, maps lock types, identifies access points, measures distances by stride. The plan is a crew product, but Bradkell is the architect and Tanenni is the foundation.

**Approach phase.** Darwhidan and Milores lead the stealth approach (+5 each). Bradkell provides social cover: distraction, persuasion, a legitimate reason to be in the area. Tanenni follows at +1, carrying Guidance or Detect Magic as the situation requires. Bradkell's Stealth +0 means he is never inside the target during the approach — he is outside, occupying the perimeter.

**Execution phase.** Darwhidan breaches locks and mechanisms. Milores neutralises guards within scope. Bradkell monitors the social perimeter and adapts the plan in real time. Tanenni coordinates via Message (120 ft, through walls) and manages magical countermeasures. If combat occurs: Sleep first, Milores anchors, Darwhidan flanks, Tanenni heals.

**Extraction phase.** Planned exits. Milores withdraws through the planned route. Darwhidan goes through the window. Bradkell walks out the front. Tanenni disappears into the temple district. If the planned exit closes: Fog Cloud to cover repositioning, Feather Fall for vertical escape, Bradkell's social skills to talk through a checkpoint.
