# Campaign Setup --- Session Zero Document

*How the crew came to exist; where each of them was before it; and the specific sequence of events, debts, and decisions that turned four independent operators into a functioning unit.*

---

## Part One: Before the Crew

### Bradkell --- The Street Organiser

Bradkell was born in the **Sullow Quarter**, the industrial district that occupies the low ground along the eastern bank of the River Thane in the city of **Corraith**. The Sullow Quarter is brewery country --- or was, before the Guild of Chartered Commerce began its consolidation campaign fifteen years ago. His father, **Edric Bradkell**, ran the **Three Chimney Brewery** on Millrace Lane, a mid-sized operation that produced a dark ale popular with dockworkers and canal bargemen. The brewery survived two rounds of guild licensing increases and one arson attempt that was never investigated. It did not survive the third licensing round, which tripled the charter fee and imposed a surety bond that required a guarantor from the guild's own approved list --- a list on which no one was willing to appear for a Sullow Quarter brewer.

Edric closed the brewery when Bradkell was eleven. The building was sold to cover the outstanding fees. It is now a guild-licensed grain storage facility. Bradkell walks past it when he has reason to be in that part of the Sullow Quarter, which is not often, and when he does he does not slow down.

By fourteen he was a runner for the **Sullow Compact** --- the loose network of mutual aid operations that provides what the guilds have priced out of reach: unlicensed medical care, food distribution during the winter shortages, the occasional quiet loan at rates that do not constitute a second catastrophe. By eighteen he was coordinating supply routes. By twenty he was planning operations: which warehouses had surplus that could be redirected, which guild inspectors could be delayed, which aldermen's schedules created windows for movement.

The sorcerous ability arrived during the **Charter Hall riot** when he was twenty-two. The riot was a response to the Guild of Chartered Commerce's announcement that mutual aid distribution would henceforth require a charitable dispensation license, purchasable for a fee that was transparently designed to make it impossible. Bradkell was coordinating a blockade of the Charter Hall's south entrance when a ward-officer seized him. The air cracked. The officer landed six feet away, unhurt but profoundly startled. The street cleared. Bradkell stood in the empty square and understood, with the practical clarity he applies to everything, that something significant had changed and that its applications were considerable.

He spent two years acquiring arcane theory by whatever means presented themselves. He borrowed texts from the **Thane-Side Circulating Library**, which lends to anyone with a deposit and does not ask questions about the borrower's guild standing. He attended three lectures at the **Corraith Arcanists' Lyceum** under a borrowed name before someone noticed he was not enrolled. He once spent an afternoon in the private study of **Alderman Varek Solenne**, ostensibly discussing a charitable dispensation application, and left the meeting having memorised the ward-schema on Solenne's desk and having relieved his shelf of a monograph on abjuration theory that Solenne has not yet noticed is missing.

He understands wards, alarms, and arcane security systems --- not as a practitioner builds them, but as an opponent analyses them. He knows how their operators think, which assumptions they make about who will try to circumvent them, and how those assumptions create exploitable patterns.

### Darwhidan --- The Courtier Who Wasn't

Darwhidan was born into the **Mael-Arath**, one of Corraith's oldest Aasimar families, whose compound occupies a walled garden in the **Thorngate District** --- the city's ecclesiastical quarter, where the great temples and the residences of their senior clergy share the high ground above the river. The Mael-Arath have produced clerics, paladins, and temple functionaries for six generations. Darwhidan's mother, **Seraya Mael-Arath**, is a senior officiant at the **Temple of the Risen Flame**, and his father, **Aldous**, administers the temple's endowment fund. They expected, with the settled confidence of people who have already made the plan, that Darwhidan would take vows and enter the temple's martial order.

He was sent instead, at nineteen, to a posting at the **Corvane Estate** --- the household of **Duke Renn Corvane**, one of Corraith's wealthiest nobles and a significant donor to the Temple of the Risen Flame. The posting was meant to season him: court manners, noble protocol, the social grammar of the city's upper tier. He learned all of these. He also learned that the Corvane Estate had fourteen external doors, three of which were never locked; that the guard rotation left the west corridor unobserved for eleven minutes every two hours; and that the duke's private vault was protected by a mechanical lock of excellent quality and a ward of adequate quality, neither of which was sufficient.

He spent his second year at the estate practising Mage Hand on borrowed texts and Minor Illusion until he could place a sound --- a footstep, a closing door, a cough --- precisely enough to redirect a guard's attention with reliable accuracy. He mapped the security of every estate he visited in the course of his duties, which was many: the Corvane household entertained frequently, and the young Aasimar attaché was a welcome guest at tables where the silver was counted after he left, though never found to be missing. He was, at this stage, still only looking.

The looking ended at the **Corvane vault**. He walked through the servants' entrance at the shift change, past two guards who were attending to a sound in the wrong corridor, through the vault's mechanical lock in thirty seconds and its ward in another twenty, and out through a third-floor window with a recently learned Feather Fall and the contents of the duke's private strongbox in a document case under his arm. He sent the correspondence --- which documented the duke's arrangements with certain harbour officials regarding undeclared cargo --- to three parties who would find it useful. He kept the gold.

His family sent two letters. The first expressed concern. The second expressed the specific kind of silence that functions as a conclusion. He did not reply to either. He has not been to the Thorngate District since, though his access credentials at the Temple of the Risen Flame have not been formally revoked, which he considers an oversight worth preserving.

### Milores --- The Soldier Who Walked Out

Milores grew up in the **Daerrow**, the lower city district that occupies the floodplain south of the Sullow Quarter. Her mother, **Tessara**, was a Tiefling metalworker who ran a small shop repairing canal-boat hardware --- hinges, locks, anchor fittings --- and who taught her daughter the particular discipline of working with metal: patience, precision, and the understanding that the material tells you what it will become if you pay attention.

She was recruited at sixteen by the **Blackshore Company**, a mercenary outfit that operated out of a compound on the Corraith waterfront and took contracts across the region. The Blackshore Company was reputable in the way that mercenary companies are reputable: it honoured its contracts, paid its soldiers, and did not ask its employers to justify themselves. Milores was young but large for her age and demonstrably capable of things that recruitment officers found promising. She spent ten years becoming very good at a specific type of work: infiltration of defended positions, silent neutralisation of sentries, and the dismantling of defensive formations from inside by someone who had no business being inside them.

The work ended at a village called **Thornfield**, three days' ride north of Corraith, where the Blackshore Company had taken a contract to make a minor lord's holdings untenable. The method proposed by her commanding officer, **Captain Drevan Horst**, was to burn the village that provided the lord's labour base. Horst's logic was sound by the company's operational standards: displacing the population permanently solved the contract without the risk of a direct assault.

Milores walked into Horst's tent. She gave him one opportunity to reconsider. He did not reconsider. She beat him with sufficient thoroughness to remove him from the remainder of the campaign, left him outside the tent for his second-in-command to find, and walked out of the camp carrying nothing that was not already hers.

She spent eighteen months checking her surroundings more carefully than usual. The Blackshore Company does not formally pursue deserters, but Horst had friends in the company who took the matter personally, and for a period the city's professional underworld included people who would have been interested to know where she was. She found work as an enforcer for **Madame Oressa's** gaming houses in the Sullow Quarter --- quiet work, professional, with a clear scope. She developed a reputation for efficiency and the specific quality of never breaking anything she was not paid to break.

She is four jobs from the deposit on a cottage. The cottage is real. It is on the coast north of Corraith, on a cliff above the sea, with a north-facing window. She has seen it once. She has painted it seven times.

### Tanenni --- The Archivist Who Noticed

Tanenni was raised in the **Temple of Measured Light**, one of Corraith's largest religious institutions, which occupies a walled compound in the Thorngate District three streets from the Mael-Arath family compound. She was assigned to the archive and records office at sixteen because she had neat scales, neater handwriting, and the quiet temperament that archival work rewards.

She spent eight years copying donation records, processing correspondence, and maintaining the internal accounts that funded the temple's charitable works --- a network of shelters, food kitchens, and small-grant programmes that served the lower city. She spent four more years understanding that the charitable works represented approximately eleven percent of the temple's total income, and that the remaining eighty-nine percent moved through the temple's private reliquaries in ways that had nothing to do with worship.

The **Temple of Measured Light** functions, in practice, as a secure storage and laundering facility for Corraith's aristocratic families. Illicit goods move through blessed vaults under religious exemption from city inspection. Tax obligations dissolve in clauses written into the temple's charter three centuries ago. Documents that would end careers rest in alcoves consecrated to saints of wisdom, where they are both metaphysically and legally untouchable. The temple's senior clergy --- **High Archivist Solen Drath** and his circle --- manage this arrangement with the unhurried confidence of people who have been doing it for a very long time and see no reason it should stop.

Tanenni did not expose this. Exposure would have destroyed the genuine charitable work, provided no structural change, and ended her access to the institution she would later need. Instead, she quietly rerouted approximately twelve percent of the skimmed surplus back into the charitable accounts, altered the relevant ledger entries with calligraphic skill that was indistinguishable from the originals, and continued her duties. High Archivist Drath has not noticed, or has noticed and attributed the discrepancy to accounting variance. The temple still considers Tanenni a senior acolyte on administrative leave. Her access credentials remain valid.

The druidic connection arrived at twenty-nine, in the temple's kitchen garden. She was repotting a bay laurel and understood, without drama, that she knew something about the plant's intention. She found the **Circle of the Threshold**, a small druidic order that operates from a garden compound on Corraith's western outskirts, learned the language and the practice, and incorporated both into her temple work in ways indistinguishable from standard religious observance. The animals she communicated with became her most reliable intelligence network: sparrows that mapped guard rotations, rats that reported on basement storage, a particular crow named **Ashen** who sits on the Thorngate bell tower and has opinions about everything that moves below.

She is the crew's architect. She has been doing this longer than the crew has existed, and the others are aware of this, and she has not corrected the impression that the crew was her idea from the beginning. It was not. She had been operating alone for four years before Darwhidan appeared on the other side of a strongbox, and the decision to build a crew around that partnership was made over three days of careful consideration, not in the moment. She does not make decisions in the moment.

## Part Two: The Formation

### The Vault

Three years before the campaign begins, Tanenni and Darwhidan independently targeted the private vault of **Lord Aldren Voss**, a textile magnate whose wealth derived from a system of contract labour in the mill districts that was technically legal and comprehensively exploitative. Tanenni had been monitoring Voss's financial arrangements through the Temple of Measured Light's records for six months. Darwhidan had been mapping the Voss estate's security through social channels --- he had attended three of the lord's dinner parties as a guest of a guest and had used the occasions to chart every door, window, and patrol pattern in the building.

They arrived at the vault from different entrances at approximately the same time. Tanenni came through the garden, past a ward she had identified and circumvented using the Druidic knowledge that wards keyed to "intruders" do not recognise someone moving with the rhythm of the garden's own growth. Darwhidan came through the servants' corridor, past a guard he had redirected with a precisely placed Minor Illusion.

They met over the strongbox. Tanenni considered him for a moment --- an Aasimar with gold eyes and a lockpick, which was not a combination the temple had prepared her for. She offered to split it. He accepted. They left through different exits, met at a predetermined public location the following morning, divided the contents, and began talking about what a partnership might look like.

Tanenni taught him Druidic over the following weeks. He uses it to leave scouting reports carved into city stonework in locations that guards walk past without examining. She reads the reports and notes that his observational priorities are consistently correct, which she considers the most important quality in a partner.

### Milores

Milores was recruited six months after the Voss job, through **Garren Thule**, a fence and information broker who operates from a back room at the **Candlewick Tavern** in the Sullow Quarter. Thule had worked with Milores during her enforcer period and with Tanenni on several prior intelligence purchases. When Tanenni described what the crew needed --- someone who could handle physical security, close-quarters neutralisation, and the specific kind of quiet violence that heist work occasionally requires --- Thule gave her one name.

Milores attended the meeting, assessed the crew, and asked three questions: What is the target profile? What is the civilian exposure? What is the pay structure? The answers were: corrupt wealth, none, and equal shares after operational costs. She accepted. She has since revised her opinion of the crew from "adequate professionals" to something she has not named but which is visible in the fact that she has not, at any point in the subsequent two and a half years, looked for alternative employment.

### Bradkell

Bradkell found the crew through the Sullow Quarter's underworld networks --- the same channels that supply the mutual aid operations with information about guild movements and warehouse schedules. He had heard, through intermediaries, that a crew was operating in Corraith that targeted guild-connected wealth with a precision that suggested planning rather than opportunism. He arranged a meeting through a contact of a contact and attended it with the specific intention of evaluating whether the crew's targeting aligned with his own priorities.

It did. He determined three things at that first meeting: the crew was competent, it targeted the institutions he had spent his adult life opposing, and it had a structural gap in its social operation --- no one who could walk into a room of guild officials and leave with their confidence, their information, and their conviction that the large man in rough clothes was someone they had independently decided to help. He filled the gap.

He joined eighteen months before the campaign begins. He is the newest member and the one whose integration changed the crew's operational profile most significantly: before Bradkell, the crew's approach was infiltration and extraction. After Bradkell, the approach includes social engineering --- the front-door entry, the manufactured invitation, the conversation that produces access to a building without anyone needing to climb through a window. Darwhidan still climbs through windows. He considers this an aesthetic choice.

## Part Three: Specific Questions Resolved

### Crew Structure and Decision-Making

Tanenni is the architect. She identifies targets, gathers intelligence, designs the operational plan, and produces the forged credentials and documentation that support cover identities. Her plans are detailed, well-margined, and presented with the confidence of someone who has checked them twice.

Bradkell is the social engineer. He builds the front-door approach: the cover story, the social map of the target environment, the identification of which people need to believe what and in what sequence. He and Tanenni plan together, and their planning sessions are the crew's most productive and least comfortable meetings --- productive because both are excellent, uncomfortable because both are precise and neither concedes a point without evidence.

Darwhidan is the infiltrator. He handles physical entry, mechanical and magical locks, the movement through defended spaces, and the specific improvisational talent of adapting when the situation inside is not what the plan predicted. He is also the crew's secondary social operator --- he can hold a cover identity under pressure with a naturalness that Bradkell, whose covers are more constructed, sometimes envies.

Milores is the specialist. She handles physical security, guard neutralisation, and the contingency work that arises when something goes wrong and the crew needs someone who can create an exit through opposition. She does not plan operations. She executes them, and she executes them within the scope that was agreed, and she will tell you when the scope is no longer clean.

Decisions are collective. There is no single leader. In practice, Tanenni sets the operational direction and Bradkell shapes the approach, and Darwhidan and Milores contribute their domain expertise, and the crew arrives at consensus through a process that is efficient but not fast. Under time pressure, this is their primary vulnerability: four independent decision-makers with four different frameworks, all of them correct, none of them identical.

### Financial Arrangements

The crew operates on an equal-share basis after operational costs. Operational costs include materials (Tanenni's forgery supplies, Darwhidan's equipment, Milores's paint), information purchases (Garren Thule's fees, which are fair but not cheap), and the maintenance of safe houses and dead drops.

Bradkell routes his share through intermediaries to the Sullow Compact's mutual aid operations. The amounts are real but not traceable. No one in the Sullow Quarter knows the funding source. The other crew members know, because Bradkell told them at the outset --- he considers it relevant to their operational security that they understand where his money goes and why.

Milores banks her share. The cottage fund is a specific number in a specific account maintained by a specific moneylender in the Daerrow. She knows, to the coin, how far she is from the deposit.

Darwhidan spends his share on tailoring, equipment, and the specific lifestyle maintenance required to move convincingly in noble circles. He also owes Tanenni half the contents of the Voss vault from three years ago, a debt that is technically outstanding and that neither of them has raised.

Tanenni reinvests in the operation. Her share funds intelligence acquisition, maintains the crew's documentation infrastructure, and covers the bribes that keep certain city officials looking in directions that are not toward the crew.

### Prior Jobs

The crew has completed **seven major operations** in the three years since formation. The target profile is consistent: individuals and institutions whose wealth derives from the exploitation of Corraith's guild system, labour arrangements, or aristocratic privilege. Specifics:

- The **Voss vault** (Tanenni and Darwhidan only; pre-crew). Securities and correspondence from a textile magnate.
- The **Solenne ledgers**. Financial records from Alderman Varek Solenne's private study, documenting payments from the Guild of Chartered Commerce to city officials. Redistributed to three broadsheet publishers. Solenne survived the scandal; his reputation did not.
- The **Harrowgate customs house**. A payroll diversion from the harbour customs office, which was skimming import duties into a fund controlled by a consortium of merchant-nobles. The crew redirected three months of skimmed funds to the Sullow Compact.
- The **Corvane correspondence** (second operation against the duke's household; Darwhidan's return visit). Additional documents establishing the duke's involvement in harbour corruption. The duke has since left Corraith for his country estate. He has not returned.
- Three additional operations of similar profile, against guild-connected targets, each producing a combination of financial returns and redistributed intelligence.

No civilian casualties. No crew injuries beyond the manageable. No identification by city authorities. The crew's operational security is excellent, which is to say Tanenni's operational security is excellent, and the others have the discipline to maintain it.

### Relationships Between Members

**Tanenni and Darwhidan** have the oldest relationship and the most developed trust. They communicate in Druidic when operational security requires it. The debt from the Voss vault is unresolved and functions as the visible surface of a more complicated question about mutual obligation that neither has articulated.

**Milores and Tanenni** have a clean professional interface. Tanenni plans; Milores executes; the handoff between planning and execution is the smoothest operational seam in the crew. They share the specific quality of carrying private facts --- Milores's paintings, Tanenni's administrative leave --- without requiring them to be discussed.

**Bradkell and Milores** have a mutual respect built on legibility. Bradkell's framework is elaborate but consistent; Milores's code is simple but absolute. Each finds the other's decision-making process readable, which matters more to both of them than agreement.

**Bradkell and Darwhidan** are the crew's two social operators, and each knows the other is performing. This produces a specific ease: neither can be the other's mark, which means the version of themselves they present to each other is closer to actual than the version they present to anyone else. Bradkell finds Darwhidan entertaining and occasionally alarming. Darwhidan finds Bradkell's need for models endearing.

**Darwhidan and Milores** are the crew's silent-approach pair and share the professional recognition of two people whose physical precision is their primary asset. They do not debrief after operations. They do not need to.

**Bradkell and Tanenni** are the crew's planning engine. Their working relationship is the one that most resembles a partnership, though neither would use that word. Bradkell calibrates his plans around Tanenni's intelligence reports and they have, so far, been accurate. He considers her the most reliable person in the crew. She has noted that he has noticed.

## Part Four: Open Threads

*Things that exist but have not yet been pulled on. For development during play.*

- **The Sullow Compact and the Guild of Chartered Commerce.** Bradkell's mutual aid network is funded by the crew's operations. The Guild's enforcement arm, the **Warden-Inspectors**, have been increasing pressure on unlicensed charitable activity. The intersection of these two facts has not yet produced a crisis, but the geometry is clear.
- **The Temple of Measured Light.** Tanenni's twelve-percent rerouting has been running for years. High Archivist Solen Drath is either oblivious or patient. Neither possibility is comfortable. Her access credentials remain valid, which is either an oversight or an invitation, and she has not determined which.
- **The Mael-Arath family.** Darwhidan's celestial heritage and his family's expectations are unresolved. His mother, Seraya, is a senior officiant at a major temple. His father administers its endowment. They know what he has become. They have stopped writing. The silence is its own kind of thread.
- **The Blackshore Company.** Milores walked out two and a half years ago. Captain Horst recovered. The company continues to operate. Whether anyone in the company is still looking for her is a question she checks the answer to periodically, and the answer has been "not actively" for some time, which is not the same as "no."
- **Edric Bradkell.** Bradkell's father is alive, in the Sullow Quarter, and unaware that his son funds the mutual aid network that now provides services the brewery used to provide informally. Whether this remains the case is Bradkell's decision, and he has not made it.
- **The cottage.** Milores is four jobs away. The arithmetic of the crew's operational tempo means this is approximately one year. What happens when the number reaches zero --- whether she stays, whether she goes, whether the crew survives her departure --- is a question none of them have raised and all of them have considered.
- **Darwhidan's debt to Tanenni.** Half the Voss vault. Three years outstanding. The amount is known to both of them. The reason it has not been called due is known to neither of them, or to both of them, and the distinction matters.
- **The crew's growing profile.** Seven operations in three years, all targeting the same category of wealth, all executed with the same operational signature. The city's intelligence apparatus --- the **Reedmen**, Corraith's plainclothes investigators --- has not yet connected the pattern. The word "yet" is doing significant work in that sentence.
