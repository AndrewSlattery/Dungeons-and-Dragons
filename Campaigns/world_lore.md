# World Lore & Setting

## Overview
A classic high-fantasy world seen from ground level. The great kingdoms, archmages, and dragon flights exist — but they're distant rumour and traveller's tale. Rixon lives in the margins: small towns, trade roads, tavern back rooms, and the company of people who work for coin.

## Brindlemark
A small walled town at the junction of two trade roads: the Fenway (running east–west between larger cities) and the Drover's Track (running south into hilly sheep country). Population perhaps 1,200. Stone walls twelve feet high, old but maintained. Two gates: the Market Gate (east, facing the Fenway) and the Shepherd's Gate (south).

### Key Locations
- **The Gatehouse.** Where contract work gets posted and pay gets collected. Run by the town's small constabulary. A corkboard of notices inside the door.
- **The Kettle & Crow.** The larger of two taverns, near the market square. Rooms upstairs, common room downstairs. Where travellers and working swords end up. The ale is adequate. The food is hearty.
- **The Lame Dog.** The smaller tavern, down a side street. More locals, fewer travellers. Quieter, cheaper, rougher.
- **The Market Square.** Open most mornings with farmers, tinkers, and a few merchants trading goods off the Fenway.
- **Brindlemark Chapel.** A modest stone chapel to no particular god in the specific — serves the community for blessings, funerals, and festivals.
- **Alderman Cray's House.** The largest house in town, near the square. Alderman Cray is the closest thing Brindlemark has to a mayor.

### Economy
Brindlemark survives on trade road traffic, sheep from the southern hills, and a modest grain harvest from farms outside the walls. It's prosperous enough to have a cobbler and a brewer but not enough to have a proper smithy — metalwork comes in by wagon.

### Atmosphere
Mud and thatch and stone. Woodsmoke. Sheep shit tracked through the market gate. The sound of carts on cobbles. People who know each other's business. Travellers regarded with pragmatic hospitality: welcome for their coin, watched for their trouble.

## The Wider Region
- To the **east and west** along the Fenway: larger towns and eventually proper cities, days of travel apart.
- To the **south** along the Drover's Track: rolling hills, sheep farms, and eventually wilder country — foothills and forest.
- To the **north**: farmland giving way to marshes. Not much up there. People don't go north much.
