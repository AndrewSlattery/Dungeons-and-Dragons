# Unified Geography — The Shared World

*How the three campaign regions exist within a single coherent setting. This document establishes the physical geography, climate, and spatial relationships that allow all three campaigns to coexist without contradiction.*

---

## The Shape of the Land

The three campaigns occupy different regions of the same landmass: a large, irregularly shaped territory whose coastline bends from a northern shore around to a southeastern one, creating a broad promontory bounded by water on three sides. The interior is mountainous, forested, and cut by river valleys. The climate is temperate — northern hemisphere, mid-latitude — with maritime weather on the coasts and more continental conditions inland.

The key to the geography is the **coastal bend**. The northern shore runs roughly east–west, facing a cold northern sea. At the eastern end, the coastline curves southward, and the southeastern shore faces warmer, more sheltered waters. This means a traveller moving east along the northern coast would eventually find themselves moving south along the eastern seaboard, with the sea now to their right rather than ahead. The interior — mountains, forests, river valleys — fills the space between these two coasts.

Three regions, three campaigns:

- **The Fenway Coast** (Campaign 1) occupies the northern shore, where the sea lies to the north and the land rises southward into highlands and hill country.
- **The Thornvael** (Campaign 2) occupies the interior, running north–south along the western flank of the central mountain range, with the vast Greenwild forest to its west.
- **Corraith and the Thanelands** (Campaign 3) occupy the southeastern coast, where the River Thane meets the Mirrenmere estuary and the land faces the southern sea.

The distance from the Fenway coast to Corraith, as the crow flies, is perhaps two to three hundred miles — several weeks of overland travel, or a shorter but more arduous voyage by sea around the coastal bend. These are not neighbouring villages; they are distinct regions with their own cultures, economies, and concerns. But they are connected by trade routes, shipping lanes, and the kind of rumour that travels with merchants and moves faster than anyone on foot.

## Regional Detail

### The Fenway Coast — The Northern Shore

The Fenway coast runs east–west along the northern edge of the landmass, facing a cold, grey, reliable sea. The Fenway road follows the coast at a short distance inland, connecting a string of settlements from Brindlemark in the west through Kelstead Port to Ashford in the east. South of the road, the land rises through a highland ridge — home to the Goliath communities of Greymantle Keep and its neighbours — and then into the wilder hill country and the Dwarven mountain holds beyond.

Between the Fenway road and the sea to the north, there is a strip of rough coastal ground: scrub oak, wet limestone, broken bluffs, and the kind of terrain that is not quite hill country and not quite flat — coastal scrub, exposed to wind and salt, cut by streams running north to the sea. This is the ground Mall-Lowick knows from her cartographic work on the northern approaches, and the terrain the party crossed on their overland route from Thornwater back to Kelstead Port.

The sea to the north is cold, the sky is often overcast, and rain is a structural feature of the climate rather than a weather event. The autumn fog that rolls off the highland ridge into Stoneveil Reach's harbour is a local institution. The Fenway's weather comes primarily off the sea — northerly and northeasterly winds carrying moisture and chill — though the ridge produces its own weather when the seasons turn.

**Key settlements, west to east:**

- **Brindlemark** — Small walled town (pop. ~1,200) at the junction of the Fenway road and the Drover's Track, which runs south into the hill country. Two gates: the Market Gate facing east onto the Fenway, and the Shepherd's Gate facing south onto the Drover's Track. The economy runs on trade road traffic, sheep from the southern hills, and grain from farms outside the walls. This is where Campaign 1 begins.

- **Kelstead Port** — The major harbour town of the Fenway coast, large enough to have a hiring hall, a port chapel, a proper harbour authority, and the kind of mixed-species working population that a busy trading port generates. Kelstead is the hub through which cargo moves between the coastal sea routes and the inland Fenway road. The party passed through Kelstead before the campaign and will use it as a resource.

- **Stoneveil Reach** — A small harbour town at the base of the highland ridge, where the Goliath communities of the upper country meet the sea. Not a Goliath settlement — a mixed town of fisherfolk, sail-makers, and small traders that grew because a natural harbour existed. Cade's home.

- **Ashford** — A proper city well east along the Fenway, large enough to have guilds, a functioning militia, and a significant Goliath blacksmithing community. Ashford sits near the point where the northern coast begins to curve southward — geographically and commercially, it is the Fenway's nearest point to the Thanelands and its guild culture. The Ashford Guild, which oversees certain categories of arcane practice, has structural similarities to the Guild of Chartered Commerce in Corraith; whether they are branches of the same institution or independent organisations with a shared lineage is not established but would not be surprising.

- **Thornwater** — A secondary harbour town accessible by a day's sail north from Kelstead, plus a short inland transit. Thornwater services fishing fleets and the occasional merchant vessel. This is where Gilda's cargo was impounded and Captain Vetha was detained.

**The highland ridge and the Dwarven holds:**

The highland ridge runs roughly parallel to the Fenway coast, a day or two's travel south of the road. The Goliath communities — Greymantle Keep and others — occupy the upper country in stone-built, practical settlements. Below the ridge, Stoneveil Reach and similar coastal towns represent the meeting point of highland and maritime cultures.

South of the ridge, the land continues to rise into the central mountain range. The Dwarven mountain holds are here — several days' travel inland and south from the Fenway — old communities built into the bones of the hills, rich in metalwork and the accumulated capital of generations. The Dwarven commercial network extends outward along the Fenway and the river routes. Gilda's trading background is in this network.

**The Halfling river communities:**

The winding river valleys east and south of the Fenway are home to the Halfling river-vine communities — small, close-knit settlements built along waterways, with a tradition of skilled craft and intimate geographic knowledge. Mall-Lowick comes from this world.

### The Thornvael — The Interior Corridor

The Thornvael occupies a long stretch of settled country running roughly north–south through the interior of the landmass. To the west lies the Greenwild — a vast, ancient, old-growth forest that covers much of the western interior. To the east, the land rises into upland valleys and the rocky hill country of the central mountain range — the same range that, seen from the Fenway coast, contains the Dwarven mountain holds.

The Thornvael road follows the corridor between the forest and the mountains, connecting a string of settlements from Kettleford in the north through Briar's Crossing to the smaller communities further south. The landscape is mixed deciduous forest in the lowlands, thinning into farmland and grazing country in the central corridor, with the eastern uplands rising into narrow valleys cut by streams feeding the Thornvael river.

The weather comes from the west — from the Greenwild — carrying the particular humidity of deep forest and the smell of leaf-rot and growing things. The sky is wide in the lowlands and narrow in the valleys. The soil in the lowlands is good; the soil in the upland valleys is better, if you know what you are doing.

**Key settlements and locations:**

- **Kettleford** — The largest settlement on the Thornvael road, where the road crosses the river. A proper market town with a constable (Aldra Venn), two inns, a smithy, and the kind of mixed-species population that a regional trading hub generates. The bridge is the only reliable crossing for twenty miles in either direction, making Kettleford simultaneously a chokepoint, a market, and a social centre. The twice-yearly Orcish trade days are the major commercial events of the season.

- **Briar's Crossing** — A crossroads settlement where the Thornvael road meets the track east to Ashgrove and the foothill communities. Smaller than Kettleford, rougher, with one inn (The Briar) and no constable. Disputes are settled by the innkeeper, Hollen Marsh, whose authority derives from being the largest person present and having been settling disputes for thirty years.

- **Greenwild Edge** — Not a settlement but a seasonal gathering point at the western edge of the lowland farms, where the Thornvael meets the Greenwild. Three markets per year: spring planting, midsummer, autumn harvest. The most recent market was half-shuttered — the wrongness is being felt.

- **Ashgrove** — A borderland settlement in the eastern foothills, mixed in the way that frontier places are mixed. Ix-Nambuth grew up here. The settlement has a blacksmith, a healer's cottage, and a community hall. The borderland Orcs came a generation ago from somewhere further east — beyond the mountain range, toward the eastern lands.

- **Mosshallow** — A Forest Gnome community in the edge-wood between the Thornvael lowlands and the deep Greenwild. Old — older than Kettleford, older than the road. Cognodec's birthplace. Zib Tumbletock lives nearby.

- **Hawthorn Valley** — A narrow upland valley east of the Greenwild (or, equivalently, in the western foothills of the central mountains), where Osctra spent six decades building his botanical laboratory. Now in ruins.

- **Dunmore** — A settlement on "the eastern road" — the route that crosses the central mountains and continues east toward the broader world. Gwenwyn's mentor Edda died on this road.

- **Millbrake, Hapworth, Thornhollow** — Smaller settlements along the Thornvael circuit. Villages where harvest suppers are held, music is played, and the wrongness is being felt through cancelled fairs and displaced wildlife.

**The Greenwild:**

The Greenwild is the dominant geographic feature of the western interior — a vast, ancient forest that is genuinely old and genuinely strange. Its ecology is being disrupted: something in the deep places is pushing the forest's predators outward, and the wave of displacement has been building for months. The edge-wood communities (Mosshallow and its neighbours) are the first line of contact between the settled lowlands and whatever is happening in the interior.

**The Orcish south:**

The Orcish merchants who trade at Kettleford and Greenwild Edge come "up from the southern lowlands." South of the Thornvael, the corridor opens into lower, broader country — the Orcish homeland or at least their commercial territory. This southern region is not detailed in the campaign documents but provides the Thornvael's primary external trade connection in that direction.

**Connection to the Fenway:**

The Thornvael's northern end — the area around Kettleford — is not far from the southern reaches of the Fenway's hill country. A traveller heading north from Kettleford and then northeast through the mountain passes would eventually reach the Fenway road somewhere near its eastern end, in the vicinity of Ashford. The "eastern road" through Dunmore likely connects to this route. The two regions are separated by the central mountains but connected by passes and trade routes.

### Corraith and the Thanelands — The Southeastern Coast

Corraith sits at the junction of the River Thane and the Mirrenmere, a broad tidal estuary that connects the city to the sea twelve miles to the south. The city is the commercial capital of the Thanelands, the broader political region that encompasses the southeastern portion of the landmass.

The river divides Corraith roughly in half: the western bank rises steeply to the bluffs where the noble estates and great temples occupy the high ground; the eastern bank spreads flat along the floodplain where the docks, warehouses, industrial works, and lower-city districts have accumulated over centuries of commerce. Population approximately 90,000.

The Thanelands' coast faces south and southeast, toward warmer and more sheltered waters than the Fenway's cold northern sea. The climate is still temperate — coal smoke, tidal flooding in bad winters, the yellow haze of an industrial city — but milder than the exposed northern shore.

**Corraith's dual coastline:**

The coastline continues north beyond Corraith, curving around the promontory toward the northern shore. This is the stretch where Milores's cottage sits — on a cliff above the sea, north of the city, with a north-facing window that provides the consistent, indirect painting light that artists in these latitudes prefer. A traveller following the coast north from Corraith would pass through increasingly rural country — Thornfield is three days' ride north — and would eventually, after many more days or weeks, reach the point where the coast bends westward and becomes the Fenway's northern shore. The sea route connecting Corraith to Kelstead Port runs this way: around the bend, hugging the coast, with the prevailing currents and the particular challenges of the coastal approaches that Mall-Lowick has spent years trying to chart.

**The city's quarters:**

- **Thorngate District** — High ground on the western bluffs. Ecclesiastical and aristocratic territory: great temples, noble estates, walled gardens. The Temple of Measured Light (Tanenni's institution) and the Temple of the Risen Flame (Darwhidan's family) are here. Security is private and professional.

- **Sullow Quarter** — Eastern bank, downriver from the harbour. Industrial territory: breweries, tanneries, chandleries, workshops. Bradkell's home ground. The Sullow Compact operates here — the mutual aid network that provides what the guilds have priced out of reach. The Charter Hall, the Guild of Chartered Commerce's district office, sits on Foundry Street like an architectural rebuke.

- **The Daerrow** — Floodplain south of the Sullow Quarter. The lowest ground in the city. Tiefling families, recent immigrants, the displaced. Milores grew up here. Madame Oressa's gaming houses on Lampblack Lane are the Daerrow's most significant informal employers.

- **The Harbour Quarter** — Waterfront where the River Thane meets the estuary. Wharves, warehouses, customs houses. The economic engine of the city.

- **The Bridgeway** — The district connecting the eastern and western banks. Commercial territory: shops, offices, the Candlewick Tavern where Garren Thule operates his brokerage.

**The Thanelands beyond Corraith:**

The Thanelands extend east and south from Corraith. The "eastern marches" are a politically active frontier — a succession question there has redirected military spending. Coastal cities to the south are in a trade dispute that is driving up import duties. The Thanelands' guild model — the Guild of Chartered Commerce, the licensing regime, the Warden-Inspectors — is a regional pattern, replicated with local variation across the Thanelands' cities. Ashford's Guild, far to the north on the Fenway coast, may be a distant cousin of this same institutional framework.

## The Central Mountains

The mountain range that runs through the interior of the landmass is the geographic spine that separates and connects the three campaign regions. It goes by no single name — different communities call it different things depending on which face they see.

- From the **Fenway coast**, looking south, these are the highlands beyond the ridge — the "wilder hill country" and the location of the **Dwarven mountain holds**, which are "several days' travel inland and south."

- From the **Thornvael**, looking east, these are the **eastern uplands** — rocky hill country with narrow valleys, rising into the foothills where Ashgrove and Hawthorn Valley sit.

- From **Corraith**, looking northwest, these are the interior highlands — remote, rural, the kind of country that produces travellers' tales and the occasional bounty notice for a Wood Elf farmer who threw a lord over a fence.

The mountains are rich in mineral content, well-watered by streams on both flanks, and old enough that the Dwarven holds built into their bones predate the human settlements on either side. The passes through the range connect the Fenway to the Thornvael and, ultimately, to the Thanelands — trade routes that carry Dwarven metalwork north to the coast and Orcish goods south to the lowlands.

## Climate and Hemisphere

The setting is in the **northern hemisphere** of its world. The evidence is consistent across all three campaigns:

- The **cold northern sea** of the Fenway (Campaign 1) — temperature increases southward, consistent with northern hemisphere.
- **Fungus grows on the north side of ash trees** (Campaign 2) — in the northern hemisphere, the north face of trees receives less direct sunlight and retains more moisture, favouring fungal and moss growth.
- **North-facing windows provide good painting light** (Campaign 3) — in the northern hemisphere, north-facing windows receive consistent, indirect daylight because the sun tracks across the southern sky. This is the traditional preference for artist studios in northern latitudes. Milores's cottage window is correct.
- **Sun sets in the west**, sky darkens from the east (Campaign 2) — universal, but consistent.
- The overall **temperate maritime-to-continental climate gradient** — wetter and cooler on the exposed northern coast, drier and more continental in the interior Thornvael — is consistent with a mid-latitude northern hemisphere landmass. Think of the climate range from northern Britain to central France.

## Cross-Campaign Connections

The three campaigns are set in the same world and the same broad region. Several threads connect them:

### Shared Rumours

Rumours that circulate in the taverns and hiring halls of all three regions suggest a shared information network — merchant shipping, overland traders, the kind of news that travels with cargo:

- **The grain blight.** Campaign 1 mentions "a blight on grain stores in two of the river towns, cause unclear." Campaign 2 mentions "a blight on grain stores in two of the southern market towns, cause unclear." These are almost certainly the same event, reported from different distances. From the Fenway, the affected towns are somewhere inland along the river routes. From the Thornvael, they are to the south — in the lowlands between the Thornvael and the Thanelands, or in the Orcish trading territory.

- **The trading company.** Campaign 1 mentions "a trading company that has been hiring heavily and quietly for reasons no one can confirm." Campaign 2 mentions "a trading company that has been buying forest timber rights aggressively and quietly." This may be the same company, operating across the region — hiring in the port towns and buying rights in the interior.

- **The succession dispute.** Campaign 1 mentions "a succession dispute in one of the eastern kingdoms." Campaign 3 mentions "a succession question in the Thanelands' eastern marches that has redirected military spending." These could be the same event — a political crisis in the eastern part of the Thanelands, which from the Fenway coast would be described as one of the "eastern kingdoms."

### Institutional Connections

- **The Guild system.** Ashford's Guild (Campaign 1), which oversees arcane practice and shut Orrath's forge, has structural parallels to the Guild of Chartered Commerce in Corraith (Campaign 3). Both are regulatory bodies that control commercial and arcane activity through licensing, fees, and enforcement. Whether they are branches of one institution, affiliates, or independent organisations that evolved from a common model is an open question — but the pattern is regional, not local.

- **The temple model.** The ecumenical port chapels of the Fenway (Campaign 1), the practical rural shrines of the Thornvael (Campaign 2), and the institutional temple-vaults of Corraith (Campaign 3) represent a gradient of religious organisation. The gods are the same; the institutions that serve them vary with the wealth and complexity of the community. A deity worshipped at the Brindlemark Chapel and at the Temple of Measured Light is the same deity, served at vastly different scales.

### Trade Routes

- **The sea route.** A vessel sailing south and east from Kelstead Port, around the coastal bend, would eventually reach the Mirrenmere and Corraith's harbour. This is a long voyage — weeks, not days — but it is the route that connects the Fenway's fishing and metalwork trade to the Thanelands' commercial economy. Akander's "southern coastal runs" (Campaign 1) likely operate on the early legs of this route.

- **The overland route.** From the Fenway, the Drover's Track south from Brindlemark leads into the hill country and, eventually, through the mountain passes to the Thornvael. From the Thornvael, the road continues south toward the Orcish lowlands and, by a longer route, east toward the Thanelands. Dwarven metalwork travels north to the Fenway coast; Orcish goods travel north to the Thornvael markets; finished goods from Corraith's manufactories travel in all directions.

- **The eastern road.** The road through Dunmore (Campaign 2) crosses the central mountains eastward. Where it goes beyond the mountains is not fully established, but it likely connects — eventually, through intervening territory — to the Thanelands' road network and ultimately to Corraith or its outlying towns.

## Notes on Cardinal Directions

All cardinal directions used in the three campaigns are internally consistent and consistent with each other under this unified geography. The following points are worth noting for reference:

- **The Fenway's "north"** is the sea. Always. The Fenway road runs east–west. South is uphill, toward the highlands and the mountains.

- **The Thornvael's "north"** is toward the Fenway coast (though the mountains intervene). The Thornvael road runs north–south. West is forest (the Greenwild). East is uplands (the mountains' western foothills). South is toward the Orcish lowlands.

- **Corraith's "south"** is the sea (via the Mirrenmere). North is inland, toward the central mountains and eventually the Fenway coast. The coast continues north of the city, curving around the promontory.

- **North-facing surfaces** behave as expected for the northern hemisphere: they receive less direct sunlight (good for painting; good for fungi), they are cooler and moister, and the associated cultural conventions (artist studios with north-facing windows) are correct.

## What This Document Does Not Establish

This geography is designed to be the most parsimonious arrangement that respects all canonical descriptions across all three campaigns. It does not establish:

- The name of the landmass, the northern sea, or the southern sea
- The political relationship between the Fenway region, the Thornvael, and the Thanelands (if any formal relationship exists)
- Whether the "eastern kingdoms" are part of the Thanelands, beyond them, or a separate political entity
- The full extent of the Greenwild or the Orcish territories to the south
- Whether any character from one campaign has heard of, visited, or has connections to the places in another campaign (beyond what the shared rumours imply)

These are available threads, not established facts. They exist to be pulled on if and when the campaigns warrant it.
