# DM Notes

---

## Campaign Tone

**Rural mystery in a grounded low-fantasy world.** The great powers exist -- they are distant and irrelevant. The party lives in the margins between villages: harvest suppers, forest paths, seasonal circuits, the places where the land speaks to people who listen. Something is wrong in the deep places of the forest, and no institution is coming to address it.

Stakes are ecological and communal. Displaced wildlife. Cancelled fairs. Villages going dark. The wrongness is patient and spreading, and the people best positioned to notice it are four wanderers who have been paying attention from different angles.

Morally coherent NPCs with practical concerns. Rural communities that function. The occasional brush with something ancient and Fey, and the growing sense that the forest has opinions.

---

## DM Principles

1. **Roll only when it matters.** Call for a roll only when both success and failure are plausible and interesting. Auto-succeed the trivial. Auto-fail the impossible. Narrate everything else.
2. **DC before dice.** Set the Difficulty Class before invoking the roll. Keep rulings honest.
3. **Show the work.** When a roll happens, display the DC, the result, and the modifier transparently.
4. **Heroic Inspiration.** Gwenwyn starts each adventuring day with Heroic Inspiration (Human: Resourceful). Ix-Nambuth's Inspiring Song grants Heroic Inspiration to allies who spend Hit Dice during a Short Rest where she plays. Track both sources explicitly.
5. **Track concentration.** Ix-Nambuth (Bless) and Gwenwyn (Faerie Fire, Sleep, or Guidance) are the primary concentrators. Default: **Ix-Nambuth = Bless; Gwenwyn = Faerie Fire.** Decide before each encounter, not during.
6. **Concentration saves on damage.** When a concentrating character takes damage, they must make a CON saving throw to maintain the spell. DC = 10 or half the damage taken, whichever is higher. **CRITICAL: Never skip this.** Roll immediately when a concentrating character is hit.
7. **Speak with Animals economy.** Three party members can speak to animals. Coordinate: Osctra reads behaviour first (no resource cost), Gwenwyn or Cognodec speak magically if needed. Do not overlap free casts on the same conversation.

| Character | Species | Class | Role | Alignment |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Cognodec | Forest Gnome | Warlock 1 | Utility Caster / Social Operator | Chaotic Good |
| Gwenwyn | Human | Bard 1 | Healer / Support / Social Operator | Chaotic Good |
| Ix-Nambuth | Orc | Paladin 1 | Frontliner / Buffer | Chaotic Good |
| Osctra | Wood Elf | Barbarian 1 | Frontliner / Damage | Chaotic Good |

**The party's moral axis:** All Chaotic Good, arrived from different starting points. They agree on what should be done; they disagree on how. No Lawful character serves as conscience. No selfish character calculates cost. The party's debates are about method, not morality -- and the absence of a brake means they may occasionally overspend on doing the right thing.

**The party's tactical shape:** Ix-Nambuth holds the line (AC 18, Topple, Bless). Osctra hits hard beside her (Rage, Topple, 17 HP). Gwenwyn supports from behind (Faerie Fire, Healing Word, Vicious Mockery). Cognodec provides range and utility (Eldritch Blast, Thorn Whip, Find Familiar). The Double Topple on a shared target is the party's signature control mechanism.

## Character Hooks & Threads

### Osctra

- **The destroyed farm.** Decades of cultivated research, a prize herd scattered, a greenhouse in ashes. He intends to rebuild every square foot. The immediate need: enough coin and stability to begin.
- **The bounty.** A local lord filed charges after Osctra dismantled his hunting party. The bounty is modest but real. It will surface when someone recognises him, or when the lord's reach extends further than expected. The lord's name and jurisdiction: develop when appropriate.
- **The field journal.** His meticulous documentation of displacement patterns is the party's most systematic record of the wrongness. Treat the journal as a physical object -- it can be consulted, shared, lost, or stolen.
- **The Orcish connection.** Decades of trade with Orcish merchants. He speaks Orcish out of professional respect. This gives him social access that the others lack.

### Ix-Nambuth

- **The oath.** Received in a forest clearing from something ancient and unhurried. She did not speak a vow she recalls. She considers the source to have had reasonable taste. The nature of the oath-giver and its relationship to the wrongness is a thread to develop slowly.
- **Zib Tumbletock.** Her teacher: elderly Gnomish musician, demanding, responsible for her technique and her Gnomish. Still alive, she believes. She tries not to worry. Zib is a potential NPC contact for information, aid, or danger.
- **The cancelled fairs.** She is taking the wrongness personally because it is erasing exactly the things her oath charges her to protect: gatherings, music, the communal fire. Track which villages on her circuit have gone dark and which have not.
- **The Gnomish thread.** She and Cognodec share Gnomish. Their private conversations have a different texture than their Common exchanges. Use this for moments of honesty that the full party does not witness.

### Gwenwyn

- **The old herbalist.** Her mentor, who taught her to listen. Died two winters ago on the eastern road. Gwenwyn still starts sentences addressed to her. The herbalist's teachings inform her approach to the natural world -- this is background texture, not a plot hook, unless it becomes one.
- **The road circuit.** Her comprehensive mental map of which roads are safe, which innkeepers are honest, and which estates to avoid is the party's social infrastructure for rural travel. Let her know things about places the party visits.
- **The wrongness from the road.** She noticed it three months ago -- earlier than anyone except Osctra. Her perspective is different: not displacement data but absence data. The fairs that weren't. The roads that went quiet. The news that stopped arriving from certain directions.
- **Luck.** She has excellent luck in a way that makes superstitious people uncomfortable. The Lucky feat (3 points/LR) is the mechanical expression of this. She has noticed it and occasionally exploits it.

### Cognodec

- **The patron.** The Green Advocate -- a Fey lord or lady whose name he received in a dream and suspects is an alias. The patron found him funny. The patron has not explained itself. The Book gets heavier when something is wrong. Develop the patron's nature and agenda slowly; it should feel like something with its own purposes that are not necessarily aligned with Cognodec's.
- **Charlatan to genuine.** The central question of his character: what happens when the performance becomes real? His Nature expertise was professional credentialing that became genuine knowledge. His patron's power arrived uninvited and is unmistakably real. He is still adjusting.
- **The Book of Shadows.** A physical object with its own qualities -- heavier when something is wrong, brass fittings that shift position, pages that open differently depending on mood. Treat it as a barometer for the wrongness and as a narrative instrument.
- **The familiar.** Find Familiar (Ritual) gives him an Owl or other scout. This is the party's primary mitigation for their critical Perception gap. Establish the familiar early and give it personality.

## Mechanical Reminders

### Double Topple (Osctra + Ix-Nambuth)
Both wield Topple weapons (Pitchfork DC 13 CON, Quarterstaff DC 13 CON). When both attack the same target, two CON saves per round. Prone = speed 0, Advantage for melee attackers within 5 ft, Disadvantage on target's attacks. **Call the shared target explicitly before the round begins.**

### Weapon Masteries
- **Topple (Osctra, Pitchfork / Ix-Nambuth, Quarterstaff):** On hit, CON save DC 13 or Prone.
- **Nick (Osctra, Light Hammers):** Extra Light-property attack as part of Attack action, freeing Bonus Action for Rage.
- **Slow (Ix-Nambuth, Javelin):** On hit, Speed reduced by 10 ft until start of Ix-Nambuth's next turn.

### Spells -- Key Notes
- **Healing Word (Gwenwyn):** Bonus Action, 60 ft range, **2d4 + 3 HP**. The party's only healing spell. Gwenwyn going into a fight without a slot for this is the party's most urgent resource crisis.
- **Faerie Fire (Gwenwyn):** Concentration, DEX save DC 13, 20-ft Cube. Advantage on all attack rolls against outlined targets. Default Concentration choice in combat.
- **Vicious Mockery (Gwenwyn):** WIS save DC 13, 1d6 Psychic, Disadvantage on target's next attack. Use defensively to protect concentrating allies.
- **Bless (Ix-Nambuth):** Concentration, up to 3 creatures gain +1d4 to attacks and saves. Default: Osctra, Ix-Nambuth, Gwenwyn.
- **Searing Smite (Ix-Nambuth):** Bonus Action after a hit, 1d6 Fire recurring, CON save DC 12 each turn.
- **Eldritch Blast (Cognodec):** 120 ft, 1d10 Force. Primary ranged damage.
- **Thorn Whip (Cognodec):** 30 ft melee spell attack, 1d6 Piercing + pull 10 ft. Re-engages fleeing enemies.
- **Armor of Agathys (Cognodec):** 5 THP, 5 Cold damage to melee attackers. Lasts 1 hour, no Concentration.
- **Hellish Rebuke (Cognodec):** Reaction, DEX save DC 13, 2d10 Fire. Slot cost -- coordinate with Armor of Agathys.

### Rage (Osctra)
Bonus Action. Resistance to B/P/S, +2 melee damage, Advantage on STR checks/saves. **Ends if no attack or damage taken since last turn.** Cannot cast or concentrate on spells while raging. 2/LR, regain 1 on Short Rest.
