# Campaign Setup — Party 2 (Bullet Reference)

---

## Part One: Before the Party

### Cognodec — The Mystic Who Became Real

- Race: Forest Gnome.
- Born in **Mosshallow** — Forest Gnome community in the edge-wood between the Thornvael and the Greenwild. Settlement shifts slightly with the seasons; most outsiders walk past without registering.
- Fourth of seven children, none particularly magical.
- Core talent: reading what people expected to see, and providing it.
- Left Mosshallow at 43 (young for a Gnome) with Minor Illusion, a borrowed coat, and a working hypothesis.
- Career: filled the gap of "genuine Forest Mystic" in human and mixed-species settlements of the Thornvael lowlands. Consultations with forest spirits, blessing ceremonies, guided encounters with nature's wisdom — all using excellent illusions and well-timed sound effects.
  - Forgery Kit: produced testimonials from Forest Councils that may or may not have existed.
  - Speak with Animals (the real gift, Gnomish birthright): kept entirely out of the performance. Unwilling to cheapen it.
- Good at this. Consistent, careful, almost never wrong about what people needed to hear.
- Circuit: small towns along the Thornvael road — **Millhaven**, **Briar's Crossing**, **Kettleford**, and the seasonal market at **Greenwild Edge**. Known and trusted as travelling specialists are trusted: limited information, considerable goodwill.

**The Patron (~1 year ago):**
- Woke to find his working notebook replaced by a bound Book that felt different in each hand, opened to different pages depending on mood, contained unlearned cantrips and rituals in unsupplied ink.
- A voice in a dream gave a name he suspects is an alias. Refers to the patron as *the Green Advocate* in notes and performances.
- Has begun to wonder whether naming a thing gives it a reality it did not previously have.

**Recent developments (6 weeks ago):**
- The animals began speaking differently — not in vocabulary, but in tenor. Fear without object. Displacement without destination.
- The Book seems heavier lately.
- The patron has not commented.

### Gwenwyn — The Road's Own Instrument

- Race: Human.
- Born on a cart (literally, though the cart wasn't going anywhere interesting). Does not remember parents with much clarity. Early memories: city streets — **Kettleford**'s lower market, back alleys of **Briar's Crossing** — learning which doors to knock on and which to read from a distance.
- Fell in with travelling performers young. Drifted between lowland settlements with people who needed another pair of hands and didn't ask questions.

**Edda (the herbalist):**
- Found Gwenwyn on the eastern road near **Dunmore**. Changed everything.
- Spoke to plants when harvesting, apologised to animals she put down, maintained the world had a great deal to say if you'd stop making noise.
- Gwenwyn absorbed this selectively: speaks to animals constantly, plays the shawm at battlefield volume. But something from those years took root.
- Edda died two winters ago on the eastern road between Dunmore and the Thornvael. Gwenwyn buried her in a birch copse, sat three days, then went back to work.

**Current circuit (past two years):**
- **Kettleford**, **Millhaven**, **Greenwild Edge**, farming hamlets along the Thornvael road, winter gatherings at **Briar's Crossing**.
- Plays dancing music, passes news village to village.
- Comprehensive mental map of: safe roads by season, worthwhile innkeepers, estates to avoid.
- Checks sleeping animals. Keeps notes.

**Recent developments (3 months ago):**
- Noticed wildlife moving wrong. Prey heading the wrong direction. Predators outside usual ranges.
- Noted it, kept moving. It did not resolve.

### Ix-Nambuth — The Viol and the Oath

- Race: Orc.
- Grew up in **Ashgrove** — borderland settlement in the foothills east of the Thornvael. Mixed enough that nobody quite belonged to anything, so everyone decided for themselves what they were.
- Decided early she was not going to be what people assumed when they looked at an Orc her size. Has been refining that decision since.
- Found performance because music crossed borders her appearance did not.
- First played for coin at 17 (a drum, Briar's Crossing, badly). Something in the crowd's response told her this was worth serious pursuit.

**Zib Tumbletock:**
- Elderly Gnomish musician. Exacting opinions about technique. Found Ix-Nambuth in a roadside inn between Ashgrove and Kettleford.
- Had a second-hand viol and the conviction that beauty was a serious undertaking and the world required more.
- Corrected her technique with patience. Introduced her to the idea that music was worth doing for its own sake, not just as a tool.
- Ix-Nambuth absorbed this completely. Zib is why she speaks Gnomish, plays the way she does, and is one of approximately three reasons she's not a completely different person.
- Zib lives (she believes) in a cottage near **Mosshallow** in the edge-wood.

**The Oath (3 years ago):**
- In a clearing in the Greenwild, stopped to sleep between villages. Woke to something watching — not a creature, more a quality of attention. Ancient, unhurried, entirely certain.
- Did not speak any vow she can recall. Woke the next morning understanding one had been made.
- Has never fully resolved this theologically. The power is real. The weight of what it asks is real. Has decided whatever claimed her had reasonable taste.

**Current circuit (past several years):**
- **Kettleford**, **Millhaven**, **Greenwild Edge**, harvest suppers, midwinter fires in foothill communities near Ashgrove.
- Known in these places. Not always welcomed warmly at first, but always welcomed back.

**Recent trigger:** Arrived at a village on the expected date to find the green empty — no stalls, no fire, no music. Began paying attention differently.

### Osctra — The Farmer with the Pitchfork

- Race: Wood Elf.
- Born into a small Wood Elf community at the head of **Hawthorn Valley** — remote frontier valley in the uplands east of the Greenwild.
- Most kin drifted toward ancient forests or the wider world. Osctra stayed — not from inability to leave, but because the soil was exceptional and there was work to do.
- Over ~60 years: transformed a modest plot into a living laboratory of botanical cultivation, centred on a magically-enhanced strain of Orcish root tubers. Variety known for extraordinary yield, remarkable shelf life, maddening sensitivity to growing conditions. Demanded constant attention; Osctra provided it.
- Decades of twice-yearly trading at the valley's foot. Regular contact with Orcish merchants from the lowland market at **Kettleford** and seasonal exchange at **Greenwild Edge**. Found their exacting quality standards oddly agreeable.
- Learned Orcish out of professional necessity; kept speaking it because Orcish has more precise agricultural vocabulary than Elvish.
- Never social exactly, but known: fair dealer, patient neighbour, descended from Hawthorn Valley twice a year with a cartload and expected to be left alone otherwise.

**The wrongness (months of buildup):**
- Wildlife wrong for months — predators in wrong ranges, frightened, disoriented.
- Documented in field journal with growing precision. Kept chalking incidents up to seasonal disruption. It did not settle.

**The Lord Torth incident (a few weeks ago):**
- **Lord Vaylen Torth** — local lord of inherited land, money, and confidence — rode into Hawthorn Valley with armoured knights for a hunting party.
- They flushed a displaced predator (large, frightened, well outside its territory) directly through Osctra's property.
- Beast demolished his greenhouse. Knights demolished his fences. Horses demolished 200 years of carefully maintained botanical beds.
- Osctra requested compensation. The lord laughed and called him a "dirt-grubbing peasant."
- Osctra went very quiet, picked up his pitchfork, and systematically dismantled the entire hunting party. Did not kill anyone. Did not need to. Threw the lord bodily over his own ruined fence and explained exactly how many individual plants had been destroyed and what he expected in restitution.
- Lord Torth filed a complaint from his sickbed. There is now a modest bounty on Osctra's head.

## Part Two: How They Came Together

**Osctra's departure from Hawthorn Valley:**
- Left with pitchfork, pickling-barrel shield, field journals, nothing else worth carrying.
- Farm in ruin, prize herd scattered across four valleys, modest but real bounty.
- Went south toward the Thornvael road because the road went somewhere and the valley no longer did.

**Gwenwyn found Osctra:**
- Good at finding people who don't want to be found, and at being present when they grudgingly admit they could use someone.
- Had been tracking the same wrongness Osctra documented.
- Heard about a Wood Elf farmer with a bounty and a field journal full of observations. Adjusted her circuit.
- Caught up at **Briar's Crossing** (inn where the Thornvael road meets the track south to Ashgrove).
- He wasn't looking for company. She offered a comparison of notes, not company.
- His field journal + her road observations described the same phenomenon from two angles: something pushing the forest's predators outward from the deep places, doing so for months.
- He didn't invite her to travel with him. She traveled in the same direction at the same pace and he didn't object. This was as close to an invitation as Osctra has ever produced.

**Ix-Nambuth:**
- Circling the same question from her own angle. Three villages cancelled seasonal gatherings in one month (animal attacks, unsafe roads, fear).
- Heard through the circuit about the farmer and the shawm player heading south together, and the farmer's journals.
- Found them at the **Greenwild Edge** market (what remained of it — half-shuttered, traders nervous, festival atmosphere replaced by watchful quiet).
- Arrived with viol case and quarterstaff. Osctra looked at her, looked at the quarterstaff, looked at the viol case, said nothing. Gwenwyn made a sideways introduction. Ix-Nambuth sat down.

**Cognodec arrived last** (will tell you this was not by design; Gwenwyn suspects otherwise):
- Had been working the Thornvael circuit as usual but finding the performance increasingly difficult to maintain — not because his skills diminished, but because the animals were genuinely afraid, the Book was genuinely heavier, and people's questions were no longer answerable with illusions and reassuring words.
- Needed real answers. Heard about people collecting them.
- Appeared at Greenwild Edge market on the second morning with precise theatrical assurance.
- Osctra assessed him with eighty years of bullshit-detection calibrated by Orcish merchants.
- Gwenwyn saw both the presentation and the actual person.
- Ix-Nambuth noticed his Gnomish — too fluent in some registers, oddly stilted in others — and filed it.

**The pooling of information (second evening, Gwenwyn's suggestion):**
- Osctra: field journal.
- Gwenwyn: road notes.
- Ix-Nambuth: cancelled fairs and the geography of absence.
- Cognodec: animal testimony — without theatrical embellishment — and the room went quiet.
- Emerging picture: something wrong in the deep places of the Greenwild, spreading outward, for longer than any of them had realised.

**Current status:** Heading south along the Thornvael road toward the edge of the Greenwild. Have not discussed how long they will travel together. Have not needed to.

## Part Three: Specific Questions Resolved

### The Zib Tumbletock / Mosshallow Connection

- Zib (Ix-Nambuth's music teacher) lives near Mosshallow (Cognodec's birthplace).
- Mosshallow is small and Gnomish communities are close-knit. Reasonable probability Zib knew Cognodec's family or knew of them.
- Neither Ix-Nambuth nor Cognodec has made the geographic connection yet.
- When they do, questions arise: what does Zib know of the Greenwild's condition? Has Mosshallow been affected? Have edge-wood communities seen what lowland settlements have seen?
- Cognodec has family in Mosshallow. Has not thought about this in some time. Will need to.

### The Oath and the Patron

- Ix-Nambuth's oath: came to her in a clearing in the Greenwild.
- Cognodec's patron (the Green Advocate): a Fey entity connected to the forest.
- May be the same source, different aspects of the same source, or entirely unrelated.
- Neither character has shared enough detail for the connection to surface yet.
- When it does: what is paying attention in the Greenwild, and what does it want from both of them?
- The Book grows heavier when something is wrong. The oath charged Ix-Nambuth to protect what's going dark. Both respond to the same phenomenon. Either coincidence or design; neither is naive enough to assume coincidence without evidence.

### The Shared Circuit

- Cognodec, Gwenwyn, and Ix-Nambuth have all been working the same rural circuit for years.
- Almost certainly at the same events (harvest suppers, fairs, midwinter gatherings) without forming a relationship.
- Realistic: itinerant performers/specialists in rural areas overlap without connecting, especially when roles are complementary not competitive.
  - Ix-Nambuth plays things that produce silence.
  - Gwenwyn plays things people dance to.
  - Cognodec performs consultations in private.
- They have been parallel lines. The wrongness is what made them converge.
- Osctra (descending twice a year to trade) would have been present at Kettleford market and Greenwild Edge exchange. Would remember Ix-Nambuth (an Orc with a viol is memorable) without having spoken to her. She would remember him as the enormous silent Elf with the cartload of root tubers. Same spaces, different worlds.

### Party Finances

- **Osctra (18 GP):** What he had when he left Hawthorn Valley. Real wealth was in farm, herd, and botanical library — all destroyed, scattered, or inaccessible. Has a pitchfork, a shield, and strong opinions about compensation.
- **Gwenwyn (25 GP):** Road earnings. Modest, consistent, carefully managed. Knows the cost of every inn on the Thornvael road.
- **Ix-Nambuth (22 GP):** Performance fees from the rural circuit. Spending less since fairs started cancelling.
- **Cognodec (30 GP):** Consultation fees plus occasional Charlatan creative accounting. Largest liquid holdings — says something about the economics of performed mysticism vs. genuine skill.
- No one destitute, no one comfortable. Correct financial pressure for adventure start.

### The Bounty on Osctra

- Lord Torth's bounty: modest. Enough to motivate an opportunistic constable, not enough for a professional bounty hunter.
- Legal nuisance, not mortal threat.
- Means Osctra cannot return to Hawthorn Valley without resolving it.
- Technically a fugitive in whatever jurisdiction Lord Torth's writ runs in.
- Party is aware:
  - Gwenwyn: minor complication.
  - Ix-Nambuth: the lord's behaviour is an offence against what she's sworn to protect.
  - Cognodec: already constructing a narrative in which Osctra is the wronged party (which has the advantage of being true).

## Part Four: Open Threads

- **The Wrongness.** What is driving the forest's predators outward? What is wrong in the Greenwild's interior? Duration: Osctra's journals suggest months; animals Cognodec speaks to suggest longer.
- **The Green Advocate.** Who/what is Cognodec's patron? Is the name genuine? What does it want? Why did it choose a charlatan?
- **The Oath's Source.** What claimed Ix-Nambuth in the clearing? Connected to the Green Advocate? To the wrongness? What does it expect?
- **Zib Tumbletock and Mosshallow.** What does the edge-wood look like now? Has the wrongness reached the Gnomish communities? Is Zib alive and well?
- **Lord Torth and the Bounty.** Bounty is a legal problem. Torth is a political problem. His hunting party flushed a displaced predator through settled land — suggesting either ignorance of or indifference to the wrongness. Either is relevant.
- **Osctra's Scattered Herd.** Decades of cultivated livestock dispersed across four valleys. Still out there. He will want them back.
- **Edda.** Gwenwyn's herbalist mentor, dead two winters ago on the eastern road. Buried in a birch copse. No thread yet — only a buried woman — but Gwenwyn still starts sentences addressed to her, and the gap she left has a shape other things could fill.
- **Cognodec's Family.** Seven siblings in Mosshallow. Has not been home in decades. Has not thought about this. Will need to.
