# Pre-Campaign Vignettes — Party 1 (Bullet Reference)

*Six scenes, one per character pair, in chronological order across the campaign setup events.*

---

## I. Gilda + Mall-Lowick

**Setting:** A room in a chandler's house, Kelstead Port — three days before *The Grey Cormorant* departed.

**Gilda's details:**
- Rented the room from a chandler who owed her a favour (wax consignment dispute).
- Spellbook on the desk (closed), beside brass travelling inkwell and small locked case.
- Quarterstaff in the corner: dark hardwood, carved head — arcane focus, not a walking stick. She treats magic as an instrument for specific purposes.
- Six rings across both hands (gold, iron, one electrum-looking) — emergency currency, not decoration. Carried where it can't be separated from her.
- Maps already on the table, weighted with inkwell, brass compass, and a small lock kept for the purpose.

**Mall-Lowick's details:**
- 2.5 feet tall. Slight, quick-eyed, curly brown hair escaping its leather tie. Three ink-stained fingers (right hand, first joint), smudge on left wrist (unnoticed).
- Studded leather armour: muted green-brown (outdoor wear).
- Map case: enormous, leather-sealed, weather-proofed, slung across back. Contains several years of professional life.

**Scene content:**
- Gilda immediately raises the secondary harbour entrance dispute.
- Mall-Lowick counters: magnetic variation. Gilda counters: 400 yards of variation in a tidal approach is dangerous.
- Mall-Lowick says she recommended re-survey with a reference instrument at the time. Gilda concedes the point without ceremony (acknowledges, files, moves on — no softening, no apology). Mall-Lowick has come to find this more honest than alternatives.
- Gilda offers the northern run contract: full coastal profile, both approaches, same rate minus outstanding balance on the three disputed sections.
- Mall-Lowick notes that the route takes her past the Cormorant Stack twice (formation she's been trying to get correct bearing on for 18 months).
- Mall-Lowick accepts without extended deliberation.

**What the scene establishes:**
- Their professional dynamic: disagreement as mutual respect. Gilda concedes without ceremony; Mall-Lowick values this honesty.
- Both are precise, pragmatic professionals.
- The disputed map points are genuinely unresolved and still referenced.
- Mall-Lowick's personal motivation (the Cormorant Stack) aligns with the contract.

## II. Cade + Mall-Lowick

**Setting:** *The Grey Cormorant*, day four — passing the Cormorant Stack.

**Scene content:**
- The formation has no agreed name (Mall-Lowick: Cormorant Stack; Gilda's chart: Breaker's Head — Mall-Lowick considers this wrong for three reasons). Long, ragged dark stone cliff dropping straight to sea, carved into four sea arches at the waterline. Largest ~40 ft span, smallest a crack of light.
- Mall-Lowick is two-thirds up the port shrouds, one foot on a ratline, one hand gripping a stay, working with oilskin paper and charcoal. Left her longbow on deck (practical concession, made without being asked).
- Cade stands below, watching without calling up (learned that shouting makes people rush). Has assessed the distance and chosen presence over intervention.
- Mall-Lowick calls down asking him to sight along his arm for a baseline measurement — first arch to fourth, degrees off the bowsprit.
- Cade sights: "Twenty-one, twenty-two degrees. Port." He has the navigator's habit of thinking in angles.
- She notes he's done this before. He says ships require it.
- She tells him to stop watching her. He says he knows, keeps watching.
- She recognises he's not overcautious — he's just there, having made a quiet decision about where he should be.
- She descends; Cade has moved to a position below her on the ratlines without making anything of it. She didn't need him. He knew. He was there anyway.
- She can triangulate from this data and her two-year-old reference for a reliable bearing.

**What the scene establishes:**
- Cade's protective instinct: watchful, non-intrusive, not patronising. Presence over intervention.
- Mall-Lowick's competence and independence. She doesn't need help but accepts Cade's presence without friction.
- Practical cooperation: his navigation skills complement her cartography.
- The Cormorant Stack as a real cartographic goal.

## III. Cade + Gilda

**Setting:** Below decks, *The Grey Cormorant*, day seven.

**Scene content:**
- Cade is treating **Pell** (cook's mate, 19-20, human, dark-haired) for a cut hand in the mess. Using his Priest's Pack (salve, cloth, instruments, infection vial). Not invoking magic — just talking, treating with practised efficiency. Also finds and treats a grease burn on Pell's forearm that Pell hadn't mentioned.
- Gilda arrives for her manifests. Observes the scene.
- After Pell leaves, exchange between Cade and Gilda:
  - "He's not your crew." / "No."
  - "You're not being paid for it." / "No."
  - Gilda presses for a complete answer.
  - Cade: "I did it because it mattered that he suffer less. That's the whole of it." Said without heat, as a fact. Something he stopped defending long ago.
- Gilda returns to manifests, makes a margin note.
- Later: "I find that position genuinely difficult to locate a flaw in. That isn't the same as agreeing with it."
- Cade: "I know." Expected nothing different, found it entirely acceptable.
- Gilda found this more interesting than anticipated. Filed it, did not discard it.

**What the scene establishes:**
- Cade's motivations: compassion without performance or expectation of reciprocity.
- Gilda's analytical nature: observes carefully, speaks little about observations, processes through a commercial/logical lens.
- Their developing dynamic: Gilda intellectually engaged by Cade's worldview without yet agreeing with it.
- Gilda recognises something genuine and files it as significant.

## IV. Cade + Rixon

**Setting:** *The Grey Cormorant*, night watch, day ten. Overcast, moonless.

**Scene content:**
- Rixon is forward, dealing with a badly-coiled line in the dark. Chain mail shifting. Longsword at left hip, two handaxes at right. Thick-fingered, calloused hands (both hammer and sword angles).
- Cade stands at the rail 20 ft aft, watching the water. Looking north-east at a restlessness in the cloud — not lightning exactly. Something he's learned to attend to from childhood below the ridge where autumn storms came off the highlands.
- The line catches. Rixon swears in Giant. Uses a Greymantle-dialect phrase — a kitchen/forge-room construction about the ancestry of inanimate objects. Not conscious; it was Orrath's language for frustration, and it became Rixon's.
- Cade goes completely still. Rixon feels the stillness from 20 ft away.
- Cade looks at Rixon. Expression: not surprise, not recognition — something between them. Something private, not yet decided on.
- "Something wrong?" / "No."
- Pause. "Where did you learn your Giant?" / "A smith. In Ashford. Why?"
- Cade: "That's Greymantle phrasing. That particular construction. It's specific to Greymantle Keep. I know it because my father used it."
- Rixon: "The smith was from Greymantle Keep."
- Cade nods slowly — confirmation rather than surprise, permitting the thought to be real. Does not ask further. Looks back to sea.
- They finish the watch in silence. Something has altered in how they occupy the same space — incremental, unnamed, known to both.

**What the scene establishes:**
- The Rixon/Cade/Orrath/Greymantle thread surfacing for the first time.
- Cade recognises the dialect through his father's contact with Greymantle traders.
- Neither pushes the connection. It remains unnamed but acknowledged.
- The bond forming: shared distant origin, recognised through language.

## V. Gilda + Rixon

**Setting:** Thornwater quay, two hours after Vetha's detention.

**Scene content:**
- Gilda reads the impound seal (Thornwater red wax, Harbour-Masters' mark) from 15 feet without approaching — approaching would appear in a clerk's ledger.
- Moves to harbour wall (correct position for appearing to watch boats). Actually watching: warehouses, impound seal, distance to market gate, light quality.
- Rixon already there, leaning on the wall, cloak over chain mail, positioned with the winch housing in peripheral vision. Handaxes covered. Appears unremarkable; clearly is not.
- Exchange:
  - "Two parties." / "I counted two. There may be a third. Someone added to my manifest. Not me." / "How certain?" / "Entirely."
  - Rixon: man by the winch housing has been there an hour, not a dockworker.
  - Gilda: woman with a cart has moved it three times without loading anything.
- Gilda's assessment: cargo is legitimate and documentable, but a magistrate's process in a town where she has no relationships takes a week minimum, and by then the additional consignment will be associated with their vessel.
- "You've already thought about exits." — "There's a track west that doesn't cross the main road. Your cartographer will know the terrain."
- Gilda reached the same conclusion simultaneously. Finds this reassuring.
- "She'll know it. I'll find her." / "The Goliath?" / "Will follow without needing to be asked."
- Two hours, before the watch changes. They separate, walking in different directions to find the others.

**What the scene establishes:**
- Gilda and Rixon's complementary skills: she reads commercial/political situations, he reads physical/security situations.
- Both arrive at the same operational conclusions independently and simultaneously.
- Mutual professional respect based on competence.
- They already know how the other two will respond (Mall-Lowick knows the terrain; Cade will follow).
- The party's decision to leave Thornwater was coordinated and fast.

## VI. Rixon + Mall-Lowick

**Setting:** Overland track, coastal scrub north of the Fenway road — day two of three (the back route from Thornwater).

**Scene content:**
- Rough ground: scrub grass, wind-bent oak, wet limestone. Main road a mile south and below. They can hear carts distantly.
- Mall-Lowick moves through scrub efficiently — Halfling in studded leather the colour of dead bracken, makes no sound of consequence. Longbow almost as long as she is tall. Map case beside it. Charcoal in right hand, notes open in left.
- Rixon behind her, making more noise (chain mail in undergrowth). Has stopped consulting his road map two hours ago in favour of watching where she's going.
- She wants 10 minutes at the ridge crest for a western bearing on the Fenway confluence.
- He objects: a ridge is poor for someone being tracked.
- She counters: 45 seconds of profile, then fast treeline on the north side.
- Negotiation: she asks 10 min, he says 5, she says 7 (two reference points minimum), they settle on 6.5.
- She diagnoses from his gait that he hasn't eaten. He is in fact hungry.
- She works: charcoal in short certain strokes, Halfling hands making reference marks with mechanical precision. View opens west — Fenway valley, river, woodsmoke, the road distantly.
- "Where are we headed after Kelstead?" / "West. Contract at Brindlemark."
- She has notes on that country, not as complete as she'd like. Was planning a proper survey this spring; other things intervened.
- At six minutes she finishes. He's already moving toward the treeline. She's on his heels in three steps.
- They go down the north slope into the trees. The ridge is empty behind them — she has now correctly placed it on her map for the first time.
- Pace picks up naturally in the trees. They make the river crossing before dark.

**What the scene establishes:**
- Practical dynamic between Rixon (security-focused) and Mall-Lowick (cartography-focused).
- They negotiate efficiently: neither dominates, both adjust.
- Mall-Lowick's observation skills extend beyond maps (reads his physical state from gait).
- She documents terrain even while on the run.
- Their skills complement: his awareness of threats, her knowledge of terrain.
- The stop gives something back rather than costing something — her mapping and his eating both had value.
