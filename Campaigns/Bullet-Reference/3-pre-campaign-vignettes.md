# Pre-Campaign Vignettes — Party 3 (Bullet Reference)

*Six scenes, one per character pair, set during prior operations, recruitment meetings, and intervals between work.*

---

## I. Bradkell + Darwhidan

**Setting:** Back room of The Millrace tavern, industrial quarter — nine days before the Aldworth job. Smell of hops, tallow, damp plaster over old brick. Single candle, two untouched mugs.

**Scene content:**
- Bradkell is already seated (unusual — Darwhidan normally arrives first to control the geometry). In working clothes: rough canvas jacket, dusty boots, broad hands. Looks like he repairs drays for a living. Eyes doing something entirely different.
- He has a hand-drawn floor plan of the east wing. Two access points: garden terrace (warded) and servants' corridor off the kitchen (not warded, but passes within 15 ft of butler's quarters — butler sleeps light).
- Darwhidan has already seen the east wing. Walked through it 6 days ago (fine clothes, prop notebook, mild scholarly confusion) while a steward showed him to a drawing room he had no intention of reaching. Mapped corridor widths by counting strides, identified lock manufacturer from maker's mark at the keyhole.
- Darwhidan identifies a third access point not on the plan: second-floor window above the garden wall. Original frame, older than the terrace lock. Hook-and-pin latch — openable from outside with a dagger blade in under 4 seconds. Drop to garden wall: ~18 ft. Acceptable (for him).
- Bradkell recalculates: second-floor approach changes the timing. Garden patrol needs to have passed the east wall before the climb, meaning Milores holds position at the servants' gate for an additional 4 minutes.
- "The window is better than the door. That's the whole of it."
- Bradkell asks for step-by-step walkthrough to build contingencies.
- "I can tell you what I'll do. I can't always tell you why one route feels right and another doesn't." / "Tell me the what. I'll build the why."

**What the scene establishes:**
- Bradkell's method: systematic, framework-based, builds contingencies, translates instinct into architecture.
- Darwhidan's method: intuitive, observational, physical assessment. Reads spaces by being in them. Can't always explain in repeatable-framework terms.
- Their complementary dynamic: Darwhidan provides the physical intelligence, Bradkell builds the operational model around it.
- Planning process: detailed, collaborative, each contributing differently.

## II. Bradkell + Milores

**Setting:** Flat rooftop overlooking Candlers' Row — forty minutes before a job. Cracked clay tile, tar patches. Smell of candle wax, chimney smoke, cold mineral edge of a clear night. City spread below in lantern-light.

**Scene content:**
- Milores is sitting against the chimney stack, spear beside her, painting.
- Canvas no larger than her hand. Cadmium yellow over Prussian blue ground — a coastal sunset against a cliff edge seen once, remembered precisely. Wooden case: compact, worn at clasps, book-sized.
- She heard Bradkell on the ladder and tracked his approach since the alley below. He sits down, does not look at the painting. Has learned the painting occupies a private space: visible, unmentioned, understood. Correct response: leave it alone until she moves the boundary.
- Operational update: "Darwhidan's in position. Tanenni's in the garden. Patrol passed the east gate two minutes ago."
- Milores (still painting): "I heard them. Heavy boots. Three guards, not two. The third is new — his stride is shorter. Recently added to the rotation."
- Third guard not in the plan. Bradkell had timing built around two.
- Milores's assessment: doesn't change the approach, changes the withdrawal. Rotation interval drops from 8 minutes to 6. Two minutes lost on the extract.
- Adjustment: Tanenni pulls the animal distraction from the garden 4 minutes earlier.
- Her painting case is closed with deliberate care (same care as everything). Spear picked up. She rolls her shoulders once — arrives rather than warms up.
- "Your plan is sound." (She says this rarely; means it when she says it.)
- She moves toward the roof edge, drainpipe, deepest shadows. He watches: a professional within her code, moving toward work inside acceptable parameters.

**What the scene establishes:**
- Milores's painting practice: private, precise, the cottage dream made physical. Subject: coastal cliff, north-facing light.
- Her sensory precision: identifies a third guard by stride pattern, diagnoses the operational impact immediately.
- Bradkell's respect for her boundaries (the painting) and her expertise (accepts her tactical adjustments).
- Their dynamic: he plans, she identifies what the plan missed, they adjust together.
- She validates his plan explicitly — rare and meaningful.

## III. Bradkell + Tanenni

**Setting:** Temple archive, after hours — three weeks before the current job. Underground stone room with vaulted ceiling, iron bracket candles, cool permanent damp. Floor-to-ceiling shelves: ledgers, parchments, sealed folios (blue wax temple stamp). 12 years of accumulated records.

**Scene content:**
- Tanenni moves through the archive without hesitation (she's mapped it). Temple robes, brass scales catching candlelight. Holy symbol at chest — large enough to see from across a room (credential, not devotion).
- She pulls a folio from the fourth shelf, west wall. Contains a property deed she has altered — two lines changed with ink matching the original so precisely the alteration is invisible unless you know where to look.
- Bradkell arrives through the side entrance (archive to root cellar — a passage Tanenni showed him on his second visit). Smells of fermented grain (permanent condition; camouflage in the industrial quarter).
- Bradkell's coded journal: notes on the **Aldworth estate**. He's been through public tax records, guild-house levy schedules, and declared charitable contributions for 3 years. Found the gap: declared income is ~60% of actual; the difference moves through channels not in any public ledger.
- "The numbers don't match." / "I know." / "How long have you known?" / "Since before the crew existed, in this specific case."
- Aldworth family has used the temple's blessed vaults since at least the year Tanenni was assigned to the archive. She identified the discrepancy in year two. Did not act then — lacked infrastructure. Now she has it.
- She describes intelligence: pigeons (not sparrows — sparrows cover roof approaches) roost on the estate's east wall with regular sight lines to garden and servants' entrance. Report movement patterns (arrival/departure times, guards visible at gate by interval).
- "You built the intelligence network before you built the crew." / "The animals were already there. I learned to listen to them. The crew arrived after the intelligence made it clear that the work was viable."
- She draws the parallel: "You built the operational theory before you found the crew. The sequence is the same."
- Bradkell's assessment: she has been doing this longer, with less infrastructure, with better information, and has never mentioned it as leverage. She communicates conclusions, not the path. He has to ask.
- She describes the Aldworth vault: animal intelligence + ledger confirmation + probable ward structure (based on the estate's contract with a guild-arcanist whose name appears in temple disbursement records).

**What the scene establishes:**
- Tanenni's long game: identifying targets years before acting, building infrastructure independently.
- Her intelligence network: animals as reliable informants (sparrows for roofs, pigeons for estate walls, rats for basements).
- The altered property deed: her calligraphic skill is indistinguishable from originals.
- Their planning dynamic: both excellent, both precise, neither concedes without evidence. Most productive and least comfortable meetings.
- Bradkell recognises her as the crew's most reliable member. She has noted this.

## IV. Darwhidan + Milores

**Setting:** Safe house on Roper Street, above a chandler's shop — two hours after the Brevary job. Rendered fat and tallow smell. One room: table, two chairs, unused straw pallet, window overlooking alley (Darwhidan identified this as best egress before anything else).

**Scene content:**
- Milores is examining her hands: each finger, knuckle, callus. Post-operation ritual. Hands: large, scarred across the backs, calloused from two kinds of precision (breaking and painting). Cadmium yellow residue from afternoon's work, newer darkness at right palm heel from striking a guard's neck 4 hours ago.
- Darwhidan at the window. Fine clothes slightly disordered (thread pulled at shoulder, knee scuff from the wall). Coat open, prop notebook in pocket. Gold eyes half-closed — looks like relaxation, is actually the post-job state where body is still running on adrenaline and mind hasn't caught up.
- Their post-operation silence is different from other pairs: not people choosing not to speak, not tired, not processing. Two people who both paid the right attention during the work and don't need to debrief.

**Exchange (minimal, reference-point style):**
- "The second guard." / "I saw." / "He was reaching for the whistle." / "I know. You were fast."
  - The guard was reaching for an alarm whistle. She crossed 4 ft of corridor in the time his hand traveled from side to chest. Strike precise enough to put him down without permanent damage. Checked his breathing after (the code requires it).
- "The lock on the vault anteroom — did you notice the delay?" / "The pin stuck."
  - His right hand was inside the mechanism, felt the resistance, adjusted, worked through it in the dark. Old mechanism, original tumblers. Should have anticipated the stiffness.
  - Time dispute: he says 4 seconds, she says 3 (she was counting from outside the door).
- This is their shared language: seconds, distances, precise mechanics of physical actions under pressure. Not outcomes or complications — the specific work.
- "The window exit." / "You went headfirst." / "Because the frame was narrower than the plan suggested and feet-first would cost two seconds of repositioning." / "You went headfirst because you find it more interesting." / "Both things are true."
- She logs the unnecessary risk and variance without requiring further discussion. Will carry the data into the next operation.
- "Tanenni will want the debrief by morning." / "Tell her about the pin."

**What the scene establishes:**
- Their shared language: the mechanics of the work, measured in seconds and distances.
- Milores's code: precise force, checked aftermath, never broke anything she wasn't paid to break.
- Darwhidan's physical risk appetite (headfirst exits, aesthetic choices) — she logs it without argument.
- Post-operation dynamic: mutual recognition of competence, no need for verbal debrief.
- The pin-stuck detail: honest self-assessment, to be reported to Tanenni.

## V. Darwhidan + Tanenni

**Setting:** A private vault beneath the Harrowell estate — three years before the campaign. This is the Voss vault job (their first meeting).

**Scene content:**
- Darwhidan entered the Harrowell estate 90 minutes earlier through the servants' entrance. Navigated 2 locked doors, 1 warded corridor, a descending staircase into limestone bedrock. Opened the reinforced oak door (iron lock, respectable quality) in 11 seconds.
- Vault: ~12 ft square, low stone ceiling, iron-banded chests. Stale enclosed air. A lit oil lamp by the door (wrong — unoccupied vault shouldn't have a lit lamp).
- Tanenni is already there. Brass-scaled Dragonborn, lean, temple robes over hide armour, sickle in one hand, document folio in the other. Dark, steady eyes, entirely unsurprised. She heard him on the stairs.
- Mutual assessment (each in the space of a few seconds):
  - Darwhidan reads: sickle held low (readiness, not threat), folio already filled (she's further along), temple robes genuine, armour non-standard, holy symbol is a credential.
  - Tanenni reads: Aasimar in fine clothes, gold irises (celestial heritage — filed, set aside), thieves' tools at belt (not a guest), prop notebook (carried for show). Hands empty and deliberately open (signalling the next seconds are negotiable).
- "Harrowell." / "Harrowell." (Context confirmation, not greeting.)
- She's been there 20 minutes. Correspondence already in the folio. Securities are in the second chest (where the actual value is). The gold in the front chest is a decoy — real gold, but a fraction of liquid assets, placed to be found first by someone in a hurry.
  - He'd been going for the front chest. The decoy would have cost ~3 minutes he wouldn't have had (guard rotation at the garden door).
- She hasn't taken the securities because she can't carry them all — 14 sealed brass tubes. She can carry 6.
- "I'll carry eight. Split it even." / "You'll carry eight and we leave separately."
- She gives him 8, keeps 6 (compensation for his longer route and additional risk — "That's arithmetic").
- "If you're caught, the tubes are unmarked. Nothing connecting them to this vault or to me."
- She exits through the cellar passage to the herb garden (has a reason to be in a garden at night). Looks exactly like a temple acolyte carrying a prayer book and documents.
- "I'll want to discuss this further. Professionally." / "The Crossed Keys. The back room. Three days from now."
- Three days later: she taught him the first seven signs of Druidic. He understood that the vault evening was not an ending but a beginning.

**What the scene establishes:**
- Their origin story: independent operators converging on the same target, recognising mutual competence.
- Tanenni's superiority in preparation: already there, already further along, knew the decoy, better intelligence on timing.
- Practical trust established immediately: split fairly, leave separately, no connection if caught.
- Her disguise is her actual identity (temple acolyte) — the best kind of cover.
- The partnership beginning: from vault split to Druidic lessons to crew formation.

## VI. Milores + Tanenni

**Setting:** Safe house on Brewer's Yard — the morning after Milores joined the crew. Better than Roper Street: two rooms, working stove, east-facing window (morning light — the reason Tanenni chose it, though she hasn't said this to anyone; it's not operational, it's about beginning a day in a reasonable place).

**Scene content:**
- Tanenni at the table: single ledger, inkwell, calligrapher's supplies. Reviewing accounts from the crew's last operation: expenses, disbursements, percentage per member, percentage quietly redirected to 3 charitable accounts that don't know where the money came from.
- Milores enters. One job with the crew so far (the Brevary operation, 4 days ago). Recruited through a gambling-house contact who described her as the most efficient enforcer he'd ever employed and the only one who'd never broken anything she hadn't been asked to. He also mentioned (as a footnote) that she'd walked out of a mercenary company rather than burn a village. Tanenni found the footnote more informative than the primary recommendation.
- Milores sits across from Tanenni without being directed. Spear leaned against wall. Painting supplies case on belt, not removed.
- Tanenni noticed the painting case during the Brevary job (Milores worked on something during the 40-minute pre-approach wait, on a warehouse roof in the dark). Does not comment. Recognises a private practice inside a public life — carries one of her own.

**Exchange:**
- "Your share from the Brevary job." Slides a pouch across. "Sixty gold, minus expenses. The accounting is on the second page if you want to verify it."
- Milores weighs the pouch without opening it. "I don't need to verify it." / "The offer stands."
- "You keep careful records." / "I keep accurate records. Careful implies something is being hidden. These are transparent to anyone in the crew who asks."
- This surprises Milores — not the accounting competence, but the transparency. In her experience of criminal operations, the books are most likely to be wrong. A leader who invites scrutiny is either foolish or genuinely clean. Tanenni does not appear foolish.
- "Four jobs." / "I'm sorry?" / "From the deposit. On the cottage. At this rate, four jobs."
- Calculated precisely, offered without decoration. Not a confession — a data point. The specific reason she's here.
- Tanenni understands immediately: a woman who has calculated the exact number of jobs between herself and a specific property is keeping her own ledger. Files this completely, without judgment.
- "What kind of cottage?" / "Coastal. North-facing window. For the light." / "For painting." / "Yes."
- Morning light comes through the east window.
- Tanenni doesn't comment on the paintings or the cottage. She understands it — 12 years in an archive counting other people's money and quietly redirecting what she could. She knows what it is to have a project requiring patience, arithmetic, and sustained willingness to do difficult work toward a definite end.
- Next job: Aldworth estate, 3 weeks. Bradkell building the plan.
- She'll leave the route marked in Druidic on the bridge abutment. / "I can't read Druidic." / "I'll draw a map. I should have remembered that. The others have been here longer."
- Milores at the door: "The books are clean." Not a question — a professional assessment, same as evaluating a guard rotation or a lock.
- "They are." / "Good."

**What the scene establishes:**
- Tanenni's financial transparency: accurate records, open to crew scrutiny. This is unusual and significant.
- Milores's cottage fund: four jobs away, calculated to the coin. Coastal, north-facing window, for painting. This is her entire motivation.
- Mutual recognition: both keep their own ledgers (literal and figurative), both have long-term projects requiring patience and arithmetic.
- Tanenni's quiet acknowledgment that Milores is newer (the Druidic oversight and correction).
- Trust built on honesty of accounting, not affection or history.
- Milores's professional assessment of the books is the foundation of her staying.
