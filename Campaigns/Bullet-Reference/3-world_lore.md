# World Lore & Setting — Party 3 (Bullet Reference)

---

## Overview

- Urban fantasy world. Gods real, aristocracy entrenched, guild system controls commerce.
- Forests, kingdoms, and epic-quest problems exist beyond the city walls. Not the crew's concern.
- The crew lives in **Corraith** — large, stratified, commercially powerful city. Wealth pools at top, labour at bottom, distance maintained by centuries-old systems.
- Tone: heist fiction, not epic fantasy. Sorcery, divine power, druidic communion exist but within a social order that absorbs, regulates, and profits from them.
- The crew operates in the gap between what the city's systems assume and what happens when someone with the right skills decides those assumptions are exploitable.

---

## Corraith — The City

- Sits at the junction of the **River Thane** and the **Mirrenmere** (broad tidal estuary, 12 miles to the sea). Coastline continues north beyond the city — cliff country, rural, few settlements.
- River divides the city: western bank rises steeply to bluffs (noble estates, great temples on high ground); eastern bank spreads flat along the floodplain (docks, warehouses, industrial works, lower-city districts).
- Population: ~90,000 (shifts with season — harbour trade swells lower city in summer, winter court draws aristocracy back from country estates).
- Old: walls rebuilt three times, each circuit larger. Oldest stonework (Thorngate temple foundations) predates any living institution's records.
- Governed in theory by the **Council of Aldermen** (12, elected from propertied classes). In practice: Council ratifies decisions made in guild halls, temple treasuries, and private studies of 6-7 families whose wealth predates the Council.
- **Lord Mayor: Alderman Corren Blackwell** — competent administrator, understands his role as public face of decisions he didn't make. Calculated exactly what his position is worth and won't jeopardise it.
- Smell: river water, coal smoke, wet stone, tannery chemical tang (from the Daerrow). Warm days: fish market contribution. Cold days: coal smoke thickens, lower city under yellow haze until wind changes.

## The Quarters

### The Thorngate District
- High ground, western bluffs above the river. Ecclesiastical and aristocratic territory.
- Great temples, noble estates, walled gardens, private chapels. Streets broad, paved in pale limestone, maintained by private levy.
- **Temple of Measured Light** — Tanenni's former institution, largest temple, occupies entire block.
- **Temple of the Risen Flame** — Darwhidan's family's temple, 3 streets from Measured Light.
- **Mael-Arath compound** — Thorngate Close, behind a wall exactly high enough to signal privacy without defensiveness.
- Security: private and professional. Estates employ own guards, temples have wardens, **Thorngate Watch** (separate constabulary funded by district residents) patrols streets. Does not answer to city's general constabulary (politically sensitive point).

### The Sullow Quarter
- Eastern bank, downriver from harbour. Industrial territory.
- Breweries (surviving ones), tanneries, chandleries, workshops, small manufactories servicing harbour trade. Narrow streets, cobbled where cobbled at all. Brick and timber buildings, crowded, extended/subdivided/repurposed across generations.
- Bradkell's territory.
- **Sullow Compact** operates here: network of safe houses, distribution points, community self-provision.
- **Charter Hall** — Guild of Chartered Commerce's district office on Foundry Street. Limestone facade, iron gates, clock tower visible from every street in the quarter. Built 30 years ago on the site of a cooperative grain exchange. The Compact remembers.

### The Daerrow
- Floodplain south of the Sullow Quarter, lowest ground (geographically: floods in bad winters; socially: least-resourced populations).
- Tiefling families, recent immigrants, displaced and dispossessed.
- Milores grew up here. Mother's metalwork shop still on Canal Street, still open.
- Economy: canal traffic, small-scale manufacture, service work for the upper city.
- **Madame Oressa's** gaming houses — 3 on Lampblack Lane. Oressa: Tiefling woman, indeterminate age, considerable influence. Survived 4 Guild attempts to bring her under licensed regulation (survived by being more useful to the right people than the regulation was).

### The Harbour Quarter
- Waterfront where River Thane meets the estuary. Wharves, warehouses, customs houses, chandlers, ropewalks, trading company offices.
- Corraith's economic engine: everything entering/leaving by water passes through. **Harbour Authority** (nominally city, in practice joint major trading house operation) controls flow with bureaucratic thoroughness.
- **Harrowgate customs house** — already robbed by the crew. Operation revealed skimming of import duties into merchant-noble consortium fund (fund has since been quietly reconstituted under different accounting).

### The Bridgeway
- District connecting eastern and western banks across the **Thane Bridge** (primary river crossing).
- Commercial: shops, offices, lawyers, money-changers serving both banks.
- Information market: **Candlewick Tavern** (Garren Thule's brokerage on Bridgeway Lane) and Bridgeway back rooms = where the informal economy conducts negotiations.

## Factions and Power

### The Guild of Chartered Commerce
- Dominant economic institution. Controls commercial licensing, sets charter fees, employs **Warden-Inspectors** for enforcement.
- Regulatory framework refined over 2 centuries for one outcome: consolidation into operations large enough to afford fees, elimination of those that can't.
- Leadership: **Guildmaster Edra Calloway** and the **Charter Board**, from the **Guildhall** on the Bridgeway (marble atrium larger than most businesses the Guild has shut down).
- Warden-Inspectors: plainclothes investigators + uniformed inspectors. Audit, cite, seize, shut down unlicensed operations. Well-funded, well-connected, city charter legal authority.
- Crew's most persistent institutional opponent (though not personal — they don't know the crew exists as a crew. They know certain operations have been disrupted. Haven't connected them).

### The Nobility
- Occupy Thorngate District and country estates beyond the walls. Old, diversified wealth entangled with every institution: temples store assets, Guild regulates on their behalf, Council ratifies preferences, harbour moves goods.
- **Corvane**, **Voss**, **Solenne** families: all already crew targets. Others remain.
- Security: private, professional, variable quality. Best estates: former military officers and arcane security specialists. Worst: locks and the assumption that social position is itself a defence. The latter assumption has been fertile for the crew.

### The Temple Hierarchy
- Temples are religious institutions, financial infrastructure, and political actors simultaneously.
- **Temple of Measured Light** + **Temple of the Risen Flame**: two largest. Between them, hold significant fraction of city's stored wealth in blessed vaults (legally untouchable under religious exemption statutes).
- **High Archivist Solen Drath** (Temple of Measured Light): most significant ecclesiastical figure. Managed the temple's dual function (worship + aristocratic vault) for 20 years with calm. Intelligent, patient. Has not (to Tanenni's knowledge) noticed the 12% discrepancy. His patience is not reassuring to her.

### The Reedmen
- Corraith's plainclothes investigative force. Nominally under Council authority, practically semi-autonomous.
- Investigate crimes the regular constabulary cannot/will not: complex fraud, institutional corruption, theft targeting the elite.
- Named for rushes along the Thane's banks (present everywhere, noticed by no one).
- Competent. Lead financial crimes investigator: **Inspector Cerani Whist** — very competent. Has not yet connected the crew's 7 operations into a pattern, but has noted victims share a profile and methodology suggests a consistent intelligence source. She is working the problem. The crew does not know this.

### The Thieves' Circuits
- Not a single organisation — loose network of independent operators, fences, information brokers, specialists.
- **Garren Thule** (Candlewick Tavern): crew's primary fence and intelligence broker.
- **Madame Oressa** (Daerrow): independent power, gaming houses as neutral ground.
- Own codes, reputation systems, informal but effective enforcement.
- The circuits know someone is working the Thorngate and Harbour Quarter with precision. Don't know who. Thule knows (he's their fence). Thule hasn't shared (discretion is his actual product).
- Crew operates within this economy but is not of it — specialists, not generalists; narrower target profile, tighter operational security.

## The Crew's Operational Environment

### How Heists Work in Corraith
- Structure: intelligence gathering (Tanenni's animal network + temple access + Bradkell's social recon) -> planning (Tanenni + Bradkell joint sessions, Darwhidan on physical security, Milores on contingencies) -> execution (coordinated social engineering + physical infiltration) -> extraction (clean exits, pre-planned routes, dead drops for materials).
- City security is layered:
  - Regular constabulary: street patrols, disturbance response.
  - Thorngate Watch: wealthy district protection.
  - Warden-Inspectors: guild compliance enforcement.
  - Reedmen: after-the-fact investigation.
  - Private security: varies by target.
- Crew's advantage: these layers don't communicate well. Constabulary and Thorngate Watch have jurisdictional rivalry. Warden-Inspectors report to the Guild, not Council. Reedmen operate independently. Information moves slowly between them; the crew operates in the gaps.

### Arcane Security
- Wards, alarms, arcane locks protect most valuable targets. Quality varies:
  - Temple wards: excellent (professional arcanists, centuries of institutional knowledge).
  - Noble estate wards: competent to theatrical.
  - Guild wards: standardised (therefore predictable).
- Bradkell: self-taught ward theory (opponent's analytical perspective).
- Tanenni: temple security practice knowledge.
- Darwhidan: Mage Hand + Minor Illusion handle physical/sensory layers.
- Milores: handles everything that can't be picked, bypassed, or misdirected.

## Religion and the Divine

- Gods real, active, institutionally managed. Temples are institutional frameworks mediating between divine and civic, not primarily houses of personal faith.
- **Temple of Measured Light:** Deity of knowledge and order. Theological position on storing aristocratic wealth in blessed vaults: "all knowledge and things of worth deserve preservation."
- **Temple of the Risen Flame:** Deity of light and justice. Martial order produces paladins. Endowment fund administered by Darwhidan's father (understands divine fire and compound interest are both powerful forces).

**Tanenni's druidic practice:**
- Outside institutional framework. **Circle of the Threshold**: small, city's western margins, not recognised by temples (suits the Circle — no interest in legitimacy, considerable interest in being left alone).
- Genuine druidic connection. Animal communion is operationally significant: sparrows, rats, and crows of Corraith are her intelligence network — more reliable than any human informant.

**Darwhidan's celestial heritage:**
- Real and physical: gold irises, radiant warmth, Healing Hands (work whether he wants them to or not).
- Mael-Arath family interpretation: heritage implies obligation to serve.
- Darwhidan's interpretation: what the heritage implies and what he does with it are his business.
- The divine has not weighed in. Whether it will is a thread.

## The Wider Context

- Corraith is not unique. Guild model, aristocratic wealth structures, temple-as-vault are regional patterns across the **Thanelands** (broader political region; Corraith is the commercial capital).
- Beyond the Thanelands: kingdoms, forests, distance. Crew has no interest in the wider world except as it affects Corraith through trade (harbour = funnel for world's goods in and city's wealth out; external pressures become local ones there).
- Rumours circulating in the Bridgeway and harbour taverns:
  - Trade dispute with southern coastal cities driving up import duties.
  - Succession question in the Thanelands' eastern marches redirecting military spending.
  - New Guildmaster in a neighbouring city aggressively recruiting Warden-Inspectors from Corraith — creating staffing gaps the crew has noticed and filed.
- Some are nothing. Some are threads.
