# World Lore & Setting — Party 1 (Bullet Reference)

---

## Overview

- High-fantasy world, but the party operates at ground level — trade roads, port towns, tavern back rooms, people who need things moved, guarded, found, or fixed.
- Low-fantasy in focus, not in fact. The wider world is extraordinary; it's just far away and not on the Fenway road this week.
- Great kingdoms, archmages, dragons, ancient evils in deep places all exist but are not the party's immediate concern.

---

## The Fenway Coast — The Region

- Campaign starts here: long stretch of settled country running east-west along the Fenway road, sea to the north, wilder hill country to the south.
- Agricultural and mercantile territory. Old enough to be settled, prosperous enough to be comfortable, unglamorous enough to be overlooked by grander stories.
- Landscape: grey-green hills; scrub oak and limestone outcrops in uplands; flat arable country near coast. Between the Fenway road and the sea: rough coastal ground (wind-bent scrub, wet limestone, broken bluffs cut by streams running north). Not wild hill country — exposed, salt-scoured terrain the road was built to avoid.
- Sea to the north: cold, reliable, smells of brine and fish.
- Sky often overcast. Rain is a structural feature of the climate.
- Communities connected by trade, road, and shared practical culture: pragmatic hospitality, cautious welcome to strangers, strong opinions about fair dealing. Not naive about dangers (Brindlemark's walls are old but maintained for a reason), but day-to-day concerns are grain harvests, market prices, road reliability.

## Key Settlements

### Stoneveil Reach
- Small harbour town at base of highland ridge where Goliath upper-country communities meet the sea.
- Not a Goliath settlement — mixed town (fisherfolk, sail-makers, small traders). Grew up because a natural harbour existed.
- Goliath families came down from the ridge a generation or two ago, settled, stayed. Their descendants are coastal people now.
- Has a port chapel, modest market, and the social texture of a place at the meeting of two worlds.
- Autumn fog from the highland ridge into the harbour is a Stoneveil institution. There's a specific Greymantle dialect word for it.
- Cade's home. He has not been back for some years.

### Greymantle Keep
- Highland Goliath settlement immediately above Stoneveil Reach, in the ridge country.
- One of several highland communities — stone-built, practical, with Storm Giant blood heritage. Some bloodlines carry the ancestry more strongly; manifestation varies.
- ~20 years ago, Orrath Stone-Handed emigrated from here to Ashford, carrying his craft, language, and whatever else. He is a significant emigrant, for reasons not yet fully understood.
- Not accessible to the party at present. Background — a real place that matters, a thread rather than a destination.

### Ashford
- Proper city east along the Fenway. Guilds, functioning militia, thieves' circuit, significant Goliath blacksmithing community, and a Guild that oversees arcane practice in not entirely transparent ways.
- Where Rixon grew up, apprenticed, served in the Ward Guard, and where Orrath's forge was shut by Guild order with records sealed.
- Does not feature in campaign opening, but its past is present in two party members. Generates threads.

### Kelstead Port
- Major harbour town of the Fenway coast. Large enough for: hiring hall, port chapel, proper harbour authority (with admin building where Corvan works), several inns, mixed-species working population.
- Hub for cargo between coastal sea routes and inland Fenway road.
- Party passed through before the campaign and returned after Thornwater. Did not stay. Left Corvan behind, checked port chapel for Sael's response (none), took a contract from the hiring hall, went west.
- A resource: Corvan knows things, port chapel handles messages, hiring hall posts work. Not home to any of them.

### Thornwater
- Secondary harbour town north of Kelstead, accessible by a day's sail and short inland transit. Smaller, less prosperous than Kelstead. Services fishing fleets and occasional merchant vessels. Harbour authority moves slowly, keeps careful records.
- Where Vetha was detained and Gilda's cargo impounded. Sealed cargo shed still there (red wax, Thornwater Harbour-Masters' mark).
- Two parties had interest in the undeclared consignment; neither identified. Party left before this could be established.
- Unfinished business — specifically Gilda's.

### Brindlemark
- Small walled town at junction of Fenway road (east-west) and Drover's Track (south, into hill country).
- Population: ~1,200.
- Stone walls: 12 ft high, old but maintained.
- Two gates: Market Gate (east, onto the Fenway) and Shepherd's Gate (south, onto the Drover's Track).
- Economy: trade road traffic, sheep from southern hills, grain harvest from farms outside the walls. Prosperous enough for a cobbler and brewer but not a proper smithy (metalwork comes by wagon).
- Correct level of town: small enough that strangers are noticed, large enough to have work.

**Key locations:**
- **The Gatehouse.** Contract posting and pay collection. Run by town's small constabulary. Corkboard of notices. Constable: pragmatic about paperwork, clear-eyed about town needs.
- **The Kettle & Crow.** Larger tavern, near market square. Rooms upstairs, common room below. Travellers and working swords. Adequate ale, hearty food, reliable fire.
- **The Lame Dog.** Smaller tavern, side street. More locals, fewer travellers. Cheaper, quieter, rougher. Things said here that wouldn't be said at the Kettle & Crow.
- **The Market Square.** Open most mornings. Farmers, tinkers, a few Fenway merchants.
- **Brindlemark Chapel.** Modest stone chapel. Not dedicated to any single god — serves whoever needs it. Alderman contributes to upkeep. Cade will check it for correspondence.
- **Alderman Cray's House.** Largest building in town, near the square. Cray is the closest thing to a mayor: practical, unsentimental, more competent than the modest setting might suggest.

**Atmosphere:** Mud, thatch, stone. Woodsmoke. Carts on cobble. Sheep occasionally through the Market Gate. People who know each other's business. Travellers: welcome for coin, watched for trouble.

## Species & Communities

### Goliaths
- Highland ridge communities: old, insular by instinct. Emigration is individual, not collective.
- Those who leave go to cities or coastal towns; those who stay maintain the Keeps and old ways.
- Storm Giant blood in many lineages, stronger in some. Does not always manifest visibly; carriers don't always understand what they carry.
- The Stoneveil Reach Goliaths (came down a generation ago): coastal people now, shaped by sea and mixed harbour community, but speak Giant among themselves, remember Greymantle, know the fog-word. Ambiguous position between two worlds.

### Dwarves
- Mountain holds: several days inland and south. Old communities built into hills. Rich in metalwork, precision instruments, accumulated capital.
- Commercial network extends along the Fenway and river routes.
- Gilda's background: this network, managing goods between holds and Halfling river communities.
- Dwarves in Fenway port towns: common sight — merchants, factors, occasional wizards. Reputation for exactness in contract and debt matters (mostly deserved).

### Halflings
- River-vine communities: winding river valleys east and south of the Fenway. Small, close-knit, built along waterways.
- Tradition of skilled craft and unmatched geographic familiarity with the land between rivers.
- Mall-Lowick comes from this world but has spent most of her adult life in hill country.
- Common in port and market towns along the Fenway: traders, scribes, specialists.

### Humans
- Majority population of Fenway towns. No single cultural character.
- Rixon is human. Most people in Brindlemark, Thornwater, and *The Grey Cormorant*'s crew are human.
- Everywhere and everything.

## The Wider World

- Campaign does not begin with the wider world pressing in. It is distant, enormous, doing what it does, and will become relevant in time.
- Rumours circulating in port towns and hiring halls:
  - Succession dispute in one of the eastern kingdoms.
  - Strange lights above the highland ridge in late autumn.
  - Blight on grain stores in two river towns, cause unclear.
  - A trading company hiring heavily and quietly for unconfirmed reasons.
  - A Guild closure in Ashford (some years back), records sealed.
- Some are nothing. Some are threads.

## Religion & the Divine

- Gods are real and present, though not uniformly attentive.
- Different communities have different patron deities. Fenway town shrines serve local, practical functions rather than grand theological ones.
- **Cade's divine connection:** To the storm — specifically to whatever force runs through the Storm Giant blood in his lineage. Not converted; recognised (or something in the blood was recognised). Storms notice what they will notice. He doesn't know exactly what is listening; he knows something is.
  - Holy symbol: silver-grey amulet, abstract shape (breaking wave or open hand). Received from a travelling divine at Stoneveil Reach at 17. At his chest since.
- **Brindlemark Chapel:** Ecumenical by necessity, serves all comers. Will accept Cade's presence without complication.
- Arcane and divine overlap in this world in ways not fully mapped. Orrath's forge sat at one end of this overlap. Cade's ancestry at another. Whether connected or parallel is not yet known.

## The Demimonde

- Below the Fenway's surface economy: mercenaries, fixers, information brokers, thieves' guilds (larger towns), loose network of operators who work outside official frameworks for clients who prefer quiet problem-solving.
- Rixon's native environment. Moves through it with ease — knows the codes, rates, people worth knowing, people worth avoiding.
- Its own geography: certain taverns, back rooms, gaming tables where Knavebones is social currency and real business is conducted alongside the dice.
- Brindlemark: small for a proper underworld, but the Lame Dog has a back room, and the Drover's Track south of town has its own economies.
- Kelstead Port: more fertile ground, a resource the party has not yet fully used.
