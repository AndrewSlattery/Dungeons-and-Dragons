# World Lore & Setting — Party 2 (Bullet Reference)

---

## Overview

- High-fantasy world experienced at ground level, from inside a forest that is going wrong.
- Great kingdoms, archmages, dragons exist somewhere. Not the party's immediate problem.
- The party lives in the working margins: rural circuits, forest roads, seasonal markets, people who grow things, play things, and move things between communities the grand stories have never heard of.
- Low-fantasy in focus, not in fact. The Greenwild is genuinely ancient and strange, but on a normal day it's a forest where people farm, hunt, and play music at harvest suppers. The extraordinary is underneath, and it is surfacing.

---

## The Thornvael — The Region

- Campaign starts here: settled country running roughly north-south along the Thornvael road.
- Greenwild (vast old-growth forest) to the west.
- Upland valleys to the east.
- Agricultural and frontier territory. Old enough for established communities, remote enough for self-reliance, governed mostly by custom and seasonal necessity rather than distant institutions.
- Landscape:
  - West: mixed deciduous forest, deep and old, thinning into farmland/grazing in the central lowlands.
  - East: rocky hill country rising to uplands, narrow valleys cut by streams feeding the Thornvael river.
  - Lowland soil is good; valley soil is better (if you know what you're doing).
  - Wide sky in the lowlands, narrow in the valleys.
  - Weather comes from the west/Greenwild, carrying deep-forest humidity and smell of leaf-rot and growth.
- Communities connected by road, seasonal markets, shared practical culture: cautious hospitality, fair dealing opinions, deep familiarity with forest/land rhythms. Maintain walls and watches. Day-to-day concerns: planting seasons, livestock prices, road passability after rain.

## Key Settlements

### Kettleford
- Largest settlement on the Thornvael road. Proper market, constable, two inns, smithy, mixed-species population.
- Located where the Thornvael road crosses the river. Bridge is the only reliable crossing for 20 miles — chokepoint, market, and social centre simultaneously.
- Economy: grain harvest, livestock from eastern valleys, twice-yearly trade with Orcish merchants from the south.
- Prosperous enough for cobbler and brewer but no proper guild — craft organised by custom and mutual suspicion.
- Constable: **Aldra Venn** — broad-shouldered human woman, 11 years in the post, considers paperwork a necessary affliction. Posts contract notices outside the constabulary.

**Key locations:**
- **The Greenman Inn.** Larger inn, near market square. Rooms upstairs, common room below. Travellers, merchants, working professionals. Local ale, honest food, social centre after dark.
- **The Sett.** Smaller inn, near the river. Cheaper, quieter, farming communities and seasonal workers. Local knowledge circulates here.
- **The Kettleford Market.** Open most mornings; larger on 1st and 3rd weeks. Twice-yearly Orcish trade days are the major commercial events.
- **The Constabulary.** Stone building near the bridge. Contract board by the door. Venn's office plus two holding cells (mostly used for confiscated livestock).

**Atmosphere:** Woodsmoke and river-water. Carts on the bridge at dawn. Community that knows its own business. Strangers assessed quickly, welcomed if useful, watched regardless.

### Briar's Crossing
- Crossroads settlement where Thornvael road meets the track east to Ashgrove and foothill communities.
- Smaller, rougher than Kettleford, transient character (exists because two roads meet).
- One inn: **The Briar** — food, drink, beds of acceptable quality. Default meeting point for anyone between lowlands and eastern hills.
- No constable. Disputes settled by **Hollen Marsh**, the innkeeper — largest person in the settlement, settling disputes for 30 years. Fair, laconic, uninterested in explanations longer than two sentences.
- Where Gwenwyn found Osctra. Where the Thornvael road starts feeling like frontier.

### Greenwild Edge
- Not a permanent settlement — a seasonal gathering point. Cleared area at the western edge of lowland farms where the Thornvael meets the Greenwild.
- Three times a year (spring planting, midsummer, autumn harvest): market assembles. Lowland farmers, foothill herders, Orcish traders from the south, and (in better times) Forest Gnome communities from the edge-wood with crafts and foraged goods.
- No permanent population. Has a tradition, a cleared field with stone fire-circles, and social function greater than its physical presence.
- Seasonal markets are where the Thornvael's communities come together across usual divisions. When disrupted, the region feels it.
- Most recent market: half-shuttered. Fewer traders, fewer visitors. Festival atmosphere replaced by watchful quiet. This is where the party came together.

### Ashgrove
- Borderland settlement in the eastern foothills, head of a valley where hill country begins.
- Mixed (frontier mixing): Orcs, humans, some Elven families, Halfling traders. Nobody quite belongs; everyone decides for themselves.
- Has a blacksmith, healer's cottage, and community hall (tavern/meeting-place/emergency shelter).
- Ix-Nambuth grew up here. Her people are borderland Orcs who settled a generation ago from further east, stayed because the valley grew on them.
- Ashgrove doesn't have strong opinions about Orcs or anyone — has strong opinions about whether you can do useful work. Applied uniformly.

### Mosshallow
- Forest Gnome community in the edge-wood between Thornvael lowlands and deep Greenwild.
- Shifts slightly with the seasons, appears differently summer vs. winter, most outsiders pass without noticing.
- Old — older than Kettleford, older than the Thornvael road. Intimate relationship with the Greenwild that lowland communities don't share.
- Cognodec's birthplace. Zib Tumbletock lives nearby.
- Edge-wood communities are first line of contact between settled lowlands and whatever's happening in the deep forest. Unusually quiet in recent months.

### Hawthorn Valley
- Narrow upland valley east of the Greenwild, accessible by track from the Thornvael road.
- Exceptional soil (mineral content + drainage combination that Osctra spent decades optimising).
- Remote enough to be largely ignored by regional authorities. Prosperous enough (under Osctra) to be self-sufficient.
- Now in ruins: greenhouse smashed, botanical beds trampled, irrigation system (20 years to build) damaged beyond quick repair. Destroyed by Lord Torth's hunting party + the displaced predator they flushed through.
- Not accessible to Osctra at present (bounty makes return inadvisable, and nothing to return to). Background: a wound, a reason, not a destination.

## Species & Communities

### Forest Gnomes
- Edge-wood communities (Mosshallow and neighbours): oldest settled population in the Thornvael region, predating human/Orcish communities by centuries.
- Small, insular by preference, deeply embedded in the forest's ecology. Their relationship with the Greenwild is participatory, not just residential. They notice when the forest changes, and they are noticed in return.
- Cognodec left decades ago, has not returned.
- Edge-wood communities likely have more detailed and more alarming knowledge of the wrongness than lowland settlements.

### Orcs
- Commercial and settled presence in the Thornvael region.
- Merchants come from southern lowlands for twice-yearly trade at Kettleford and Greenwild Edge — bringing goods, opinions, and exacting quality standards the local farmers have learned to respect.
- Borderland communities (Ashgrove and neighbours): Orcish families settled a generation ago, now part of the landscape.
- Osctra speaks Orcish (agricultural vocabulary precision). Ix-Nambuth speaks it as heritage tongue.
- Orcish merchants at Kettleford know Osctra by reputation (silent Elf farmer, extraordinary root tubers). His market absence will be noted.

### Wood Elves
- Scattered, solitary — individual families or small clusters, not organised settlements. Distributed through upland valleys and Greenwild margins.
- Osctra is typical: single individual in a remote valley, connected to the wider world through twice-yearly trading.
- Patient, territorial, profoundly connected to the land they inhabit. When the land goes wrong, they feel it in their work before they understand it in their thinking.

### Humans
- Majority population of lowland settlements (Kettleford, Briar's Crossing, farming communities).
- Gwenwyn is human. Most of the people she plays for, passes news between, and sleeps under roofs with.
- Farmers, traders, innkeepers, constables — practical infrastructure of a working region.

## The Wrongness

- Something is wrong in the Greenwild, spreading outward.
- Ground-level appearance: predators outside normal ranges (frightened, disoriented), prey moving wrong directions, seasonal patterns disrupted (bird migrations off schedule, denning animals emerging at wrong times). Ecology is not collapsing — it is being displaced. Something in the deep places is pushing inhabitants outward. Building for months.

**Perspectives:**
- **Osctra:** Most systematic documentation. Field journals: species, locations, dates, behavioural observations (80 years of land-reading precision). Data suggests displacement originates in the deep Greenwild, intensifying gradually for at least 6 months.
- **Gwenwyn:** Road perspective. Which villages report attacks, which roads are unsafe, where fear has reached. Comprehensive, current mental map of regional disruption.
- **Ix-Nambuth:** Cultural perspective. Cancelled fairs, empty village greens, communities pulling inward. Tracks the wrongness by social footprint — things that should be present and are not.
- **Cognodec:** Animal testimony. Fear without object, displacement without destination. Consistent report: something wrong in the deep places, for longer than 6 weeks. Animals can't say what they're running from, only that it's there.

- The wrongness is not yet understood. Felt, documented, tracked, feared. Whatever is causing it has not been seen by anyone the party has spoken to. Known only by its effects.

## Religion & the Divine

- Gods are real, present, not uniformly attentive.
- In the Thornvael: divine practice is local, practical, informal. Kettleford chapel serves all comers. Roadside shrines mark safe passages and dangerous crossings. Farmers bless fields with whatever divine name the family has always used. Not a region of cathedrals — a region of small observances and working faith.

**Ix-Nambuth's oath:**
- Did not come from a temple (has never been inside one she found convincing).
- Something older and less institutional — a force/intelligence associated with the Greenwild itself. Ancient, unhurried. Looked at her and decided she was adequate to a purpose it didn't explain.
- Her divine power is real. Its theology is her own to work out.

**Cognodec's patron (the Green Advocate):**
- Different category entirely. Fey, or presents as Fey.
- Relationship: less a covenant than an ongoing situation. Name may be an alias. Power is genuine.
- What it wants beyond what it's already given is not clear.
- Whether connected to what claimed Ix-Nambuth (same attention, different aspects, or separate forces with overlapping interests) is an open question the party lacks information to answer.

**Overlap:**
- Arcane and divine overlap in the Greenwild in ways not fully mapped.
- Forest Gnome communities have their own understanding of this overlap — older and more nuanced than anything lowland settlements possess.

## The Wider World

- Campaign doesn't begin with the wider world pressing in. Distant, enormous, will become relevant when local threads connect to larger ones.
- Market chatter and common-room talk:
  - Lord's hunting party that went badly wrong in the uplands (Osctra's story, told with varying accuracy).
  - Blight on grain stores in two southern market towns, cause unclear.
  - A trading company buying forest timber rights aggressively and quietly.
  - Gnomish edge-wood communities going silent in unprecedented ways.
  - Something seen above the deep Greenwild tree canopy on clear nights — not a dragon probably, but large and not previously noted.
- Some are nothing. Some are threads.

## Factions & Forces

*(Placeholders; to be developed during play.)*

- **Lord Vaylen Torth.** Landed lord of the eastern uplands. Estate, retinue, grievance against Osctra. Regional but real reach. Interest in the Greenwild is recreational, not ecological — nuisance and potential obstacle.
- **The Kettleford Constabulary.** Aldra Venn and her small operation. Most likely initial contact with regional authority. Pragmatic, competent, increasingly aware something is wrong without resources to investigate.
- **The Edge-Wood Communities.** Mosshallow and neighbouring Gnomish settlements. Silent. Possibly affected by the wrongness. Possibly informed about it. Not currently accessible without going into the Greenwild margin.
- **The Orcish Merchants.** Twice-yearly trading partners. Commercial network with its own information channels, observations about regional disruption, and interests in Thornvael stability.
- **Whatever Is in the Deep Greenwild.** Unknown. Present. Getting closer.
