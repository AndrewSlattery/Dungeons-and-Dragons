# Pre-Campaign Vignettes — Party 2 (Bullet Reference)

*Six scenes, one per character pair, set during the weeks they found each other.*

---

## I. Osctra + Gwenwyn

**Setting:** Harvest supper, Millbrake village — six weeks before the road.

**Millbrake:** ~40 souls plus a dozen from outlying farms. Tables on the green, old style (planks on trestles). Food: root vegetables, hard yellow cheese, mutton stew. Gwenwyn had been playing the shawm.

**Osctra's position:** Far end of the table (nearest the road — always his choice). Arrived with a cartload of late-season tubers. Traded with usual efficiency: fair weights, exact change, no pleasantries beyond confirming goods were sound. Pitchfork leaning against the cart. Eating stew, watching the treeline. Has been watching treelines for three weeks — seven pages of observations in his journal that he can't yet assemble into a conclusion.

**Gwenwyn's approach:** Sits across from him. Slight, angular, battered wide-brimmed hat, braids pinned up. Has been playing music for three hours.

**Key exchange:**
- Gwenwyn mentions his tubers — Erdwell's wife said they were the best in four years, but this load was half what he usually carries.
- Osctra: "It was a difficult season."
- Gwenwyn counters: Erdwell, Hapworth, Linney farm all had good yields this year. Only the high ground — only him — was bad.
- She reveals she plays a circuit and carries news. "People talk to the person who played the dancing." She listens.
- She reports: Linney farm lost a goat to a daytime wolf attack (too far south for wolves by 30 miles, alone — wolves that far from range don't hunt alone unless driven out). West Briar had a bear in a kitchen garden 3 weeks ago. East Briar hasn't seen deer on the north trail since midsummer (huntsman tracked that herd 6 years, they moved overnight).
- He says he has notes. She says she thought he might.
- He produces his journal — leather-bound, soil-stained, dense precise hand. Opens to the most recent entry.
- She reads it: "You've been tracking displacement patterns." / "For three weeks. Since the ridge." / "I've been tracking them for three months. From the road. Different angle, same picture."
- He tells her to come back through in a fortnight. She agrees.
- She leaves. He didn't ask her name. Realises he didn't need to — she told him everything that mattered in six sentences.

**What the scene establishes:**
- Gwenwyn's circuit as an information network (people talk to the musician).
- Osctra's systematic documentation vs. Gwenwyn's road-level observation — different angles, same phenomenon.
- Their interaction is data-driven, not social.
- First convergence of evidence: displacement patterns identified from two independent sources.

## II. Osctra + Ix-Nambuth

**Setting:** A dry-stone wall on the ridge road — three weeks before the road.

**Scene:** Osctra has stopped to reset a frost-shifted capstone on an old, well-laid wall. Hears a viol below — not a performance, but practising. Phrases repeated and adjusted. Recognises the quality: someone treating the instrument as a problem to be solved.

**Ix-Nambuth:** Sitting on a boulder at the treeline, 60 ft below. 6'3" grey-green Orc, single dark braid with tooled leather, traveller's clothes over chain mail, quarterstaff propped beside her. Large hands holding the bow with precision that doesn't match their size.

**Exchange:**
- She knew he'd been up there for 10 minutes (assumed either fixing the wall or deciding whether to come down). "Good wall." / "Good stone."
- He notes the viol, chain mail, quarterstaff: "You play the festival circuit."
- "Ix-Nambuth. I was meant to play Thornhollow last week. The fair was cancelled."
- He goes still — not fear, but confirmation. The quality of hearing something expected and finding no comfort in being right.
- She describes: animals on the northern road, predators that shouldn't be there, village decided it wasn't safe to gather. Third cancellation in six weeks on her circuit. Hapworth cut their supper short. West Briar moved theirs indoors.
- He produces his journal. She reads it: "Displacement patterns. You're tracking where they've moved from." / "And when. The timing matters. It's not random." / "No. It's not."
- Her assessment: "Something is pushing them. Not hunting them — pushing them. Out of the deep places."
- He mentions Gwenwyn — a woman on the road with a shawm, working some of the same villages, noticing the same absences.
- She identifies Gwenwyn by name — knows her by reputation. "We've played the same suppers but not the same evenings."
- She identifies him: "You're the farmer with the bounty." / "Small bounty." / "I heard you threw a lord over a fence." / "He was standing near it."
- She proposes they converge: heading south, Gwenwyn's circuit brings her through the Briar villages in a fortnight, useful to compare notes in the same room.
- He proposes the Millbrake supper in two weeks. She agrees.
- His journal entry: *Thornhollow fair cancelled. Third in six weeks on the eastern circuit. Confirmed by Ix-Nambuth (viol, Orc, paladin?). Independent observation consistent with displacement pattern.*

**What the scene establishes:**
- Third independent source confirming the displacement pattern.
- Ix-Nambuth tracks the wrongness by its social footprint (cancelled fairs, empty village greens).
- Both share precise observation habits.
- Convergence plan: Millbrake supper as meeting point.
- The bounty on Osctra is circulating as a story on the circuit.

## III. Osctra + Cognodec

**Setting:** Edge of the Ashen Wood — ten days before the road.

**Scene:** Osctra is on a deer path examining soil — a faint greyness in the topsoil that doesn't correspond to any mineral deficiency he recognises. On one knee, sniffing a pinch of earth.

**Cognodec's entrance:** Voice behind him: "The soil's wrong." 3'4" Forest Gnome in dark green coat, amber eyes, dark grey hair, orb at his shoulder catching light in not-entirely-natural ways.

**Exchange:**
- "I know the soil's wrong. That's why I'm looking at it."
- Cognodec introduces himself: "Forest mystic. Consultant on matters of the natural and, when called upon, the slightly supernatural."
- His smile is exactly the right width and warmth — and Osctra immediately identifies it as long practice rather than spontaneous feeling.
- "You're a performer." Cognodec prefers "interpretive naturalist."
- Osctra: "You're a performer who works the villages. The mushroom blessings, the field consecrations."
- Cognodec identifies Osctra: "You're the farmer from the upper valley. The one with the bounty." Notes the bounty story travels quickly on his circuits.
- Osctra notices the Book at Cognodec's side — leather tooled to resemble bark, brass fittings that seem to have shifted position. Looks heavier than a book that size should be.
- Cognodec drops the performance voice: "The soil. I've been noticing it for a fortnight. The greyness follows the stream west, deeper into the wood. The animals are aware of it. They won't cross it."
- Osctra: "You talk to the animals." / "It's what I do." / "Not the performance version. The real version."
- Cognodec, after a silence: "Yes. The real version." Still becoming accustomed to the distinction.
- Animal report: fear, nothing specific. Something wrong in the deep places for weeks. Phrasings differ but content is the same. Something in there that should not be, for longer than he's been listening.
- Osctra offers his journal: five weeks of displacement data (predator movements, herd shifts, den abandonments, mapped pattern).
- Cognodec reads carefully, not pretending to understand — and Osctra revises some initial estimates of him.
- Cognodec mentions the Millbrake supper in a few days (asked to perform a harvest blessing). Others are noticing — a bard on the road, a paladin on the circuit.
- Osctra has heard of them.
- Cognodec: "I think we are all looking at the same problem from different positions. Comparing those positions would be useful."
- Osctra's assessment: Cognodec is still a charlatan (fine clothes, theatrical manner, constructed persona). But the soil concern is real, the animal reports are real, and the Book is heavier than it should be in a way neither of them can explain.
- They agree: Millbrake. Cognodec brings the blessing; Osctra brings the journal.

**What the scene establishes:**
- New data: the greyness in the soil (animals won't cross it, follows the stream deeper into the wood).
- Cognodec's dual nature: performer/charlatan exterior, genuine Speak with Animals gift and real concern underneath.
- The Book of Shadows as a physically anomalous object (heavier than it should be, fittings shift).
- Osctra can read through Cognodec's performance but recognises the real thing underneath.
- Millbrake confirmed as convergence point.

## IV. Ix-Nambuth + Gwenwyn

**Setting:** The Briar crossroads — twelve days before the road. Crossroads where the Briar road meets the south track. A well (good one — Gwenwyn's been drinking from it for two years, never sick), a milestone, a much-repainted signpost.

**Scene:** Gwenwyn is sitting on the well wall eating an apple (payment for three songs and news that the Hapworth bridge was passable again). Hears a viol approaching — not a performance but iterative, precise practice. Years of demanding training evident.

**Ix-Nambuth arrives** playing, walking with steady ground-covering stride. Sees Gwenwyn, finishes the phrase, sets bow across strings.

**Exchange:**
- Gwenwyn identifies her: "You're Ix-Nambuth. You played the Thornhollow midsummer two years ago. I was there — doing the dancing music. You played something afterwards that made the entire room go quiet, and then you left."
- Ix-Nambuth: "I remember the room going quiet. I don't remember the dancing music." / "You wouldn't. Nobody remembers the shawm — they remember the dancing. That's the point."
- "The Thornhollow fair was cancelled." / "I know. That's three." / "Four, if you count the Linney gathering (moved indoors, half the valley didn't come). I've been keeping count. It's accelerating."
- Gwenwyn describes her observations sideways, through detail, connecting observations like a road connecting villages — builds a pattern, not a list: the Linney wolf, the West Briar bear, the vanished deer herd, the quiet in the upper pastures, the huntsman who stopped going out alone.
- Ix-Nambuth listens completely still, asks precise questions only after Gwenwyn finishes.
- Key concern: timing. If the deer herd moved at midsummer, whatever is causing this has been building for months — earlier than Ix-Nambuth thought.
- Ix-Nambuth mentions Osctra (farmer, Wood Elf, field journal of displacement patterns, met on the ridge road).
- Gwenwyn knows him: "I know his tubers, which is almost the same thing."
- Both were told by Osctra to come back through Millbrake in a fortnight.
- Ix-Nambuth also mentions Cognodec (forest mystic, Gnome, works these woods). She's heard the name but they haven't met.
- Gwenwyn has seen his work (Hapworth field blessing — old women in tears). Couldn't tell if it was real.
- Ix-Nambuth: "Does it matter?" — and Gwenwyn recognises this is the kind of question she usually asks other people.
- Gwenwyn's answer: "It might. If whatever is happening in the forest needs something real to answer it."
- They agree: Millbrake, the supper. Both playing.
- Ix-Nambuth: "We could play together, if the room has space for it." / "A viol and a shawm. That's either going to be beautiful or extremely confusing." / "Both, probably."
- Gwenwyn plays a new phrase after Ix-Nambuth leaves — something that wants a viol under it.

**What the scene establishes:**
- Gwenwyn's method: builds information as a landscape, not a list. Lateral, connective.
- Ix-Nambuth's method: listens completely, asks precise questions.
- The timeline is established: displacement has been building since at least midsummer (months).
- Their complementary performance styles (one produces silence, one produces dancing).
- Genuine connection forming — the new musical phrase is a signal of this.
- Millbrake confirmed as the gathering point.

## V. Ix-Nambuth + Cognodec

**Setting:** A clearing in the Ashen Wood — eight days before the road. Small, ringed with old ash trees. Diffuse golden-grey autumn light. Quality of stillness; sense of being observed by something ancient and unhurried.

**Context:** This is the clearing where Ix-Nambuth received her oath 3 years ago. She returns when the circuit brings her near — sits, does not play. She has decided the oath asks this of her: to return and be still.

**Scene:** Ix-Nambuth is sitting against an ash trunk, eyes closed. Hears Cognodec before seeing him — not footsteps but a murmur, low and theatrical, directed at something near the ground.

- Cognodec is kneeling 20 ft away, speaking to a hare in Gnomish. The hare is sitting upright, ears forward, apparently listening.
- "Lle thau vaen" (*You are safe here*). "Mas erthiel?" (*What frightened you?*)
- The hare responds. Ix-Nambuth (who speaks Gnomish) understands the shape: fear, displacement, territory abandoned because something changed.
- "Henion" (*I understand*). He gives the hare a piece of dried apple. It eats and departs.
- He turns and sees Ix-Nambuth.
- "That was not a performance," she says, in Gnomish.
- He goes genuinely still — seen doing something private. His Gnomish is fluent in the wrong registers and slightly formal in the right ones — native-born, but trained to be other voices.
- "No. It was not."
- She studies him: constructed persona from coat to orb to amber eyes, but the hare conversation was not constructed. The concern was real.
- "You speak Gnomish as a birthright." / "And you speak it as Zib Tumbletock taught it." He knows Zib by reputation from the Gnomish musicians' circuit. She corrects his pronoun for Zib ("him").
- She notices the Book has a quality she can't name — weight not corresponding to size, presence not quite inanimate. Brass fittings have shifted since she first looked. She is certain.
- "You come here." / "I come here." / "May I ask why?"
- In Gnomish (the language Zib taught her to be precise in): "Something spoke to me here. Three years ago. I don't know what. I come back because the oath requires it."
- He recognises: not the specific experience, but the quality of being claimed by something unchosen and inexplicable.
- "The patron came to me approximately one year ago. Or made itself apparent — I suspect it had been watching longer." Touches the Book (habitual, slightly nervous). "I was a charlatan before that. I am still, by trade, a charlatan. But the power is real, and the Book is real, and the animals' fear is real, and I am becoming something I was pretending to be. I find this theologically irregular."
- She almost smiles: "I find my own situation theologically irregular."
- "Then we are well matched."
- The quality of attention in the clearing is present — ancient, unhurried — both aware of it, neither commenting.
- She mentions the Millbrake gathering. He says he'll be there (performing a blessing).
- "Will it be real?" (Precise Gnomish question, in the register that doesn't permit evasion.)
- "I don't know yet. It is becoming more real every time. I think that may be the point."
- She considers this the most honest thing she's heard him say, and probably the most honest thing he's said in considerable time.

**What the scene establishes:**
- Ix-Nambuth's oath and Cognodec's patron both potentially connected to the clearing / the Greenwild.
- Both have been claimed by something they can't fully explain. Mutual recognition of this.
- Cognodec's genuine Speak with Animals gift vs. his performance persona — he is becoming what he was pretending to be.
- The Book of Shadows as anomalous (fittings shift, weight doesn't match size).
- Gnomish as a language of precision and honesty between them.
- The clearing itself has an ongoing presence/attention.

## VI. Gwenwyn + Cognodec

**Setting:** The lane outside Millbrake, the evening before the harvest supper. Lane between hedgerows (hawthorn and blackthorn, old style). Amber evening light.

**Scene:** Gwenwyn is walking toward Millbrake from the east with shawm, pack, and a bundle of dried herbs (rosemary and thyme — payment for a song about a pig that escaped and was found in a chapel).

- Hears a voice before seeing the speaker — warm, measured, describing the properties of a fungus on the north side of ash trees. Confident, detailed, authority rehearsed until indistinguishable from the genuine article.
- Finds Cognodec sitting on a stile, speaking to a cat. Large tabby, one torn ear, tolerating him with contempt. He is enjoying his own material.
- He finishes his point, addresses a remark to the cat (which ignores him), turns to her.
- "The cat is not impressed." / "Cats rarely are. That's what makes them reliable critics."
- Mutual identification: "Gwenwyn. The shawm." / "Cognodec. The blessings."
- She was at Hapworth for his spring planting ceremony — he made three old women cry. She identifies the mechanics: timing, incense, Minor Illusion of a sprouting seedling.
- "You see the mechanics." / "I also see that the seedling illusion was backed by something that wasn't an illusion. The light changed when you spoke the closing words. Not an effect. Something responded."
- Cognodec: "The patron. The entity I work for. It does that occasionally. Adds emphasis at moments I haven't planned."
- "Is that comfortable?" / "Not entirely."
- He describes his situation: charlatan for 40 years, extremely good, the work was genuine (people needed it), but the mechanism was performance. Now the performance is becoming the thing it was performing. He is not certain what that means.
- She nods and gives space.
- "I answer questions sideways. It's what I do. People tell me I've changed the subject, and then three sentences later they realise I answered completely. I do it because sideways is usually the truest angle on anything worth seeing."
- "That's an excellent description of misdirection." / "That's an excellent description of perspective."
- Her sideways question: "When the Book gets heavy, what story are you in?"
- He is surprised — a man who spent decades asking the right questions, not accustomed to having one asked of him.
- "I don't know yet." / "That's the first honest answer you've given me." / "I know."
- Walking into Millbrake together. She outlines tomorrow: Osctra's journal, Ix-Nambuth's circuit observations, his blessing (may or may not be real).
- "It will be as real as I can make it. Which is, at this point, more real than I'm comfortable with."
- She says they should all talk properly — not separately, not at the edges of conversations. After the supper, when the village has gone to bed and the fire is coals.
- He agrees. She tells him to bring the Book: "I want to see how heavy it is."

**What the scene establishes:**
- Gwenwyn sees through performances professionally. She sees both the mechanics and the genuine thing behind them.
- Cognodec is forced into honesty — she asks the kind of question he usually asks others.
- Her sideways communication style as a deliberate method ("the truest angle").
- The patron adds genuine responses to Cognodec's performances — the transition from charlatan to genuine practitioner is physically observable.
- She organises the proper group discussion for after the supper.
- The Book's weight as a barometer.
