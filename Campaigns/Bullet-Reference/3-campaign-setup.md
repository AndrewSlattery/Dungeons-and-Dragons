# Campaign Setup — Party 3 (Bullet Reference)

---

## Part One: Before the Crew

### Bradkell — The Street Organiser

- Race: Human. Sorcerer (Wild Magic implied — power emerged spontaneously at a moment of crisis).
- Born in the **Sullow Quarter** — industrial district, eastern bank of the River Thane, city of **Corraith**. Brewery country (or was, before the Guild of Chartered Commerce's consolidation campaign 15 years ago).
- Father: **Edric Bradkell**, ran the **Three Chimney Brewery** on Millrace Lane. Mid-sized operation, dark ale popular with dockworkers and canal bargemen.
  - Brewery survived two rounds of guild licensing increases and one uninvestigated arson attempt.
  - Did not survive the third licensing round (tripled charter fee + surety bond requiring a guarantor from the guild's approved list — no one on the list would appear for a Sullow Quarter brewer).
  - Edric closed the brewery when Bradkell was 11. Building sold to cover fees. Now a guild-licensed grain storage facility. Bradkell walks past it occasionally and does not slow down.
- By 14: runner for the **Sullow Compact** — mutual aid network providing what the guilds priced out (unlicensed medical care, food distribution in winter, quiet loans at non-catastrophic rates).
- By 18: coordinating supply routes.
- By 20: planning operations (which warehouses had surplus, which inspectors could be delayed, which aldermen's schedules created windows).

**Sorcerous ability arrived at 22 during the Charter Hall riot:**
- Riot was a response to the Guild announcing that mutual aid distribution would require a charitable dispensation license (fee transparently designed to make it impossible).
- Bradkell was coordinating a blockade of Charter Hall's south entrance. A ward-officer seized him. The air cracked. The officer landed 6 ft away (unhurt but startled). Street cleared.
- Understood immediately: something significant had changed, with considerable applications.

**Arcane education (self-directed over 2 years):**
- Borrowed texts from the **Thane-Side Circulating Library** (lends to anyone with a deposit, no questions about guild standing).
- Attended 3 lectures at the **Corraith Arcanists' Lyceum** under a borrowed name before being noticed.
- Spent an afternoon in **Alderman Varek Solenne's** private study (ostensibly discussing a charitable dispensation application). Left having memorised the ward-schema on Solenne's desk and having stolen a monograph on abjuration theory (Solenne hasn't noticed yet).
- Understands wards, alarms, and arcane security systems — not as a practitioner builds them, but as an opponent analyses them. Knows how operators think, what assumptions they make about who will try to circumvent them, and how those assumptions create exploitable patterns.

### Darwhidan — The Courtier Who Wasn't

- Race: Aasimar. Gold irises (celestial heritage — physical, not cosmetic). Healing Hands work whether he wants them to or not.
- Born into the **Mael-Arath** — one of Corraith's oldest Aasimar families. Compound in the **Thorngate District** (ecclesiastical quarter, high ground above the river, great temples and senior clergy residences).
- Mother: **Seraya Mael-Arath**, senior officiant at the **Temple of the Risen Flame**.
- Father: **Aldous**, administers the temple's endowment fund.
- Family expected him to take vows and enter the temple's martial order.

**Posting at 19 to the Corvane Estate:**
- Household of **Duke Renn Corvane** (wealthy noble, significant Temple of the Risen Flame donor).
- Meant to season him: court manners, noble protocol, upper-tier social grammar.
- He learned all of these. Also learned: 14 external doors (3 never locked), guard rotation left west corridor unobserved for 11 min every 2 hours, duke's vault had a mechanical lock (excellent quality) and a ward (adequate quality) — neither sufficient.
- Second year: practised Mage Hand and Minor Illusion until he could place sounds (footstep, closing door, cough) precisely enough to redirect guards reliably. Mapped security of every estate he visited through duties (Corvane entertained frequently; the young Aasimar attaché was welcome at tables where silver was counted after he left, though never found missing). At this stage: still only looking.

**The Corvane vault job:**
- Walked through servants' entrance at shift change, past two guards attending to a sound in the wrong corridor, through the mechanical lock in 30 seconds and the ward in 20, out through a third-floor window with Feather Fall.
- Took the duke's private strongbox contents in a document case. Sent correspondence (documenting duke's arrangements with harbour officials re: undeclared cargo) to three parties who would find it useful. Kept the gold.

**Family aftermath:**
- Two letters. First: concern. Second: the specific silence that functions as a conclusion. He did not reply.
- Has not been to the Thorngate District since.
- Access credentials at the Temple of the Risen Flame have not been formally revoked — an oversight he considers worth preserving.

### Milores — The Soldier Who Walked Out

- Race: Tiefling. Deep scorched crimson skin, obsidian horns.
- Grew up in the **Daerrow** — lower city district, floodplain south of the Sullow Quarter.
- Mother: **Tessara**, Tiefling metalworker. Small shop repairing canal-boat hardware (hinges, locks, anchor fittings). Taught Milores metalwork discipline: patience, precision, material tells you what it will become.
- Recruited at 16 by the **Blackshore Company** — mercenary outfit, compound on Corraith waterfront, took contracts across the region. Reputable (honoured contracts, paid soldiers, didn't question employers).
- 10 years of specialisation: infiltration of defended positions, silent sentry neutralisation, dismantling defensive formations from inside.

**The Thornfield incident:**
- Village 3 days' ride north of Corraith. Blackshore Company contracted to make a minor lord's holdings untenable.
- Captain **Drevan Horst** proposed burning the village (the lord's labour base) — sound by company operational standards.
- Milores walked into Horst's tent. Gave him one opportunity to reconsider. He did not.
- She beat him with sufficient thoroughness to remove him from the campaign. Left him outside the tent for his second-in-command. Walked out carrying nothing that wasn't hers.

**Aftermath (18 months of caution):**
- Blackshore Company doesn't formally pursue deserters, but Horst had friends who took it personally.
- Found work as enforcer for **Madame Oressa's** gaming houses in the Sullow Quarter. Quiet, professional, clear scope. Reputation: efficiency, never breaking anything she wasn't paid to break.

**The cottage:**
- Four jobs from the deposit. Real cottage on the coast north of Corraith, cliff above the sea, north-facing window. Has seen it once. Has painted it seven times.

### Tanenni — The Archivist Who Noticed

- Race: Dragonborn (brass-scaled).
- Raised in the **Temple of Measured Light** — one of Corraith's largest religious institutions, walled compound in the Thorngate District.
- Assigned to archive and records office at 16 (neat scales, neater handwriting, quiet temperament).
- 8 years copying donation records, processing correspondence, maintaining internal accounts for the temple's charitable works (shelters, food kitchens, small-grant programmes for the lower city).
- 4 more years understanding: charitable works = ~11% of total income. Remaining 89% moved through private reliquaries with nothing to do with worship.

**What the Temple of Measured Light actually is:**
- Secure storage and laundering facility for Corraith's aristocratic families.
- Illicit goods through blessed vaults under religious exemption from city inspection.
- Tax obligations dissolved via 300-year-old charter clauses.
- Career-ending documents resting in alcoves consecrated to saints of wisdom — metaphysically and legally untouchable.
- Senior clergy (**High Archivist Solen Drath** and circle) manage this with unhurried confidence.

**Tanenni's response:**
- Did not expose (would have destroyed genuine charitable work, provided no structural change, ended her access).
- Quietly rerouted ~12% of skimmed surplus back into charitable accounts.
- Altered relevant ledger entries with calligraphic skill indistinguishable from originals.
- Continued duties. Drath has not noticed (or has noticed and attributes to accounting variance).
- Temple still considers her a senior acolyte on administrative leave. Access credentials remain valid.

**Druidic connection (arrived at 29):**
- In the temple's kitchen garden, repotting a bay laurel. Understood, without drama, that she knew something about the plant's intention.
- Found the **Circle of the Threshold** — small druidic order, garden compound on Corraith's western outskirts.
- Incorporated druidic practice into temple work indistinguishably from standard religious observance.
- Animal intelligence network: sparrows map guard rotations, rats report on basement storage, a crow named **Ashen** sits on the Thorngate bell tower and has opinions about everything below.

**Role in the crew:**
- The architect. Has been doing this longer than the crew has existed.
- Others are aware of this; she has not corrected the impression the crew was her idea from the beginning. (It was not — she operated alone for 4 years before Darwhidan appeared on the other side of a strongbox.)
- Decision to build a crew around that partnership: 3 days of careful consideration, not in the moment. She does not make decisions in the moment.

## Part Two: The Formation

### The Vault (3 years before campaign)

- Tanenni and Darwhidan independently targeted the vault of **Lord Aldren Voss** — textile magnate, wealth from technically-legal but comprehensively-exploitative contract labour in the mill districts.
- Tanenni: monitoring Voss's finances through Temple of Measured Light records for 6 months. Came through the garden — bypassed a ward using Druidic knowledge (wards keyed to "intruders" don't recognise someone moving with the garden's own growth rhythm).
- Darwhidan: mapping Voss estate security via social channels (attended 3 dinner parties as guest of a guest, charted every door, window, and patrol pattern). Came through the servants' corridor, past a guard redirected by Minor Illusion.
- They met over the strongbox.
- Tanenni offered to split it. Darwhidan accepted. They left through different exits, met the next morning, divided contents, and began discussing partnership.
- Tanenni taught him Druidic over the following weeks. He uses it to leave scouting reports carved into city stonework in locations guards walk past without examining.

### Milores (recruited 6 months after Voss job)

- Recruited through **Garren Thule** — fence and information broker, back room at the **Candlewick Tavern** in the Sullow Quarter.
- Thule had worked with Milores during her enforcer period and with Tanenni on intelligence purchases.
- Tanenni described the need: physical security, close-quarters neutralisation, the quiet violence heist work occasionally requires.
- Milores attended the meeting, asked 3 questions: target profile? civilian exposure? pay structure?
- Answers: corrupt wealth, none, equal shares after operational costs. She accepted.
- Has since revised her opinion from "adequate professionals" to something unnamed but visible in the fact that she has not looked for alternative employment in 2.5 years.

### Bradkell (joined 18 months before campaign)

- Found the crew through Sullow Quarter underworld networks (same channels supplying mutual aid with intel about guild movements and warehouse schedules).
- Heard about a crew targeting guild-connected wealth with precision suggesting planning, not opportunism.
- Arranged a meeting. Evaluated alignment with his own priorities. It aligned.
- Determined 3 things: crew was competent, targeted the institutions he'd spent his adult life opposing, and had a structural gap in social operation — no one who could walk into a room of guild officials and leave with their confidence, information, and conviction that the large man in rough clothes was someone they'd independently decided to help.
- He filled the gap.
- Before Bradkell: crew approach was infiltration and extraction.
- After Bradkell: approach includes social engineering — front-door entry, manufactured invitations, conversations that produce building access without window-climbing. (Darwhidan still climbs through windows — he considers this an aesthetic choice.)

## Part Three: Specific Questions Resolved

### Crew Structure and Decision-Making

- **Tanenni** — Architect. Target identification, intelligence gathering, operational plan design, forged credentials and documentation for cover identities. Plans are detailed, well-margined, presented confidently (checked twice).
- **Bradkell** — Social engineer. Front-door approach: cover story, social map of target environment, identifying who needs to believe what in what sequence. Plans with Tanenni (most productive and least comfortable meetings — both excellent, both precise, neither concedes without evidence).
- **Darwhidan** — Infiltrator. Physical entry, mechanical/magical locks, movement through defended spaces, improvisation when inside doesn't match the plan. Secondary social operator (holds cover identities under pressure with naturalness Bradkell sometimes envies).
- **Milores** — Specialist. Physical security, guard neutralisation, contingency work when something goes wrong and the crew needs an exit through opposition. Does not plan operations. Executes within agreed scope. Will tell you when scope is no longer clean.
- Decisions are collective. No single leader. In practice: Tanenni sets operational direction, Bradkell shapes the approach, Darwhidan and Milores contribute domain expertise. Consensus through efficient but not fast process.
- Primary vulnerability under time pressure: four independent decision-makers, four different frameworks, all correct, none identical.

### Financial Arrangements

- Equal shares after operational costs (materials, information purchases via Thule, safe house/dead drop maintenance).
- **Bradkell:** Routes share through intermediaries to the Sullow Compact's mutual aid operations. Amounts real but not traceable. Nobody in the Sullow Quarter knows the source. Other crew members know — Bradkell told them at the outset (considers it relevant to operational security that they understand where his money goes and why).
- **Milores:** Banks her share. Cottage fund = specific number in specific account with specific moneylender in the Daerrow. Knows to the coin how far from the deposit.
- **Darwhidan:** Spends on tailoring, equipment, lifestyle maintenance for moving convincingly in noble circles. Owes Tanenni half the Voss vault from 3 years ago — technically outstanding, neither has raised it.
- **Tanenni:** Reinvests in operation — intelligence acquisition, documentation infrastructure, bribes to keep certain officials looking away.

### Prior Jobs (7 major operations in 3 years)

Target profile consistent: individuals/institutions whose wealth derives from guild system exploitation, labour arrangements, or aristocratic privilege.

1. **Voss vault** (Tanenni + Darwhidan only, pre-crew). Securities and correspondence from textile magnate.
2. **Solenne ledgers.** Financial records from Alderman Solenne's private study documenting Guild of Chartered Commerce payments to city officials. Redistributed to 3 broadsheet publishers. Solenne survived the scandal; his reputation did not.
3. **Harrowgate customs house.** Payroll diversion from harbour customs office (skimming import duties into a fund controlled by merchant-noble consortium). Crew redirected 3 months of skimmed funds to the Sullow Compact.
4. **Corvane correspondence** (second operation against duke's household; Darwhidan's return visit). Additional documents on duke's harbour corruption involvement. Duke left Corraith for country estate. Has not returned.
5. Three additional operations of similar profile — financial returns + redistributed intelligence.

- No civilian casualties. No crew injuries beyond manageable. No identification by city authorities. Tanenni's operational security is excellent; others have the discipline to maintain it.

### Relationships Between Members

- **Tanenni + Darwhidan:** Oldest relationship, most developed trust. Communicate in Druidic for operational security. The unresolved Voss vault debt functions as the visible surface of a more complicated question about mutual obligation neither has articulated.
- **Milores + Tanenni:** Clean professional interface. Planning-to-execution handoff is the smoothest seam in the crew. Share the quality of carrying private facts (Milores's paintings, Tanenni's administrative leave) without requiring discussion.
- **Bradkell + Milores:** Mutual respect built on legibility. Bradkell's framework is elaborate but consistent; Milores's code is simple but absolute. Each finds the other's decision-making readable (matters more than agreement).
- **Bradkell + Darwhidan:** Both social operators, each knows the other is performing. Creates ease: neither can be the other's mark, so the version of themselves they present to each other is closer to actual. Bradkell finds Darwhidan entertaining and occasionally alarming. Darwhidan finds Bradkell's need for models endearing.
- **Darwhidan + Milores:** Silent-approach pair. Professional recognition of two people whose physical precision is their primary asset. Don't debrief after operations. Don't need to.
- **Bradkell + Tanenni:** Planning engine. Most resembles a partnership (neither would use the word). Bradkell calibrates plans around Tanenni's intelligence reports (so far accurate). He considers her the most reliable person in the crew. She has noted that he has noticed.

## Part Four: Open Threads

- **The Sullow Compact and the Guild of Chartered Commerce.** Bradkell's mutual aid network funded by crew operations. Guild's enforcement arm (**Warden-Inspectors**) increasing pressure on unlicensed charitable activity. Intersection hasn't produced a crisis yet, but the geometry is clear.
- **The Temple of Measured Light.** Tanenni's 12% rerouting has been running for years. High Archivist Drath is either oblivious or patient — neither is comfortable. Her access credentials remain valid (oversight or invitation — she hasn't determined which).
- **The Mael-Arath family.** Darwhidan's celestial heritage and family expectations unresolved. Mother: senior officiant at a major temple. Father: administers its endowment. They know what he's become. They've stopped writing.
- **The Blackshore Company.** Milores walked out 2.5 years ago. Horst recovered. Company still operates. Whether anyone is still looking: "not actively" for some time, which is not the same as "no."
- **Edric Bradkell.** Bradkell's father is alive in the Sullow Quarter, unaware his son funds the mutual aid network that now provides services the brewery used to provide informally. Whether this remains the case is Bradkell's unmade decision.
- **The cottage.** Milores is 4 jobs away (~1 year at current tempo). What happens when the number reaches zero — whether she stays, goes, whether the crew survives her departure — none have raised it, all have considered it.
- **Darwhidan's debt to Tanenni.** Half the Voss vault. 3 years outstanding. Amount known to both. Reason it hasn't been called due: known to neither, or to both. The distinction matters.
- **The crew's growing profile.** 7 operations in 3 years, same target category, same operational signature. The city's investigative force (the **Reedmen**) has not yet connected the pattern. The word "yet" is doing significant work.
