# Campaign Setup — Party 1 (Bullet Reference)

---

## Part One: Before the Party

### Cade — The Sailor's Chaplain

- Race: Goliath. Storm Giant Ancestry (not merely cosmetic — something in his blood is listening).
- Hometown: **Stoneveil Reach** — small mixed-species coastal harbour town at the base of the highland ridge near the Goliath upper-country strongholds. Exists because a natural harbour existed first. Population: fisherfolk, sail-makers, small traders.
- Family has been in Stoneveil Reach for generations; one of the few Goliath lineages that came down from the highlands and stayed near the sea.
- Grew up large, unusually calm, with an early pull toward the divine — connected to the autumn storms off the ridge and the deep water.
- Childhood friends were harbour children. When they were old enough, they signed on with a trading vessel. Cade went with them as unofficial chaplain, medic, and protector.

**Cade's childhood companions** (all adults; none are current party members):

- **Akander** — Human woman. Compact, quick-tempered, close-cropped red hair, scar under left eye (boom injury in heavy weather). Made bosun within three years, stayed at sea. Currently first mate on a trading ketch on the southern coastal runs. In sporadic letter contact with Cade via port chapels; addresses letters to "the tall one." Status: fine. Wants Cade to stop worrying.
- **Sael** — Elf. Quiet, observant, served as navigator. Gifted with charts, gentle manner, calm in bad weather. Disappeared during the Kelstead Port events (see Part Two). Cade is quietly worried. Sael had a talent for landing on their feet. Cade is giving it time.
- **Corvan** — Goliath. Was the youngest of the group, gangly and anxious as a boy. Now a dock factor (harbour administrator) at Kelstead Port: logs cargo manifests, manages berth assignments. Stayed behind when the others scattered. No longer an adventurer; knows where adventurers have been.

### Rixon — The Discharged Guard

- Race: Human.
- Hometown: **Ashford** — proper city east along the Fenway. Large enough for guilds, militia, thieves' circuit, and a significant Goliath blacksmithing community (emigrated from highland settlements a generation back).
- Apprenticed at 15 to **Orrath** (Goliath blacksmith from Greymantle Keep, emigrated ~20 years before). Orrath was older, methodical; his smithing methods went beyond ordinary — something arcane in how the metal moved. Guild inspectors asked pointed questions.
- Orrath taught Rixon: the craft, basics of arcane metalworking, and the Giant tongue (Orrath thought in Giant; easier to explain concepts in the original language; also pleased to pass his home culture to a genuinely interested human boy).
- The arrangement ended when Rixon was 21. Specifics are his private business. What is documented: Orrath's forge was shut by the Ashford Guild on unspecified charges, records sealed, Orrath disappeared. Whether he returned to Greymantle Keep, died, or found a third resolution — Rixon doesn't know with certainty. He has an opinion he keeps private.
- After the forge: joined the Ashford Ward Guard (market quarter and lower docks). Learned shield-wall discipline, halberd maintenance, and the social skills of the working city's demimonde. Discharged three years later — technically honourable, circumstances complicated, not volunteered.
- Since then: guard contracts, escort work, occasional group ventures. Arrived at Kelstead Port on a short-term contract; hired as security for a coastal trading run.

---

### Gilda — The Merchant with a Map Problem

- Race: Dwarf.
- Built her trading operation from the inside out: logistics first (moving goods from mountain holds to river docks faster and cheaper), then investment (financing other traders' routes), then direct high-end commerce.
- By the time she hired Mall-Lowick, she had a respectable network, reasonable liquid coin, and a specific problem: northern coastal routes above Kelstead Port were badly charted, and the trade margin with port communities up there was considerable if the approaches could be navigated.
- Hired Mall-Lowick to survey the northern coastal approaches. The resulting maps were mostly correct but had three specific disputed points (see below). Dispute never formally resolved.
- For the northern trading run: moving worked Dwarven goods (tools, precision instruments, specialty alloys) toward buyers in river communities. Booked passage on *The Grey Cormorant*, hired Mall-Lowick and Rixon.

---

### Mall-Lowick — The Cartographer

- Race: Halfling.
- Had been working the northern coastal routes on and off for two years before Gilda's commission — mostly for her own records, supplemented by commissioned work.
- Gilda's commission: specific objectives, reasonable fee, interesting terrain she wanted documented anyway.
- Completed the survey, delivered maps, collected most of the payment (outstanding balance on three disputed points still technically in negotiation).
- Was already back near Kelstead Port on unrelated work when Gilda contacted her about the trading run.
- Accepted: passage on *The Grey Cormorant*, reasonable daily rate, chance to document the coastal route properly in both directions. Key motivation: a stretch of coast about a day's sail north of Kelstead where the cliffs form a feature she has been trying to chart accurately for 18 months.

## Part Two: The Thornwater Run

- *The Grey Cormorant*: working ketch, 17 crew including officers, built for reliability. Captain: **Vetha** — practical, no unnecessary questions. Cargo: standard Dwarven metalwork consigned to a river factor in Thornwater (accessible by short inland transit from a secondary harbour north of Kelstead).
- The four spent 12 days aboard together. Formation period — not as adventurers, but as people in close quarters with professional connections and no reason to dislike each other.
  - Ate at the same table, overlapping watch rotations.
  - Cade resolved a minor dispute between Rixon and a crew hand without casualties.
  - Gilda and Mall-Lowick argued about the northern cliff feature; turned into a two-hour discussion both found stimulating.
  - Rixon won a sum at Knavebones, lost some back the next evening (correct social calibration).
  - Nothing required them to act as a unit. They were simply people sharing a ship, becoming familiar.
- Voyage ended at Thornwater's secondary harbour on schedule.

**What happened at Thornwater:**
- The cargo manifest was not entirely accurate. Someone (not Gilda — she was furious) had added an undeclared consignment relevant to a dispute between two parties in Thornwater.
- Vetha was detained by local authorities within hours.
- Crew dispersed. Cargo (legitimate and undeclared) was impounded.
- Result: the four were left in Thornwater — employed by a detained captain, with impounded goods, in a town with no connections and at least two sets of interested parties who knew they'd been on the ship.

**The departure from Thornwater:**
- They left together (practical, not sentimental).
  - Gilda had resources to move quickly.
  - Rixon had instincts to move quietly.
  - Mall-Lowick knew the coastal scrub between Thornwater and the Fenway from her cartographic work; had a route avoiding the main road.
  - Cade had Healing Word, Guidance, and steadiness.
- Reached Kelstead Port by back route in three days.

**At Kelstead Port:**
- Sael was not there. Corvan had no info on Sael's whereabouts — only that Cade's message at the port chapel had been collected but not answered.
- Akander's ketch was a week out; no contact possible.
- Whatever happened to Sael was after the *Grey Cormorant* left for Thornwater; the trail was cold.
- They did not stay. Took a contract from the hiring hall for work along the Fenway, west toward Brindlemark.
  - Gilda: adequate short-term revenue while sorting out the cargo situation.
  - Mall-Lowick: interested in the stretch of country between Kelstead and Brindlemark (has notes but no complete maps).
  - Rixon: nothing against Brindlemark, no reason to stay.
  - Cade: would go where the group was going (the group was now his to watch out for).

## Part Three: Specific Questions Resolved

### The Rixon/Orrath/Cade Thread

- Rixon and Cade did not know each other before the *Grey Cormorant* voyage.
- Connection: Orrath (the smith who taught Rixon Giant) left Greymantle Keep before Cade was grown. Cade's community was Stoneveil Reach (coastal, below the ridge), not Greymantle Keep (highland). Adjacent, not identical.
- Cade first noticed the connection when Rixon swore at a tangled rope in Giant during a night watch — using a Greymantle-dialect idiomatic phrase (about the futility of effort in bad weather). Not standard highland dialect. Not in textbooks. You learn it from someone who speaks it at home. Cade recognised it because his father (a harbour man who dealt with Greymantle traders) used the same phrase.
- Cade said nothing that night. Later, during the overland journey from Thornwater, asked Rixon where he learned Giant. Rixon told him (Orrath, the forge, the broad outline — not the specific end).
- Cade does not know Orrath personally. Knows the name vaguely as a Greymantle emigrant. Knows Greymantle Keep as the settlement over the ridge, whose autumn fog rolls down into Stoneveil Reach's harbour.
- Tenuous connection, but meaningful when far from home with people known for 12 days. Cade has not made a large thing of it. When Rixon speaks Giant, Cade's attention has a specificity Rixon has noticed and not yet asked about.
- The Orrath thread itself (why the forge was shut, what happened to him, what he was making) is a dormant story thread. Answer exists; party doesn't have it yet.

### The Arcane Blacksmith Connection

- Orrath was not running a legitimate metalworks by the end.
- What he was doing — what he was teaching Rixon, what the sealed Guild records document, why relevant parties moved quickly — connects to things larger than a single disgraced smith.
- Rixon has pieces of this knowledge but doesn't fully understand what he knows.
- Intended to surface around Rixon's progression toward Eldritch Knight — arcane principles Orrath taught, embedded in forge muscle memory, finding expression at moments of crisis.

### Cade's Childhood Friends — Current Status

- **Akander:** At sea, operational, intermittent contact. Not in danger. Not connected to current situation.
- **Corvan:** At his desk at Kelstead Port. Nervous. Not connected to current events but may know relevant things.
- **Sael:** Missing. Message collected, no response. Cade checks for letters at postal/port authority points in any town large enough to have one. Nothing yet.
- Cade's friends are not a resource to call on and are not party-adjacent. They are his people, separate from present circumstances; the tension between those two worlds is his to manage.

### Party Finances

- **Rixon (65 GP):** Guard contract pay plus Knavebones net winnings. Largest liquid holdings; good pay plus careful spending.
- **Cade (27 GP):** Ship's chaplain pay. Modest, consistent.
- **Gilda (27 GP liquid):** Operational pocket money. Actual wealth is in merchant network, material inventory, and rings (estimated 80–120 GP as emergency currency). Running on 27 GP while Thornwater cargo situation resolves.
- **Mall-Lowick (30 GP):** Cartography commission (partially paid) and voyage fee; mostly spent on equipment/supplies before departure.
- No one destitute, no one comfortable. Correct financial pressure for adventure start.

### The Gilda/Mall-Lowick Prior Relationship

- The cartography commission was their first professional engagement. Ran several weeks the previous year.
- Three disputed points on the northern coastal map:
  1. Rock formation labelling: Gilda calls it "Breaker's Head," Mall-Lowick calls it "the Cormorant Stack," with different coordinate notations.
  2. Tidal zone: Mall-Lowick marked as navigable at high water; Gilda says mark "avoid — approach only with local pilot."
  3. ~400-yard discrepancy in placement of a secondary harbour entrance: Mall-Lowick attributes to magnetic variation, Gilda attributes to surveying error.
- All three are genuinely disputable. Both right about at least one. Neither has conceded.

### The Party's Default Stance on Authority

- Will work with local authority when it advances objectives or represents least complication. Pragmatic, not ideological. Not bound by institutional loyalty. Take contracts, deliver, expect payment; will not be conscripted without negotiation and compensation.
- **Cade:** Respects legitimate authority as a community's protective framework. Will cooperate with local law. Will not follow unjust orders. The distinction is clear to him; others sometimes find it less so.
- **Rixon:** Authority is a system to navigate, not respect. Knows guard/militia/law enforcement structures from the inside. Can convincingly perform belonging. Cooperates or works around, depending on which produces better outcomes. No institutional loyalties.
- **Gilda:** Authority is another counterparty. Complies when efficient, circumvents when not. Knows which category applies before others have finished reading the regulation.
- **Mall-Lowick:** Works within systems that serve their stated purpose. Ignores systems that do not. "But it's the law" is not a complete sentence.
- In Brindlemark: the Gatehouse, the Alderman, and Alderman Cray represent practical, unsentimental local authority. Broadly compatible with the party. They take contracts; Brindlemark posts contracts. Workable.

## Part Four: Open Threads

- **The Thornwater undeclared consignment.** What was it? Who added it? Who were the two interested parties in Thornwater, and what did they want?
- **Sael's disappearance.** Where did the navigator go? Voluntary?
- **Orrath.** What was the Ashford forge producing? Where is Orrath now? What do the sealed Guild records say?
- **Gilda's network and the cargo loss.** She is owed a reckoning. Who arranged the undeclared consignment? How does she find out, and what does she do?
- **Cade's divine connection.** Storm Giant Ancestry is not cosmetic. Something in his blood is listening. What, and to what end?
- **Rixon's discharge.** Honourable in the technical sense. Specific circumstances unspecified. Intentional.
