## 8. Notes & Backstory

Cade is a towering Goliath, standing at eight feet tall with the broad, barrel-chested build of a lifelong sailor and tavern brawler. His skin bears a stormy, tempest-grey hue, and his arms are thick with muscle and faded nautical tattoos. Despite his intimidating, rough-and-tumble exterior — often sporting a fresh split lip or a previously broken nose from a dockside scrap — his eyes hold a deep, contradictory calmness and warmth. He wears a sturdy chain shirt over his functional, salt-stained sailor's clothing, with his holy symbol prominently displayed. To look at him is to see the blending of a ruthless deck-enforcer and a gentle, devoted holy man.

### Background

Raised in a close-knit coastal community, Cade never originally intended to take to the sea or the cloth. He was content living a simple life, but his tight-knit group of childhood friends were restless, eventually signing on with a merchant fleet to seek their fortunes. Cade knew their reckless nature would get them killed. Drawing on his immense physical might and a newly awakened divine connection to the storms of his giant ancestors, he joined them as the ship's chaplain, medic, and unofficial bodyguard.

He uses his imposing size to break up deck brawls before they start, and his magic to mend the wounds when he isn't fast enough. To the rest of the world and rival crews, he's a terrifying, thunder-wielding giant descendant; to his crew and childhood friends, he's the ultimate protective older brother who will brave any tempest to keep them safe.

## 8. Notes & Backstory

### Appearance

Gilda stands a full five feet tall — a height that, combined with her broad shoulders and stout, powerful frame, makes her an imposing presence among other Dwarves. Her skin is the deep, rich colour of polished mahogany, often shimmering slightly under the application of expensive oils. She eschews the typical dusty rags of a travelling mage for robes of heavy, dark velvet trimmed with gold thread, reflecting her status as a woman of means. Her dark hair is braided with silver wire, and her fingers are heavy with rings that are as much "emergency currency" as they are decoration. She carries her quarterstaff not as a walking stick, but like a sceptre.

### Background

Gilda was never one for the "honour and heritage" of the deep mines; she found the clink of gold coins a far more beautiful sound than the strike of a pickaxe. She established herself early as a shrewd logistics expert, managing the hazardous trade routes between the mountain holds and the Halfling river-vines. To Gilda, magic was never a mystical calling, but a series of high-end tools for a high-end career. 

She learned the Halfling tongue to ensure she was never cheated at the docks, and she mastered *Tenser's Floating Disk* simply because it was more cost-effective than hiring extra porters. Her decision to adventure is purely a business venture: the risks are high, but the profit margins of ancient dungeons are far better than selling iron ore. She is True Neutral not out of malice, but because she views every encounter through the lens of a contract — if it doesn't pay, or if it costs too much in resources, she isn't interested.

## 8. Notes & Backstory

### Appearance

Mall-Lowick is a svelte, sharp-eyed Halfling woman, 2'6" with wind-tousled, curly brown hair that she keeps tied back with a simple leather strap. Her fingers are perpetually stained with black and sepia inks from her cartography work. She wears practical, rugged traveler's clothing draped comfortably underneath her studded leather armour, favoring muted forest greens and earthy browns. Her most distinctive feature is the oversized, weather-proofed map case slung securely across her back, sitting right alongside her beautifully crafted Longbow—a weapon that looks almost as tall as she is, yet she handles it with effortless grace. 

### Background

As a researcher of the wilds and an incredibly talented cartographer, Mall-Lowick has always preferred the rustle of autumn leaves over the hushed, stuffy whispers of city scriptoriums. She is driven by a burning desire to map the unmapped and document the strange, forgotten magic of deep forests and hidden valleys. 

Her chaotic good nature means she views borders, treaties, and "keep out" signs as mere suggestions—if ignoring them means uncovering a new geographical wonder or saving travelers lost in the wilderness, she won't hesitate to trespass. To her, the truth of the land belongs to everyone, and she intends to draw the definitive map of it, no matter what monsters or grumpy lords stand in her way.

## 8. Notes & Backstory

At 28 years old, Rixon is a swarthy, physically imposing human — built broad and sturdy at 5'9" rather than sleek or graceful. Reflecting his lack of agility and immense physical power, he moves with a heavy, deliberate tread. His hands are thick and heavily calloused from his youth spent at a blacksmith's forge and his years in the city militia. His dark hair is trimmed rather short on the sides with loose curls on top, framing a face that naturally settles into the gruff, unbothered expression of a career tough guy — a look he gleefully cultivates. However, behind that theatrical henchman exterior, his eyes are sharp, betraying the keen intellect and practical imagination that keep him alive in the demimonde.

### Background

Rixon spent his youth apprenticed to a blacksmith with a touch of the arcane about him. He stuck around long enough to pick up a few cantrips and a decent knack for the theoretical side of magic, but the arrangement fell through. The reasons why are his own business.

After that went sour, he fell in with a small city militia. That's where he learned how to really hold a line — they taught him his way around a halberd and the brutal discipline of a shield wall. He's discharged now, making his way on guard contracts, escort jobs, the occasional group venture... honestly, whatever pays the tab.

He likes to think he's a practical man, but he's got enough imagination to keep life interesting. He considers himself a true denizen of the demimonde: down here, morals are fickle and loyalties are worse. True friends are as rare and precious as diamonds. He knows he projects the image of a tough, bruised-knuckle henchman, and he'll admit he gleefully revels in playing that part. Depending on how the mood takes him, he's just as capable of rakish heroics as he is of theatrical villainy.
# Party Synergy — Strategic Analysis

---

## Overview

The party of Cade (Cleric), Gilda (Wizard), Mall-Lowick (Ranger), and Rixon (Fighter) covers the core combat and exploration roles without a dedicated Rogue or Bard. Each character is capable individually; together, they are formidable. The key insight is that their abilities do not merely stack — several combinations produce outcomes that no single character could achieve alone, and those combinations are worth understanding and using deliberately.

---

## Combat Synergy Chains

### Chain 1: The Initiative Setup — Rixon → Anyone

**Rixon's Alert feat** lets him swap his initiative with any willing ally immediately after the roll, before anyone acts. This is the party's most powerful "turn zero" ability and costs nothing.

The correct recipient depends on what the party needs this fight:

| Swap To | Effect |
|---|---|
| **Cade** | He casts Shield of Faith on Rixon as a Bonus Action before Rixon's first turn: Rixon enters combat at AC 20 |
| **Mall-Lowick** | She applies Hunter's Mark (BA) or drops Entangle before enemies have moved into position |
| **Gilda** | She uses Mind Sliver to debuff a key target's save before Cade's Command or Mall-Lowick's Ensnaring Strike lands the same round |

The swap decision should be made *immediately* after rolling. It costs nothing and can completely reshape the first round of combat. Treat it as a standing question at every initiative roll, not a situational special move.

---

### Chain 2: The Save Debuff Chain — Gilda → Cade or Mall-Lowick

**Step 1 — Gilda: Mind Sliver** on a priority target (INT save DC 13 → 1d6 Psychic damage; target suffers −1d4 on its *next* saving throw).

**Step 2 — Cade: Command** (WIS save DC 13, now effectively DC 13 vs. a reduced roll) or **Mall-Lowick: Ensnaring Strike** (STR save DC 12, now reduced) fired the same round or the round immediately after.

Mind Sliver does not require Concentration and takes Gilda's action. The −1d4 debuff persists to the very next save the target makes, which means the follow-up save-requiring spell must come promptly. This chain tips borderline WIS and STR saves in the party's favour without spending additional resources.

---

### Chain 3: The Advantage Web — Mall-Lowick → Everyone

**Mall-Lowick: Entangle** → multiple targets Restrained → every party member attacks with Advantage against affected creatures.

When Entangle catches two or more enemies:
- **Rixon** swings with Advantage (Longsword + Sap on a reliable hit)
- **Cade** attacks or casts Word of Radiance with Advantage setup
- **Gilda** fires Chromatic Orb with Advantage — dramatically increasing average damage
- **Mall-Lowick** switches to Hunter's Mark or follows up with her Longbow at Advantage

This is the party's highest-efficiency opening against a grouped or advancing enemy. When Mall-Lowick sees multiple enemies moving toward Rixon, Entangle is almost always the correct choice over any single-target option.

---

### Chain 4: The Sap/Vex Feedback Loop — Rixon + Mall-Lowick

Applied to the **same target** in the same round:

**Rixon hits with Longsword** → Sap mastery: target has Disadvantage on its next attack roll.
**Mall-Lowick hits the same target with Shortsword** → Vex mastery: she gains Advantage on her next attack roll against it.

Result: Rixon is harder to hit; Mall-Lowick hits more reliably. The mastery effects compound across rounds as long as both keep targeting the same creature. This loop should be established through explicit target coordination — call the mark.

---

### Chain 5: The AC Stack — Cade + Rixon (+ Rixon's own Shield)

**Cade: Shield of Faith (BA)** → Rixon +2 AC (AC 18 → 20).
**Rixon: Shield (Reaction, 1 free/LR)** → +5 AC vs one incoming attack (AC 20 → 25 briefly).

Rixon at AC 20 is exceptional at Level 1. His free Shield reaction as a backup means a determined assault on him still faces AC 25 on its most dangerous single hit. This combination makes him functionally unkillable in most Level 1 encounters and is the correct default setup when entering any fight where the enemy configuration is unknown.

---

### Chain 6: The Prone Combo — Gilda → Cade → Rixon

**Gilda: Mind Sliver** → target's next save reduced by 1d4.
**Cade: Command "GROVEL"** → WIS save DC 13 (now reduced) → target falls Prone on its next turn.
**Rixon: Longsword** → attacks Prone target within 5 ft with Advantage (Prone grants Advantage to melee attackers within 5 ft).

Setup cost: two actions and a spell slot. Payoff: a Prone enemy at Advantage that also has Disadvantage on its counterattack from Rixon's Sap. Against a single powerful enemy, this combination can shift the trajectory of an encounter. Note that Command takes effect on the target's *next* turn — there is a one-round delay between Cade's spell and the Prone landing.

---

### Chain 7: Thunderwave as a Repositioning Tool — Gilda + Mall-Lowick

**Gilda: Thunderwave** → enemies pushed 10 ft on a failed CON save.
**Mall-Lowick: Entangle zone** → positioned to catch pushed enemies.

If Mall-Lowick has pre-positioned an Entangle zone behind a cluster of enemies, Gilda's Thunderwave can knock them backward into it, combining AoE damage with Restrained. This requires communication and pre-positioning — not always achievable — but when the terrain allows it, it produces a devastating outcome.

**Coordination note:** Gilda should *never* Thunderwave when Rixon or Cade is in the 15 ft cube. Establish a clear signal or rule about this before it matters in combat.

---

## Turn Order Guidelines

At Level 1, the optimal turn order within a round is roughly:

1. **Gilda** (if going first via Alert swap): Mind Sliver on the priority target
2. **Rixon**: Attack with Longsword (Sap); use Alert swap if he has already rolled
3. **Cade**: Shield of Faith on Rixon as Bonus Action; Command, Word of Radiance, or attack as Action
4. **Mall-Lowick**: Hunter's Mark as Bonus Action + Longbow; or Entangle if facing multiple enemies

In practice, initiative is random and the ideal order is rarely achieved. The **core principle**: Gilda's Mind Sliver should precede any party save-based spell in a given round; Rixon should establish his position before Gilda advances into the fight; and Cade's Shield of Faith is most valuable in round one before damage has accumulated.

## Exploration Synergy

### Navigation

Three party members have Navigator's Tools proficiency (Cade, Gilda, Mall-Lowick), plus Mall-Lowick has Cartographer's Tools and Survival +4. The party will never be lost and will generally have useful maps of wherever they have been. Assign Mall-Lowick as primary navigator; the others provide redundancy and can independently confirm her readings.

### Perception Coverage

Cade (Perception +5, Passive 15) and Mall-Lowick (Perception +4, Passive 14) are the party's twin sentinels. Running two high-Passive Perception characters together means the DM must work deliberately to surprise this party. On shared watch, this pairing is the correct assignment.

### Darkvision Gap

Only Gilda has Darkvision (120 ft). Rixon's Light cantrip covers the party but broadcasts their position. In stealth-sensitive dark environments: Gilda leads and describes; the party navigates on her direction. In environments where noise and light are acceptable: Rixon's Light covers everyone.

### Scout Protocols

**Full stealth approach:** Impossible with Rixon (permanent Disadvantage in Chain Mail). Never attempt a full-party stealth approach.

**2+2 split:** Mall-Lowick + Gilda scout ahead (Mall-Lowick has Stealth +3 and Halfling Luck; Gilda has Darkvision and Stealth +2). Cade + Rixon hold position and cover the retreat.

**Solo advance:** Mall-Lowick alone, hiding behind Cade until she leaves the perimeter (Naturally Stealthy). This maximises her concealment during the departure phase.

## Resource Management Between Encounters

After a fight, assess the party's state before moving on:

1. **Cade's slots:** If both are expended, a Long Rest is increasingly necessary. If one remains, he can manage.
2. **Gilda's Mage Armour:** Still active (8-hour duration)? If she was knocked unconscious and it dropped, she needs to recast it before the next encounter.
3. **Mall-Lowick's free Hunter's Mark casts:** How many remain of the 2/Long Rest? Her spell slots as backup?
4. **Rixon's Shield cast:** Used? Refreshes on Long Rest.
5. **Arcane Recovery:** Has Gilda used it today? A Short Rest after Gilda's first slot expenditure is efficient.
6. **Second Wind charges:** Has Rixon used his 2/Long Rest? Recovers 1 on Short Rest.

**When to Short Rest:** When Gilda has expended at least one slot (Arcane Recovery) *and/or* Rixon has used at least one Second Wind charge (recovers 1 on SR). The combination makes every Short Rest valuable for two party members simultaneously.

**When to Long Rest:** When Cade has used Healing Word and/or Shield of Faith significantly; when the party's aggregate HP is substantially reduced; and at the end of each full adventuring day. Cade going into a fight without spell slots is the party's most serious resource crisis.
# Party Dynamics — Analysis

---

## Overview

Four characters with four distinct worldviews, four different relationships to law, money, duty, and risk. Their alignments span from Lawful Good (Cade) to Chaotic Good (Mall-Lowick) with Neutral Good (Rixon) and True Neutral (Gilda) in between. The tensions this creates are not obstacles to the campaign — they are the engine of it. A party that agrees about everything produces no interesting decisions. This party will disagree in ways that illuminate who all of them are.

---

## Alignment & Motivation at a Glance

| Character | Alignment | Core Motivation |
|---|---|---|
| Cade | Lawful Good | Loyalty; sacred duty; protection of people he has claimed as his own |
| Rixon | Neutral Good | Practical decency; good outcomes pursued by flexible means |
| Mall-Lowick | Chaotic Good | Knowledge; freedom; the greater good as she defines it |
| Gilda | True Neutral | Self-interest; profit; the integrity of agreements she has made |

**The central fault lines:** The Law axis (Cade vs. Mall-Lowick) and the Good axis (Cade vs. Gilda). Rixon sits near the intersection of both and frequently serves as the swing vote. These fault lines produce the party's most interesting decisions.

---

## Pairwise Dynamics

### Cade + Rixon — The Protector and the Professional

**The dynamic:** Mutual respect built on physical and moral complementarity, expressed through understatement.

Both characters are built around keeping others safe, but from entirely different motivations. Cade does it from sacred duty and deep personal love. Rixon does it from professional training and a decency he performs less enthusiasm for than he actually feels. They fight together as though they were designed for it — Rixon holds the line, Cade keeps him standing — and they have an efficiency together that comes from understanding, without needing to discuss it, what the other person will do.

**The tension:** Rixon cultivates cynicism as a lifestyle. He makes a show of being unmoved by sentiment, noble causes, and divine devotion. Cade, with Insight +5, sees through this immediately and does not comment — which Rixon finds simultaneously irritating and relieving. He knows Cade knows. They have reached an unspoken agreement not to bring it up. Cade accepts Rixon's performed hardness as the armour it is; Rixon accepts Cade's genuine warmth as the force it is. Neither of them puts any of this into words. They don't need to.

**Alignment friction:** Low. They agree on what should happen in most situations; they sometimes disagree on how much principle should be sacrificed to make it happen. Rixon operates comfortably outside the rules, deals with unsavoury operators, and accepts short-term moral compromise for long-term outcomes without the friction of guilt. Cade notices and registers disagreement, but rarely pushes hard against Rixon specifically — partly because he trusts Rixon's underlying intentions and partly because Rixon rarely proposes anything genuinely wrong, just sometimes something he finds uncomfortable.

**Shared language:** Giant. In a crowded room, they can speak privately in a language that almost no one else understands. This is both mechanically useful and emotionally significant: it is the first currency of genuine trust between them, the language of Cade's people gifted to Rixon by a blacksmith who carried it across a sea. When Cade first heard Rixon speak Giant, with the particular coastal inflection of the highland communities near Stoneveil Reach, something shifted.

**The Orrath thread:** Rixon learned Giant from a Goliath blacksmith named Orrath Stone-Handed, who came from the highland settlement of Greymantle Keep — a Goliath community in the hills above the same coastal stretch where Cade grew up. Cade doesn't know Orrath personally; Orrath had already emigrated to Ashford before Cade was grown. But when Rixon speaks Giant and mentions the name Greymantle Keep, Cade recognises it. The specific word for the mountain fog that rolls off the ridges in autumn. The particular phrasing for a debt of honour. These are not things you learn from a textbook. This recognition is quiet — Cade doesn't make a production of it — but it means something.

---

### Cade + Gilda — The True Believer and the True Neutral

**The dynamic:** Mutual professional indispensability masking a slow, grudging, genuine regard.

Cade acts from sacred conviction and personal loyalty. Gilda acts from calculated self-interest and the cold logic of contract. This is the party's sharpest philosophical divide, and it produces the most interesting debates.

**The tension:** Gilda finds Cade's generosity inefficient and his devotion baffling. She has asked, on at least one occasion, why he is doing something for free. He answered, and she found the answer unsatisfying from a business standpoint but could not find a specific flaw in the reasoning. Cade finds Gilda's neutrality morally incomplete — he would like her to care more — but he has Insight +5 and he sees, beneath the contracts and cost-benefit analyses, that Gilda has her own form of integrity. She keeps her word even when it costs her. She does not cheat people who have dealt with her honestly. She does not take more than her agreed share. Cade recognises this as a foundation to build on, even if it isn't the foundation he would choose.

**Alignment friction:** The highest in the party. When the group must choose between a morally correct action with a real cost and a pragmatic action with a moral compromise, Cade and Gilda are at opposite ends. She asks what it costs and what it buys; he asks what is right regardless of cost. Neither position is unreasonable. Their debates will be, at their best, genuinely difficult.

**Shared language:** Dwarvish. A bridge between two very different relationships to Dwarven culture — Gilda grew up in it commercially, navigating the mountain hold trade networks; Cade learned it as a language of the deep harbours and the dockside merchants. They occasionally slip into Dwarvish in conversation, usually when they want to discuss something without Rixon or Mall-Lowick following. Rixon has learned to be mildly suspicious when this happens.

**The slow thaw:** Gilda comes to trust Cade not because she is moved by his faith — she isn't, particularly — but because he consistently honours his end of a working relationship. He heals when healing is needed. He tells the truth even when a lie would be easier. He does not take more than his agreed share. In her moral vocabulary, this makes him a reliable contractual partner, and that is the highest category she knows how to offer. She would never say, in so many words, that she is glad he's there. But she allocates appropriate resources to keeping him alive, and for Gilda, this is the equivalent.

---

### Cade + Mall-Lowick — The Giant and the Halfling

**The dynamic:** Fond exasperation, in both directions, on top of genuine mutual trust.

Mall-Lowick is chaotic, curious, and constitutionally incapable of treating a "keep out" sign as a binding legal document. Cade is lawful, warm, and gently horrified by this on a regular basis. And yet he is enormously fond of her. She reminds him of the childhood friends he left home to protect: the restless ones who would have gotten themselves killed without someone watching the door. He has been watching doors for people like Mall-Lowick his entire adult life. He is very good at it.

**The tension:** Cade sometimes wants to pick Mall-Lowick up and deposit her somewhere safe. Mall-Lowick sometimes wants to drag Cade through a gap in a fence into somewhere interesting. Neither of them ever fully wins this negotiation. They have reached a working arrangement: she tells him, where possible, before doing the hazardous thing, and he agrees to follow rather than try to stop her, provided the hazardous thing is not actively wrong as well as merely illegal. This arrangement breaks down approximately once per adventure, which is acceptable to both of them.

**Alignment friction:** Moderate. Their practical disagreements cluster around authority, permissions, and the meaning of "private property." Cade's Lawful Good means he cares about the rules that communities make to organise themselves — not blindly, but genuinely. Mall-Lowick's Chaotic Good means she cares about outcomes, specifically the outcomes she has decided matter, and will route around rules that she considers obstacles rather than guidelines. They agree on what matters; they disagree sharply on whether the route to it should involve a stamped permit.

**Mechanical comedy:** Cade is 7'6". Mall-Lowick is 2'6". She can hide behind him using Naturally Stealthy. The image of a halfling cartographer using a Goliath cleric as a bush recurs throughout their adventures, and it is never entirely not funny, including to both of them. Cade has stopped commenting on it. Mall-Lowick has started doing it slightly more extravagantly.

**Shared strength:** Their Perception scores (+5 and +4 respectively) make them the party's twin sentinels. On watch together, almost nothing approaches without them knowing. This shared competence, unspoken and practical, underpins their trust at a level beneath the comedy and the friction.

---

### Gilda + Mall-Lowick — The Patron and the Cartographer

**The dynamic:** A pre-existing professional relationship with genuine mutual respect, some unresolved texture, and a comfortable shared language in more than one sense.

Gilda managed trade routes between Dwarven mountain holds and Halfling river-vine communities. Mall-Lowick mapped those same routes as a professional cartographer. The simplest explanation for their acquaintance is that Gilda hired her, and the evidence supports this: they work together without the friction of people who are getting to know each other, they share the Halfling language, and they have an instinctive shorthand for logistical problems that suggests prior collaboration.

**The history:** Their working relationship was almost certainly successful — Gilda values competence above personality, and Mall-Lowick is genuinely excellent at what she does. Whether all the invoices were settled on time, and whether Mall-Lowick's maps fully satisfied the scope of what Gilda commissioned, is a small outstanding question that adds texture to their interactions without poisoning them. It is the kind of thing that comes up at quiet moments: "You still owe me the coastal approaches from the third survey." "I provided the coastal approaches." "You provided *your* interpretation of the coastal approaches, which differed from the agreed specification on three points." "That's because the agreed specification was wrong." This is how they talk to each other, and they both enjoy it.

**The tension:** Their motivations for adventure run in parallel without being identical. Gilda views discovery as inventory — something to be assessed, valued, and sold or leveraged. Mall-Lowick views discovery as knowledge — something to be documented, shared, and added to the sum of what is known. They want the same things for different reasons, which means they rarely compete but frequently have different opinions about what to do with what they find.

**Shared language:** Halfling. Gilda learned it for commerce; Mall-Lowick was born to it. Their private conversations sometimes happen in Halfling, and Mall-Lowick has strong, specifically articulated opinions about how Gilda speaks it. (Precise vocabulary; correct grammar; a Dwarven commercial cadence that makes her sound like she is reading from a trading ledger. Mall-Lowick has started correcting her, which Gilda accepts in the spirit of professional development.)

**The luck pair:** Both have exceptional luck mechanisms — Gilda's Lucky feat (2 Luck Points per LR) and Mall-Lowick's Halfling Luck (reroll 1s). This makes them the most reliable pairing for high-stakes reconnaissance where a single catastrophic failure would end the mission. They are the correct team to send somewhere the party cannot afford to fail.

---

### Gilda + Rixon — The Calculator and the Cynic

**The dynamic:** Instant mutual recognition between two people who understand the world the same way, requiring no explanation.

Neither Gilda nor Rixon is naive. Both have worked in systems where sentiment is expensive, competence is the only currency that holds value, and trust is extended specifically, not generally. They understand each other in a way that needs no introduction. They are both operators, and they recognise the type.

**The dynamic in practice:** Gilda says what the situation requires. Rixon communicates, non-verbally and efficiently, what the cost of not cooperating would look like. This is not cruelty; it is an effective division of labour. In cities, in tavern back rooms, in negotiations with people who are not inclined to make deals, they are a formidable pair.

**The investigation duo:** Gilda's Investigation +5 and Arcana +5 locate and analyse the information. Rixon's Intimidation +3, History +4, demimonde connections, and Knavebones gaming set extract information from sources that prefer not to provide it. They are an excellent urban team: she identifies what is worth finding; he helps people remember where it is.

**Alignment friction:** Minimal. Gilda is True Neutral; Rixon is Neutral Good. They land in the same place on most decisions. The difference is that Rixon has a genuine moral floor that Gilda lacks — he will not participate in something he has decided is actually wrong, and this threshold is lower than Gilda's. When Cade wants the party to do the morally correct thing at real cost, Gilda and Rixon exchange a look, but Rixon is more likely to end up on Cade's side of the argument than Gilda is, and Gilda is aware of this.

**The risk:** Their combined pragmatism, untempered, can produce genuinely questionable conclusions. When Cade and Mall-Lowick are both distracted or outvoted, the party's moral centre of gravity shifts toward "what works" in ways that occasionally need to be examined.

---

### Rixon + Mall-Lowick — The Soldier and the Chaos Agent

**The dynamic:** Professional friction that has, slowly and without either party fully intending it, produced unlikely mutual trust.

Rixon and Mall-Lowick share no reverence for authority or official permission — they've each settled that question for themselves, from different directions. The friction is something else: Rixon is a professional who lives by risk management, and Mall-Lowick is an enthusiast who lives by curiosity. He always has an exit planned. She is frequently already inside before she has considered the exit. By all reasonable prediction, they should drive each other mad. They do. But it is productive madness: Rixon disciplines Mall-Lowick's plans toward the executable; Mall-Lowick pulls Rixon into ventures that his own caution would have talked him out of. The net effect is that they both operate closer to the correct level of risk than they would separately.

**The tension:** Specific, recurring, and mostly expressed through short exchanges. Rixon says "the exit's wrong on this one." Mall-Lowick says "there's always an exit." Rixon says "that's not what I meant." Mall-Lowick is already through the door. This cycle repeats with variations. Rixon has stopped explaining the problem and started positioning to cover the exit — which is, he will privately concede, what she was doing all along.

**Shared mastery:** Both use the **Vex** weapon mastery — Mall-Lowick on her Shortsword, Rixon on his Handaxe. This mechanical overlap is a shorthand for the way they operate in a fight: one tags the target, the other capitalises. In practice, they have developed an informal call-and-response in combat that doesn't require discussion.

**The alignment arithmetic:** Rixon's Neutral Good and Mall-Lowick's Chaotic Good both land on "good outcomes by imperfect means," but they arrive there from different compass bearings. Mall-Lowick's flexibility is philosophical: she doesn't recognise that the rules apply to her, and builds her justifications from there. Rixon's flexibility is equally thorough, but more ironic: he is perfectly aware that the rules nominally apply to him, and simply proceeds anyway, wearing the appropriate face for the occasion. Both positions produce similar decisions in practice. The difference surfaces in tone — she is cheerfully untroubled; he is drily entertained.

---

## Group Dynamics

### The Deliberation Pattern

When the party must make a collective decision with a moral dimension, they tend to break as follows:

| Option | Who Supports It |
|---|---|
| The costly, principled right thing | Cade (always); Mall-Lowick (usually, if it doesn't require following rules) |
| The pragmatic, morally flexible thing | Gilda (usually); Rixon (often) |
| The unconventional, technically-right-but-unorthodox thing | Mall-Lowick (enthusiastically); Rixon (when in the mood) |

**Rixon is the swing vote.** He sits between Cade's principled clarity and Gilda's transactional calculus, and his decision in any given moment determines which way the party goes. This makes him, practically speaking, the most powerful voice in group deliberations — a role he is mildly uncomfortable with and projects no awareness of.

### What Holds the Party Together

Despite the significant differences in worldview and method, this party shares three things that make it functional:

**Competence.** Each member is excellent at what they do. This creates mutual professional respect that predates personal trust and will survive its occasional failures. They know they are better at their respective jobs than most people they will encounter, and they know the others know this too.

**Self-reliance.** None of them came from a position of safety. Cade left home to protect people; Rixon's formative arrangements fell through; Gilda built her operation from scratch; Mall-Lowick works alone in the wilderness by default. They know how to function under pressure and do not require the team to be stable in order to operate within it.

**Adaptability.** None of them are ideologues. Cade will bend method for loyalty. Gilda will accept a lower return if the alternative costs too much of what she considers her integrity. Rixon will fight past his practiced cynicism when he has decided something matters. Mall-Lowick will stop at a fence if someone she cares about is on the other side of it. They each have a floor beneath which they will not go, and they have each learned — provisionally, carefully — where the others' floors are.

### Flashpoints

The scenarios most likely to produce genuine intra-party conflict:

- **Cade asks the party to do something morally right at significant personal cost** → Gilda resists → structured debate ensues → Rixon's decision determines the outcome
- **Mall-Lowick's trespassing creates a legal or political problem** → Rixon, who knows exactly how these situations work from the inside, becomes the party's most useful asset for making the problem disappear — which he does with a certain practiced ease, and sometimes bills for separately
- **A financial opportunity that requires ethical compromise** → Gilda is interested, Cade is not, Mall-Lowick assesses the epistemic value of what would be discovered, Rixon is the deciding vote
- **Rixon's past surfaces publicly** — the discharged militia, the arrangement with the blacksmith that fell through — in a way that creates political complications for the group
- **Cade's childhood friends reappear**, and their need conflicts with the party's current obligations — who does he choose?
- **Gilda makes a unilateral decision on the party's behalf** based on her own cost-benefit analysis without consulting the others — and turns out to be right about the outcome, which makes the breach of trust more rather than less complicated
# Cade — Strategic Analysis

---

## Role

**Primary Healer / Secondary Melee / Battlefield Support**

Cade occupies a rare hybrid role: the party's only source of magical healing and a capable close-quarters brawler. He is not a backline priest. Think of him as a brawling medic — someone who belongs near the fighting, reacting to wounds and threats alike, never far from where things are hottest. The key to playing him well is understanding that his value comes precisely from not retreating to safety: he heals, then punches something.

---

## Combat Priorities

### The Bonus Action Is Everything

Cade's Bonus Action is his most contested and valuable resource. Decide what to do with it *before* the round begins:

| Priority | Bonus Action | When |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | **Healing Word** (2d4+3 HP, 60 ft range) | When an ally is down or critically low |
| 2 | **Shield of Faith** (+2 AC, Concentration) | Round one, before Rixon takes damage |
| 3 | **Tavern Brawler Push** (after Unarmed Strike) | When repositioning an enemy matters |

He cannot do all three in one round of combat. Pick based on the situation.

### Concentration Budget

Cade will usually want to concentrate on **Shield of Faith** applied to Rixon (bumping him from AC 18 to AC 20) or himself. This means **Guidance** is a non-combat resource — he shouldn't be maintaining both. The decision point at the start of each encounter: is this a fight (Shield of Faith on), or a challenge where ability checks matter (Guidance)?

---

## Spell Usage

**Healing Word** — his most important spell, and the reason he is irreplaceable. The Bonus Action cast lets him heal *and* still act offensively on the same turn. At 60 ft range, he rarely needs to be adjacent to a downed ally. He can drag someone back from 0 HP and then swing a fist at a goblin, all in the same moment. This is what makes him so effective: he doesn't sacrifice his action to heal.

**Shield of Faith** — default target is **Rixon** (AC 18 → 20 is exceptional at Level 1). Secondary target is himself to preserving the party's access to healing or **Gilda** in situations where she has lost Mage Armour or is under sustained threat. Costs a Bonus Action to cast and Concentration to maintain.

**Command** — use strategically on high-value single targets. "GROVEL" (target falls prone on its next turn) synergises powerfully with Rixon's melee attacks, which hit prone adjacent targets at Advantage. "HALT" (target doesn't move) neutralises fast enemies. "FLEE" disrupts spellcasters. Requires a WIS save vs DC 13 — a decent hit rate at Level 1. Consider pairing with Gilda's Mind Sliver, which reduces the target's next save by 1d4.

**Word of Radiance** — situationally excellent when 2+ enemies are within 5 ft. CON save (DC 13) means many low-CON enemies fail. Good for: clearing enemies who swarm Rixon's position, punishing anything that charges Cade directly, and asserting presence in tight corridors where melee becomes a scrum.

**Create or Destroy Water** — mostly utility. *Create water* can extinguish fires, turn a dust floor into difficult terrain (wet stone), or create a fog effect in some scenarios. Think creatively rather than treating it as purely logistical.

---

## Key Traits in Play

**Storm Giant Ancestry — Reaction (2/LR):** When Cade takes damage, he can deal 1d8 Thunder to the attacker if they are within 30 ft. This is free damage on a reaction — remember it exists. It also communicates to enemies that hitting Cade has a cost, which subtly discourages targeting him. Use both charges in fights where enemies are swinging at him repeatedly.

**Tavern Brawler:** Unarmed Strike (1d4+1 Bludgeoning, reroll 1s) with a push of 5 ft once per turn is more useful than it looks. Pushing a spellcaster away from the group, shoving an enemy into difficult terrain or a hazard, or repositioning a flanker — all of these cost only his action, and the push stacks with his normal movement. His Unarmed Strike is also a fallback weapon when he's in tight spaces where a dagger is less appropriate.

**Insight +5:** In social encounters, Cade is the party's emotional intelligence. Even when Gilda leads a negotiation, Cade should be in the room — his read on whether the other party is lying, frightened, or hiding something is more reliable than anyone else's by a considerable margin. He doesn't need to speak; he just needs to observe and pass a note.

**Perception +5 / Passive Perception 15:** Along with Mall-Lowick, Cade is one of the party's two sentinels. Almost nothing approaches the party on watch without him knowing. On shared watch duties, he and Mall-Lowick are the ideal pairing.

**Thaumaturgy:** Don't underestimate pure performance. A Goliath Cleric causing minor tremors, making his eyes glow the colour of a storm sky, and speaking in a voice that fills the room is *impressive* in a way that no skill check can fully replicate. Remember that the Booming Voice effect grants Advantage on Intimidation checks. Use it for dramatic effect in negotiations, when the party needs to make an entrance, and whenever Cade wants to remind someone that he is, in fact, very large and connected to a storm deity.

---

## Where Cade Shines

- When allies are at low HP and he can Healing Word (BA) *and* attack or cast on the same turn
- Protecting the party's backline by engaging enemies who break through to Gilda
- Reading social situations that Gilda and Rixon — focused on transaction and threat respectively — miss
- Against enemies who cluster: Word of Radiance punishes groups within 5 ft
- In maritime, coastal, or nautical situations (Sailor background, Navigator's Tools, Water Vehicles proficiency)
- When the party needs someone who is simultaneously physically imposing and spiritually credible — negotiating with religious authorities, calming frightened civilians, dealing with communities who don't trust the party

## Where Cade Struggles

- When he goes down: the party has no redundancy on magical healing whatsoever
- Maintaining Concentration on Shield of Faith while being targeted frequently — CON +2 means he'll fail some of those saves under sustained fire
- Intelligence-based tasks: INT −1 means he should defer entirely to Gilda (Arcana, Investigation) and Rixon (History)
- Stealth: his +1 Stealth and 8' Goliath frame make him a liability in any silent approach — he should hold back and cover the retreat

---

## Roleplaying Notes

Cade is the party's emotional centre. His Lawful Good alignment means he will push back — clearly and without flinching — against choices that sacrifice principle for efficiency. He and Gilda represent the party's clearest philosophical counterpoint, and their debates will be among its most interesting moments. He will not shout. He will not threaten. He will simply be disappointed, which is somehow worse.

His warmth is genuine and his protectiveness is instinctive. He is not naive — he has seen enough of the world from a ship's deck to know that people lie and people die, often simultaneously — but he chooses, consciously and repeatedly, to lead with trust. He has Insight +5, which means he usually knows when the trust is misplaced. He extends it anyway.

He is perceptive enough to see through Rixon's cultivated cynicism, warm enough to be fond of Mall-Lowick's chaos, and patient enough to maintain a working relationship with Gilda's cold ledger-book morality. He is not the party's loudest voice, but he is probably its most important one.
# Gilda — Strategic Analysis

---

## Role

**Arcane Blaster / Investigator / Secondary Face**

Gilda is the party's arcane firepower and its sharpest intellectual instrument. She is also its most fragile member, with 8 HP and no armour proficiency. Her strategic value is enormous; her survivability is the party's most pressing tactical concern. The key to playing her well is understanding three things: her spell slots are *scarce and decisive* (not opening-volley tools); her ritual spells are *free and chronically underused*; and her positioning in combat is always wrong until she moves.

---

## Combat Priorities

### Mage Armour Is Non-Negotiable

Cast it every morning. Every single morning. It lasts 8 hours and the AC difference (12 → 15) is enormous on a 1d6 hit die character. If she somehow doesn't have it up when combat begins, this is the first spell cast before anything else happens, full stop. Treat the slot cost as a daily tax, not a tactical choice.

### Spell Slot Economy

Gilda has 2 Level 1 slots and Arcane Recovery (1/day: recover 1 Level 1 slot on a Short Rest). She effectively gets approximately 3 slots per adventuring day. Chromatic Orb and Thunderwave are not opening salvos — they are decisive strikes, deployed when a single powerful action changes the shape of the fight.

| Slot Usage | Priority |
|---|---|
| **Mage Armour** | Cast each morning — non-negotiable |
| **Chromatic Orb** | Use against high-value or high-HP single targets; or when an element match creates vulnerability |
| **Thunderwave** | Use reactively when Gilda is cornered, or to reposition enemies into the party's zones |

Her cantrips (Mind Sliver, True Strike, Prestidigitation) are her primary combat actions most rounds. She should rarely need a spell slot to contribute meaningfully to a fight.

### Positioning

Gilda should never be adjacent to an enemy voluntarily. Her standard combat position: behind Rixon, with 15–20 ft of buffer. If an enemy closes to melee, Thunderwave is the answer. If Thunderwave is expended, she uses True Strike as a last resort and the party has a positioning problem to solve.

---

## Spell Usage

**Mind Sliver (Cantrip):** 1d6 Psychic damage (INT save, DC 13) and the target suffers −1d4 on its *next* saving throw. This cantrip is deceptively powerful precisely because of that secondary effect. The debuff persists to the next save the target makes — meaning if Cade plans to use Command, or Mall-Lowick plans Ensnaring Strike, Gilda should use Mind Sliver *first* that round to soften the resistance. The Psychic damage also bypasses many common resistances. This is her default combat cantrip.

**True Strike (Cantrip):** Uses INT modifier (+3) for a weapon attack dealing Radiant or Bludgeoning damage. Gilda's quarterstaff becomes a +5 to hit, 1d6+3 (or 1d8+3 two-handed) Radiant damage weapon. This is an emergency melee option when she is out of spell slots and has been cornered, or against creatures with elemental resistance that makes Chromatic Orb inefficient.

**Prestidigitation:** Utility. Minor sensory effects — cleaning, flavouring, making objects smell, producing small sounds, creating cold or warmth. Often overlooked; frequently the most elegant solution to a problem that doesn't require violence.

**Chromatic Orb (Level 1):** 3d8 elemental damage — type chosen at casting from Acid, Cold, Fire, Lightning, Poison, or Thunder. Average 13.5 damage with the ability to match vulnerabilities makes this the most *flexible* Level 1 damage spell available. The "leaps on doubles" property adds a chance of secondary hits. Reserve for: high-HP enemies, creatures with specific elemental vulnerabilities, and moments where a single decisive hit changes the trajectory of the fight.

**Thunderwave (Level 1):** 2d8 Thunder damage + 10 ft push in a 15 ft cube originating from Gilda (CON save, DC 13). Hits every creature in the area — **including allies**. Coordinate before use. Never fire it when Rixon or Cade is within the cone. Best applications:
- Clearing melee range when Gilda has been cornered or swarmed
- Pushing enemies into Mall-Lowick's Entangle zone or toward environmental hazards
- Knocking enemies prone or off ledges in appropriate terrain
- Creating separation between the party's frontline and a dangerous enemy closing too fast

**Identify (Ritual):** Costs no spell slot when cast as a ritual (10-minute casting time). Learns all magical properties of an object or the spells affecting a creature. Use on every unidentified item the party finds. This is free information — there is no reason not to.

**Alarm (Ritual):** Sets a 20 ft radius alarm — either a mental ping to Gilda, or an audible bell — lasting 8 hours. Cast every time the party rests in a dangerous location. This is free camp security. It should be a standing habit, not a special occasion.

**Tenser's Floating Disk (Ritual):** A 3 ft diameter floating platform following Gilda, carrying up to 500 lbs. Pure logistics — it removes the encumbrance problem entirely for long carries, enables creative problem-solving (lowering heavy objects slowly, carrying an unconscious ally, building an improvised barrier), and is the single most useful thing a Level 1 Wizard can do for a party that has been clearing a dungeon. Cost: a 10-minute ritual. Worth it constantly.

---

## Key Traits in Play

**Lucky (Feat, 2/LR):** Spend 1 Luck Point to gain Advantage on any d20 test, or to give an enemy Disadvantage on an attack roll against Gilda. The defensive use — forcing Disadvantage on an incoming attack — is essentially a situational +5 AC for a single strike. Save both charges for genuinely pivotal moments: a Concentration save on a critical spell, a save against something that would knock Gilda unconscious, a crucial Persuasion roll. Do not spend them on routine checks.

**Darkvision (120 ft):** Gilda sees in darkness as if it were dim light. She is the party's designated lead in dark environments — when the party wants to avoid advertising their position with a light source, Gilda navigates ahead and communicates what she sees. This is a significant tactical advantage underground and in unlit spaces.

**Dwarven Resilience:** Advantage on saving throws vs. Poison and Resistance to Poison damage. Niche, but Poison damage and the Poisoned condition are among the most common threats at low levels (venomous creatures, traps, contaminated food and water). This saves her on a meaningful number of rolls.

**Dwarven Toughness:** Already factored into her HP total (+1 max HP per level). Gives her marginally more durability than a typical Wizard — though "marginally more" on 8 HP is still 8 HP.

**Stonecunning / Tremorsense (2/LR, Bonus Action):** While on stone, Gilda can spend a Bonus Action to gain Tremorsense (60 ft) for 10 minutes. In stone dungeons or underground environments, she can sense creatures moving through floors and walls before they arrive. This is situationally exceptional as an ambush warning system and should not be forgotten.

**Arcane Recovery (1/day):** Once a day, after a Short Rest, recover one expended Level 1 slot. Coordinate Short Rest timing — if Gilda has expended a slot, a Short Rest benefits her (Arcane Recovery) and Rixon simultaneously (Second Wind charge). If she hasn't expended anything, the Short Rest primarily benefits Rixon.

**Investigation +5 / Arcana +5:** Gilda is the party's foremost investigator and arcane scholar. Any discovered object, inscription, arcane mechanism, written code, or unfamiliar magical phenomenon should be referred to her first and without hesitation.

**Persuasion +4:** The highest Persuasion modifier in the party. She leads in mercantile, logistical, and transactional negotiations — any situation where the other party wants to make a deal rather than a friend. In situations requiring warmth, moral authority, or genuine empathy, Cade should be present to complement.

**WIS Save +2 (Proficient):** As a Wizard, Gilda has proficiency in Wisdom saving throws. This is not nothing — she resists Charm and Fear effects better than Rixon, who has the same total but without proficiency's scaling.

---

## Where Gilda Shines

- Identifying magical items and arcane phenomena — the party's irreplaceable expert
- Investigating clues: Investigation +5 is the highest in the party by a significant margin
- Mercantile, transactional, and logistical social encounters
- Dark and underground environments — Darkvision 120 ft is a major advantage
- Ritual casting — Identify, Alarm, and Tenser's Floating Disk cost no slots and solve problems constantly
- Concentrated, decisive spell use when slot economy is carefully managed
- Situations where Lucky prevents a catastrophic failure at the worst possible moment

## Where Gilda Struggles

- Melee range — she must never be there voluntarily. Any situation that puts enemies adjacent to her is an emergency
- Spell slot conservation — if she uses Chromatic Orb or Thunderwave casually, she runs dry before the party has a chance to rest
- Being identified and targeted by intelligent enemies: 8 HP means any enemy smart enough to kill the wizard first can succeed in two hits
- Surviving without Mage Armour — the first thing to check at the start of every session

---

## Roleplaying Notes

Gilda views the world as a ledger. Every action has a cost; every outcome has a value; every person she deals with is assessed as a counterparty in an ongoing negotiation. This is not cruelty or callousness — it is a complete and internally coherent moral framework. She helps the party because helping the party is in her interest, she maintains her commitments because a merchant who breaks contracts has no future, and she is genuinely excellent at everything she does because incompetence is expensive.

Her True Neutral alignment means she will not cross into cruelty — she has standards — but she will not sacrifice herself for abstract principles either. When the party debates a morally difficult decision, Gilda asks: "What does it cost? What does it buy?" She means this as the relevant and sufficient question. Cade finds it insufficient; their debates are among the party's most interesting moments.

Her Merchant background gives her a practical literacy that most academic wizards lack. She has negotiated across cultural lines, moved physical goods through dangerous terrain, and managed people who were trying to cheat her. She is not a sheltered bookish mage. She has been in the world, and it shows.
# Mall-Lowick — Strategic Analysis

---

## Role

**Scout / Skirmisher / Battlefield Controller**

Mall-Lowick is the party's most mobile member and its primary intelligence-gatherer in the field. She operates in two distinct modes: **Scout** (pre-combat and exploration — advance reconnaissance, tracking, cartography) and **Controller/Skirmisher** (in combat — establishing zones of denial and sustained single-target damage output). She is the smallest member of the party and the most frequently underestimated, which she exploits without hesitation.

---

## Combat Priorities

### The Concentration Choice: Hunter's Mark vs. Entangle

Mall-Lowick's two most powerful combat contributions both require Concentration. She can only maintain one at a time. This is her central tactical decision each fight:

| Concentration | Best Used When |
|---|---|
| **Hunter's Mark** | Single dominant enemy; sustained fights where the same target stays alive for multiple rounds |
| **Entangle** | Multiple enemies; enemies approaching the party en masse; the opening round of a large engagement |

**Hunter's Mark Rule of thumb:** Apply it as a Bonus Action at the start of combat when facing one strong enemy. Then attack every subsequent round — the +1d6 Force damage on every hit accumulates rapidly, and the Advantage on Perception/Survival to find the target is a useful bonus against enemies that flee or go invisible. With 2 free casts per Long Rest, she should never need to spend a spell slot for Hunter's Mark under normal circumstances.

**Entangle Rule of thumb:** Drop it on a cluster of enemies before they reach Rixon. The Restrained condition from Entangle gives the *entire party* Advantage on attack rolls against affected creatures — this is a multiplicative benefit, simultaneously upgrading Rixon's Longsword swings, Cade's Word of Radiance, Gilda's Chromatic Orb, and Mall-Lowick's own follow-up attacks. Against two or more enemies, Entangle almost always produces more value than Hunter's Mark.

### Weapon Selection

| Situation | Weapon | Why |
|---|---|---|
| Standard combat | **Longbow** | 150 ft range, out of retaliatory melee reach, Slow mastery |
| Enemy closes to melee | **Shortsword** | Vex mastery, Finesse (DEX +3), Light |
| Melee with Rixon in coordination | **Shortsword** | Vex/Sap feedback loop (see Synergies) |
| No obvious priority target | **Longbow + Slow mastery** | Controlling enemy approach speed to protect Gilda |

The Scimitar is a third Light Finesse weapon available for theoretical dual-wielding alongside the Shortsword. Without the Two-Weapon Fighting style, the off-hand attack adds no DEX modifier to damage — it is a secondary strike for a minor bonus, not her standard configuration. The Longbow and Shortsword should be her primary tools.

---

## Spell Usage

**Hunter's Mark (2 free casts/LR, Bonus Action):** Her bread-and-butter. +1d6 Force damage on every attack that hits the marked target, plus Advantage on Perception and Survival checks to locate them. This damage bonus applies whether she hits with the Longbow, the Shortsword, or the Scimitar. Switching the mark to a new target costs a Bonus Action — worth doing if the original target dies or ceases to be the priority.

**Entangle (Level 1, Action):** 20-ft square area of grasping plants (up to 90 ft away), turning the area into Difficult Terrain and forcing STR saves or Restrained. The Restrained condition:
- Sets the creature's speed to 0 (it cannot move)
- Gives all attack rolls against it Advantage
- Gives the creature Disadvantage on its own attack rolls
- Persists until the creature succeeds on an Athletics check (DC 12) as an Action, or the spell ends

Drop this in a doorway, corridor, or chokepoint and it neutralises an approach for multiple rounds. Drop it on already-engaged enemies and the whole party attacks at Advantage. A Restrained creature trying to escape spends its action on an Athletics check — that's an action it isn't spending on the party.

**Ensnaring Strike (Level 1, Bonus Action — used immediately after a hit):** Single-target Restrained applied through a weapon hit rather than an area. Good for singling out a specific dangerous enemy — a spellcaster who must be kept stationary, an archer who needs to stop moving, or a big single monster that is too dangerous to let act freely. Less area coverage than Entangle but more targeted. Competes with Hunter's Mark for concentration.

---

## Key Traits in Play

**Halfling Luck:** When Mall-Lowick rolls a 1 on a d20 Test, she automatically rerolls and must use the new result. This is passive, always active, and effectively removes her worst-case outcomes from the table. Combined with Gilda's Lucky feat, the two "lucky" party members are the most reliable in high-stakes situations where failure is catastrophic.

**Naturally Stealthy:** She can take the Hide action when obscured only by a creature at least one size larger than her. With Cade in the party — a Large-adjacent Goliath at 7'6" — she can hide *directly behind him* without any other cover or obstacle. This is both mechanically powerful and visually absurd, and she should use it consistently for advantageous positioning in combat and reconnaissance. She can also hide behind the other members of the party when required.

**Halfling Nimbleness:** She can move through the space of any creature larger than herself. In combat, this means she can pass through Rixon's square to reach flanking positions or escape engagement without triggering opportunity attacks. She moves *through*, never stops *in*. This makes her effectively un-cornerable in most indoor environments.

**Brave:** Advantage on saves to end or avoid the Frightened condition. The party has low collective Wisdom scores, making Fear one of its biggest vulnerabilities. Mall-Lowick is specifically resistant to this, which makes her the correct character to send into situations where morale-breaking effects are expected.

**Longbow — Slow Mastery:** On a hit, the target's Speed is reduced by 10 ft until the start of its next turn. Applied every round to a fast-moving enemy, this consistently prevents it from closing on Gilda or escaping engagement. Used on a charging enemy: it moves 10 ft less this turn, which may be exactly enough to keep it 5 ft away from the backline.

**Shortsword — Vex Mastery:** On a hit, Mall-Lowick gains Advantage on her next attack roll against that same target before the end of her next turn. Chains naturally with her own attacks and complements Rixon's Sap mastery — she Vexes a target (gaining Advantage), Rixon Saps the same target (giving it Disadvantage on its counterattack). The feedback loop runs in both directions.

---

## Exploration & Out-of-Combat Role

Mall-Lowick is the party's foremost exploration specialist, and this role is as valuable as her combat contribution.

**Scouting:** Stealth +3 with Naturally Stealthy (hiding behind Cade) and Halfling Luck (rerolling 1s) make her the correct character to advance ahead of the party through unknown territory. She should be the first one through any unopened door or unknown terrain feature. Her Perception +4 and Passive Perception 14 mean she spots most threats before they spot her.

**Tracking:** Survival +4 and Hunter's Mark (which grants Advantage on Perception and Survival checks to find the marked target) make her the party's tracker. If a target has been marked and then lost, she finds it again.

**Navigation:** Cartographer's Tools + Navigator's Tools + Survival +4 + Perception +4 make her the primary navigator for all wilderness and coastal travel. She can produce an actual, working map of where the party has been, which has in-game utility: selling maps, navigating back to previously discovered locations, and understanding the strategic geography of the region. This is something she should be doing as a matter of course, not only when explicitly asked.

**Wilderness Survival:** She can find food and water, identify safe camps and environmental hazards, navigate by stars and terrain features, and manage animals (Animal Handling +4). In outdoor environments, she leads by a significant margin.

**Investigation +3:** Not the party's best investigator — Gilda has +5 — but competent, especially for field investigation: tracks, disturbed vegetation, signs of passage, the physical evidence of what happened in a space rather than the arcane or textual analysis of it.

**Calligrapher's Supplies:** Often overlooked. She can transcribe discovered texts accurately, copy inscriptions from ruins, forge or produce documents, and take precise field notes. In a world with limited literacy, a skilled calligrapher has genuine practical value.

---

## Where Mall-Lowick Shines

- Opening combat from concealment — she frequently fires before enemies know her position
- Locking down multiple enemies with Entangle, creating party-wide Advantage simultaneously
- Sustained single-target damage via Hunter's Mark across a long fight
- Scouting and advance reconnaissance: she gathers the intelligence the rest of the party acts on
- Wilderness survival, navigation, and tracking — these are hers, uncontested
- Situations requiring mobility — she is the hardest character in the party to pin down, corner, or trap

## Where Mall-Lowick Struggles

- Head-on melee without Entangle: AC 15 and 12 HP are respectable but she does not want to trade blows toe-to-toe with anything serious
- Concentration management: choosing between Hunter's Mark and Entangle every combat is a genuine constraint, and losing concentration means starting over
- Social encounters: CHA +0 and no social proficiencies (beyond Animal Handling) make her one of the weaker social characters. She is direct, which is not always helpful
- Anything requiring physical strength: STR −1 and no Athletics proficiency mean she cannot contribute to feats of raw power

---

## Roleplaying Notes

Mall-Lowick is driven by a hunger for the undocumented and unmapped. To her, the world is a document in progress — every dungeon is an architectural survey waiting to be filed, every wilderness is a cartographic challenge, every unfamiliar creature is a field note to be recorded in ink. Her Chaotic Good alignment means she views the letter of the law as advisory and its spirit as the only authority worth respecting — and sometimes she decides that the spirit also doesn't apply here, specifically.

She is not reckless. She scouts carefully, plans approaches, and records findings meticulously. Her chaos is methodical: she will absolutely trespass, ignore posted warnings, and cross guarded borders in the dark, but she will do so with ink-stained fingers and a careful map of what she found on the other side. The distinction she draws — between "reckless" and "unauthorised" — is one that Rixon finds technically-defensible and fundamentally aggravating.

Her relationship with authority is one of professional assessment: she respects genuine expertise and ignores credentials she didn't verify herself. A ranger captain who says "there's something wrong in that valley" is a source. A lord who says "that valley is mine, keep out" is an obstacle. She does not always correctly identify which is which before it's too late, which is why the party having a Lawful Good cleric is probably good for everyone's survival.
# Rixon — Strategic Analysis

---

## Role

**Primary Tank / Front-Line Controller / Initiative Anchor**

Rixon is the party's wall. His job is to stand between dangerous things and the people he is working with — and he does this with AC 18, a self-heal in Second Wind, an initiative advantage via Alert, and enough offensive output to make enemies regret focusing on him. He is not just a damage dealer; he is a *platform*. The rest of the party operates around him.

---

## Combat Priorities

### Standard Loadout

**Longsword (1H) + Shield.** The Duelling fighting style gives +2 damage when using a one-handed weapon with no other weapon drawn. This is his consistent default:
- Longsword 1H + Shield: +5 to hit, **1d8+5** damage (average 9.5), Sap mastery, AC 18

**A note on the two-handed option:** Longsword 2H deals 1d10+3 (average 8.5). This is *less* average damage than 1H+Duelling and loses the +2 AC from the shield. There is almost no situation where switching to two-handed is correct. Keep the shield.

### Alert — The Tactical Priority

Alert gives Rixon **+PB to Initiative** (his +1 already factors this in: DEX −1, PB +2, net +1) and the ability to **swap his initiative with one willing, non-incapacitated ally immediately after rolling**. The swap costs nothing and occurs before anyone takes a turn.

Before every combat, immediately after rolling, ask: who benefits most from going first?

| Swap Recipient | Why |
|---|---|
| **Cade** | So he can cast Shield of Faith on Rixon as a Bonus Action before Rixon has taken any damage — Rixon enters his first turn at AC 20 |
| **Mall-Lowick** | So she can apply Hunter's Mark or Entangle before enemies have moved into position |
| **Gilda** | So she can debuff a key target with Mind Sliver before Cade's Command or Mall-Lowick's Ensnaring Strike lands |

This decision is the most tactically significant action Rixon takes that costs him nothing. Treat it as a standing priority at the start of every encounter.

---

## Spell Usage

**Shield (Reaction, 1 free cast/LR):** +5 AC until the start of his next turn, plus immunity to Magic Missile. This is reactive — the correct time to use it is after a die has been rolled against him and would hit, not preemptively. The free cast refreshes on a Long Rest. AC 18 briefly becoming AC 23 is an emergency brake that can prevent being dropped in a single exchange.

**Blade Ward (Cantrip, Concentration, Action):** Attackers subtract 1d4 from their attack rolls against him for 1 minute. Burns his action for the turn, so he cannot attack. Best used in rounds where he is deliberately holding a chokepoint or doorway rather than advancing — where his job is to *absorb* rather than *inflict*. Note that this is a Concentration spell.

**Light (Cantrip):** Practical and frequently important. The party has a significant Darkvision gap — only Gilda can see in true darkness. Rixon providing a light source covers the rest of the party, but be aware that it also signals the party's position in dark environments. Coordinate with Gilda: when stealth matters, she navigates in the dark and describes; when it doesn't, Rixon lights up.

---

## Weapon Mastery

| Weapon | Mastery | Effect | Use It When |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Longsword** | **Sap** | Target has Disadv on their next Attack Roll | Every round — standard output |
| **Handaxe** | **Vex** | Rixon gains Adv on his next Attack Roll vs this target | Opening throw, or setting up a follow-on Longsword hit |
| **Halberd** | **Cleave** | Free swing at a second adjacent creature | Not currently active — consider swapping in for clustered fights |

**Sap is his primary mastery and should apply every round.** Hit with the Longsword → target has Disadvantage on its next attack against Rixon → Rixon is statistically harder to hit back. This compounds: he takes less damage per round, reducing Cade's healing burden, keeping him in the fight longer.

**The Vex setup:** Throw a Handaxe on the opening of a fight (Vex applies: Rixon gains Advantage on his next attack against that target), then close to melee and swing with Advantage on the Longsword. This opening sequence is his highest first-round damage ceiling.

**The Vex/Sap loop with Mall-Lowick:** When Mall-Lowick hits a target with her Shortsword (she gains Advantage on her next attack via Vex), and Rixon hits the same target with his Longsword (target has Disadvantage on its next attack via Sap), the two mastery effects combine. She is more likely to connect; the enemy is less likely to hit him back. They should coordinate target selection explicitly.

---

## Second Wind

Self-heal (Bonus Action, 1d10+1 HP) with 2 uses per Long Rest, recovering 1 use on a Short Rest. This is his safety valve when Cade is occupied or out of spell slots — it means Rixon can often keep himself functional for another round without consuming party resources. Use it proactively when he drops below ~4 HP rather than waiting until he's at 1.

---

## Key Traits in Play

**Heroic Inspiration (Resourceful):** Rixon regains Heroic Inspiration at the end of every Long Rest. This is a free Advantage on any one d20 roll per day. Save it for moments of genuine consequence — a key attack, a save against something debilitating, a Persuasion or Intimidation roll that changes the direction of a session. Do not spend it on routine checks.

**History +4:** Rixon is the party's institutional memory, particularly for military, political, and regional knowledge. He and Gilda together cover most knowledge domains — he handles historical fact and social context; she handles arcane theory and investigation. Ask him first about who these people are, what this city has been through, and what this faction wants.

**Intimidation +3 / Performance +3:** Rixon projects an image, and he does it deliberately. He knows he looks like a henchman — broad, scarred, direct — and he weaponises this. These skills come up in urban and social encounters, particularly in the demimonde he moves through. The Performance proficiency extends further: he can inhabit a range of personas entirely convincingly — the legitimate guard, the respectable escort, the bored municipal heavy — wearing whatever face the situation calls for and discarding it when it has served its purpose. Paired with Gilda's cold Persuasion, the combination is effective: she explains the deal; he implicitly communicates the cost of refusing.

**Knavebones (Gaming Set):** A social tool, not just a pastime. Gambling is how Rixon gets into conversations, into trust, into back rooms. DC 20 INT to win, DC 15 WIS to spot cheating, with Advantage and Proficiency from his Proficiency with the set — he is competent but not unbeatable. The real value is the context: people talk at a gaming table in ways they don't in a tavern common room.

**Athletics +5:** The highest in the party. Covers climbing, grappling, breaking down doors, forcing locks, lifting heavy objects, swimming under pressure, and physically restraining or throwing creatures. His Crowbar gives Advantage on Strength (Athletics) checks using leverage. When the party needs raw physical force, this is where to go.

**Common Sign Language:** The only party member who knows Common Sign, Rixon can conduct silent, short-range communication with anyone who understands it. Useful in contexts where vocally signalling allies would be overheard.

---

## Where Rixon Shines

- Absorbing damage while the rest of the party operates safely behind him
- Applying consistent Sap-debuffed pressure every round, reducing incoming damage to himself
- Controlling initiative flow for the whole party via Alert's swap — a near-zero-cost tactical advantage
- Urban environments: a fluent performer across social registers — Guard, operator, hired muscle, respectable escort — he moves through city environments with the ease of someone who belongs in all of them and has allegiance to none
- Physical challenges: Athletics +5 solves many practical problems without magic
- Social encounters involving intimidation, underworld contacts, historical knowledge, or a bluff

## Where Rixon Struggles

- Stealth: Disadvantage, permanently, in Chain Mail. He cannot participate in silent approaches. This is absolute.
- Ranged combat: no ranged mastery, low DEX. If kept at distance and unable to close, his effectiveness drops significantly. The Handaxe at 20/60 ft is his only meaningful ranged option.
- Wisdom saves: WIS +0, no proficiency. He is vulnerable to Charm, Fear, Hold Person, and any enchantment that targets Wisdom. Intelligent enemies that assess the party will target him with these effects.
- Magic: two cantrips and one free Shield cast per day. He cannot support the party magically beyond these.
- Delicate work: DEX −1 and no Sleight of Hand or Thieves' Tools proficiency mean anything requiring finesse — picking locks, threading a wire through a mechanism, delicate handling — is not his problem to solve.

---

## Roleplaying Notes

Rixon is intelligent and emotionally perceptive behind a carefully cultivated exterior. He projects the image of the battered bruiser — deliberate, physical, direct — because it is professionally useful. People underestimate him, treat him as a blunt instrument, and talk freely around him. The sharp eyes behind the theatrics are always cataloguing.

His Neutral Good alignment means he genuinely wants good outcomes and will occasionally inconvenience himself to achieve them — but he is flexible about methods in a way that Cade is not. Rules, permissions, and official channels are working assumptions at best, obstacles at worst; he left that concern behind when he left the militia, and he navigates around them with the ease of long practice. He will deal with unsavoury people, use morally ambiguous means, and move through whatever social register the situation demands, wearing the appropriate face without discomfort. He will not let innocents be harmed if he can stop it, and he will not work for people he has identified as genuinely bad, though his threshold for "genuinely bad" is calibrated to his experience of the demimonde rather than Cade's standards.

The tension between his cultivated cynicism and his actual decency is his core character dynamic. He has Heroic Inspiration every morning for a reason — he keeps finding himself caring more than is strictly profitable.
