## 5. Features & Traits

### Class Features *(Druid)*

**Druidic**
You know Druidic — the secret language of Druids. You can use it to leave hidden messages. You and others who know this language automatically spot such a message. Others spot the message's presence with a successful DC 15 Wisdom (Perception) check but cannot decipher it without magic. You always have **Speak with Animals** prepared, and it doesn't count against the number of spells you can prepare.

**Primal Order: Warden**
Motioned by a connection to the cycle of life and death, you gain proficiency with **Martial weapons** and training with **Medium armour**. You become more capable of defending the wild and those within it.

**Spellcasting**
You have prepared a number of Druid spells equal to your **Wisdom modifier + your Druid level** (currently 4). You prepare these spells from the Druid spell list after each Long Rest and can swap one prepared spell per Long Rest. You cast these spells using **Wisdom** as your spellcasting ability.

### Species Traits *(Brass Dragonborn)*

**Darkvision**
You have Darkvision with a range of 60 feet.

**Draconic Ancestry — Brass (Fire)**
You have **Resistance** to Fire damage.

**Breath Weapon** *(Magic Action)*
You exhale a burst of fire. Recharge on a Short or Long Rest (2 uses).
- **Cone (15 ft)** or **Line (5 ft × 30 ft)** of fire.
- Each creature in the area makes a **DEX saving throw** (DC 12). On a failed save: **1d10 Fire damage**. On a success: half damage.
*The damage increases to 2d10 at level 5, 3d10 at level 11, 4d10 at level 17.*

### Origin Feat

**Magic Initiate (Cleric)** *(Acolyte)*
You learn two cantrips from the Cleric spell list (**Light** and **Thaumaturgy**) and one Level 1 Cleric spell (**Command**), which is always prepared and can be cast once per Long Rest without expending a spell slot. If you have spell slots of the appropriate level, you may also cast it using a slot. **Wisdom** is your spellcasting ability for these spells.

## 8. Notes & Backstory

### Appearance

Tanenni is a Brass Dragonborn who has spent a great deal of care on being imperceptible in the places that count. Her scales are the warm tarnished gold of old candlesticks — kept meticulously clean, which in a working city reads less as vanity and more as religious fastidiousness, which is precisely the point. She is built on the lean, compact lines of someone who has spent years in high-ceilinged temple rooms and narrow archive passages, and she moves with the measured deliberateness of a woman who has learned that urgency attracts attention.

Over her hide armour she wears temple robes in layered deep blues and burnished gold — the colours of no specific god but the colours of *a* god, and in the right light and the right doorway that is usually sufficient. The holy symbol on its chain is thick enough to be seen from across a garden party. Her Quarterstaff doubles as a walking staff, which doubles as a Druidic focus, which doubles as something that can break a jaw with two hands and adequate motivation.

Her eyes, when she chooses to activate Thaumaturgy, shift to bright amber with vertical pupils. She deploys this rarely, and only when she wants to be remembered. Most of the time, she prefers not to be.

### Personality Traits

- **Meticulous.** She treats heist plans with the same rigorous, detail-oriented scrutiny she once applied to sacred texts. There is no room for imprecision — she has seen what imprecision costs, and what it costs is usually someone else's problem becoming hers.
- **Pragmatic.** She views the city's accumulated elite wealth as a stagnant pool: unhealthy for the ecosystem, correctable by redistribution. This is not ideology. It is ecology.

### Ideal

**Balance.** The city's systems are not broken because evil people exist. They are broken because weight has accumulated in the wrong places for a very long time, and the people holding it have confused weight for worth. She is in the business of redistributing load.

### Bond

The temple where she served for twelve years still considers her a senior acolyte on administrative leave. Her access credentials remain valid. She has not corrected the impression.

### Flaw

She is not good at people. Animals, yes — she reads them clearly, speaks to them honestly, and trusts them implicitly. People she approaches as administrative problems: things with motives to map, access points to identify, vulnerabilities to log. She is working on this. Progress is slow.

### Backstory

#### Origin

Tanenni was raised in the incense-and-marble silence of a prominent city temple, assigned to the archive and records office at sixteen because she had neat scales and neater handwriting. She spent eight years copying donation records, correspondence, and the internal accounts that paid for the temple's charitable works — and four more years quietly realising that the charitable works were a very small percentage of what the accounts showed coming in.

The temple's private reliquaries, she eventually understood, functioned primarily as a secure storage and laundering facility for the city's aristocracy. Illicit goods moved through blessed vaults. Tax obligations dissolved in religious exemption clauses. Blackmail documents rested in alcoves consecrated to saints of wisdom. The divine sanctity was real; it had simply been redirected.

Tanenni did not expose this. Exposure would have destroyed the temple's genuine charitable work along with the corruption, would have provided no lasting structural change, and would have made her name mud in every institution she might need access to later. Instead she quietly rerouted approximately twelve percent of the skimmed surplus back into the charitable accounts, altered the relevant ledger entries with the calligraphic skill she had been practising her entire adult life, and began making herself genuinely useful to people with resources worth redistributing.

#### The Druid

The druidic connection arrived late — she was twenty-nine, which is not late for a Dragonborn but is not early either. She had always been competent with the herbalism kit, attentive to the temple's sacred groves, more interested in the garden's annual rhythms than in the liturgical calendar that governed them. She does not describe the moment of recognition in mystical terms. She was repotting a bay laurel, noted that she understood something about the plant's intention, and accepted this as new information.

She found the Druidic circle that operated on the city's outskirts, learned the language and the practice, and incorporated both into her temple work in ways that were not obviously distinguishable from standard religious observance. The animals she subsequently communicated with became her most reliable informants about the movement of guards, the presence of unfamiliar people, and the general texture of a building's inhabited spaces. She did not announce this capability. She simply noted that her information quality improved considerably.

#### The Crew

She encountered Darwhidan when a heist she was running independently crossed paths with one he was running from the other direction, which is to say they robbed the same lord's vault on the same night from different entrances and met in the dark over the same strongbox. She considered him professionally for a moment and offered to split it. He accepted. She has since taught him Druidic for use in leaving field-reports in city stonework, which she considers a reasonable investment in operational security.

The others she recruited or accepted as they arrived. The crew has the right shape for the work: someone who can move in silence, someone who can talk their way through a front door, someone who can carry the rough work. What she did not expect was to find herself mildly invested in their success as people as well as as colleagues. She is still calibrating what to do about this.
