# Tanenni — Strategic Analysis

---

## Role

**Environmental Scout / Inside Man / Support Caster**

Tanenni is the crew's reconnaissance engine, logistical backbone, and on-site authority figure. She is not the primary damage source in a fight, and she knows it — her WIS-heavy build exists to serve the crew's pre-combat and non-combat operations more than its encounters. The Warden subclass makes her a capable combatant in a pinch, but her greatest value is in what happens before the vault door opens: animal-sourced intelligence, environmental concealment, Guidance on critical skill checks, and the religious authority that walks through restricted doors that would take Darwhidan twenty minutes to pick. Play her as the architect of the situation the crew enters, not as a combatant within it.

---

## Combat Priorities

### The Two Spell Slots

At Level 1, Tanenni has two Level 1 Druid spell slots, refreshed on a Long Rest. Both are meaningful, and both have an obvious correct use:

| Slot Choice | Use When |
|---|---|
| **Fog Cloud** *(Concentration)* | Combat opens in an unfavourable position; the crew needs obscurement to reposition, or a bottleneck exists where making 20 feet of space Heavily Obscured prevents enemies from targeting the group effectively |
| **Healing Word** *(Bonus Action)* | An ally drops or is badly hurt; the Bonus Action cost is the critical advantage — it costs no action, allowing her to cast it and still attack or use another action spell on the same turn |

**The core principle:** Fog Cloud creates a situation; Healing Word manages a crisis. Do not spend Fog Cloud as a combat opener if the tactical situation does not require it — save it for the moment it changes the fight. Healing Word, similarly, is for genuine emergencies: spending a slot on a conscious ally who has lost a quarter of their HP is not a good investment; spending it to get an unconscious Bradkell back on his feet is.

**Command** from Magic Initiate is a third option and costs no slot (1 free cast per Long Rest). Against a single threatening enemy, a one-word command that removes their action for a turn — Grovel (Prone, action gone), Halt (no movement or action) — can prevent more damage than Fog Cloud costs in the same round. Reserve the free Command cast for the moment it shuts down a real threat.

### Concentration Management

Fog Cloud and Detect Magic both require Concentration. Tanenni's Guidance also requires Concentration. **Only one of these can run at a time.** The choice:

| Scenario | Concentration Choice |
|---|---|
| Pre-infiltration scouting | Detect Magic (ritual — free; audit for wards) |
| Skill-heavy non-combat moment | Guidance on the ally making the critical roll |
| Combat in a defensible bottleneck | Fog Cloud on the most effective choke point |
| Combat in an open space | Healing Word (no concentration) + attack, saving Fog Cloud for a better moment |

Do not activate Guidance and then walk into combat — it will end on the first saving throw forced by damage. Cast Guidance before high-stakes skill moments, not as a standing buff.

### Bonus Action Economy

Tanenni has two primary Bonus Action options:
- **Healing Word** (emergency heal, costs a slot)
- **Jump** (prepares an ally for high-mobility engagement — costs a slot, but the recipient can leap 30 ft per turn for a minute without a STR check)

At Level 1, Jump is a premium investment in Milores or Darwhidan for a vertical entry or egress situation. Healing Word is the higher-priority hold. Unless the operation specifically involves dropping from height, keep the Bonus Action slot reserved for Healing Word.

## Spell Usage

**Fog Cloud:** The crew's best tactical reset. 20 feet of Heavily Obscured space means attack rolls against targets within it have Disadvantage, and attackers within it attack targets they can't see with Disadvantage. Note that the crew has three Darkvision members (Tanenni herself, Darwhidan, Milores) and one Human (Bradkell) who sees nothing in magical fog the same as anyone else — coordinate before casting. If Bradkell is the one in the cloud, he needs to have already acted or the obscurement helps the enemy as much as it helps the crew.

**Healing Word:** The most efficient healing spell at Level 1, period. Bonus action delivery means it never costs the action economy of a concentrated healer — she can cast it and still use her action to quarterstaff, Command, or cast Guidance. 2d4+3 averages 8 HP, which is substantial at Level 1 HP totals. Do not save this forever; a crew member at 0 HP costs more turns (and danger) to revive than the slot is worth.

**Detect Magic (Ritual):** Free. Costs 10 minutes. Use it every time the crew enters a space with unknown magical security. Do not skip this step in operations. Magical wards that go undetected are the single most common failure mode in urban infiltration. The ritual does require Concentration while active, so don't begin it when trouble is expected imminently.

**Speak with Animals (Free, 2/LR or Ritual):** Tanenni's most undervalued asset in the urban context. City animals — rats, pigeons, cats, street dogs — are present in every building, every sewer line, every garden and rooftop. They notice patrols. They feel the presence of people who shouldn't be there. They do not have complex vocabulary, but *something is wrong in the east wing tonight* or *the big man comes back every hour* or *she feeds the birds but not the ones on the third floor* is intelligence that no other crew member can obtain. Use at least one free cast per operation. The information is almost always worth it.

## Key Traits in Play

**Perception +5 (proficient):** She is the party's best-equipped watcher — the highest Passive Perception in the crew by a significant margin (Milores at 14, everyone else below 10). She should be on watch during Long Rests, should be positioned at doorways when the crew needs advance warning, and should be the one whose attention is pointed at the room while others are speaking to it.

**Insight +5 (proficient):** She reads people with the precision of someone who spent twelve years in a temple environment full of people performing piety. She will notice when an NPC is lying, uncomfortable, or concealing something, even when that NPC is a very practiced liar. Deploy her in any interaction where a follow-up or calibration read on the contact matters.

**Guidance (Concentration, 1 minute):** Choose a skill at cast time; the target adds 1d4 to any ability check using that skill for up to a minute — +1 to +4 per roll. The skill must be declared at casting, not when the check arrives — anticipate the moment. Pre-cast it on Darwhidan *(Sleight of Hand)* before a difficult lock. On Bradkell *(Persuasion)* before a social check where the contact is resistant. On herself *(Insight)* before a crucial NPC read. Don't forget it exists — it is the most frequently-overlooked tool in her kit.

**Warden: Martial Weapon Proficiency:** She can use martial weapons. At Level 1 with STR 10 and DEX 12, she won't hit hard, but she can hold a line. A Spear (Versatile, 1d8) with two hands while she coordinates through Guidance or Message is a plausible combat contribution when the crew needs a third warm body in range rather than a support caster at the back.

**Message cantrip:** 120-foot range, whisper to a specific target only. No one else hears it. No one watching knows she said anything. In an infiltration with multiple positioned crew members, this is the coordination layer — real-time updates on patrol position, confirmation of entry signal, warning of incoming threat. It requires a component (a piece of copper wire) and line of sight to the target, but in practical terms it is a one-way, one-target, line-of-sight radio. Use it as one.

**Calligrapher's Supplies (proficient):** Forged credentials, altered ledger entries, duplicated authorisation documents. This is an operations capability, not a combat one, but it is the capability that opens the front door in the first place. With advantage-level execution and the appropriate materials, she can produce documents that pass casual scrutiny in the religious and administrative contexts she knows best.

**Herbalism Kit (proficient):** Craft antitoxins and healing poultices during Long Rests, if the ingredients are available. An antitoxin worth 50 GP in a shop can be produced for component costs. In a campaign involving potentially poisoned locks or guards with paralytic supplies, this is worth thinking about before each operation.

## Where Tanenni Shines

- Pre-mission intelligence: animals are everywhere, notice everything, and cost only ten minutes of ritual casting to interrogate
- Magical security detection: Detect Magic as a ritual is a free step in every infiltration plan that cannot be skipped
- Critical skill check moments: Guidance as a Bonus Action pre-cast turns a DC 15 check with a +5 modifier into effectively a +6 to +9 attempt
- Restricted access: religious authority opens doors in temple environments, archives, noble chapels, and the kind of consecrated spaces where guards let a known face through rather than challenge it
- Long-rest watch duty: Passive Perception 15 means things don't approach camp without being noticed
- Healing in the action economy: Healing Word's Bonus Action delivery makes her the single most efficient healer relative to what it costs her to heal

## Where Tanenni Struggles

- Direct damage: her weapons hit at +2 and deal modest die sizes; she is not the answer to "we need someone to hurt this enemy quickly"
- INT −1 across the board: Arcana, Investigation, and History checks are not hers. She should not attempt to identify magical items by inspection, research archives, or recall historical facts
- Stealth +1: she moves quietly for a medium-sized scaled humanoid in armour and carrying a shield, which is to say: not very quietly. She should not be in the stack during a full-party stealth approach — she creates the conditions for the stealth approach and then finds a legitimate-looking position
- High-pressure social contexts outside her domain: she is credible in religious, administrative, and noble-charity contexts. She is considerably less credible in criminal taverns, guild halls, and situations where her careful, official bearing reads as conspicuous
- Concentration fragility: she has no feature that helps her maintain Concentration saves (CON +2, no War Caster or Resilient). If she is hit while concentrating, she is more likely than a hardier caster to lose the spell

## Roleplaying Notes

Tanenni moves through the world as though she has already assessed it and concluded it is approximately what she expected. This is not disdain — she is not contemptuous of people, animals, or situations — it is a professional posture that she adopted somewhere in her twenties and has maintained since as a baseline from which surprise occasionally departs. She is observant, measured, and genuinely good at waiting.

She does not explain her reasoning unless asked, and often not when asked. She tends to arrive at a conclusion and act on it, and if the action is questioned, she will describe the conclusion, but she will not always describe the path from observation to conclusion, which is a form of communication gap that occasionally creates friction with Bradkell, who is process-oriented and wants to understand the model, not just the output.

Her relationship with animals is not tender in any obvious way — she communicates with them practically, in a low murmur, with the matter-of-fact tone of someone consulting a reliable but limited reference source. The animals appear to find this reassuring. Several of the city's pigeons have begun following her at a distance. She has not commented on this.

She is four years from retirement if the mathematics hold. She has not told anyone this. She is not sure she believes it herself.
