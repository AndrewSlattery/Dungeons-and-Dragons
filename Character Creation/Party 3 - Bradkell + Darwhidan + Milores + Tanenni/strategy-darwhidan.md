# Darwhidan — Strategic Analysis

---

## Role

**Primary Infiltrator / Precision Striker / Social Second**

Darwhidan is the crew's lockpick, second face, and primary damage spike. He is the person who goes first and alone into the dark, comes back with a report, and then goes back in to open the thing that needed opening. In combat, he is a controlled detonation: relatively low-impact unless the conditions for Sneak Attack are met, at which point he is the crew's single highest-damage round. The key to playing him well is understanding the interplay between his Nick mastery, his freed Bonus Action, and Sneak Attack positioning — and understanding that his WIS −1 means he should never be the one making the Perception or Insight call.

---

## Combat Priorities

### Sneak Attack: The Central Resource

Darwhidan's Sneak Attack (1d6 extra damage on a qualifying hit) is the single most important mechanical feature he has. At Level 1, it is the difference between being a character who deals 1d6+3 per round and a character who deals 2d6+3 per round. The conditions:

| Method | Notes |
|---|---|
| **Advantage on the attack** | Easiest to achieve via Milores's adjacent positioning, Sleep, or Hiding first |
| **Ally within 5 ft of target** | Milores is almost always in melee range if she's engaged with the same enemy — this is the default trigger |

**The Milores adjacency trigger is the crew's most reliable Sneak Attack setup.** When Milores is fighting the same enemy, Darwhidan can attack with a Finesse or Ranged weapon at normal odds and still get Sneak Attack, as long as Milores is within 5 feet of that enemy. This does not require Advantage — just Milores being where she usually is. Call the same target as Milores before acting.

Against a target that Bradkell has put to Sleep: that target is Unconscious. Attacks against Unconscious targets are automatic critical hits if made within 5 feet. A melee critical hit with Sneak Attack at Level 1 is 4d6+3, which is likely lethal on a sleeping guard. This is the crew's cleanest elimination option.

### The Nick Mastery: The Hidden Action Economy Advantage

Darwhidan's Dagger has the Nick mastery property: *the extra attack from the Light property can be made as part of the Attack action instead of as a Bonus Action*.

In practical terms, a standard dual-wielder must:
1. Attack action with main-hand weapon
2. Bonus Action with off-hand weapon (Light property)

With Nick, Darwhidan does:
1. Attack action with Shortsword + Dagger (both within the Attack action)
2. **Bonus Action: available for anything else**

This is significant at Level 1. His freed Bonus Action can now be used for:
- **Hide** (Bonus Action with Cunning Action — when he gains it at level 2; note this at level 1)
- **Healing Hands** (his Aasimar feature, 2d4 HP on a touched ally — costs his Bonus Action, not a spell slot)
- **Feather Fall** (if a fall is incoming — this is a Reaction, not a Bonus Action, but worth tracking as a separate resource)
- **Mage Hand** setup on a previous turn (spectral hand for lock manipulation or item retrieval)

At Level 1 before Cunning Action is available, the freed Bonus Action is most likely to go unused or toward Healing Hands. At Level 2, it becomes the hide-and-vanish mechanism that makes Darwhidan genuinely difficult to pin down.

### Vex Mastery: The Attack Chain

When the Shortsword hits and deals damage, the Vex mastery property grants Advantage on the next attack roll against that same target before the end of his next turn. The flow:

**Turn 1:** Shortsword hits → Vex applies → Dagger attack (Nick, same action) → potential Sneak Attack on one of these if Milores is adjacent.
**Turn 2:** Opens with Advantage on the first attack against the Vexed target → higher chance of Sneak Attack qualification → can go Shortsword again to continue the Vex chain.

In a sustained fight against a single significant enemy, this chain self-perpetuates: hit with Shortsword to gain Vex, open next turn with Advantage to hit more reliably, repeat. The chain breaks if the Shortsword misses on turn N or the target is defeated. Against a single dangerous enemy, this makes Darwhidan's damage output surprisingly consistent across rounds.

## Skill Usage

**Sleight of Hand +7 (Expertise):** The highest modifier in the crew on any skill. This is the lockpicking roll, the pickpocket roll, the document-extraction-from-a-pocket roll, the sleight-of-hand check for hiding a stolen item on your person while being searched. With Thieves' Tools and this modifier, most locks at Level 1 open reliably. Use it for every physical manipulation that requires precision.

**Deception +6 (Expertise):** The crew's primary social deception skill. While Bradkell handles social engineering and persuasion (his domain), Darwhidan handles the specific skill checks where someone is actively trying to catch a lie — the guard who is suspicious, the functionary who has seen the documents before, the fence who knows the market value of what you're selling. Deception +6 is robust enough to hold up under most scrutiny.

**Stealth +5:** He moves quietly. He should be the lead scout on every entry — going first into a space to assess it before the rest of the crew commits. His Darkvision (60 ft) means he can do this in darkness without a light source.

**Thieves' Tools (proficient):** Open locks, disable mechanical traps. The primary mechanical tool in every planned infiltration. He should assess every vault door and secured space himself; do not delegate lockpicking to a character without this proficiency.

**Acrobatics +5:** Climbing through windows, dropping from heights (with Feather Fall backup), navigating unstable surfaces, escaping grapples. The physical vocabulary of his job.

## Spell and Feature Usage

**Mage Hand:** Manipulation from 30 feet away without touching. Uses: retrieve items from sealed-but-accessible containers, trigger pressure plates remotely to test for traps, turn handles and open unlocked doors from outside a room, pour a vial's contents at range. Not all of these will come up every session, but the habit of asking "can Mage Hand handle this first?" before physically approaching an unknown object is worth developing.

**Minor Illusion:** Sound or image within 30 feet. Creates diversions — footstep sounds from the east corridor while the crew moves through the west, a brief image of a figure in a doorway to redirect a guard's attention, a voice saying something in a guard's own apparent voice, once. Uses Charisma DC 12 for disbelief if investigated. Most credulous targets will not investigate the first distraction; most skeptical ones will. Know your guard.

**Feather Fall (Reaction; 1 free/LR):** The signature exit. The mechanism is: act normally until the window is the better option, then go through it and cast on the way down. The 60-feet-per-round fall rate is slow enough to land safely and controlled enough to have a conversation on the way. Up to 5 creatures within 60 feet can benefit — this means the whole crew, if they are in range when the jump happens. A coordinated multi-floor exit is possible; it requires everyone to jump at approximately the same moment and be within 60 feet of Darwhidan.

**Healing Hands (2 uses/LR; Magic Action, Bonus Action):** 2d4 HP on a touched ally. At Level 1 HP totals (8–10 HP across the crew), this is a meaningful emergency triage — Bradkell at 2 HP is considerably less useful than Bradkell at 8 HP, and Healing Hands costs no spell slot. Use it on the party's squishiest member (Bradkell) before it becomes a recovery-from-zero situation. It is a Bonus Action in 2024, meaning it doesn't compete with his attack action.

## Key Traits in Play

**Celestial Resistance (Necrotic + Radiant):** Against enemies or traps that deal Necrotic or Radiant damage, Darwhidan halves the damage received. In an urban infiltration campaign, this most commonly applies to: cursed vault mechanisms, undead guardians, and certain magical ward triggers. It is passive and requires no activation — just note it when it applies.

**Darkvision (60 ft):** He operates comfortably in the dark. The crew's standard approach: Darwhidan scouts the dark space first, returns with information, the crew then uses a light source (Tanenni's Light cantrip, a torch) for the entry phase. In fully dark extractions where a light source would draw attention, he and Milores (also Darkvision 60 ft) can operate without one; Tanenni (Darkvision 60 ft) can follow; Bradkell is at a disadvantage and should remain at a held position until the coast is clear.

**Druidic language:** The hidden-message system with Tanenni allows him to leave carved rune-reports in city stonework for the crew to read later. In campaign terms, this is the crew's dead-drop communication network. Characters who know Druidic are: him and Tanenni. Characters who know Thieves' Cant are: him alone. He has access to two separate hidden communication registers.

**AC 14 with DEX +3:** Reasonably durable for a Rogue at Level 1, but not a frontliner. If enemies begin targeting him in preference to Milores, something has gone wrong with positioning or the tactical situation. He should be at the flanks or behind the encounter, reaching in for Sneak Attack opportunities and retreating to the edge of engagement distance.

## Where Darwhidan Shines

- Locked spaces: Sleight of Hand +7 with Thieves' Tools opens most Level 1 locks routinely
- Stealth approach: highest consistent damage output of any crew member who can operate quietly at range
- Sneak Attack against sleeping or prone targets: auto-crit in melee against unconscious targets turns his damage into a lethality tool that cleanly resolves a single guard without alarm
- Social deception: when a lie needs to hold up under scrutiny, he is the check to make
- Vertical and unusual egress: Feather Fall makes multi-storey exits trivial
- Sustained damage against a single priority target: Vex + Nick mastery self-perpetuates in a focused fight

## Where Darwhidan Struggles

- WIS −1 everywhere: Perception at −1 and Insight at −1 mean he will not notice things and will not read people correctly on Wisdom-based checks. He should not be on watch. He should not be making the NPC read. He should absolutely not be doing both simultaneously while Bradkell is talking to a contact.
- No spell slots: all his magical effects are cantrips or once-per-day freebies. He cannot contribute nova damage or have a dramatic spell moment.
- Single-target orientation: Sneak Attack only applies once per round against one target. Against a crowd of four guards, he is considerably less efficient than Bradkell's Sleep or Tanenni's Command.
- Relatively low HP (10): two hits from a reasonably dangerous enemy will threaten him. He needs Milores or Tanenni between him and trouble, not the reverse.

## Roleplaying Notes

Darwhidan has two modes, and the transition between them is his defining characteristic. In social contexts, he is warm, apparently slightly guileless, and possessed of the easy manner of a large man who has learned that people relax when he does. In operational contexts, he becomes very still and very precise — the warmth doesn't disappear exactly, but it stops being the primary thing present, and what replaces it is a quality of complete attention that some people find more unsettling than hostility.

He is not ideological about the work. He does not share Bradkell's framework of systemic change, and he finds Tanenni's ecological metaphors accurate but dry. He thinks the correct targets are the ones they are targeting, for reasons that are more aesthetic than philosophical — people who use their resources to harm others deserve to have them removed; people who don't are not his problem — and he is comfortable with this framework. He does not interrogate it.

He is genuinely fond of the crew, in the unsentimental way of a person who has spent time with competent people and found this surprising. He trusts Tanenni's planning implicitly. He trusts Milores's execution equally. He trusts Bradkell's social judgment conditionally, which is to say: in rooms where Bradkell is the most charismatic person present, he defers; in rooms where that condition does not hold, he watches more carefully.

He owes Tanenni half the contents of a vault from three years ago. He has not paid this debt. He is aware she knows. He is enjoying the ambiguity.
