# Party Synergy — Strategic Analysis

---

## Overview

The crew of Bradkell (Sorcerer), Darwhidan (Rogue), Milores (Monk), and Tanenni (Druid) presents a distinctive tactical profile: **no heavy armour, no dedicated healer, and a full suite of stealth-viable movement options.** Unlike Party 1's single-frontliner configuration and Party 2's double-armoured front rank, this party fights at medium-to-close range, with Milores as the durable anchor and three supporting members who contribute precise, well-positioned pressure rather than sustained hits. The key insight is that this party's power comes not from combat buffs stacked on frontliners but from **action economy** — Darwhidan's Nick mastery freeing his Bonus Action, Milores's free Bonus Action unarmed strike, Bradkell's Sleep converting an encounter before it starts, and Tanenni's Healing Word recovering a party member without spending her action. Every character contributes on multiple axes simultaneously. The party is weakest in head-on sustained combat against multiple resilient enemies; it is strongest when it controls the terms of engagement.

## Combat Synergy Chains

### Chain 1: The Clean Takedown — Bradkell's Sleep + Darwhidan's Sneak Attack

The party's single most efficient combat sequence, and the correct approach to any encounter involving isolated human-type guards:

**Step 1 — Bradkell: Sleep** (WIS save DC 13, 5-ft Sphere, two-stage Incapacitated → Unconscious) → Creatures that fail both saves fall Unconscious. Note: the Sphere is 5 feet — position the centre between tightly-clustered targets for maximum effect.

**Step 2 — Darwhidan: Melee attack vs. Unconscious target** → Attacks against Unconscious creatures from within 5 feet are **automatic critical hits**. A critical hit with Sneak Attack at Level 1 is **4d6+3** damage, which is likely lethal on a standard guard (assuming ~10 HP) and guaranteed to produce the deepest possible wound on anything.

**Step 3 — Milores (if a second target remains):** Unarmed Strike + Bonus Action Unarmed Strike → clean, bludgeoning, subdual-appropriate.

The cost: one of Bradkell's two Level 1 spell slots. The return: an encounter resolved without alarm, without noise, and without the kind of wound pattern that creates subsequent complications. This is the party's standard operating procedure for guarded spaces.

**Coordination rule:** Bradkell identifies the target cluster before combat. Darwhidan positions within 5 feet of where the nearest target will fall. The sequence runs as: Bradkell casts → wait for both fails to resolve → Darwhidan walks to the nearest unconscious target and takes the auto-crit → done. Do not have Darwhidan attack before Sleep's first-save Incapacitation resolves — the auto-crit requires the Unconscious condition, which doesn't arrive until the second failed save at the end of the target's next turn.

### Chain 2: The Milores Anchor — Milores in Melee + Darwhidan Sneak Attack Trigger

The party's most reliable sustained-combat damage chain, and the default when Sleep is unavailable or inappropriate:

**Milores engages a target in melee.** She is within 5 feet of the enemy.

**Darwhidan attacks the same target with a Finesse or Ranged weapon.** The Rogue Sneak Attack condition: *another enemy of the target is within 5 feet*. Milores fulfils this condition permanently while engaged. Darwhidan gets Sneak Attack **every round** against any target Milores is fighting, regardless of his own positioning, with no need for Advantage.

The result: Milores contributes ~10 damage per round (Unarmed + Bonus Unarmed). Darwhidan contributes ~12 damage per round (Shortsword + Nick Dagger + Sneak Attack 1d6). Against a single target, that is approximately **22 damage per round** from two characters, without using any limited resources.

**Call the target.** Both should announce the same target at the start of the round. Milores strikes first to establish adjacency; Darwhidan follows. If Milores is attacking Target A and Darwhidan is attacking Target B, the chain does not function. The party's best combat rounds are the ones where all physical attacks point at a single priority.

### Chain 3: The Fog Repositioning — Tanenni's Fog Cloud + Darkvision Crew

**Tanenni: Fog Cloud** (Concentration, 20-ft sphere) → All creatures in the sphere are Heavily Obscured. Attack rolls against targets in the sphere have Disadvantage. Attack rolls made by targets in the sphere have Disadvantage.

Three of the four crew members have Darkvision (60 ft). The important clarification: **Darkvision does not pierce magical Fog Cloud.** The fog creates Heavy Obscurement regardless of light — it is not a darkness effect, it is a visibility effect. However, a creature with Darkvision who cannot *see* through the fog still benefits from the *tactical cover* it provides: enemies targeting the crew from outside the fog have Disadvantage on attacks into the Heavily Obscured area, even if the crew is moving within it.

**The practical use:** Fog Cloud placed on a doorway, corridor junction, or enemy ranged position creates a Disadvantage zone on all attacks originating from that area. The crew can advance behind it, using it as mobile cover. Bradkell, who has no Darkvision and AC 10, should move *behind* the cloud rather than through it — the cloud screens incoming fire while he operates just outside it.

**Coordination rule:** Before casting Fog Cloud, identify where Bradkell is relative to the cloud's position. He cannot operate inside it usefully. Position the sphere to block the enemy's line of sight on him while leaving his own line of sight clear. If this is not possible, Fog Cloud may be the wrong choice for this encounter.

### Chain 4: The Guidance Check — Tanenni + Pre-Roll Buff

**Tanenni: Guidance** (Concentration, 1 minute) → touches Darwhidan (or any crew member) → **Tanenni chooses a specific skill at cast time**; the target adds 1d4 to any ability check using that skill for up to a minute.

Darwhidan's Thieves' Tools check (Sleight of Hand +7 + 1d4 Guidance) is one of the most reliable mechanical operations in the game at Level 1. Most DC 15 locks become trivially accessible. DC 20 locks become reliably accessible. On a critical operation where the lock is known to be exceptional, this combination is worth using.

Guidance also applies to:
- **Bradkell's Persuasion (+5 + 1d4)** in a high-stakes social interaction where the contact is resistant
- **Tanenni's own Insight (+5 + 1d4)** before a crucial NPC read
- **Milores's Intimidation (+3 + 1d4)** for the pre-combat Thaumaturgy intimidation sequence

**Concentration note:** Guidance conflicts with Fog Cloud. Tanenni cannot maintain both simultaneously. In practice: use Guidance during approach and skill phases; switch to Fog Cloud if combat begins. If combat begins unexpectedly while Guidance is active, the Guidance ends unless the Concentration save is made — this is typically the correct trade.

### Chain 5: The Musician Recovery — Bradkell + Short Rest

**After any Short or Long Rest where Bradkell plays 10+ minutes of music:**

Up to 2 allies who can hear him gain **Heroic Inspiration** when the rest ends. Heroic Inspiration is one instance of Advantage on any d20 roll, spent when the player chooses.

The party should plan Short Rests to include a musical component whenever safe. Bradkell also receives Heroic Inspiration from his Human Resourceful trait at the end of every Long Rest, meaning he can hold his own inspiration and pass Heroic Inspiration to two others simultaneously.

**The optimal sequence entering a significant encounter:** Bradkell holds his own Heroic Inspiration (from Resourceful), and two allies enter with Inspiration from the Short Rest. Three crew members with Advantage available on demand. Heroic Inspiration is best saved for: a saving throw against a critical effect (Milores against a paralysis save), a decisive attack roll where the difference between hit and miss is the encounter (Darwhidan's Sneak Attack on a high-AC target), or a crucial social check under time pressure (Bradkell's Persuasion with a resistant contact).

### Chain 6: The Speak with Animals Intelligence Web — Tanenni + Pre-Operation Recon

Not a combat chain — the party's primary pre-mission intelligence operation.

**Tanenni: Speak with Animals** (2 free casts per LR, or Ritual) → any Beasts in the area of the planned operation → patrol timing, guard positions, unusual presences, recent disturbances, physical layout of sections the crew hasn't accessed.

City animals are the party's most reliable free intelligence source:
- **Pigeons and sparrows** patrol the exterior, note arrivals/departures, observe rooftop access
- **Rats** move through building interiors, know the occupied vs. unoccupied spaces, follow food sources to kitchen areas and guard common rooms
- **Cats** map territorial boundaries with high precision and notice irregularities in human patterns
- **Dogs** track scent trails and can identify how recently spaces were occupied

The quality of the information depends on the Beast's intelligence and range of experience, but at minimum: *something is wrong in the east wing tonight* or *the big one comes back at the same time, always, except not three days ago* is intelligence that changes a plan. Use at least one Speak with Animals cast per major operation. The cost is negligible; the information is often decisive.

## Turn Order Guidelines

At Level 1, the optimal combat-opening sequence is:

1. **Bradkell** (if acting early): Cast Sleep, centring the 5-ft Sphere between the most tightly-grouped enemies. Move behind cover or Milores.
2. **Darwhidan**: Hold one turn — Sleep's Unconscious condition doesn't arrive until the target fails its second save. On the following turn, move to within 5 feet of the nearest Unconscious target → auto-crit with Shortsword + Nick Dagger.
3. **Milores**: Engage the highest-threat non-sleeping target with Spear or Unarmed Strike → Bonus Action Unarmed Strike.
4. **Tanenni**: Healing Word if someone needs it (Bonus Action) + Guidance on Darwhidan if he needs a skill check, or Command on a remaining threat. Cast Fog Cloud if positioning warrants it.

In practice, initiative is random and this ideal sequence rarely occurs exactly as written. The core principles:
- **Sleep before melee attacks**, if possible — the auto-crit dividend is worth waiting two turns (first save → Incapacitated; second save → Unconscious).
- **Milores enters first, draws attention**, so that Darwhidan's Sneak Attack trigger is established before he acts.
- **Bradkell stays behind Milores**, at 30 feet of distance minimum from any enemy that is not already down.
- **Tanenni's Healing Word is never spent speculatively** — keep a slot in reserve for the round someone drops.

## Exploration Synergy

### The Stealth Advantage

Unlike Party 2, which had Chain Mail as a hard constraint on full-party stealth, this party has **no heavy armour anywhere**. All four crew members can make Stealth checks without Disadvantage. The actual party Stealth values:

| Character | Stealth Modifier | Darkvision |
|---|---|---|
| Darwhidan | +5 | 60 ft |
| Milores | +5 | 60 ft |
| Tanenni | +1 | 60 ft |
| Bradkell | +0 | None |

**The full-party stealth approach:** Darwhidan and Milores lead at +5. They are the advance team — scouts, lock-breakers, and guard-neutralisers before the rest of the crew commits. Tanenni follows at +1 (serviceable; she should not be the lead). Bradkell follows last at +0 (a liability in a stealth context — he should either be established at a different entry point or in a held position while the others proceed).

**Bradkell's stealth problem:** His DEX +0 makes him the party's loudest member. In full-stealth operations, his role is not infiltration — it is cover. He is in the bar where the mark's household staff drink, asking about their day. He is at the front gate with a legitimate reason to be there. He is the reason the staff are distracted and two guards are having a conversation they didn't plan to have. He enables the stealth approach by ensuring the perimeter is occupied.

### Darkvision Coverage

Three Darkvision members (60 ft each) and one Human with no Darkvision. Bradkell's darkness vulnerability is similar to Gwenwyn's in Party 2 — he is dependent on a light source in dark environments, and a light source in a dark infiltration is a liability. Solutions:

- **Tanenni's Light cantrip on an object** (controllable — can be smothered by wrapping the object): provides full lighting for Bradkell while the Darkvision members can operate ahead of the light bubble
- **Hold position with light source** while Darkvision members advance, reuniting once the area is confirmed clear
- **Plan around lighting conditions**: most urban interior environments have functional lighting by default; the darkness problem is specific to certain settings (sewers, vault interiors, roof approaches at night)

### Magical Security Detection

The party has two layers of magical security detection:

1. **Bradkell's Arcana (+4):** Active check when approaching a known or suspected magical security element. Identifies what it is from description and appearance if it is within his knowledge base.
2. **Tanenni's Detect Magic (Ritual):** Free detection of magical effects within 30 feet, with school identification. Takes 10 minutes; Concentration for up to 10 minutes. Run this as standard procedure on every new space before the crew physically approaches mechanisms, doors, or objects of value.

The combination means the party almost never enters a warded space without prior knowledge of what the wards are. This is a significant operational advantage.

## Resource Management Between Encounters

After any significant operation or fight, assess:

1. **Bradkell's slots:** Two Level 1 slots, Long Rest. Slot one is pre-spent on Mage Armour each morning. If Sleep has also been used, all levelled spell contribution is exhausted — he is operating on cantrips and social skills only. A Long Rest is necessary before the next significant encounter.
2. **Tanenni's slots:** Two Level 1 slots, Long Rest. If Healing Word has been used, carefully consider whether to continue with a reduced safety margin. The second slot should be held in reserve until it is clearly needed.
3. **Bradkell's Innate Sorcery:** 2 uses per Long Rest. With Chromatic Orb gone, the primary pairing is Innate Sorcery + Sorcerous Burst for maximised cantrip output. One use remaining covers the highest-priority cantrip moment of the remaining day.
4. **Darwhidan's Feather Fall:** 1 free cast per Long Rest. Once expended, unexpected falls require a plan. Note this when planning high-altitude egress.
5. **Darwhidan's Healing Hands:** 2 uses per Long Rest. If expended, the Aasimar triage option is gone; Milores's Healer's Kit becomes the stabilisation fallback.
6. **Tanenni's Breath Weapon:** 2 uses per Short or Long Rest. Refreshes on Short Rest — this is the most accessible refresh in the party. If both were used and combat is expected, a Short Rest restores them.

**When to Short Rest:** When Tanenni's Breath Weapon is depleted (restores on SR) and any further encounter is expected. Short Rests are cheap and Bradkell should always play during them — the Heroic Inspiration dividend from Musician is worth the time investment.

**When to Long Rest:** When Bradkell has used both spell slots and Sleep is expected to be needed; when Tanenni has used both spell slots and healing reserve is needed; when the party's aggregate HP is substantially reduced and Milores in particular is below half. Milores going into a serious fight at 5 or fewer HP is the party's most urgent physical crisis — she is the frontliner, and a frontliner who is trying not to die is an ineffective anchor for Darwhidan's Sneak Attack chain.

**The non-negotiable Short Rest habit:** After any encounter in which Bradkell plays music and at least two allies spend Hit Dice — guaranteed Heroic Inspiration distributed, resources partially replenished, crew enters the next phase of the operation at full capacity. This should be the default behaviour between significant encounters, not an optional recovery step.
