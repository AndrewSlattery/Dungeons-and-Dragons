# Bradkell — Strategic Analysis

---

## Role

**The Face / Battlefield Controller / Plan Architect**

Bradkell is the crew's social engineer, crowd-control caster, and operational planner. He is the person talking to the mark, the one who has already learned the guard schedule from three casual conversations with three separate people who each thought they were talking to someone else. In combat, he is a threat multiplier rather than a damage dealer: Sleep is the most powerful Level 1 spell in a heist context, and Mage Armour is what keeps him functional long enough to cast it. His AC of 13 with Mage Armour active is still the worst in the crew, and this should inform every positioning and engagement decision he makes. He is never in the line of fire; he is in the position that lets him reshape the encounter.

---

## Combat Priorities

### The Two Spell Slots

Bradkell has two Level 1 Sorcerer spell slots, refreshed on a Long Rest. Both have a clear, fixed purpose: one goes to Mage Armour at the start of each adventuring day; one goes to Sleep when the moment demands it.

| Slot | Purpose |
|---|---|
| **Slot 1 — Mage Armour** | Cast on himself at the start of each adventuring day. AC rises from 10 to 13. 8-hour duration, no Concentration. This is not a combat decision — it is a morning ritual. Do not enter an operation without it active. |
| **Slot 2 — Sleep** | The crew's primary encounter-resolution tool. Hold it until the optimal target cluster is identified, then spend it decisively. |

**Mage Armour is mandatory.** The difference between AC 10 and AC 13 is the difference between being targeted first and being targeted second. Three additional points of AC on a character with 8 HP does not make him durable — it makes him slightly less fragile, which at Level 1 is enough to matter. Cast it without deliberation every morning.

**Sleep is the correct default combat-slot expenditure in almost all infiltration contexts.** A 5-foot-radius Sphere centred on a point within 60 feet; each creature of your choice within it must succeed on a Wisdom saving throw (DC 13) or be Incapacitated until the end of its next turn — then save again, falling Unconscious on a second failure. Against a guard with +0 or −1 WIS, the probability of both saves failing is high. Against two guards standing close together, this is a decisive opening — one cast, encounter controlled.

**The practical implication of this split:** Bradkell effectively has one combat spell slot per day. Once Sleep is expended, his levelled-spell contribution is exhausted. He should be positioned and resourced with this constraint in mind — Sleep is the single shot, and it should be the shot that ends the encounter, not the one that reduces it.

### Innate Sorcery: The Amplifier

Innate Sorcery (Bonus Action, 1 minute, 2 uses per Long Rest) raises spell save DC by 1 and gives Advantage on Sorcerer spell attack rolls.

| Pairing | Effect |
|---|---|
| **Innate Sorcery → Sleep** | Spell DC rises to 14 — directly improves the chance targets fail their WIS save; activate before casting Sleep when fighting enemies with WIS +1 or higher, where the DC difference matters |
| **Innate Sorcery → Sorcerous Burst** | Advantage on ranged spell attack; the 8-chaining mechanic becomes notably more likely to trigger; the correct use once Sleep is expended and he is operating on cantrips alone |

**The optimal use of Innate Sorcery once Mage Armour is the second spell:** Pair it with Sleep to raise the save DC and improve the chance of landing both required fails, or hold it for Sorcerous Burst when levelled slots are exhausted. Against Sleep-immune enemies (undead, constructs), activate Innate Sorcery and spend rounds on Sorcerous Burst — Advantage on the attack roll turns a cantrip into his best available damage option.

**Do not waste Innate Sorcery on cantrips when a levelled spell is available this encounter.** It refreshes on a Long Rest, not a Short Rest — two uses over an entire day is not generous. Spend it on the moment it changes the outcome of the encounter, not on improving a routine cantrip round.

### AC 13 Is Still The Information

His AC with Mage Armour active is 13 — still the lowest in the crew (Milores: 15, Tanenni: 15, Darwhidan: 14), but no longer the kind of liability that invites immediate targeting. Every positioning decision should still reflect this gap. He should never be the first to enter a space, never be adjacent to an enemy that has not already been dealt with, and never be the person standing between an aggressive threat and the crew's exit. His job is to be behind Milores, behind Tanenni, and at sufficient distance that an enemy would need to cross the full engagement zone to reach him.

When he is targeted, three things are true:
1. Something has gone wrong with the crew's positioning
2. He is two or three hits from unconsciousness regardless of Mage Armour — the AC improvement buys time, not survival
3. He has a Spear, two Daggers, two Handaxes, and True Strike, and can contribute physically in an emergency — this is not ideal, but it exists

**True Strike as an emergency option:** When no spell slots remain and the cantrips have done their work, True Strike (CHA-based weapon attack; weapon damage type or Radiant, your choice) gives him a +5 attack roll, which is the same as Darwhidan's weapon attack. Against a target within Spear or thrown Dagger range, this is a meaningful last-round option rather than a desperate swing.

## Social Operation Priorities

Bradkell is the crew's social engine. Outside of combat, his priority ordering is:

**1. Information gathering before the operation.** Three casual conversations with three separate people — a guard's off-duty drinking companion, the quartermaster's delivery supplier, the scribe who handles the estate's correspondence — produces more reliable intelligence than a single risky infiltration attempt. He builds the picture through Persuasion (+5) and Performance (+5) in contexts where his working-class appearance and natural credibility make him invisible to the people he is talking to.

**2. Establishing the cover story.** He is the person who decides what legitimate reason the crew has for being where they are, creates the social and documentary infrastructure for that cover, and rehearses it with each crew member to the point where it can withstand questions. Tanenni handles the calligraphy; he handles the script.

**3. Live social management during the operation.** When something unexpected happens — a guard who wasn't scheduled, a visitor who wasn't expected, a functionary who notices a detail that is slightly wrong — he is the one who intercepts the conversation and redirects it. Persuasion +5, Performance +5, and the practiced ease of someone who has been talking his way into restricted spaces for most of his adult life.

**4. Friends (Concentration) as a targeted social tool.** Friends is powerful and creates a specific problem when it ends: the target knows they were magically influenced and becomes hostile. The correct use of Friends is in situations where the post-spell hostility can be managed — the target is isolated, the crew is about to leave, or the target will not retain access to the group long enough for the hostility to matter. Do not use Friends on a recurring contact or on someone whose relationship the crew needs to preserve.

## Skill Usage

**Arcana +4:** The crew's primary magical knowledge skill. When the crew encounters an unfamiliar magical effect, a warded door, or an artifact of uncertain provenance, Bradkell makes the Arcana check. This is one of two INT-based skills that matter in an infiltration campaign (Investigation is the other). He should be the one examining the magical alarm before Tanenni casts Detect Magic — if the alarm is obvious enough to see, Arcana identifies it first.

**Investigation +4:** Searching spaces for hidden compartments, false floors, concealed mechanisms, and non-obvious document locations. In a vault environment, Investigation is the skill that finds the real strongbox, not the decoy. He should systematically investigate spaces the crew enters on planned operations rather than relying on Passive Perception.

**Performance +5:** He can play the drum, flute, and shawm well enough to be plausible as a street musician, a festival performer, or a court entertainer. This provides a cover identity that places him naturally in diverse social environments. It also triggers the Musician feat — see below.

**Persuasion +5:** High-end social negotiation. The difference between Persuasion and Deception: Persuasion is for genuine attempts to convince someone of something you want them to believe, without them being hostile. When a contact needs to agree to something, when a fence needs to accept a price, when a lord needs to be convinced that the crew belongs in his house — Persuasion is the tool.

## Feature Usage

**Musician feat (Short and Long Rests):** During any Short or Long Rest where Bradkell plays for 10+ minutes, up to 2 allies gain Heroic Inspiration when the rest ends. Heroic Inspiration is Advantage on any single d20 roll — saved for the moment it matters most (a crucial save, a decisive attack, a high-stakes social check). **He should always play at rest.** The crew benefits from Heroic Inspiration entering encounters, and the cost is zero beyond the time invested in the rest they were taking anyway.

**Resourceful (Human):** He gains Heroic Inspiration at the end of every Long Rest. This is personal insurance — he can spend it before passing it to an ally, or retain it through a high-stakes moment. Combined with the Musician feat, it means he often enters encounters with his own Heroic Inspiration already in reserve.

**Tavern Brawler (Rebel):** Unarmed Strikes deal 1d4+STR (+1) damage and can push the target 5 feet. This is a last-resort combat option — 1d4+1 is not a significant contribution to a Level 1 fight. However, the 5-foot push creates specific tactical value: pushing a melee enemy away from himself (or from Darwhidan, who needs to stay adjacent to Milores), or setting up positioning before Milores closes.

**Prestidigitation:** Underestimated utility cantrip in an urban context. Clean or soil an object (restore a prop's appearance after use; soil a guard's uniform to create a distraction incident); warm or chill food or drink (trivial but occasionally useful in an NPC interaction); create a small illusory image for one round (very small, but visible at point-blank); produce an odd odour (scent-based misdirection in a small space). He should ask "can Prestidigitation handle this?" before taking a significant action to produce a minor effect.

## Where Bradkell Shines

- Social encounters — the party's most effective face and only dedicated social engineer
- Sleep — the single most impactful Level 1 spell in a guard-heavy infiltration campaign, and he is the only crew member who has it
- Mage Armour — a mandatory morning cast that buys meaningful survivability for a single slot; frees his second slot to be Sleep without compromise
- Musician — Heroic Inspiration generation at every rest is multiplicative with the crew's effectiveness over a session
- Pre-operation intelligence — gathering information through social channels before a planned operation costs no resources and produces genuinely reliable intelligence
- Sorcerous Burst burst potential — the chain mechanic with CHA +3 maximum extra dice produces occasional exceptional cantrip rounds

## Where Bradkell Struggles

- AC 10 — the worst physical durability in the crew; he is two hits from danger in almost any direct combat scenario
- DEX +0 and Stealth +0 — he cannot participate in a quiet approach and should not attempt one; his role in a stealth infiltration is to create a legitimate-presence distraction while the others go through the quiet entrance
- WIS −1 — he will miss things that Tanenni or Milores would catch; he should not be on watch; his Passive Perception of 9 is the lowest in the crew
- Sleep against non-standard enemies — constructs, undead, and anything immune to the Charmed condition is entirely immune to Sleep; his backup is Innate Sorcery + Sorcerous Burst, which is a significant step down from Sleep in terms of encounter impact
- One effective combat slot per day — Mage Armour consumes slot one; Sleep consumes slot two; once both are gone, he is a cantrip caster and a social asset, not a spell threat

## Roleplaying Notes

Bradkell has decided, at some point in his early twenties, that the most dangerous thing a person can be is underestimated, and has been systematically cultivating that condition ever since. He moves through the world in rough clothes, speaks in the straightforward register of the industrial quarter, and has a face that is distinctive once noticed and forgettable until then. He is the most strategic person in any room he enters, but he maintains this as a private fact rather than a demonstrated one — demonstrated intelligence is a resource that announces itself and then gets managed; private intelligence can be deployed.

He genuinely believes in what the crew is doing. This distinguishes him from Darwhidan (who is in it for the pleasure of the game) and from Milores (who is in it for the specific arithmetic of the cottage). His investment in the targets being the right targets — the corrupt, the extractive, the systemically harmful — is real, and it shows up in his planning. He researches the crew's marks with more thoroughness than operational efficiency requires, because he wants to be certain. He has turned down jobs. He has not always explained why.

His relationship with each crew member is distinct:
- **Tanenni**: professional alignment and mutual respect; he trusts her planning without question and finds her nature-ecological-redistribution framing accurate if slightly depersonalised relative to his own framework
- **Darwhidan**: genuine warmth and tactical trust, complicated by the awareness that Darwhidan's motivation is fundamentally aesthetic rather than principled; he finds this useful and occasionally unsettling
- **Milores**: deep and unsentimental respect; he considers her code the most reliable framework in the group and defers to her professional judgment on operational questions even when they diverge from his own read

He plays at rest because the crew performs better after it. He would play anyway, but this is the version of the reason he tells himself.
