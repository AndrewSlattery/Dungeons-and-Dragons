## 5. Features & Traits

### Class Features *(Rogue)*

**Expertise**
Choose two skill proficiencies. Your Proficiency Bonus is **doubled** for those skills: **Deception** and **Sleight of Hand**.

**Sneak Attack**
Once per turn, you deal **1d6 extra damage** to one creature you hit with an attack using a Finesse or Ranged weapon, if you have **Advantage** on the attack roll, or if another enemy of the target is within 5 feet of it (and you don't have Disadvantage). You don't need both conditions — only one.
*Increases to 2d6 at level 3, 3d6 at level 5, etc.*

**Thieves' Cant**
A secret mix of dialect, jargon, and code that lets you hide messages in ordinary conversation. Takes four times longer to convey a message that way. You also understand a set of secret signs and symbols used by criminals to identify safe houses, traps, marks, and whether a location is under surveillance.

**Weapon Mastery**
You may apply the mastery properties of two weapons with which you are proficient: **Shortsword (Vex)** and **Dagger (Nick)**. See table above.

### Species Traits *(Aasimar)*

**Darkvision**
You have Darkvision with a range of 60 feet.

**Celestial Resistance**
You have **Resistance** to Necrotic damage and Radiant damage.

**Healing Hands** *(Magic Action)*
You touch a creature and cause it to regain **2d4 Hit Points**. You can use this trait a number of times equal to your Proficiency Bonus (**2/LR**), and regain all expended uses when you finish a Long Rest.

**Light Bearer**
You know the **Light** cantrip. Charisma is your spellcasting ability for it.

### Origin Feat

**Magic Initiate (Wizard)** *(Courtier)*
You learn two cantrips from the Wizard spell list (**Mage Hand** and **Minor Illusion**) and one Level 1 Wizard spell (**Feather Fall**), which is always prepared and can be cast once per Long Rest without expending a spell slot. **Charisma** is your spellcasting ability for all three.

## 8. Notes & Backstory

### Appearance

Darwhidan does not look like a celestial herald. He is built like a tavern bouncer of particular quality — broad-chested and thick-necked, with the angular ruggedness of a face that has been in a few disagreements and emerged with its structure intact. His celestial heritage is there if you look for it: metallic gold irises that catch light in a way ordinary eyes don't, a faint warmth that radiates from his skin in cold rooms, and the overall impression that something significant lives behind the ordinary arrangement of his features. He has cultivated a careful nonchalance about all of it.

He moves through high society in impeccably tailored fine clothes that stretch comfortably over his heavy frame — he looks, in the right context, like a very expensive and lightly intimidating bodyguard to someone important. This is useful. Nobody searches the bodyguard. Nobody follows the bodyguard's hands. His hands, which look as though they belong wrapped around a greatsword, can pick a deadbolt in three seconds in total darkness, or slip a lord's signet ring off during a handshake while shaking the lord's hand, while maintaining direct eye contact, while asking about the lord's horses.

He carries the prop notebook — a convincing *Book of Arcane Formulae* — in his coat pocket. Several people have expressed admiration for it. He accepts this graciously. It contains three old letters, a shopping list, and forty-seven pages of his own notes on estate security layouts.

### Personality Traits

- **Effortlessly credible.** He understands that people see the broad shoulders and assign a category, and he uses the category against them with genuine, sustained pleasure. He plays the affable, slightly dim-witted court enforcer right up until the vault is empty and the window is three stories below him.
- **Gravity is a negotiating position.** He has a thrill-seeker's specific relationship with heights. Why take stairs when you can leap from a third-storey window, turn to your colleagues mid-fall, say something appropriate, and cast Feather Fall at approximately the last moment that counts as not too late?

### Ideal

**The Game.** He has no grand theory of justice. He has a finely calibrated set of opinions about whose vaults deserve to be empty, and a genuine and detailed enjoyment of the process of emptying them. If the targets happen to be people who have done harm with their resources, that is satisfying. It is not the primary motivation.

### Bond

He owes Tanenni precisely half of the contents of a particular vault from an evening three years ago. He has not paid this debt. He is not sure she has noticed. He is fairly sure she has noticed. The uncertainty is part of what makes her interesting.

### Flaw

He has very poor Wisdom, and he knows it, and he has compensated for this by becoming very good at reading rooms — which is a Charisma skill, not a Wisdom skill, and the difference matters in certain situations. He misses things that should be obvious. He will notice that an earl is nervous; he will not notice the guard outside the door the earl is nervous about.

### Backstory

#### Origin

Darwhidan was born into a family of Aasimar whose celestial heritage had sharpened over generations into genuine religious conviction. They expected, with the quiet certainty of people who have already decided, that he would become a paladin or a cleric — that the celestial fire in his lineage would express itself as devotion, as duty, as some form of armoured service to a cause larger than himself.

He was instead sent, at nineteen, to serve as a guard and attaché in the courts of the city's highest nobility: a posting meant to season him before taking holy vows. He found the courts extremely interesting, though not in the way intended. He spent his first year mapping estate layouts, studying patrol rotations, and developing a precise understanding of where the valuables were relative to where the guards weren't. He spent his second year teaching himself Mage Hand from a borrowed text, practicing Minor Illusion until he could place sounds precisely enough to make a guard look at exactly the wrong door at exactly the right moment, and identifying which nobles had the best locks and the most to hide.

The celestial fire expressed itself, in the end, as a conviction that the people in these houses had accumulated far more than they were owed, and as a practical, technical solution to that imbalance.

#### The Duke's Estate

The specific event that ended his court career was the complete removal of the contents of a corrupt duke's private vault — securities, correspondence of the sort that becomes actionable when shared, and a collection of objects whose chain of acquisition bore close examination. He walked through the servants' entrance, past two guards who were looking at the wrong door, through a locked vault that required thirty seconds of attention, out through a window on the third floor, and down with the assistance of a small feather and basic arcane physics.

He sent the correspondence to three separate parties who would find it useful. He kept the gold.

He has not been back to the court. His religious order, to which he never actually took vows, sent two letters. He did not reply to either.

#### Tanenni

He met Tanenni in the dark, over a strongbox, from opposite sides of a vault they had independently decided to rob on the same evening. He considered the situation for a moment — she was a Dragonborn in temple robes holding a Sickle, which was not a combination he had prepared scenarios for — and she offered to split it. He accepted.

She subsequently taught him Druidic, which he uses to leave scouting reports in carved runes on city stonework in places that guards walk past without looking at. He considers this one of the most practically useful skills he possesses. She considers it operational security. They have not discussed whether this constitutes the beginning of a debt or its repayment. He suspects she knows exactly which it is.
