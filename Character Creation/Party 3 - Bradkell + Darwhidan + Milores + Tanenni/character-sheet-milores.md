## 5. Features & Traits

### Class Features *(Monk)*

**Martial Arts**
Your practice of Martial Arts gives you mastery of combat using Unarmed Strikes and Monk weapons (Simple Melee weapons and Shortswords).

You gain the following benefits:
- You can use **DEX instead of STR** for the attack and damage rolls of Unarmed Strikes and Monk weapons.
- You can roll a **d6** in place of the normal damage of your Unarmed Strike or Monk weapon (at this level: 1d6).
- **Bonus Unarmed Strike:** When you take the Attack action using only an Unarmed Strike or Monk weapon, you can make one Unarmed Strike as a **Bonus Action** on the same turn.

**Unarmoured Defense**
While you are not wearing armour and not using a shield, your **AC = 10 + DEX modifier + WIS modifier** (currently **AC 15**).

### Species Traits *(Tiefling — Infernal)*

**Darkvision**
You have Darkvision with a range of 60 feet.

**Fiendish Legacy — Infernal**
You have **Resistance** to Fire damage. You know the **Fire Bolt** cantrip. Wisdom is your spellcasting ability for it.

**Otherworldly Presence**
You know the **Thaumaturgy** cantrip. Wisdom is your spellcasting ability for it.

### Origin Feat

**Savage Attacker** *(Soldier)*
Once per turn when you hit a creature with a weapon attack, you can roll the weapon's damage dice twice and use either roll. This applies to weapons with damage dice — **Spear** and **Daggers** — but not to Unarmed Strikes (which don't use damage dice in the same way).

## 8. Notes & Backstory

### Appearance

Milores is physically imposing in the particular way of someone who has done heavy work for a long time and was not slight to begin with. Her skin is the deep, scorched crimson of cooling forge-iron, and her horns are obsidian black, curving tightly over the crown of her head in a shape that her former company used to joke about, until they saw her fight and stopped finding it funny. Her arms and shoulders carry the dense, functional muscle of a career soldier, crisscrossed with faded white scars she has never bothered to explain and does not consider significant.

She dresses in dark canvas reinforced with leather — practical, utilitarian, nothing that will catch on a windowsill or restrict a kick. When she is irritated or preparing to fight, a faint smell of sulfur enters the air and wisps of smoke occasionally curl from her nostrils, a Tiefling tell she stopped trying to suppress years ago. She considers it adequately intimidating without any effort.

What her appearance does not suggest: at every point of rest, her calloused fingertips are stained with paint. Cadmium yellow at the moment. She is working on a series of cliff-edge coastal landscapes based on a drawing she made from memory of a place she visited once and intends to return to, when the current situation is concluded.

### Personality Traits

- **Disciplined.** She rarely speaks unless she has something worth saying. She treats violence as a technical problem — the most efficient path between a situation and its resolution — and applies the same focused, dispassionate attention to it that she gives to mixing paint or assessing a patrol rotation.
- **The Artist.** She finds her life noisy, unpredictable, and aesthetically chaotic. She paints to find the inverse of that. On stakeouts, before vault approaches, in the forty minutes between arrival and action, she will calmly remove the small case from her belt and work on something careful and beautiful, which is not a performance of calm but an actual production of it.

### Ideal

**The Code.** She has a strict personal framework. It is not a legal framework, or a moral framework in any conventional sense — it is a professional one. Certain things are within scope; certain things are outside it. Hurting civilians is outside it. Harming people who cannot defend themselves is outside it. Everything else is a technical question. She applies this code with the consistency of arithmetic.

### Bond

She owes the cottage. That is the entire bond: a specific piece of property on a specific section of coast, with a north-facing window good for painting in the afternoon light, and enough quiet to actually use it. Every job brings it closer. This is not sentiment. It is a project plan.

### Flaw

She is not good at explaining herself. She makes decisions she considers correct and then does not narrate them, which means that people around her occasionally experience her actions as opaque or alarming. She is aware that communication is a skill. She considers it lower-priority than her other skills. This assessment has not yet caused a catastrophic problem, but it will.

### Backstory

#### Origin

Milores grew up in the lower city, the daughter of a Tiefling metalworker whose craft discipline she inherited more directly than she recognised at the time. She was recruited into a mercenary company at sixteen — young, but large for her age and demonstrably capable of things her size suggested, plus things it didn't — and spent the following decade becoming very good at a specific type of work: infiltration of defended positions, silent neutralisation of guards, and the breaking of defensive formations from inside them by someone who had no business being inside them.

She was exceptional at this. The company recognised it, which meant she was given the difficult jobs, which meant she had a great deal of practice at the difficult jobs, which meant she became more exceptional. This cycle had a natural ending: eventually the difficult jobs will include one you will not do.

#### The Incident

Her company took a contract to drive out a target — a minor lord who had wronged a larger lord — by making his holdings untenable. The method chosen by her commanding officer was to burn the village that provided his labour base. The officer's logic, which Milores understood and did not accept, was that displacing the civilians permanently solved the problem without requiring the risk of a direct assault.

She walked into the officer's tent. She gave him one opportunity to reconsider. He did not reconsider. She beat him with sufficient thoroughness to remove him from the remainder of the operation, left him outside the tent for his second-in-command to manage, and walked out of the camp. She took nothing that wasn't already hers.

She does not describe this as a crisis of conscience. She describes it as her professional code encountering a situation that fell outside acceptable parameters, and the appropriate response to that situation. She is not emotionally troubled by the memory. She is occasionally annoyed by the consequences, which included eighteen months of having to check her surroundings more carefully than usual.

#### The Underworld

Hunted by her former company and lacking the kind of references that get a person legitimate work, she disappeared into the urban underworld and found employment as an enforcer for gambling houses and private interests that needed disputes settled quietly. She developed a reputation for efficiency and precision — she rarely broke anything she wasn't paid to break, and she never broke anything twice.

Tanenni's crew found her through a mutual contact and made an offer: one high-value job per season, clean targets, professional crew, no civilians. She agreed because the mathematics worked. The coastal cottage is a real place at a real price, and the crew's payouts have made it a realistic near-future acquisition rather than an eventual abstract one.

She is four jobs away from the deposit.

#### The Painting

She started painting during her first year with the mercenary company, on the recommendation of a Halfling camp surgeon who had noticed she didn't sleep well and suggested making something with her hands before trying to sleep. The surgeon was correct. She has continued.

The current series is coastal landscapes. She has seven completed and is working on the eighth. She will hang them in the cottage when the cottage exists. Until then, she carries the smaller completed ones rolled in a leather tube at the bottom of her pack, which she does not mention to her colleagues and which none of them have yet commented on, though she is aware they have noticed.
