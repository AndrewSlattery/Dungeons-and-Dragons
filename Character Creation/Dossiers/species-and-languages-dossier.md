# D&D 2024 — Species & Languages Dossier

> All information sourced from the Player's Handbook (2024) via dnd2024.wikidot.com.

---

## How Species Works in 2024

- **Species no longer grant Ability Score Increases.** Those come entirely from your Background.
- **Species no longer grant bonus languages.** Language proficiencies come from your Background (2 languages chosen from the Standard Languages table) and from Common, which all characters know by default.
- Every species entry specifies **Creature Type**, **Size**, **Speed**, and a set of **Special Traits**.
- Some species offer an internal choice (a Lineage, Legacy, or Ancestry) that shapes which specific traits you receive.
- All members of a species reach physical maturity at roughly the same age as humans; life span varies per species.

## 1. AASIMAR

*Aasimar are mortals who carry a spark of the Upper Planes within their souls. Whether descended from an angelic being or infused with celestial power, they can fan that spark to bring light, healing, and heavenly fury. Aasimar can arise among any population of mortals — they resemble their parents but live for up to 160 years and have features that hint at their celestial heritage: metallic freckles, luminous eyes, a halo, or the skin colour of an angel (silver, opalescent green, or coppery red).*

| Trait | Detail |
|---|---|
| Creature Type | Humanoid |
| Size | Medium (4–7 ft.) **or** Small (2–4 ft.) — chosen at character creation |
| Speed | 30 feet |
| Lifespan | Up to 160 years |

### Celestial Resistance
You have **Resistance** to **Necrotic damage** and **Radiant damage**.

### Darkvision
You have **Darkvision** with a range of **60 feet**.

### Healing Hands
As a **Magic action**, you touch a creature and roll a number of **d4s equal to your Proficiency Bonus**. The creature regains Hit Points equal to the total rolled.

**Uses:** Once per **Long Rest**.

### Light Bearer
You know the **Light** cantrip. **Charisma** is your spellcasting ability for it.

### Celestial Revelation *(unlocked at character level 3)*
When you reach character level 3, you can transform as a **Bonus Action** using one of the options below (choose each time you transform). The transformation lasts for **1 minute** or until you end it (no action required).

**Uses:** Once per **Long Rest**.

Once on each of your turns before the transformation ends, you can deal extra damage to one target when you deal damage to it with an attack or a spell. The extra damage equals your **Proficiency Bonus**, and the type is either Necrotic (for Necrotic Shroud) or Radiant (for Heavenly Wings and Inner Radiance).

| Option | Effect |
|---|---|
| **Heavenly Wings** | Two spectral wings sprout from your back. You gain a **Fly Speed equal to your Speed** for the duration. |
| **Inner Radiance** | Searing light radiates from your eyes and mouth. You shed **Bright Light in a 10-foot radius** and **Dim Light** for an additional 10 feet. At the end of each of your turns, each creature **within 10 feet** of you takes **Radiant damage equal to your Proficiency Bonus**. |
| **Necrotic Shroud** | Your eyes briefly become pools of darkness; flightless wings sprout from your back. Creatures other than your allies **within 10 feet** must succeed on a **Charisma saving throw (DC 8 + your Charisma modifier + your Proficiency Bonus)** or have the **Frightened** condition until the end of your next turn. |

## 2. DRAGONBORN

*The ancestors of dragonborn hatched from the eggs of chromatic and metallic dragons. Dragonborn look like wingless, bipedal dragons — scaly, bright-eyed, thick-boned, with horns on their heads. Their colouring is reminiscent of their draconic ancestors.*

| Trait | Detail |
|---|---|
| Creature Type | Humanoid |
| Size | Medium (5–7 ft.) |
| Speed | 30 feet |

### Draconic Ancestry
Your lineage stems from a dragon progenitor. Choose one dragon type from the table below. Your choice determines the damage type for your **Breath Weapon** and **Damage Resistance** traits, as well as your appearance.

| Dragon | Damage Type |
|---|---|
| Black | Acid |
| Blue | Lightning |
| Brass | Fire |
| Bronze | Lightning |
| Copper | Acid |
| Gold | Fire |
| Green | Poison |
| Red | Fire |
| Silver | Cold |
| White | Cold |

### Breath Weapon
When you take the **Attack action** on your turn, you can replace one of your attacks with an exhalation of magical energy in either a **15-foot Cone** or a **30-foot Line (5 feet wide)** — choose the shape each time.

Each creature in the area must make a **Dexterity saving throw (DC 8 + your Constitution modifier + your Proficiency Bonus)**. On a **failed** save, a creature takes damage of the type determined by your Draconic Ancestry. On a **successful** save, a creature takes half as much damage.

| Character Level | Damage |
|---|---|
| 1–4 | 1d10 |
| 5–10 | 2d10 |
| 11–16 | 3d10 |
| 17–20 | 4d10 |

**Uses:** A number of times equal to your **Proficiency Bonus** per **Long Rest**.

### Damage Resistance
You have **Resistance** to the damage type determined by your **Draconic Ancestry**.

### Darkvision
You have **Darkvision** with a range of **60 feet**.

### Draconic Flight *(unlocked at character level 5)*
As a **Bonus Action**, you sprout spectral wings on your back that last for **10 minutes** or until you retract them (no action required) or have the **Incapacitated** condition. For that duration, you have a **Fly Speed equal to your Speed**. The wings appear to be made of the same energy as your Breath Weapon.

**Uses:** Once per **Long Rest**.

## 3. DWARF

*Dwarves were raised from the earth in the elder days by a deity of the forge (known as Moradin, Reorx, or other names on different worlds). They have an affinity for stone, metal, and underground life — carving cities into mountainsides and underground. Squat and often bearded, dwarves live for around 350 years.*

| Trait | Detail |
|---|---|
| Creature Type | Humanoid |
| Size | Medium (4–5 ft.) |
| Speed | 30 feet |
| Lifespan | About 350 years |

### Darkvision
You have **Darkvision** with a range of **120 feet**.

### Dwarven Resilience
You have **Resistance** to **Poison damage**. You also have **Advantage** on saving throws you make to avoid or end the **Poisoned** condition.

### Dwarven Toughness
Your **Hit Point maximum increases by 1**, and it increases by 1 again whenever you gain a level.

### Stonecunning
As a **Bonus Action**, you gain **Tremorsense** with a range of **60 feet** for **10 minutes**. You must be on a stone surface or touching a stone surface to use this Tremorsense. The stone can be natural or worked.

**Uses:** A number of times equal to your **Proficiency Bonus** per **Long Rest**.

## 4. ELF

*Created by the god Corellon, the first elves could change their forms at will — they lost this ability when Corellon cursed them after they plotted with the deity Lolth. Elves have pointed ears, lack facial and body hair, and live for around 750 years. Rather than sleeping, they enter a 4-hour trance to rest, remaining aware of their surroundings while immersing themselves in memories and meditations.*

*An environment subtly transforms elves after they inhabit it for a millennium or more, granting certain kinds of magic. Three lineages appear in the PHB: Drow, High Elf, and Wood Elf.*

| Trait | Detail |
|---|---|
| Creature Type | Humanoid |
| Size | Medium (5–6 ft.) |
| Speed | 30 feet (Wood Elf: 35 feet) |
| Lifespan | About 750 years |

### Darkvision
You have **Darkvision** with a range of **60 feet**. *(Drow lineage extends this to 120 feet.)*

### Elven Lineage
You are part of a lineage that grants you supernatural abilities. Choose one lineage from the table below. You gain the **Level 1 benefit** of that lineage immediately. At character levels 3 and 5 you learn a higher-level spell from the lineage.

You **always have** those spells prepared. You can cast each once **without a spell slot**, and you regain the ability to cast them that way when you finish a **Long Rest**. You can also cast them using any spell slots you have of the appropriate level.

**Intelligence, Wisdom, or Charisma** is your spellcasting ability for all spells from this trait (choose when you select the lineage).

| Lineage | Level 1 Benefit | Level 3 Spell | Level 5 Spell |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Drow** | Darkvision increases to **120 feet**. You know the **Dancing Lights** cantrip. | Faerie Fire | Darkness |
| **High Elf** | You know the **Prestidigitation** cantrip. Whenever you finish a Long Rest, you can replace that cantrip with a different cantrip from the **Wizard** spell list. | Detect Magic | Misty Step |
| **Wood Elf** | Your **Speed increases to 35 feet**. You also know the **Druidcraft** cantrip. | Longstrider | Pass without Trace |

### Fey Ancestry
You have **Advantage** on saving throws you make to avoid or end the **Charmed** condition.

### Keen Senses
You gain proficiency in one of the following skills of your choice: **Insight, Perception,** or **Survival**.

### Trance
You **don't need to sleep**, and magic can't put you to sleep. You can finish a **Long Rest in 4 hours** if you spend those hours in a trancelike meditation, during which you retain consciousness.

## 5. GNOME

*Gnomes are magical folk created by gods of invention, illusions, and life underground (Garl Glittergold, Baervan Wildwanderer, Baravar Cloakshadow). Petite folk with big eyes and pointed ears, gnomes live for around 425 years. Many feel most at home with a roof overhead — even if it's just a hat.*

| Trait | Detail |
|---|---|
| Creature Type | Humanoid |
| Size | Small (3–4 ft.) |
| Speed | 30 feet |
| Lifespan | About 425 years |

### Darkvision
You have **Darkvision** with a range of **60 feet**.

### Gnomish Cunning
You have **Advantage** on **Intelligence, Wisdom, and Charisma saving throws**.

### Gnomish Lineage
You are part of a lineage that grants you supernatural abilities. Choose one of the following options. **Intelligence, Wisdom, or Charisma** is your spellcasting ability for spells cast with this trait (choose when you select the lineage).

---

**Forest Gnome**

- You know the **Minor Illusion** cantrip.
- You always have the **Speak with Animals** spell prepared. You can cast it **without a spell slot** a number of times equal to your **Proficiency Bonus**, and you regain all expended uses when you finish a **Long Rest**. You can also cast it using any spell slots you have.

---

**Rock Gnome**

- You know the **Mending** and **Prestidigitation** cantrips.
- You can spend **10 minutes** casting Prestidigitation to create a **Tiny clockwork device** (AC 5, 1 HP), such as a toy, fire starter, or music box. When you create the device, you choose one effect from Prestidigitation; the device produces that effect whenever you or another creature takes a **Bonus Action** to activate it with a touch. You can have **three such devices** in existence at a time; each falls apart **8 hours** after creation or when you dismantle it with a touch as a **Utilize action**.

## 6. GOLIATH

*Towering over most folk, goliaths are distant descendants of giants. Each goliath bears the favors of the first giants — manifesting as supernatural boons, including the ability to temporarily grow to Large size. A goliath's physical characteristics are reminiscent of the giant bloodline in their family (stone, fire, cloud, etc.).*

| Trait | Detail |
|---|---|
| Creature Type | Humanoid |
| Size | Medium (7–8 ft.) |
| Speed | 35 feet |

### Giant Ancestry
You are descended from Giants. Choose **one** of the following boons. You can use the chosen benefit a number of times equal to your **Proficiency Bonus**, and you regain all expended uses when you finish a **Long Rest**.

| Ancestry | Trigger | Effect |
|---|---|---|
| **Cloud's Jaunt** *(Cloud Giant)* | Bonus Action | You magically **teleport up to 30 feet** to an unoccupied space you can see. |
| **Fire's Burn** *(Fire Giant)* | When you hit a target with an attack | You can also deal **1d10 Fire damage** to that target. |
| **Frost's Chill** *(Frost Giant)* | When you hit a target with an attack | You can also deal **1d6 Cold damage** to that target and **reduce its Speed by 10 feet** until the start of your next turn. |
| **Hill's Tumble** *(Hill Giant)* | When you hit a Large or smaller creature with an attack | You can give that target the **Prone** condition. |
| **Stone's Endurance** *(Stone Giant)* | Reaction when you take damage | Roll **1d12 + your Constitution modifier** and **reduce the incoming damage** by that total. |
| **Storm's Thunder** *(Storm Giant)* | Reaction when you take damage from a creature within 60 feet | Deal **1d8 Thunder damage** to that creature. |

### Large Form *(unlocked at character level 5)*
As a **Bonus Action**, you change your size to **Large** (if you're in a big enough space) for **10 minutes** or until you end it (no action required). For that duration, you have **Advantage on Strength checks**, and your **Speed increases by 10 feet**.

**Uses:** Once per **Long Rest**.

### Powerful Build
- You have **Advantage** on any ability check you make to end the **Grappled** condition.
- You **count as one size larger** when determining your **carrying capacity**.

## 7. HALFLING

*Cherished and guided by gods who value life, home, and hearth (Yondalla, Brandobaris, Charmalaine), halflings gravitate toward bucolic havens but many have a brave and adventurous spirit. Their small size — similar to a human child — helps them slip through crowds unnoticed. The legendary "luck of the halflings" is real: when a halfling is in mortal danger, an unseen force seems to intervene. Halflings live for about 150 years.*

| Trait | Detail |
|---|---|
| Creature Type | Humanoid |
| Size | Small (2–3 ft.) |
| Speed | 30 feet |
| Lifespan | About 150 years |

### Brave
You have **Advantage** on saving throws you make to avoid or end the **Frightened** condition.

### Halfling Nimbleness
You can **move through the space** of any creature that is a size larger than you, but you **can't stop** in the same space.

### Luck
When you roll a **1 on the d20** of a D20 Test, you can **reroll the die**, and you must use the new roll.

### Naturally Stealthy
You can take the **Hide action** even when you are obscured only by a creature that is **at least one size larger** than you.

## 8. HUMAN

*Found throughout the multiverse, humans are as varied as they are numerous — ambitious, resourceful, and diverse in appearance. One of the earliest known human gatherings is said to have occurred in Sigil, where the Common language was born.*

| Trait | Detail |
|---|---|
| Creature Type | Humanoid |
| Size | Medium (4–7 ft.) **or** Small (2–4 ft.) — chosen at character creation |
| Speed | 30 feet |
| Lifespan | About 80 years |

### Resourceful
You gain **Heroic Inspiration** whenever you finish a **Long Rest**.

### Skillful
You gain proficiency in **one skill of your choice**.

### Versatile
You gain **one Origin feat of your choice**. *(Skilled is recommended.)*

> **Note:** Humans are the only species that gets a bonus Origin feat from their species (in addition to the one from their background). This gives them a total of **two** Origin feats at character creation.

## 9. ORC

*Orcs trace their creation to Gruumsh, a powerful god who roamed the Material Plane. Gruumsh equipped his children with endurance, determination, and darkvision. Orcs are, on average, tall and broad with gray skin, sharply pointed ears, and prominent lower canines resembling small tusks.*

| Trait | Detail |
|---|---|
| Creature Type | Humanoid |
| Size | Medium (6–7 ft.) |
| Speed | 30 feet |
| Lifespan | About 80 years |

### Adrenaline Rush
You can take the **Dash action** as a **Bonus Action**. When you do so, you gain a number of **Temporary Hit Points equal to your Proficiency Bonus**.

**Uses:** A number of times equal to your **Proficiency Bonus** per **Short or Long Rest**.

### Darkvision
You have **Darkvision** with a range of **120 feet**.

### Relentless Endurance
When you are reduced to **0 Hit Points** but not killed outright, you can drop to **1 Hit Point** instead.

**Uses:** Once per **Long Rest**.

## 10. TIEFLING

*Tieflings (pronounced TEE-fling) are either born in the Lower Planes or have fiendish ancestors from there. Linked by blood to a devil, demon, or other Fiend, their connection — the fiendish legacy — promises power but has no inherent effect on moral outlook. Each tiefling chooses whether to embrace or lament their heritage.*

*Three legacies exist, each tied to different Lower Planes:*
- **Abyssal** — tied to the Abyss, Pandemonium, and Carceri; often with horns, fur, or tusks.
- **Chthonic** — drawn toward Carceri, Gehenna, and Hades; some look cadaverous, others have the unearthly beauty of a succubus.
- **Infernal** — connected to Gehenna, the Nine Hells, and Acheron; often with horns, spines, tails, golden eyes, and a faint odour of sulfur.

| Trait | Detail |
|---|---|
| Creature Type | Humanoid |
| Size | Medium (4–7 ft.) **or** Small (3–4 ft.) — chosen at character creation |
| Speed | 30 feet |

### Darkvision
You have **Darkvision** with a range of **60 feet**.

### Fiendish Legacy
You are the recipient of a fiendish legacy. Choose one legacy from the table below. You gain the **Level 1 benefit** of the chosen legacy immediately. At character levels 3 and 5, you learn a higher-level spell from the legacy.

You **always have** those spells prepared. You can cast each once **without a spell slot**, and you regain the ability to cast them that way when you finish a **Long Rest**. You can also cast them using any spell slots you have of the appropriate level.

**Intelligence, Wisdom, or Charisma** is your spellcasting ability for all spells from this trait (choose when you select the legacy).

| Legacy | Level 1 Benefit | Level 3 Spell | Level 5 Spell |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Abyssal** | **Resistance to Poison damage.** You also know the **Poison Spray** cantrip. | Ray of Sickness | Hold Person |
| **Chthonic** | **Resistance to Necrotic damage.** You also know the **Chill Touch** cantrip. | False Life | Ray of Enfeeblement |
| **Infernal** | **Resistance to Fire damage.** You also know the **Fire Bolt** cantrip. | Hellish Rebuke | Darkness |

### Otherworldly Presence
You know the **Thaumaturgy** cantrip. When you cast it with this trait, the spell uses the same **spellcasting ability** you chose for your **Fiendish Legacy** trait.

## 12. LANGUAGES

### How Languages Work at Character Creation

- **Common** is the baseline. All characters speak and read Common automatically — it does **not** count as one of the languages granted by your background.
- **Backgrounds** grant **2 additional languages** chosen from the Standard Languages table (neither of which needs to be Common). *(Note: the Backgrounds dossier covers the other elements of each background; the 2 language choices are made freely from the Standard table below.)*
- **Species** in 2024 do **not** grant bonus languages as a species trait — this is a change from 2014.
- Some class features grant specific languages (Druidic for Druids, Thieves' Cant for Rogues — both are listed in the class dossier).
- Feats such as **Skilled** can grant additional language proficiencies.
- The **Linguist** feat (a General feat, available later) grants 3 additional languages.

---

### Standard Languages

These are the languages typically available to player characters at character creation, and the ones you choose from when your background grants 2 languages.

| Language | Typical Speakers | Script |
|---|---|---|
| Common | Humans (and most peoples) | Common |
| Common Sign Language | Many peoples | — |
| Draconic | Dragons, Dragonborn | Draconic |
| Dwarvish | Dwarves | Dwarvish |
| Elvish | Elves | Elvish |
| Giant | Giants, Goliaths | Dwarvish |
| Gnomish | Gnomes | Dwarvish |
| Goblin | Goblinoids | Dwarvish |
| Halfling | Halflings | Common |
| Orc | Orcs | Dwarvish |

### Rare Languages

These languages are not typically available at character creation without specific class features, feats, or DM permission.

| Language | Typical Speakers | Script |
|---|---|---|
| Abyssal | Demons | Infernal |
| Celestial | Celestials | Celestial |
| Deep Speech | Aberrations, Mind Flayers, Beholders | — |
| Druidic | Druids | Elvish |
| Infernal | Devils, Tieflings | Infernal |

---

### Language Notes

- **Scripts** are shared across language families: Dwarvish script is used by Dwarvish, Giant, Gnomish, Goblin, Orc, and Primordial; Elvish script is used by Elvish, Druidic, Sylvan, and Undercommon; Infernal script is used by Abyssal and Infernal.
- **Common Sign Language** is a distinct language from Common — knowing one does not grant the other.
- **Druidic** and **Thieves' Cant** are class-granted languages (Druid and Rogue respectively) that cannot be learned simply by choosing them from the Standard Languages table.
- Characters with the **Sage background** begin with Calligrapher's Supplies, which suggests literacy in Common is the default assumption.
