## 5. Features & Traits

### Class Features *(Cleric)*

**Spellcasting**
You can cast Cleric spells and use an Amulet as a spellcasting focus. 

**Divine Order: Thaumaturge**
You know one extra cantrip from the Cleric spell list. You also gain a +3 bonus to your Arcana and Religion checks.

### Species Traits *(Goliath)*

**Giant Ancestry (Stone's Endurance)**
When you take damage, you can take a Reaction to roll 1d12 and add your Constitution modifier (currently **1d12+3**); reduce the damage taken by that total.
Uses: **Proficiency Bonus (2) per LR**.

**Large Form** *(Unlocks at character level 5 — not yet available to Daroc.)*
Starting at character level 5, if you're in a big enough space, you can change your size to Large as a Bonus Action. For 10 minutes (or until ended — no action required), you have Advantage on Strength checks and your Speed increases by 10 ft. Usable once per Long Rest.

**Powerful Build**
You have Advantage on any **saving throw** you make to end the Grappled condition. You also count as one size larger when determining your carrying capacity.

### Origin Feats

**Magic Initiate (Wizard)** *(Sage)*
You learn two cantrips (*Mage Hand*, *True Strike*) and one 1st-level spell (*Find Familiar*) from the Wizard spell list. Wisdom is your spellcasting ability for these spells. You can cast the 1st-level spell once per Long Rest without expending a spell slot, or by using your Cleric spell slots. On each level-up you may swap one of these spells for another of the same level from the Wizard list.

### Somatic Components — Hand Availability

Per *PHB 2024, Chapter 7*: **Somatic (S)** requires "at least one of their hands" to perform the gestures. **Material (M)** needs a free hand too, but the *same* hand can do both S and M; and a Spellcasting Focus substitutes for non-costly M. The Amulet Holy Symbol is listed as "worn or held", so **when worn it satisfies the focus requirement with zero hands occupied**.

**Daroc's loadout (staff one-handed, no shield) leaves his off-hand free**, which means:

- **V-only spells need no hands at all.** Covers *Healing Word*, *Command*, and *Thaumaturgy* — usable even if both hands are full.
- **V/M spells with non-costly M also need no hands** (worn Amulet is the focus). *Word of Radiance* is in this category — V + non-costly M (a sunburst token), no Somatic — so it can be cast while two-handing the staff.
- **All V/S and V/S/M (non-costly M) spells are castable with one free hand.** The off-hand performs Somatic gestures while the worn Amulet handles the focus; the staff stays in the dominant hand. This covers *Bless*, *Guiding Bolt*, *Shield of Faith*, *Sanctuary*, *Cure Wounds*, *Inflict Wounds*, *Bane*, the detection spells, *Spare the Dying*, *Mage Hand*, and *True Strike*.
- **Two-handed staff grip occupies both hands.** If swinging the staff two-handed for 1d8 Versatile damage, drop back to one-handed grip (free object interaction, once per turn) before casting an S spell.
- **Costly Material components still need a free hand to handle the component itself.** *Protection from Evil and Good* (holy water 25+ GP) and re-summoning *Find Familiar* (incense 10+ GP) require physically holding the component — not just a focus.

By dropping the shield, Daroc no longer needs to juggle his loadout to cast in combat, and the *War Caster* feat is no longer required to resolve hand-availability issues (it would still be nice for Advantage on Concentration saves).

## 8. Notes & Backstory

### Appearance

Daroc is a towering Goliath, standing a massive seven feet, seven inches tall and weighing four hundred pounds. His skin is a striking, deep olivine hue, contrasting sharply with his piercing emerald eyes and charcoal-dark hair cropped short on the sides. Despite his incredibly intimidating stature and chain shirt, he carries himself with a deliberate, studied slowness. He is almost always seen with an assortment of books and parchments tucked into his gear, a finely crafted holy amulet rests proudly over his armour, and his ferret familiar lounges across his shoulders or in his lap.

### Background

A deeply devoted follower of Arrhene, Daroc's life has been heavily shaped by the quiet, dusty halls of the Archivist's Guild and the sacred spaces of his faith community. Despite his towering physical presence, he is a Sage through and through—far more energetic and curious when surrounded by ancient texts and calligraphic scrolls than he is on a battlefield. He was mentored by a respected Arcanist, which afforded him an unusual but potent grounding in arcane utility magic (*Mage Hand*, *True Strike*, and the bond of *Find Familiar* with his celestial-touched ferret companion) to complement his divine calling. 

Daroc is fundamentally driven by a twin ideal of Knowledge and Care. However, having spent so much of his life within cloisters and libraries, he can be noticeably reserved in social situations—often communicating indirectly or appearing unsteady when forced outside of his comfort zone. Still, those who mistake his scholarly awkwardness for weakness are quickly corrected; he wields his quarterstaff with brutal, magically guided precision when his allies or his tomes are threatened.

## 9. Strategic Analysis

### Party Role

Daroc is a **back-line support caster with melee capability** — a Thaumaturge Cleric is built to blend scholarly utility with reliable divine support. His job in any party is, in order of priority:

1. **Keep allies alive** via *Healing Word* (Bonus Action, 60 ft, 2d4+3). The range and BA action cost makes this the single most valuable thing he does in combat: a downed ally can be brought back to fighting from 60 feet away while Daroc still uses his action for something else.
2. **Concentration buffs** — *Bless* is the default. +1d4 to attacks AND saves for 3 allies for up to 1 minute. In any party of 3+ martials this is a massive throughput boost; it consistently outperforms direct damage at level 1.
3. **Scholarly utility** — Arcana +6, Religion +6, History +3 (all boosted by Thaumaturge bonus) make Daroc the party's *lore bot*. If anyone needs to identify a magical phenomenon, recognise a deity's symbol, or recall historical context, Daroc is almost guaranteed the highest check at the table.
4. **Frontline supplement** — With Chain Shirt AC 14, HP 11, and a reach of 5 ft (staff), Daroc can hold a flank but is not a primary tank.

### Action-Economy Template

A productive Daroc combat round typically uses **all three** of Action, Bonus Action, and Reaction:

| Slot | Default pick | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| **Action** | *True Strike* (1d6+3 Radiant, +5 to hit) or Guiding Bolt | True Strike is your cantrip-based attack — it uses your WIS (+3) instead of STR (−1), so your melee is +5 to hit / 1d6+3 damage on a hit. This is strictly better than the STR-based quarterstaff swing. |
| **Bonus Action** | *Healing Word* if ally is down; otherwise *Sanctuary* or hold | Healing Word is reactive. If nobody needs healing, you generally have no BA spells unless you've dipped into Sanctuary. |
| **Reaction** | *Stone's Endurance* (1d12+3 damage reduction) | 2 per long rest. Use on any hit that would drop you below half HP or would kill you outright. |

A common pattern: round 1 cast *Bless* (action + concentration); round 2+ use *True Strike* (action) + *Healing Word* if needed (BA). Bless persists for 10 rounds so the investment pays back after ~2 ally attacks.

### Opener Decision Tree

- **Ambush / surprise round / single tough enemy:** Open with *Guiding Bolt* (4d6 Radiant = 14 avg, and grants advantage on the next attack against it). This is your alpha-strike.
- **Drawn-out fight / multiple weak enemies:** Open with *Bless* on 3 martials. The concentration pays for itself by round 2.
- **Ally caster needs protection:** Open with *Sanctuary* (BA) on them, then Action to attack / Guiding Bolt.
- **Multiple enemies in melee around you:** Open with *Word of Radiance* (1d6 Radiant in 5-ft emanation, chosen targets only, so allies are safe). Very efficient if 2+ enemies are adjacent.

### Spell Slot Economy

At level 1 Daroc has **only 2 first-level slots per long rest**. This is tight. Guidelines:

- Don't spend both slots in the first encounter unless the party is in real trouble.
- *Healing Word* is almost always the right slot to spend — a reaction to a downed ally is the highest-value use of a slot at level 1.
- *Find Familiar* has **one free cast per long rest** via Magic Initiate. Always cast this at the start of the day (1 hour / ritual). Don't burn a slot on it if the free cast is available.

### The Ferret (Force Multiplier)

Find Familiar is genuinely overpowered for level 1. The ferret's **Help action** grants an ally Advantage on their next attack — that's roughly a +5 to-hit bonus to a friendly martial every turn, for *free*, with no spell slot cost once summoned. Over a typical combat, that's worth more than almost any level 1 spell Daroc could cast.

Additional uses:
- **Touch delivery.** The ferret can deliver *Cure Wounds*, *Inflict Wounds*, or *Spare the Dying* (via Reaction, within 100 ft of Daroc). This turns touch-range spells into 100-ft-range spells.
- **Scouting.** Stealth +5, Tiny, Climb 30 ft, Darkvision 30 ft, and Daroc shares its senses via Bonus Action (within 100 ft telepathy). Before combat, send it ahead — this is the party's primary intelligence asset.
- **Fragility.** It has 1 HP. Do not put it in AoE range or in melee. Position it 5 ft from a target to Help, then Disengage/Dash away.

### Goliath Durability

- **Stone's Endurance** (2/LR): 1d12+3 damage reduction as a Reaction. Average 9.5 reduction, up to 15. This is enormous at level 1 — it will often fully negate a hit. Save it for heavy blows; don't burn it on a 3-damage scratch.
- **Powerful Build:** Advantage on saves to escape Grappled; counts as Large for carrying capacity (240 lb encumbered, 480 lb push/drag/lift). Daroc won't struggle with any loot haul.
- **Large Form** unlocks at level 5 — no effect yet, but worth remembering the character has a powerful spike at that milestone.

### Strengths and Weaknesses Summary

**Strengths:**
- Best-in-class level-1 support kit: Bless + Healing Word + Sanctuary + Guiding Bolt.
- True Strike + WIS 17 gives a reliable +5 melee attack, bypassing the STR 8 dump.
- Arcana/Religion +6 makes him the knowledge authority of any party at level 1–4.
- Stone's Endurance provides a per-encounter damage sponge independent of spell slots.
- Ferret familiar is a free Advantage generator and scout.

**Weaknesses:**
- **AC 14** is below modern front-liner standard (16+ is typical). Don't tank.
- **11 HP** is fragile. Two hits of 6 damage each is a death save.
- **CHA 8** = social liability. Persuasion +1 (proficient) is your best Cha skill — barely break-even. Deception/Intimidation at −1 are dead. Let bards/paladins talk in roleplay.
- **No ranged weapon** other than Guiding Bolt (1/day realistically) and improvised Holy Water throw.
- **Spell slots are rare.** Conserve them for Healing Word emergencies.

### Recommended Near-Term Planning

- **Level 2**: Channel Divinity unlocks (2/short rest). *Divine Spark* deals or heals 1d8+3. Gives Daroc a reliable non-slot resource.
- **Level 3**: Subclass choice. Given Daroc's radiant-damage theme (True Strike Radiant, Word of Radiance, Guiding Bolt) and scholar identity, **Light Domain** is the most synergistic — it adds Burning Hands, Scorching Ray, and Warding Flare (a reaction that imposes Disadvantage on an attack 3/day). **Life Domain** is the safer party-support pick; it adds +2+slot-level to any healing spell. **War Domain** doesn't fit Daroc's scholarly theme.
- **Level 4**: Feat choice. *War Caster* gives Advantage on Concentration saves (huge for keeping Bless up) + the Somatic shortcut (less important now without a shield). Alternatives: *Fey Touched* (WIS +1 and gain Misty Step) or plain ASI +2 WIS (17→19, Spell DC 14, Spell Atk +6).

### Cantrip Swap Suggestion

At each level-up Daroc can swap one cantrip. Consider swapping *Thaumaturgy* (role-play flavor only) for ***Guidance*** — +1d4 to any ability check, at will, on a willing ally. This is arguably the strongest utility cantrip in the game for a support caster and Daroc does not currently have it. *Spare the Dying* can also be swapped once Channel Divinity's Divine Spark arrives, since Divine Spark out-heals it.

## 10. RPG Taverns Meetup — First-Timer Guidance

### What to Expect at an Over-the-Table D&D Meetup

Meetup-style D&D events like RPG Taverns typically run **4–5 hour one-shots** (or session-length modules) where strangers are grouped into random parties of 4–6 with a volunteer or paid GM. Some key practical notes:

- **Character is usually pre-made or level-capped for the event.** Confirm the level and any house rules (2014 PHB vs 2024 PHB especially — Daroc is explicitly 2024). Most modern events have moved to 2024 rules, but not all; if the event is 2014, some mechanics change (e.g., True Strike is totally different, Magic Initiate grants less, Goliath is a different stat block).
- **You will not know your party in advance.** Expect a mix of veterans and newbies. Daroc's role — reliable support healer with knowledge skills — is *extremely welcome* in a random party; almost every group is short a healer.
- **Session time is shorter than a home campaign.** Turns need to be decisive. Plan your turn *before* it comes up (especially if Bless is already cast — Daroc's turns are typically: True Strike + Healing Word if needed).
- **Tone varies table to table.** Some GMs run gritty and lethal, some run heroic and punchy. Ask the GM in the first 5 minutes: "*How lethal is the tone? Are you using tactical grid combat or Theater of the Mind? Are you using the 2024 PHB?*"
- **Alcohol may be present** (it's a tavern-type venue). Pace yourself; casting spells with precise rules calls is easier when sober, especially as a new player.

### What to Bring

- **Printed character sheet** (the updated markdown, or a clean D&D Beyond PDF). Bring two copies in case one gets spilled on.
- **Your own dice set** (1d20, 1d12, 1d10, 2d8 at minimum — note the 2d8 for Divine Spark next level, and the 1d12 for Stone's Endurance). Many players also bring extras to lend.
- **Pencil + eraser** (not a pen — you'll edit HP a lot).
- **Ferret token / mini** for the familiar. It's a combat participant with its own initiative and position, so the GM will want to track it. Any small token works (a coin, a bead, a meeple). If the venue uses a grid, the ferret occupies a Tiny square.
- **Notes with spell descriptions.** At least bring the text of *Bless*, *Healing Word*, *Guiding Bolt*, *Sanctuary*, and *Find Familiar*; GMs appreciate players who can quote the rule when a question comes up.
- **Cash / card for the GM tip and any food.**

### How Daroc Fits Into a Random Party

Almost all likely party compositions benefit from having Daroc:

- **Party has a dedicated tank (Fighter/Barbarian/Paladin):** Ideal. Daroc sits 5–10 ft behind the tank, Blesses the frontline round 1, Healing Words them when they drop, and uses Guiding Bolt to set up the fighter's crit turn.
- **Party has another healer (Bard/Druid/other Cleric):** Daroc leans more into buff/control (Bless, Sanctuary, Bane) and Radiant damage (Guiding Bolt, True Strike) while the other healer does the reactive patching.
- **Party is all damage dealers, no healer:** Daroc becomes the lifeline. Prep *Cure Wounds* over *Command*, keep the ferret touch-delivery option active, and don't burn both spell slots before turn 5 of any encounter.
- **Party has a Rogue / scout:** Pair the ferret with them for extra Help action advantage on Sneak Attacks — the damage synergy here is outstanding.
- **No arcanist in party:** Daroc's *Mage Hand* and ability to ritual-cast *Detect Magic* (if prepared) fills a good chunk of the wizard's utility niche out of combat.

### Social/Table Etiquette Tips

1. **Introduce Daroc in 1–2 sentences.** "Daroc is a Goliath cleric — scholarly, devoted to Arrhene, travels with a celestial ferret. I heal, buff, and I know a lot about history and religion." That's all anyone needs.
2. **Flag Daroc's awkwardness as a feature, not a shield.** You can let the party Face (probably a Bard or Paladin) do the talking; Daroc's low CHA is in-character and funny.
3. **Offer the ferret for scouting early.** GMs love when players use their kit creatively, and the ferret is Daroc's signature mechanic. It's also a great icebreaker.
4. **Don't hoard Healing Word.** The temptation is to save slots "in case things get worse" — but if an ally is at 0, *use the slot*. Healing Word at 2d4+3 on a 60-ft BA is the most efficient spell in the game at level 1. The encounter is always worse if a party member is rolling death saves.
5. **Volunteer for knowledge checks.** Arcana and Religion at +6 means you should roll these first; at a table of strangers, demonstrating competence early earns trust.
6. **Keep the ferret safe.** If it dies, you need 10 GP of incense and 1 hour (or a ritual window) to re-summon it — not ideal mid-session. Recall it to the pocket dimension (Magic action) if things get dicey.

### First-Turn Script (For When Your Brain Freezes)

If you're nervous and combat starts:
> "On my turn, I'll use my action to cast **Bless** on [the fighter], [the paladin], and [myself]. That's a 1st-level spell, concentration, up to 1 minute. They each add 1d4 to attack rolls and saves until it ends. I'll stay 10 feet behind the front line."

That's a complete, decisive, correct-rules turn. After that, round 2+ is just True Strike attacks and Healing Word as needed.
