## 8. Notes & Backstory

### Appearance

Cognodec stands at three feet and four inches, which he has spent several decades treating as a precision instrument. He is narrow-shouldered and bright-eyed, with amber irises common to certain Forest Gnome bloodlines — though he has, in certain towns and certain lights, implied they are the result of a blessing. He has never corrected the impression. His complexion is the warm brown of forest soil, and his hair is a dark grey that has always been dark grey; he was born with it, his grandmother had the same, and he considers it distinguished.

He dresses in fine clothes in deep greens and dark copper tones, with small embroidered acorns along the cuffs that he added himself. His coat is technically two sizes too large for him, falls to his knees, and he considers this a feature rather than a defect. The orb that serves as his arcane focus is cradled in a sling of his own construction, nestled at his shoulder and visible above the coat collar, catching light in ways that are not entirely explicable. His Book of Shadows is bound in leather tooled to resemble bark, with brass fittings that appear to shift position if you look away from them. He carries it pressed to his side with the ease of long habit.

He looks, in total, exactly like a Forest Gnome performing the role of Forest Gnome Mystic. The unsettling detail is that he now is one.

### Personality Traits

- He will freely tell you he is a charlatan. This is itself a form of misdirection, and he knows it, and will tell you that too if you ask the right question. No one ever does.
- When he communicates with animals, he uses the same theatrical murmur he developed for the performance. The animals don't seem to mind. Some appear to find it reassuring.

### Ideal

**The Right Story.** He has spent his career giving people the story they needed — sometimes a comfort, sometimes a con. He is beginning to suspect that what is wrong with the world right now does not yet have its right story, and that finding one, or building one, may be the most genuinely useful thing he has ever done.

### Bond

His patron: a Fey lord or lady whose name he received in a dream, who may have given him the wrong name, and whom he has referred to in performance as *the Green Advocate* for long enough that he is no longer entirely certain that isn't accurate. He does not know whether the patron is fond of him, testing him, or simply amused. He has noticed that whenever something is wrong in the forest, the Book seems heavier than it should be.

### Flaw

He is excellent at appearing to understand things he does not understand, which has occasionally prevented him from admitting — to himself as much as to others — when he is genuinely lost.

### Backstory

#### Origin

Cognodec was the fourth of seven children in a Forest Gnome community tucked into the edge-wood between two larger forests — the sort of settlement that appears in winter slightly differently than in summer, and which most outsiders pass without noticing. He was not the most magical of his siblings, nor the most woodwise. He was, however, by far the most watchful, and from early childhood he had a talent for understanding what people expected to see.

He left at forty-three — young for a Gnome — with Minor Illusion, a borrowed coat, and the working theory that a traveling Forest Mystic willing to provide genuine-seeming encounters with the natural world was an underserved market in the human settlements to the south. He was correct. He spent the following decades refining the performance: consultations with forest spirits, blessing ceremonies for new fields and marriages, interpretations of signs and portents, guided encounters with nature's wisdom that involved very good illusions and well-timed sound effects. He was careful, consistent, and almost never wrong about what people needed to hear. The Forgery Kit was for credentials — testimonials from Forest Councils that may or may not have existed, certification from Gnomish sanctums he may or may not have visited.

The Speak with Animals — the real one, the gnomish birthright — he had always kept quietly out of the performance, unwilling to cheapen it.

#### The Patron

The patron arrived approximately one year ago. Or, more precisely, made itself apparent one year ago; Cognodec has since developed the suspicion that it had been present considerably longer, watching the performance and finding it entertaining.

He woke one morning to find his working notebook replaced by something that was not a notebook — a bound thing that felt different in each hand, that opened to different pages depending on his mood, that contained three cantrips he had not learned through any normal process and the beginnings of two rituals written in an ink he hadn't supplied. A voice in a dream gave a name he suspects is an alias. The power that followed was unmistakably real, which was both remarkable and, he will admit in private, slightly alarming.

He has since formed the working theory that the patron found him funny. He is not entirely certain this is a comfortable thing to be. He refers to the patron as *the Green Advocate* in his notes and in his performances, and has begun to wonder whether naming a thing gives it a kind of reality it didn't previously have.

#### The Wrongness

He first noticed it six weeks ago — the animals speaking differently. Not in vocabulary (animals have limited vocabulary and heavy concerns) but in tenor. Fear without object. Displacement without destination. He has investigated in the way he investigates everything: carefully, from angles that do not look like investigation, assembling small fragments into a picture he cannot quite yet see.

His Nature training, which began as professional credentialing and became genuine knowledge somewhere in his third decade on the road, tells him that what is happening falls outside normal seasonal disturbance. His Investigation instincts tell him it is spreading. His Speak with Animals, which was never a performance, tells him the animals cannot say what they are running from — only that something is wrong in the deep places, and that it has been wrong for longer than six weeks.

The Book seems heavier lately. The patron has not commented.

#### The Party

He has found himself in proximity to people who are also noticing. This is either coincidence or it isn't, and Cognodec is professionally suspicious of coincidences that arrive at useful moments.

He has not yet told any of them exactly how much of his history is performed and how much is real. He is still working this out for himself. He is, cautiously and in ways he is still becoming accustomed to, glad of the company. People who are looking at the same problem from different angles tend to produce better answers than people looking at it alone.

He considers this observation to be genuine. He is fairly certain.
