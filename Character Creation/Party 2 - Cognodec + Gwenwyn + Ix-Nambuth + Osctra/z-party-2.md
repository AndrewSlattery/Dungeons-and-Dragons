## 8. Notes & Backstory

### Appearance

Cognodec stands at three feet and four inches, which he has spent several decades treating as a precision instrument. He is narrow-shouldered and bright-eyed, with amber irises common to certain Forest Gnome bloodlines — though he has, in certain towns and certain lights, implied they are the result of a blessing. He has never corrected the impression. His complexion is the warm brown of forest soil, and his hair is a dark grey that has always been dark grey; he was born with it, his grandmother had the same, and he considers it distinguished.

He dresses in fine clothes in deep greens and dark copper tones, with small embroidered acorns along the cuffs that he added himself. His coat is technically two sizes too large for him, falls to his knees, and he considers this a feature rather than a defect. The orb that serves as his arcane focus is cradled in a sling of his own construction, nestled at his shoulder and visible above the coat collar, catching light in ways that are not entirely explicable. His Book of Shadows is bound in leather tooled to resemble bark, with brass fittings that appear to shift position if you look away from them. He carries it pressed to his side with the ease of long habit.

He looks, in total, exactly like a Forest Gnome performing the role of Forest Gnome Mystic. The unsettling detail is that he now is one.

### Personality Traits

- He will freely tell you he is a charlatan. This is itself a form of misdirection, and he knows it, and will tell you that too if you ask the right question. No one ever does.
- When he communicates with animals, he uses the same theatrical murmur he developed for the performance. The animals don't seem to mind. Some appear to find it reassuring.

### Ideal

**The Right Story.** He has spent his career giving people the story they needed — sometimes a comfort, sometimes a con. He is beginning to suspect that what is wrong with the world right now does not yet have its right story, and that finding one, or building one, may be the most genuinely useful thing he has ever done.

### Bond

His patron: a Fey lord or lady whose name he received in a dream, who may have given him the wrong name, and whom he has referred to in performance as *the Green Advocate* for long enough that he is no longer entirely certain that isn't accurate. He does not know whether the patron is fond of him, testing him, or simply amused. He has noticed that whenever something is wrong in the forest, the Book seems heavier than it should be.

### Flaw

He is excellent at appearing to understand things he does not understand, which has occasionally prevented him from admitting — to himself as much as to others — when he is genuinely lost.

### Backstory

#### Origin

Cognodec was the fourth of seven children in a Forest Gnome community tucked into the edge-wood between two larger forests — the sort of settlement that appears in winter slightly differently than in summer, and which most outsiders pass without noticing. He was not the most magical of his siblings, nor the most woodwise. He was, however, by far the most watchful, and from early childhood he had a talent for understanding what people expected to see.

He left at forty-three — young for a Gnome — with Minor Illusion, a borrowed coat, and the working theory that a traveling Forest Mystic willing to provide genuine-seeming encounters with the natural world was an underserved market in the human settlements to the south. He was correct. He spent the following decades refining the performance: consultations with forest spirits, blessing ceremonies for new fields and marriages, interpretations of signs and portents, guided encounters with nature's wisdom that involved very good illusions and well-timed sound effects. He was careful, consistent, and almost never wrong about what people needed to hear. The Forgery Kit was for credentials — testimonials from Forest Councils that may or may not have existed, certification from Gnomish sanctums he may or may not have visited.

The Speak with Animals — the real one, the gnomish birthright — he had always kept quietly out of the performance, unwilling to cheapen it.

#### The Patron

The patron arrived approximately one year ago. Or, more precisely, made itself apparent one year ago; Cognodec has since developed the suspicion that it had been present considerably longer, watching the performance and finding it entertaining.

He woke one morning to find his working notebook replaced by something that was not a notebook — a bound thing that felt different in each hand, that opened to different pages depending on his mood, that contained three cantrips he had not learned through any normal process and the beginnings of two rituals written in an ink he hadn't supplied. A voice in a dream gave a name he suspects is an alias. The power that followed was unmistakably real, which was both remarkable and, he will admit in private, slightly alarming.

He has since formed the working theory that the patron found him funny. He is not entirely certain this is a comfortable thing to be. He refers to the patron as *the Green Advocate* in his notes and in his performances, and has begun to wonder whether naming a thing gives it a kind of reality it didn't previously have.

#### The Wrongness

He first noticed it six weeks ago — the animals speaking differently. Not in vocabulary (animals have limited vocabulary and heavy concerns) but in tenor. Fear without object. Displacement without destination. He has investigated in the way he investigates everything: carefully, from angles that do not look like investigation, assembling small fragments into a picture he cannot quite yet see.

His Nature training, which began as professional credentialing and became genuine knowledge somewhere in his third decade on the road, tells him that what is happening falls outside normal seasonal disturbance. His Investigation instincts tell him it is spreading. His Speak with Animals, which was never a performance, tells him the animals cannot say what they are running from — only that something is wrong in the deep places, and that it has been wrong for longer than six weeks.

The Book seems heavier lately. The patron has not commented.

#### The Party

He has found himself in proximity to people who are also noticing. This is either coincidence or it isn't, and Cognodec is professionally suspicious of coincidences that arrive at useful moments.

He has not yet told any of them exactly how much of his history is performed and how much is real. He is still working this out for himself. He is, cautiously and in ways he is still becoming accustomed to, glad of the company. People who are looking at the same problem from different angles tend to produce better answers than people looking at it alone.

He considers this observation to be genuine. He is fairly certain.

She has dark, quick eyes and her brown hair is kept in tight, practical braids — usually pinned up under a battered wide-brimmed traveling hat that she treats with a superstitious regularity, straightening it and tapping the brim before crossing bridges. Her clothes are layered traveler's browns and faded greens, adorned with small road-collected tokens: dried seedpods strung at her collar, a red thread around one wrist, a wooden button she claims came from somewhere interesting and won't specify further.

She carries a shawm slung across her back — an instrument that, when played, sounds like a cheerful argument between a goose and a trumpet — and two polished hardwood clubs at her belt that she has never once introduced as weapons. They are, she will tell you if asked, walking sticks. She uses them, when not otherwise occupied, as walking sticks.

### Personality Traits

- She answers questions sideways — appearing to change the subject, and you realise three sentences later that she answered it completely.
- She is constitutionally incapable of passing a sleeping animal without first confirming it is all right. This adds time to every journey.

### Ideal

**Passing It On.** Every village on her circuit knows something the next one doesn't — about the road, about the weather, about what's coming. She plays music and moves news. It's the same thing, done differently.

### Bond

The old herbalist woman who taught her to listen. She died two winters ago somewhere on the eastern road. Gwenwyn still sometimes catches herself starting sentences addressed to her.

### Flaw

She has excellent luck, in the specific way that makes superstitious people uncomfortable. She has noticed this and occasionally exploits it, which is worse.

### Backstory

Gwenwyn was born on a cart — she says this literally, though she doesn't mean the farm cart her mother happened to be riding at the time was going anywhere interesting. She doesn't remember her parents with much clarity. What she remembers is city streets, learning which doors to knock on and which to read from a distance, and then — eventually, gratefully — the road.

She fell in with traveling performers young, then with a wandering herbalist who changed the shape of things. The old woman spoke to plants when she harvested them, apologised to animals she had to put down, and maintained that the world had a great deal to say if you would stop making noise long enough to hear it. Gwenwyn absorbed this selectively: she speaks to animals constantly, and plays the shawm at a volume that would embarrass a battlefield. But something from those years took root — a sense of the land as a thing that communicates, a feeling for when it's speaking wrongly.

For the past two years she has been working a circuit of rural settlements — seasonal fairs, harvest suppers, midwinter gatherings. She plays music people can dance to, passes news from village to village, and has accumulated a comprehensive mental map of which roads are safe at which seasons, which innkeepers are worth the coin, and which estates are best given a wide berth. It is a good life, and one she has become quietly expert at.

**The Wrongness**

She first noticed something amiss in the wildlife three months ago. Prey moving the wrong direction. Predators appearing far outside their usual ranges. The land saying something she couldn't quite parse. She noted it and kept moving, telling herself it would resolve.

It did not resolve.

**The Road**

Then the story caught up with her — a Wood Elf farmer, decades of careful work destroyed by a lord's hunting party, a modest bounty on his head, and apparently a talent for reading the same wrongness she'd been noticing from her own angle.

Gwenwyn is good at finding people who don't want to be found. She is also good at being present when they grudgingly admit they could use someone. She considers these related skills.

She is keeping notes. The old herbalist would have wanted her to keep notes.

## 8. Notes & Backstory

### Appearance

Ix-Nambuth is six foot three of deliberate contradiction. She has the broad shoulders, grey-green skin, prominent lower canines, and general architectural scale of someone built to end arguments — and she carries a viol case on her back and a quarterstaff in her hand, which means she always looks simultaneously like she is going to perform and like she is going to hit something, and the ambiguity is entirely intentional.

Her hair is dark and kept in a single thick braid, wound with a strip of leather tooled with a pattern she carved herself one winter and has never explained. Her hands are large — noticeably so even on an Orc of her size — but anyone who watches her play notices the precision with which she holds the bow, and the contrast tends to produce a specific quality of silence in a room that she has found professionally useful. She wears two costumes for performing: one very plain, one very not. She alternates them with a strategic sense of occasion.

Her eyes are unexpectedly warm. This catches people off guard, and she has long since decided to let it.

### Personality Traits

- She is quieter than people expect. An Orc who plays the viol has learned to let the dissonance work for her before she opens her mouth.
- She notices small beautiful things with the exact, unsentimental attention of a craftsperson — a chord that resolves unexpectedly, the particular quality of late afternoon light, a bird doing something technically interesting. She does not comment on these observations. She just stops for a moment, and then continues.

### Ideal

**Presence.** A village where the fair has been cancelled, where the harvest supper went unplayed, where the fire went unlit — that is not a small thing. Joy is not decoration. She treats its absence with the same seriousness other people reserve for war.

### Bond

Zib Tumbletock — an elderly Gnomish musician who heard her humming one afternoon in a market, told her she had terrible technique and extraordinary instinct, and then spent four years correcting the former. Still alive, she believes. She tries not to worry about this with any regularity.

### Flaw

She assumes that being present communicates that she cares. She is consistently surprised when it doesn't, and consistently too late to say it another way.

### Backstory

Ix-Nambuth grew up in the kind of place that doesn't appear on careful maps — a borderland settlement, mixed enough that nobody quite belonged to anything, which meant everyone had to decide for themselves what they were. She decided early on that she was not going to be what people assumed when they looked at her, and she has been revising the specifics of that decision ever since.

She found her way into performance because music crossed borders that her appearance didn't. She was seventeen when she first played for coin — a drum, at a market, badly — and something in the response of the crowd told her that this was worth pursuing with more seriousness than she had applied to anything else. She pursued it with considerable seriousness.

Zib Tumbletock found her four years later, corrected her technique, gave her a second-hand viol, and introduced her to the idea that music was not a tool for crossing borders but a thing worth doing for its own sake, because beauty was a serious undertaking and the world required more of it, not less. She absorbed this completely. Zib is the reason she speaks Gnomish, and the reason she plays the way she does, and one of approximately three reasons she is not a completely different person.

She has been working a circuit of rural settlements for the past several years — festivals, harvest suppers, the winter gatherings that keep isolated communities from going entirely dark during the long months. She is known in these places. She is not always welcomed warmly at first, but she is always welcomed back.

**The Oath**

The oath did not come to her from a temple. She has never been inside a temple she found convincing.

It came to her three years ago in a forest clearing, on a night when she had stopped to sleep between villages and woken to find something watching her — not a creature, exactly. More like a quality of attention. Ancient, unhurried, and entirely certain about what it was doing.

She did not speak any vow she can recall. She woke the next morning understanding one had been made. She found this theologically irregular and has never fully resolved it, but the power is real, and the weight of what it asks of her is real, and on balance she has decided that whatever claimed her had reasonable taste.

**The Wrongness**

She first noticed it at a fair that wasn't. She arrived at a village on the expected date to find the green empty — no stalls, no fire, no music. Animals on the northern road, she was told. Attacking without cause or pattern. It wasn't safe.

She had heard similar things from the last two villages. And the one before that. Something was moving through the land and removing, one by one, exactly the things the oath had charged her to protect.

She is taking this personally.

**The Road**

She has heard, since then, of a Wood Elf farmer with a bounty on his head and a field journal full of observations about predators moving wrong. She considers this worth investigating. She is also fairly certain that somewhere on the same roads there is a young woman with a shawm who plays some of the same circuits she does, and who has probably noticed the same silences.

She is not, by nature, a person who travels in groups. She is reconsidering.

## 7. Notes & Backstory

### Appearance

Osctra does not look like a traditional Elf. He has traded ethereal grace for raw, sun-baked mass. At roughly 80 years old — old enough that he stopped counting with any precision — he has had an extraordinarily long time to accumulate calluses. After decades of hauling soil, digging irrigation trenches, and wrestling stubborn livestock, he is built like a brick oven: broad, thick-muscled, and covered in a permanent dusting of dirt. His skin is deeply tanned and weather-beaten, his hands thick with the calluses of decades of hard labour — marked here and there by old fertiliser stains and the faint burn scars of a glasswork accident he declines to discuss.

His dark hair, the colour of good loam, is tied back in a utilitarian knot that mostly — but never quite entirely — conceals his pointed ears. His eyes are the clear, sharp green of a healthy leaf held up to sunlight: the one detail that gives his heritage away to anyone who looks closely enough, and the one feature that doesn't seem to fit the rest of him at all.

He wears heavy, mud-stained canvas trousers, thick leather work boots, and a workman's apron worn smooth with decades of use. His "trident" is a beautifully balanced, wickedly sharp pitchfork — clearly a craftsman's tool, not a soldier's weapon. Two heavy iron framing hammers hang from his belt. His shield is the reinforced ironwood lid of a massive pickling barrel, strapped to his forearm with worn leather, and it fits there with the comfortable naturalness of something that has always been there.

He moves with the steady, unhurried efficiency of someone who has never once needed to rush. When he uses Druidcraft, there is no mystical flourish: he snaps his fingers to check the wind direction, sniffs the air to gauge soil composition, or once — to win an argument about alkalinity — made an entire hillside of wildflowers bloom mid-sentence and then went back to eating.

### Personality Traits

- He communicates in brief sentences, long silences, and the occasional pointed clearing of the throat. He considers small talk a form of agricultural waste.
- He assesses the health of every living thing he encounters as a reflex — plants, animals, and, disconcertingly, people. He will interrupt a conversation to quietly inform someone that they look malnourished.

### Ideal

**Growth.** Anything worth having takes time, patience, and the willingness to get your hands dirty. He has no patience for anyone who expects results without tending, and no respect for anything that takes without giving back.

### Bond

Decades of cultivated research. A prize herd scattered across four valleys. A greenhouse in ashes. He will rebuild every last square foot of it.

### Flaw

His contempt for authority and entitlement is so deeply ingrained that it can misfire against genuinely reasonable people simply because they happen to be wearing a title or a fine coat.

### Backstory

Osctra was born into a small, isolated Wood Elf community on the edge of a remote frontier valley. Unlike most of his kin who eventually drifted toward the ancient forests or the wider world, Osctra stayed — not out of any inability to leave, but because the valley's soil was exceptional and there was work to do.

Over the next decades, he quietly transformed a modest plot of land into something remarkable: a living laboratory of botanical cultivation, centred on a magically-enhanced strain of Orcish root tubers. The variety was known among those who knew it at all for its extraordinary yield, its remarkable shelf life, and its maddening sensitivity to grow conditions. It demanded constant attention. Osctra gave it constant attention. The arrangement suited both parties.

Decades of twice-yearly trading at the valley's foot brought him into regular contact with Orcish merchants, whose exacting quality standards — and even more exacting opinions about soil composition — he found oddly agreeable. He learned Orcish out of professional necessity; he kept speaking it because Orcish, in his considered view, has far more precise words for agricultural concepts than Elvish does. He was never social, exactly. But he was known: as a fair dealer, a patient neighbour, and someone who descended from his valley twice a year with a cartload of produce and expected to be left alone the rest of the time.

**The Snap**

For months, something had been wrong with the valley's wildlife. Predators were appearing in the wrong ranges, frightened and disoriented — driven out of their territories by something Osctra hadn't yet been able to identify. He had been noting it in his field journal with growing unease, chalking individual incidents up to seasonal disruption, telling himself it would settle.

It did not settle. A few weeks ago, an arrogant local lord and his retinue of armoured knights decided that Osctra's valley was an ideal hunting ground. They flushed one of these displaced predators — something large, frightened, and well outside its natural territory — directly through his property. The beast demolished his greenhouse. The knights demolished his fences. Their horses demolished two hundred years of carefully maintained botanical beds.

When Osctra emerged from the ruins of his glasswork and politely requested compensation, the lord laughed. He referred to him, specifically, as a "dirt-grubbing peasant."

Whatever remained of Osctra's Elven etiquette evaporated on the spot. He didn't raise his voice. He made no threats. He went very, very quiet — the kind of quiet that had the knights' hands moving toward their hilts before they fully understood why — and then he picked up his pitchfork and systematically dismantled the entire hunting party. He did not kill anyone. He didn't need to. He threw the lord bodily over his own ruined fence and explained, in a voice of absolute calm, exactly how many individual plants had been destroyed and what he expected by way of restitution.

The lord filed a complaint from his sickbed. There is now a modest bounty on Osctra's head.

**The Road**

With his farm in ruin and his herd scattered, Osctra has been forced onto the road. He is not adventuring for glory. He is adventuring because something has been driving the valley's predators out of their natural ranges for months, and he intends to find out what it is, stop it, and earn enough coin to rebuild his greenhouse. The fact that this mission incidentally involves a significant amount of violence is, in his view, an unfortunate but manageable inconvenience.

He is, however, keeping a meticulous field journal of every plant species he hasn't previously catalogued. There is no reason to let the whole enterprise go to waste.

The party of Cognodec (Warlock), Gwenwyn (Bard), Ix-Nambuth (Paladin), and Osctra (Barbarian) presents a distinctive tactical profile: **two armoured frontliners**, a support/healer behind them, and a utility caster providing range and manipulation. Unlike Party 1's single-frontliner configuration, this party can hold a wide battle line, create overlapping physical pressure, and sustain it — at the cost of less arcane depth and a narrower perception coverage. The key insight is that this party's power comes from stacking layered buffs on two capable melee characters simultaneously. Bless, Faerie Fire, and Topple coordination together produce a front rank that becomes dramatically better than either fighter alone.

---

## Combat Synergy Chains

### Chain 1: The Buff Stack — Ix-Nambuth + Gwenwyn → Osctra

The party's single most efficient combination, and the correct default opening for almost any significant fight:

**Step 1 — Ix-Nambuth: Bless** (Concentration, up to 3 creatures) → Osctra, Ix-Nambuth, and one other gain +1d4 to all attack rolls and saving throws.

**Step 2 — Gwenwyn: Faerie Fire** (Concentration, up to 20-ft Cube) → Enemies in the area that fail a DEX save shed light and grant Advantage on all attack rolls against them.

When both are active simultaneously:
- Osctra attacks with **Advantage** (Faerie Fire) and adds **+1d4** to each roll (Bless). His effective attack bonus at Level 1 is approximately +5 (STR) +1d4 (Bless) with Advantage on the full roll.
- Ix-Nambuth attacks with the same Advantage + 1d4 stack.
- Every Topple imposed via a hit benefits from this enhanced hit probability.

The cost: Ix-Nambuth concentrates on Bless; Gwenwyn concentrates on Faerie Fire. Both are unavailable for other Concentration spells. Gwenwyn cannot maintain Guidance while Faerie Fire is active; Ix-Nambuth cannot maintain any other spell. These are the correct trades to make in most combat encounters.

**Coordination rule:** Decide in advance who concentrates on what. Default: **Ix-Nambuth = Bless; Gwenwyn = Faerie Fire.** Do not have both try to run Bless, and do not let Gwenwyn switch to Sleep if Faerie Fire is already painting advantage on two enemies the frontliners are hitting.

---

### Chain 2: The Double Topple — Ix-Nambuth + Osctra → Same Target

**Ix-Nambuth hits with Quarterstaff** → Topple: CON save DC 13 or Prone.
**Osctra hits the same target with Pitchfork** → Topple: CON save DC 13 or Prone.

Two CON saves on the same creature per round. Prone:
- Sets speed to 0 (can't move without spending half movement to stand)
- Grants **Advantage** to all melee attackers within 5 ft
- Imposes **Disadvantage** on the target's own attack rolls

If either Topple application lands, both frontliners gain Advantage on that same creature for the rest of the round while it stays Prone. Against a single dangerous enemy, this is the party's most reliable control mechanism. **Call the target explicitly before the round begins.** The chain only works when both Topple weapons are pointed at the same creature.

Against a Prone target that also happens to be in the Faerie Fire zone (Advantage from the spell), the Advantage sources do not add beyond a single Advantage — but they provide insurance against the condition dropping. Even if Faerie Fire ends (Gwenwyn's Concentration breaks), the Prone condition continues to provide Advantage for melee attacks.

---

### Chain 3: The Thorn Whip Pull — Cognodec → Ix-Nambuth or Osctra

**Cognodec: Thorn Whip** → melee spell attack (+5 to hit) against an enemy that is ranging at distance or trying to back away → on a hit: 1d6 Piercing + pull target 10 ft closer.

A fleeing or ranged-distance enemy pulled 10 ft toward the front rank may enter the reach of Ix-Nambuth or Osctra, triggering their opportunity attacks. Against an enemy that has backed out of melee range (which would normally cost a frontliner's full movement to re-engage), Thorn Whip re-establishes contact without the frontliner sacrificing their movement or action.

The Thorn Whip also works against Prone targets: melee spell attacks against Prone enemies within 5 ft have Advantage. If a Double Topple has landed, Cognodec can use his action to Thorn Whip with Advantage, dealing damage and sustaining Prone if the target tries to stand and back away.

**Range note:** Thorn Whip is a 30 ft melee spell attack. Cognodec needs to be within 30 ft of the target — closer than his Eldritch Blast comfort zone. Position him behind the frontliners, at the edge of the 30 ft range, rather than at extreme Eldritch Blast distance.

---

### Chain 4: The Vicious Mockery Protect — Gwenwyn → Enemy Attacking a Concentrating Ally

**Gwenwyn: Vicious Mockery** (WIS save DC 13) → on a fail: 1d6 Psychic + Disadvantage on the target's next attack roll.

When Ix-Nambuth is concentrating on Bless and an enemy is about to swing at her, Gwenwyn's Vicious Mockery applied on her turn before the enemy's attack forces that attack into Disadvantage. A missed attack means no Concentration save. A Concentration save avoided means Bless stays up. The Disadvantage on the attack roll is therefore worth more than the direct damage — it protects a sustained buff that benefits three party members simultaneously.

Use Vicious Mockery defensively: identify the enemy most likely to break Ix-Nambuth's or Gwenwyn's own Concentration, and Mockery them before they act.

---

### Chain 5: The Intelligence Web — Cognodec + Gwenwyn + Osctra → Speak with Animals

Three party members have Speak with Animals: Cognodec (2 free/LR + Ritual), Gwenwyn (1 free/LR + Ritual), and Osctra by virtue of Animal Handling +3 (no magical speech, but can communicate intuitively with animals and read their behaviour).

This is not a combat chain — it is the party's primary investigative mechanism for the campaign's central mystery. When the party encounters displaced or frightened wildlife:

1. **Osctra** reads behaviour first (Animal Handling, Nature, field journal comparison) — no resource cost, immediate information about what they are experiencing.
2. **Gwenwyn** uses Speak with Animals if more specific information is needed and the animals are accessible — 1 free cast per LR.
3. **Cognodec** uses Speak with Animals if Gwenwyn's cast was insufficient or for a different animal encounter the same day — 2 free casts per SR (effectively limitless over a full adventuring day).
4. **Ritual fallback:** Both Cognodec and Gwenwyn can cast Speak with Animals as a Ritual (10 minutes, no slot) in camp, at rest stops, or whenever time permits.

**Coordination rule:** Do not overlap free casts on the same conversation. Agree in advance who speaks first. Reserve Ritual casts for situations where the free casts are exhausted or where 10 minutes of conversation time is available.

---

### Chain 6: The Inspiring Song Recovery — Ix-Nambuth → Short Rest

**After a Short Rest where Ix-Nambuth plays 10+ minutes of music:**

Any ally who heard her play and spent one or more Hit Dice during the rest gains **Heroic Inspiration**.

This makes every Short Rest materially better for the whole party. Heroic Inspiration (free Advantage on any one D20 roll) is valuable precisely in the moments that matter most — a saving throw against a debilitating effect, a decisive attack, a crucial skill check. The party should plan Short Rests to include the musical component whenever safe. Ix-Nambuth should always play.

**Stacking with Gwenwyn's Heroic Inspiration:** Gwenwyn (Human — Resourceful) regains Heroic Inspiration every Long Rest. She can give hers away immediately. After a Long Rest, both Gwenwyn (from Resourceful) and any Short Rest recipient (from Inspiring Song) have Heroic Inspiration. In the best case, the party enters a major encounter with three or four members all holding Heroic Inspiration.

---

## Turn Order Guidelines

At Level 1, the optimal early-round sequence is:

1. **Ix-Nambuth** (if acting early): Cast Bless on herself, Osctra, and Gwenwyn. Move toward enemies.
2. **Osctra**: Rage as Bonus Action; advance to melee range; attack with Pitchfork (Topple).
3. **Gwenwyn**: Cast Faerie Fire on the enemy cluster; Bardic Inspiration to whoever needs it next round.
4. **Cognodec**: Eldritch Blast or Thorn Whip from range depending on positioning; Minor Illusion or social utility if the encounter has a non-combat component.

In practice, initiative is random and this ideal sequence is rarely achieved. The core principle: **Bless goes up before Osctra commits to a target, and Faerie Fire goes up before the frontliners have depleted their attack resources.** If Ix-Nambuth and Gwenwyn both act before the frontliners, round one is optimised. If not, the synergy still applies on subsequent rounds once both are established.

## Exploration Synergy

### Perception Gap — The Party's Significant Limitation

This party has **no Perception proficiency among any member.** The highest passive Perception in the party is 11 (Gwenwyn and Osctra, WIS +1). This is the single most important collective vulnerability in exploration and travel. The party will miss things that a single proficient character would catch automatically.

**Mitigation strategies:**

- **Cognodec's familiar (Find Familiar ritual):** An Owl familiar with its own Perception score, active Darkvision, and silent flight can scout ahead of the party and alert Cognodec via telepathy within 100 ft. This is the closest the party gets to dedicated scouting — and it is accessible from Level 1 via ritual.
- **Osctra's Trance:** He finishes Long Rests in 4 hours while remaining conscious. He should volunteer for extended watch duty and operate as the overnight sentinel. Any threat approaching camp has at most a +0 Passive Perception of 10 to avoid being heard, but Osctra is alert.
- **Active Perception as a standing habit:** When entering new spaces, explicitly declare that characters are looking for threats rather than relying on Passive Perception. Active Perception (roll) can exceed passive even without proficiency, and all four characters should declare it at chokepoints.

### Stealth Approach

Ix-Nambuth's Chain Mail (Disadvantage on Stealth) is the hard constraint. No full-party stealth is possible.

**Scout pair:** Gwenwyn (Stealth +5) + Cognodec (Stealth +4), supplemented by Cognodec's familiar for aerial coverage. These two can advance in near-silence while Ix-Nambuth and Osctra hold position.

**Ix-Nambuth as diversion:** If a scout approach requires splitting the party, Ix-Nambuth can deliberately create legitimate noise elsewhere — music, announcement, direct approach through the front. She is credible as a travelling performer and has the social skills and costumes to support a "front door" approach while the others go quiet through a side entrance.

### Darkvision Coverage

| Character | Darkvision |
|---|---|
| Ix-Nambuth | 120 ft |
| Cognodec | 60 ft |
| Osctra | 60 ft |
| Gwenwyn | None |

Gwenwyn cannot see in darkness without a light source. In dark environments, she is dependent on the others' descriptions. Assign Ix-Nambuth as the front of the column in dark spaces (best range); Gwenwyn stays between Osctra and Ix-Nambuth with a bullseye lantern when stealth is not critical. In stealth-sensitive dark environments, Gwenwyn relies on Ix-Nambuth or Cognodec to narrate what they see.

### Wilderness and Rural Travel

Osctra leads on all wilderness navigation, terrain assessment, and animal-related investigation. Gwenwyn's knowledge of rural circuits, road conditions, innkeepers, and village dynamics complements his natural world expertise with social infrastructure knowledge. Together they form the party's travel competency: he knows what the land says; she knows what the people say about the land.

## Resource Management Between Encounters

After any significant fight, assess:

1. **Gwenwyn's slots:** If both are expended, a Long Rest is likely necessary before the next encounter. One remaining buys flexibility.
2. **Ix-Nambuth's slots:** Similarly 2 slots. If Bless was cast and sustained for multiple rounds, the slot was well spent. If Searing Smite was used — assess whether the ongoing pressure paid off.
3. **Osctra's Rages:** 2/LR; regain 1 on Short Rest. If one was used in the last fight, a Short Rest restores it. If both were used, a Long Rest is a stronger option.
4. **Gwenwyn's Bardic Inspiration:** 3/LR. Were any spent on trivial checks or attacks that didn't require them? Bank remaining dice for high-stakes moments.
5. **Cognodec's slot:** Refreshes on Short Rest. If expended and the adventuring day continues, prioritise the next Short Rest for him before Ix-Nambuth, who gets Adrenaline Rush back but has more HP to absorb the wait.

**When to Short Rest:** When Osctra has used a Rage (restores 1 Rage on SR), Cognodec has expended his slot (restores on SR), or Ix-Nambuth has used Adrenaline Rush charges (restores on SR). If two or more of these are true simultaneously, a Short Rest benefits the party significantly and Ix-Nambuth should play during it.

**When to Long Rest:** When Gwenwyn has used both healing slots; when the party's aggregate HP is substantially reduced; or at the end of a full adventuring day. Gwenwyn going into a serious fight without Healing Word is the party's most urgent resource crisis — even more urgent than Osctra being out of Rages, because the frontliners cannot sustain through Lay on Hands alone.
# Party Dynamics — Analysis

---

## Overview

Four characters, all Chaotic Good, with four different relationships to deception, duty, beauty, and patience. Party 1's defining tension was between competing alignments — the Lawful Good cleric and the True Neutral wizard generating real moral friction. This party's alignment uniformity creates a different kind of drama: they generally agree on what should be done, but they disagree, sometimes sharply, on how it should be done, who should say so, and who can be trusted to execute it. The absence of a Lawful character means no one is automatically the conscience; the absence of a selfish character means the party's debates are not about whether to help, but about how and at what cost to their own methods. This is a more internally coherent party, and in some ways a more dangerous one — a party that agrees on ends can take actions that a more fractious group would argue themselves out of.

---

## Alignment & Motivation at a Glance

| Character | Alignment | Core Motivation |
|---|---|---|
| Cognodec | Chaotic Good | Finding and telling the right story; genuine usefulness after a lifetime of performed usefulness |
| Gwenwyn | Chaotic Good | Passing things on; moving information and music through the world as a form of care |
| Ix-Nambuth | Chaotic Good | Presence as protection; joy as a serious undertaking; an oath whose source she cannot fully explain |
| Osctra | Chaotic Good | Growth; rebuilding what was destroyed; understanding what is breaking the system he has tended for decades |

**The central dynamic:** All four are Chaotic Good, but they arrived there from different starting points and through different means. Cognodec's goodness is recent, genuine, and slightly alarming to him. Gwenwyn's is practical and unsentimental. Ix-Nambuth's is structured around an oath she didn't choose consciously. Osctra's is rooted in a philosophy of reciprocity — things that take without giving back are the enemy, and he applies this standard broadly. These are not identical worldviews, and the friction between them is worth playing.

**The shared urgency:** Unusually for an adventuring party, all four members have independently noticed the same wrongness. Osctra has the most systematic documentation. Gwenwyn has noticed it from the road perspective. Ix-Nambuth has watched it erase what she is sworn to protect. Cognodec has heard it in the animals' fear and felt it in the weight of his Book. They came together because they were all looking at the same problem from different angles, and that convergence is either coincidence or it isn't, and none of them have fully settled which.

---

## Pairwise Dynamics

### Osctra + Ix-Nambuth — The Farmer and the Performer

**The dynamic:** Two people built for the same environment — rural circuits, seasonal communities, physical presence — who have almost nothing in common in how they occupy it.

Osctra goes to harvest suppers and fair days to trade. He wants accurate weight measurements, fair exchange, and to be left alone thereafter. Ix-Nambuth goes to harvest suppers and fair days to make them worth attending. She wants the fire lit and the music playing and the people dancing, because a cancelled fair is a village that has gone dark in a way she takes personally. They have almost certainly been at the same events. They have almost certainly never spoken.

**The initial friction:** Osctra's contempt for entitlement and title can misfire. Ix-Nambuth has a kind of authority — not from rank, but from oath, from presence, from the quality of a person who has decided what she is and acts accordingly. In an Orc this can read, to a Wood Elf farmer who has spent eighty years developing precise scepticism about large people with strong opinions, as exactly the kind of confidence he mistrusts. He is wrong about this, and he will eventually know it. The question is what makes him look more carefully.

**What builds the relationship:** They share Orcish, and they share a physical world-view — both assess situations by what is present and what is missing, not by what has been said. When Osctra watches Ix-Nambuth at a Short Rest, playing for the party with the particular unsentimental attention of a craftsperson, not performing but making something real — he will recognise the species of care, even if the expression is alien to him. She is tending the party the way he tends a field. Different crop; same principle.

**Alignment arithmetic:** Both Chaotic Good. Both distrust authority structures. Both trust demonstrated competence and genuine presence. Their friction is aesthetic and personal, not moral. They will not fight about what is right; they will occasionally want to go about it differently, and Ix-Nambuth will assume her presence communicates things that Osctra will need said explicitly.

---

### Osctra + Gwenwyn — The Farmer and the Wayfarer

**The dynamic:** Prior acquaintance from the same rural circuit, a shared observation, and a mutual respect for quiet competence.

Gwenwyn works the same roads Osctra descends to trade. There is a reasonable chance they have met at a harvest supper or winter gathering before — she plays; he sells; both end up near the fire by the end of the evening. He would remember her as the one with the shawm. She would remember him as the enormous, silent Elf farmer who left immediately after finishing his business and who seemed, despite this, to be paying close attention to everything.

**The shared observation:** Both noticed the wrongness in the wildlife months ago. Gwenwyn kept moving and kept notes; Osctra stayed and kept notes. They are the party's two earliest documenters of the phenomenon, and when they compare observations they will find that what they have been tracking is the same event from different vantage points. This produces a particular kind of trust that precedes personal relationship — the trust of two people who have independently verified the same fact.

**What the relationship looks like:** Gwenwyn answers questions sideways and completely. Osctra communicates in brief sentences and long silences. In another context this might produce frustration; in practice, they understand each other because they are both people who have learned to read what is present in what isn't said. She notes that he asks the right questions. He notes that she answers them. Neither of them needs to comment on this arrangement for it to function.

**Tension:** Gwenwyn moves and Osctra plants. She considers information as something to be passed on; he considers it as something to be verified and documented before use. When the party needs to make a decision quickly on incomplete information, she is more comfortable than he is with acting on the available evidence. He will push back when he thinks the information is insufficient. She is usually right that they need to move; he is usually right that they need to know more. They will not always resolve this tension cleanly.

---

### Osctra + Cognodec — The Farmer and the Charlatan

**The dynamic:** Two people with deep nature knowledge from entirely different angles, who communicate in entirely different registers, and who will find — slowly and with mutual surprise — that they are asking the same questions.

Osctra is what he is: a Wood Elf farmer of eight decades, not performing anything, communicating exactly what he means in as few words as possible. Cognodec has spent decades being what people needed him to be and is only recently learning which parts of that were real. They are in superficially opposite positions — one defined by the complete absence of artifice, the other by an elaborate construction of it — and yet their current relationship to their own identity is more similar than it appears. Osctra has been growing things that outlast him; Cognodec has been finding, for the first time, that something he built may be genuine.

**The friction:** Osctra's bullshit-detection is finely calibrated by eighty years of trading with Orcish merchants who know their weights. Cognodec is excellent at appearing to understand things he does not, and Osctra will notice this — the theatrical murmur, the assured manner, the convenient expertise — in ways that Cognodec is not used to being noticed. He cannot quite perform for Osctra the way he performs for others, because Osctra is looking at what is actually there rather than what is being presented.

**What builds the relationship:** Cognodec's Nature +3 is professional credentialing that became real knowledge over three decades of working the circuit as a forest mystic. Osctra's Nature +2 is eight decades of agricultural observation. When they compare notes on what they have observed — Osctra's meticulous field journal, Cognodec's careful pattern-reading from a different angle — they find that the expertise is genuine on both sides, even if one came to it honestly and one came to it sideways. Osctra respects competence. He will come to respect Cognodec's, grudgingly and without saying so, when the evidence is sufficient.

**Languages:** They share no private language. Osctra speaks Elvish and Orcish; Cognodec speaks Gnomish, Common, and Common Sign Language. They always operate in Common around each other. This is, in Cognodec's taxonomy, a form of exposure that he finds mildly unnerving.

---

### Ix-Nambuth + Gwenwyn — The Viol and the Shawm

**The dynamic:** Two musicians who work the same circuit, with entirely different aesthetics and almost certainly prior acquaintance. A professional overlap that produces mutual recognition before personal connection.

Ix-Nambuth plays the viol because beauty is a serious undertaking. Gwenwyn plays the shawm because music moves people the way news moves people — it connects things. They have almost certainly performed at the same events, and almost certainly not collaborated. Ix-Nambuth plays things that produce silence in a room; Gwenwyn plays things people can dance to. These are not incompatible, but they are distinct.

**The recognition:** Both have noticed the fairs that weren't. Both have arrived at empty greens. When they first compare these observations — Gwenwyn's road perspective and Ix-Nambuth's performer's perspective — they will find not just overlap but a kind of mirroring: they were tracking the same absences. The absence of music, of people gathered, of the things both of them separately exist to provide.

**The relationship:** Gwenwyn's quick, sideways intelligence and Ix-Nambuth's precise, careful attention produce an interesting dynamic. Ix-Nambuth is quieter than people expect and more observant than she lets on. Gwenwyn is more observant than she appears and more strategic about what she reveals. Each of them would, quietly, respect the other's intelligence before fully trusting it. This is a relationship that takes time to deepen, but the foundation of professional respect and shared observation is there from the start.

**Tension:** Ix-Nambuth assumes that being present communicates that she cares. Gwenwyn, who reads people well, will notice when this doesn't land — will notice the moments when Ix-Nambuth has done the right thing and then not said the right words and cannot understand why the room has not adjusted. Gwenwyn will sometimes supply the words Ix-Nambuth doesn't know she needs. Ix-Nambuth will notice this eventually and find it, simultaneously, unnecessary and slightly relieving.

---

### Ix-Nambuth + Cognodec — The Oath-Holder and the Charlatan

**The dynamic:** A shared language (Gnomish) that creates private access, and a fundamental difference in how each of them relates to what is real.

Ix-Nambuth received an oath she doesn't fully understand from a source she cannot name, and has accepted it because the power is real and the weight of what it asks is right. Cognodec received a patron he doesn't fully understand in a name he suspects is an alias, and has accepted it because the power is real and the performance he built around it may have become genuine. They are both people who have been claimed by something they did not choose and cannot fully explain, and who have decided to honour it anyway.

**The Gnomish thread:** Ix-Nambuth learned Gnomish from Zib Tumbletock. Cognodec speaks it as a birthright. Their private conversations — the ones that happen in Gnomish, away from the others — have a different texture than their Common exchanges. Cognodec uses the same theatrical register in Gnomish that he uses in everything else, but Ix-Nambuth's Gnomish is precise and slightly formal, Zib's influence apparent in every sentence. She will notice when his Gnomish is too fluent in some registers and oddly stilted in others — the tells of someone who learned a language late enough to have gaps. She doesn't comment. She has decided to wait and see.

**The tension:** Ix-Nambuth is the person in the party most likely to ask Cognodec a direct question. Not confrontationally — she is not confrontational — but with the specific quality of attention she gives to small, technically interesting things. When she asks him about the patron, she will use the word the way she uses Gnomish vocabulary: precisely, not as a performance opener. He will give his standard answer, and she will listen to it, and she will not say what she has noticed about it. They will have this conversation several times over the course of the campaign, each version slightly more honest than the last.

---

### Gwenwyn + Cognodec — The Road and the Performance

**The dynamic:** Two social operators, two performance-based personas, one significant difference: hers is real.

Gwenwyn passes news and plays music for people who need connection. Cognodec performs forest mysticism for people who need comfort. Both of them have developed a sensitivity to what people need to hear and a craft for delivering it. The difference is that Gwenwyn's circuit is genuinely functional — she does pass news, she does connect communities, the music is real — while Cognodec's was, for decades, a constructed service backed by good illusions and well-timed sound effects.

**The recognition (and its complications):** Gwenwyn notices things. She will notice that Cognodec is very good at appearing to understand things he does not. She will notice the theatrical murmur, the precision-crafted persona, the moments when his expertise is real and the moments when it is impersonated confidence. She is not hostile to any of this — she uses Disguise Self herself and answers questions sideways for a reason — but she will not be fooled by it the way others sometimes are.

What she will also notice, because she pays attention: the moments when it stops being a performance. When he speaks with an animal and there is no theater in it. When the Book is heavier and he doesn't say so but she can tell. When he gives a genuine answer to the right question and then seems slightly surprised by it.

**What holds the relationship:** They are the party's two social engines, and they are better together than alone. Cognodec leads; Gwenwyn reads the room and adjusts. She passes observations to him through small gestures and sideways comments; he incorporates them without acknowledging them openly. The party's best social performances happen when both are present and both are paying attention. They have developed an informal shorthand in Common that operates at a speed the others don't always follow.

**The fundamental question Gwenwyn will eventually ask Cognodec:** Not "are you a real mystic?" — she has already concluded, in her quiet way, that he is something, and that the Book and the patron and the animals' responses are evidence enough. The question she will ask eventually is "what story are you telling yourself now?" She asks questions sideways; she always means exactly that; and the answer will be the most honest thing he has said in three decades.

---

## Group Dynamics

### The Deliberation Pattern

When the party must make a collective decision under pressure, they tend to break as follows:

| Option | Who Supports It |
|---|---|
| Act immediately with available information | Gwenwyn (usually); Cognodec (if the story requires it) |
| Wait for better information | Osctra (usually); Ix-Nambuth (if the oath-stakes are unclear) |
| The unconventional approach that sidesteps the apparent choice | Cognodec (enthusiastically); Gwenwyn (if it involves useful misdirection) |
| The approach that requires being physically present in the difficult place | Ix-Nambuth (always); Osctra (once committed) |

**There is no obvious swing vote.** Party 1 had Rixon as its swing; this party is more distributed. The party's decisions emerge from a genuine four-way negotiation rather than a 2v2 split with a casting vote. This makes them slower to decide and more likely to find unexpected solutions — and occasionally more likely to deadlock until something external forces the issue.

### What Holds the Party Together

Despite the differences in style, history, and approach, this party shares three things that make it genuinely functional:

**A shared subject matter.** They are all experts, from different angles, in the same territory: rural communities, woodland ecosystems, the rhythms of seasonal life. When they compare notes they find that they have been observing the same wrongness for months from different vantage points, and this shared evidence creates a foundation that is stronger than personal affection.

**Respect for demonstrated competence.** Not one of them arrived at their expertise through institutional channels. Osctra grew it across eight decades of observation. Ix-Nambuth was trained by a demanding Gnomish craftsman. Gwenwyn learned from a wandering herbalist and two years of hard road. Cognodec's expertise was initially constructed but became real through decades of practice. None of them mistake credentials for capability, and none of them dismiss capability because it arrived through unorthodox routes.

**A shared Chaotic Good baseline.** They agree, without needing to discuss it, that the rules of the road are advisory and the protection of living things is not. None of them will watch a community go dark without trying to prevent it. None of them will accept a lord's authority as a reason to stop doing what needs to be done. When the party faces a decision with a moral dimension, the shape of their disagreement is usually about method, not conclusion.

### What Is Different From Party 1

The absence of a Lawful character removes the internal compass that challenged the party's more pragmatic choices. Cade in Party 1 would push back — clearly, without flinching — when the party drifted toward expediency at the expense of principle. This party has no equivalent. They are all, individually, capable of principled choices; they are all, individually, capable of chaotic workarounds. There is no one whose role is to stop and say: *"But is this actually right?"* They will need to develop that function collectively, or find that they occasionally miss it.

The absence of a selfish character means the party will sometimes overspend on doing the right thing. There is no one calculating costs. Cognodec is the closest approximation — he weighs information like an asset — but his current trajectory is toward genuine usefulness rather than transactional calculation. The party will occasionally need someone to notice that they cannot afford to do the next right thing without resources they haven't secured yet.

### Flashpoints

The scenarios most likely to produce genuine intra-party tension:

- **Osctra's contempt for authority misfires on a genuinely reasonable authority figure**, creating a political problem that the others — especially Gwenwyn and Cognodec, who depend on good relations with NPCs — have to manage
- **Cognodec's deception is used on someone the others consider part of the party's community** — a village they have come to care about, an NPC who has extended trust — and the others must decide whether the deception was justified by outcome
- **Ix-Nambuth is present and present and present and still cannot communicate what she feels** in a moment when the words would have changed something, and Gwenwyn or Osctra notices and has to decide whether to supply what she couldn't
- **The patron acts through Cognodec in a way that is clearly deliberate and clearly beyond what he expected**, and the party must decide what it means that he is a conduit for something with its own agenda
- **The wrongness escalates before the party has enough information to act**, and Gwenwyn's instinct to move with available evidence runs directly into Osctra's insistence on understanding what they are facing before committing — and the delay, or the premature action, has consequences
- **Someone asks Cognodec the right question and gets a genuine answer**, and the party must recalibrate what they thought they knew about him and what that changes going forward
# Cognodec — Strategic Analysis

---

## Role

**Social Operative / Utility Caster / Ranged Support**

Cognodec is the party's information broker, social engine, and battlefield disruptor. He is not a damage dealer — his cantrips are reliable rather than spectacular — but he is the reason the party learns things other parties don't, navigates social encounters no one else can crack, and has options (pull an enemy into melee, summon a scout familiar, detect magic ritually) that never show up on a straightforward class audit. The key to playing him well is understanding that his greatest power is *off the battlefield*, and that in combat his job is to apply pressure from range, reposition enemies for the frontliners, and keep his one spell slot in reserve until it genuinely matters.

---

## Combat Priorities

### The One Spell Slot

Cognodec has a single Level 1 Warlock slot, which refreshes on a Short or Long Rest. This is dramatically different from every other caster in the party, who all have Long Rest slots. The implication: **his slot is not a daily resource, it is an encounter resource** — in a typical adventuring day with Short Rests, he can use it more freely than Gwenwyn can use her two. However, within a single fight, it is still one decision:

| Slot Choice | Use When |
|---|---|
| **Armor of Agathys** *(pre-combat)* | Enemies are likely to close on him; he wants the 5 THP buffer and the 5 Cold retaliatory damage |
| **Hellish Rebuke** *(reactive)* | He expects to stay at range; saves the slot for a Reaction after being hit — 2d10 Fire damage is exceptional value on a Reaction |

**The core principle:** Never enter a fight where he expects melee contact without deciding in advance which choice to make. If uncertain, Armor of Agathys is the safer default — it goes up before damage lands. Hellish Rebuke is higher-ceiling but requires him to be hit first, which is a worse position.

### Cantrip Selection

He has six cantrips. Most rounds he will choose between:

| Cantrip | When to Use |
|---|---|
| **Eldritch Blast** | Default ranged damage, 120 ft, 1d10 Force — consistent, reliable, no resource cost |
| **Sorcerous Burst** | When he wants burst potential: 1d8 (chosen type), chain on an 8 (up to +3 bonus d8s from CHA). Against a wounded enemy or when maximising one round of output matters |
| **Thorn Whip** | When pulling a fleeing, ranging, or awkwardly-positioned enemy 10 ft toward the frontliners is more valuable than raw damage. 30 ft melee spell attack — requires closing range relative to Eldritch Blast, but the repositioning value is frequently decisive |
| **Minor Illusion** | Out-of-combat pressure, misdirection, creating sounds across a room, distracting guards. Rarely the combat choice but don't overlook it in tactical creativity |

**Eldritch Blast vs. Sorcerous Burst:** Eldritch Blast averages 5.5 on a hit with 1d10; Sorcerous Burst averages 4.5 on a hit with 1d8, but with a ~12.5% chance of chaining into a second d8 and a small chance of further chains. At Level 1, they are close — favour Eldritch Blast for reliability, Sorcerous Burst when an enemy is low and you want to finish them this round.

**Thorn Whip as a tactical tool:** The pull triggers on any hit, which means it requires a melee spell attack at +5 rather than the ranged spell attack at +5 of Eldritch Blast. Against a creature Prone (from Ix-Nambuth or Osctra's Topple), melee attacks — including Thorn Whip — have Advantage. This synergy is worth noting: Topple someone Prone, then Thorn Whip with Advantage to drag them back into the frontliners' reach.

---

## Spell Usage

**Armor of Agathys:** The correct pre-combat self-buff when he expects to be targeted. 5 Temporary Hit Points act as a buffer; any creature that hits him while those THP persist takes 5 Cold damage automatically. Against enemies that attack in clusters or close to melee, this is better value than a reactive spell because it effectively taxes the first attack against him regardless of what Cognodec is doing on his own turns. Cast it before combat when possible — it doesn't require concentration and lasts 1 hour.

**Hellish Rebuke:** A Reaction, meaning it doesn't cost his action or movement. When he does get hit, this turns the attack into a 2d10 Fire damage counterpunch (DEX save DC 13 for half). Against enemies with mediocre DEX saves, this averages around 7 damage at no cost to his action economy. Reserve this if he genuinely expects to be at range and have Eldritch Blast/Sorcerous Burst handling his damage needs; use Armor of Agathys if he thinks he will be in the thick of it.

**Speak with Animals (2 free/LR; also Ritual):** This is the party's most important investigative tool for the central threat. Three party members have access; Cognodec should coordinate with Gwenwyn on use to avoid wasting both their free casts on the same conversation. Prioritise the ritual version for unhurried situations (camp, a quiet moment in town) and the free casts when encountering animals mid-travel. Any frightened or displaced beast is worth interrogating — they know more than they can articulate, and Cognodec knows how to read what they cannot say.

---

## Ritual Spells

These cost no spell slot. They cost 10 minutes of casting. Use them constantly.

**Find Familiar:** The single most powerful strategic investment Cognodec can make in any extended situation. An Owl familiar specifically has: Flyby (no opportunity attacks), exceptional Perception, silent flight, and 120 ft Darkvision. Use it to scout rooms before the party enters, monitor approaches while they sleep, observe guard patterns, and deliver touch spells (when Cognodec eventually gains any). The 10 GP incense cost is significant at Level 1 — factor it into resource planning, but do not delay casting it once funds allow. The familiar can communicate what it senses telepathically within 100 ft. This is the party's stealth scout, replacing what Mall-Lowick (not in this party) would do on foot.

**Detect Magic:** 10-minute ritual, Concentration for up to 10 minutes. Senses magical effects within 30 ft and allows identifying schools of magic on visible objects. Cast whenever the party enters an unfamiliar space and wants to audit it for enchantments, hidden magic items, or active spell effects. It requires Concentration while active — don't start it mid-fight.

---

## Key Traits in Play

**Gnomish Cunning:** Advantage on Intelligence, Wisdom, and Charisma saving throws. At Level 1, the most common save-based threats are enchantment (WIS), illusion (INT), and social manipulation (CHA). Cognodec resists all of these with Advantage, making him the party's most resilient mind against magical interference. When the party is walking into a situation that might involve Charm, Hold Person, Fear, or deception magic, Cognodec is the least likely to fall victim.

**Deception +5 / Persuasion +5:** These are the highest social skill modifiers in the party. Cognodec leads negotiations, talks his way past guards, establishes false identities, and manages first contact with NPCs the party needs something from. His Forgery Kit and Costumes are tools in the same toolkit — fake credentials, impersonated affiliations, assumed identities. The Charlatan background gives him Cover (fake identity documentation already in place) as a mechanical resource the DM can engage with.

**Nature +3 / Arcana +3 / Investigation +3:** He is the party's closest approximation of a scholar. None of these are exceptional by comparison to Kilda's Party 1 dominance, but they are respectable by Party 2 standards, and combined they give him competence across the intellectual skill range. He should be the default reader of inscriptions, assessor of arcane artefacts (until Detect Magic is active), and identifier of natural phenomena.

**Stealth +4 / Mage Hand:** He is a plausible scout, quieter than almost any frontliner. Mage Hand lets him manipulate objects (retrieve items from containers, open unlocked doors, pour vials) from 30 ft away without touching them — this is useful for handling suspected traps, retrieving objects from across a room, or interacting with a locked chest before Gwenwyn picks it.

**Thaumaturgy:** Performance and misdirection. Alter his eye appearance, triple the volume of his voice, cause tremors underfoot, make a door slam open or shut, create phantom sounds. In a social encounter, Thaumaturgy transforms his Forest Gnome Mystic performance into something genuinely unnerving. In a negotiation, a voice that fills the room and eyes that shift colour is worth two points of Intimidation. Use it whenever theatrical presence would help — and remember that his whole background is built on exactly this.

**Small size:** Cognodec is 3'4". He can move through the space of creatures one size larger. In combat, he can pass through human-sized enemies without stopping. He can hide behind medium-sized party members in a pinch. He occupies almost no space, can position unusually, and is generally not where enemies expect to find him.

---

## Where Cognodec Shines

- Social encounters — the party's most effective face by a meaningful margin
- Pre-combat intelligence: Speak with Animals, Detect Magic ritual, Find Familiar scouting
- Situations where deception, impersonation, or manufactured credibility solves a problem that force cannot
- Long adventuring days — his Short Rest slot recovery means he contributes magically even when others are dry
- Against mages and enchanters — Gnomish Cunning makes him frustratingly difficult to affect mentally
- Thorn Whip positioning when an enemy is running, ranging, or pulling away from the frontliners

## Where Cognodec Struggles

- Sustained direct damage: Eldritch Blast is reliable but not spectacular; without Agonising Blast he will never be a primary damage dealer
- If the familiar is killed or he has not yet cast Find Familiar: his scouting capacity drops dramatically
- Physical confrontation at melee range: AC 13 and 10 HP mean he cannot absorb hits. If enemies close on him and he has neither Armor of Agathys up nor Hellish Rebuke in reserve, he is in serious trouble
- Wisdom-based checks: WIS −1 across the board. He will not notice the thing that requires Perception. He should not be the one on watch.
- Common Sign Language: he speaks it; no one else in the party does. A useful tool with no current conversational partner.

---

## Roleplaying Notes

Cognodec has spent several decades being what people needed him to be, and has recently discovered — with some alarm — that the performance may have cured itself into something real. He is still working out which parts. The warlock power is real. The Book of Shadows is real. Whether *the Green Advocate* is real in the way he described it in performance, or in some other way entirely, he cannot yet say.

He is not dishonest with the party — or rather, he is honest in the way a craftsman is honest: he gives them what they need from him, which is accurate and true, and does not volunteer what they don't need yet. He will tell any of them who the patron is if they ask the right question. The unsettling part is that no one has asked the right question.

His relationship to the party is cautious goodwill. He notices things — the way Osctra assesses every living creature he encounters, the specific quality of Ix-Nambuth's silences, the fact that Gwenwyn answers questions sideways and gets them exactly right. These observations go into the same file as the animal reports: evidence of a picture he cannot yet see clearly. He considers himself a late arrival to a process that has been underway for longer than he knows, and he intends to be useful to it.

The Book grows heavier when something is wrong. He has stopped pretending not to notice.
# Gwenwyn — Strategic Analysis

---

## Role

**Primary Healer / Battlefield Controller / Support Buffer**

Gwenwyn is the party's healer, its luck reserve, and its most versatile support character. She operates primarily from a protected middle position — not in the front rank with the melee fighters, not at the extreme range Cognodec typically occupies, but close enough to Healing Word anyone in seconds and far enough back to survive. The key to playing her well is resource discipline: she has two spell slots, three Bardic Inspiration dice, three Luck Points, and one Heroic Inspiration, and she needs to distribute these across a full adventuring day. The instinct to burn them early in the first fight is almost always wrong.

---

## Combat Priorities

### The Bonus Action Is Healing Word

Gwenwyn's Bonus Action should be treated as a lifeline. **Healing Word** (BA, 60 ft, 2d4+3 HP) is the most important thing she can do in any round where an ally is at low HP or at 0. Unlike most spells that compete for the action slot, Healing Word leaves her action free to control the battlefield, apply Vicious Mockery, or activate Shillelagh. A round where Gwenwyn heals an ally *and* applies control or damage is a good round. A round where she sacrifices her action to heal via a touch spell would be a bad one.

| Priority | When |
|---|---|
| **Healing Word (BA)** | Whenever an ally drops to 0 HP or is dangerously low |
| **Bardic Inspiration (BA)** | When no immediate healing need exists and an ally faces a high-stakes check or attack |
| **Shillelagh (BA)** | When she expects to be in melee and wants her club to hit reliably at +5 for 1d8+3 |

She cannot use all three in the same round. Decide at the start of each round which Bonus Action matters most.

### Concentration: The Critical Choice

Gwenwyn has three Concentration options competing for the same resource:

| Concentration | Best Used When |
|---|---|
| **Faerie Fire** | Multiple enemies in a cluster; Ix-Nambuth and Osctra both in melee; the party needs party-wide Advantage on attack rolls against them |
| **Sleep** | Multiple low-HP enemies that can be removed from the fight entirely; weaker clustered creatures in the opening of an encounter |
| **Guidance** | Out of combat; a party member faces a key skill check where 1d4 could shift the outcome |

**The opening round default:** If facing multiple enemies in melee range of the frontliners, **Faerie Fire** is almost always correct over Sleep. Sleep removes enemies from the fight only if they fail their save and remain low HP; Faerie Fire guarantees Advantage for Ix-Nambuth, Osctra, and any other melee attacker against every affected creature, and it has no HP threshold. The Advantage on attacks is worth more than the control value of Sleep in most Level 1 encounters because Ix-Nambuth and Osctra are already hitting hard.

**Sleep's window:** Sleep is strongest when the party is facing several enemies that are meaningfully weakened — partial HP from a previous round, or small creatures with low HP pools. Against fresh, full-HP enemies of moderate constitution, the Sleep output may not cover enough targets to justify the slot. Never use Sleep when allies are close to enemies (there is no friendly-fire exclusion by default in the 2024 rules for Sleep's targeting, though in practice Gwenwyn can choose which creatures to target in the sphere).

---

## Spell Usage

**Healing Word:** The slot cost is her primary constraint on healing. She has 2 Level 1 slots, which means at most two rounds of magical healing per long rest (unless she manages slots carefully and uses Bardic Inspiration as a partial substitute for maintaining allies). Use Healing Word to bring downed allies back to consciousness and into the fight; do not use it as HP maintenance on allies who are functioning at reduced HP. A fighter at 4/12 HP who is still attacking is not an emergency. A fighter at 0 HP who is making death saves is.

**Faerie Fire (Concentration):** 20-ft Cube within 60 ft. Affected creatures shed dim light, cannot benefit from Invisible, and attack rolls against them have Advantage. It requires a DEX save (DC 13) — solid odds at Level 1 against most creatures. This is her highest-value opener when fighting multiple enemies, because every melee attack from Ix-Nambuth and Osctra benefits. The Advantage compounds: it increases their hit probability, makes Topple procs more likely (they need to hit to impose the Topple save), and synergises with any party-wide advantage source. If Faerie Fire and Bless are both active simultaneously — Gwenwyn maintaining Faerie Fire, Ix-Nambuth maintaining Bless — the frontliners are attacking with Advantage *and* adding +1d4 to their attack rolls. This combination is exceptional.

**Sleep (Concentration):** A 5-ft Sphere at 60 ft. Target creatures make a WIS save (DC 13); on a fail, they are Incapacitated until end of next turn, then save again; a second failure = Unconscious for up to 1 minute. Elves and creatures immune to Exhaustion are immune. Against non-Elf humanoid enemies at Level 1, this can shut down multiple enemies simultaneously if they fail. Osctra is a Wood Elf — immune. Ix-Nambuth is an Orc — not immune, but she is a party member and a friendly target. Position Sleep away from the party's own frontliners.

**Disguise Self:** No concentration. 1 hour. Changes apparent physical features, clothing, equipment. The party has both Disguise Self (Gwenwyn) and Deception +5 (Cognodec) — coordinate these. Gwenwyn can look like a completely different person; Cognodec can provide the social cover story. Together they are the party's infiltration pair.

**Vicious Mockery (Cantrip):** WIS save (DC 13), 60 ft. On a fail: 1d6 Psychic damage and Disadvantage on the target's next attack roll before the end of its next turn. This is her best cantrip combat option when she is not maintaining Concentration: the Disadvantage on the target's attack is reliable debuffing, especially useful when the target would otherwise be swinging at Ix-Nambuth or Osctra. At Level 1, a creature with a −1 WIS modifier that fails this save is going to miss its next attack more often than it otherwise would — that missed attack is potentially the hit that would have broken concentration on Faerie Fire or Bless, and avoiding it has cascading value.

**Minor Illusion (Cantrip):** Sound or image within 30 ft, 1 minute. A persistent, non-Concentration sound source or visual effect with an Investigation DC 13 to disbelieve. She can create a credible noise in one location while the party moves in another. She and Cognodec share this cantrip — coordinate rather than duplicate.

**Shillelagh (Cantrip):** Bonus Action. Her club or quarterstaff hits using CHA, deals 1d8, and the damage type is Force or Bludgeoning. At +5 to hit for 1d8+3, this is genuinely effective melee output — not expected from a Bard. If the frontliners are handling multiple enemies and something breaks through to Gwenwyn's position, she does not have to run; she can Shillelagh and hold. She carries two clubs specifically for this. The limitation: it requires a Bonus Action to activate and she usually has better Bonus Action choices in combat. Shillelagh is for rounds when no healing is needed and she happens to be in melee.

**Guidance (Cantrip):** Concentration, touch, 1d4 bonus to one skill check. Out-of-combat only. The correct default before any important ability check the party makes. Cannot be maintained during combat.

**Speak with Animals (1 free/LR; also Ritual):** See the party's synergy notes. Her one free cast per LR should be coordinated with Cognodec's two free casts per SR so the party isn't wasting overlap. The ritual version costs 10 minutes and no slot — use it whenever they have time and an animal worth questioning.

---

## Key Traits in Play

**Bardic Inspiration (3/LR, d6):** The ability to grant an ally a d6 to add to any D20 Test within the next 10 minutes is exceptional utility. She cannot use it on herself. The three correct moments to spend an Inspiration die:

1. An ally is making an attack roll against a target that must die this round and is at low HP
2. An ally is saving against a devastating effect that would remove them from the fight
3. Out of combat, a high-stakes skill check where failure creates serious consequences

Do not spend Bardic Inspiration on routine checks or attacks that aren't pivotal. Three dice across an adventuring day is not many; the value per die is highest when it changes an outcome rather than improves one.

**Lucky (3 Luck Points/LR):** Spend 1 point to roll an extra d20 on any D20 Test and choose the better (or worse) result; or to give an enemy Disadvantage on an attack roll against Gwenwyn. The defensive use — forcing Disadvantage on an incoming attack — is effectively a situational +5 AC for a single hit. Save these for genuine inflection points: maintaining Concentration on Faerie Fire after being hit, a Saving Throw against something that would incapacitate her, or a Persuasion check where failure closes a door permanently.

**Heroic Inspiration (every LR, Human — Resourceful):** She regains Heroic Inspiration after every Long Rest. Unlike Luck Points, this Heroic Inspiration die can be given away immediately — she can hand it to whichever party member faces the most important early roll. Do not sit on it; it refreshes free every morning.

**Stealth +5 / Sleight of Hand +5 / Thieves' Tools:** Gwenwyn is the party's closest analogue to a Rogue, without actually being one. Her Stealth is the highest in the party by a significant margin — she can move quietly when needed. Her Thieves' Tools proficiency means she can pick locks. Her Sleight of Hand means she can lift objects from pockets, palm small items, and generally operate in the way that requires nimble fingers rather than brute force. When the party needs a door opened quietly or an object retrieved without alerting the room, this is Gwenwyn's assignment.

**Insight +3:** The party has a meaningful Perception gap — no one has Perception proficiency, so Passive Perception caps at 11 across the party. However, Gwenwyn has Insight (WIS-based reading of people's intentions and truthfulness) at +3, which is the party's best. In social encounters, she assesses whether the other party is telling the truth, frightened, hiding something, or playing a role. She doesn't need to lead negotiations — that's Cognodec's territory — but she should be in the room and passing observations.

**Survival +3:** She navigates the wilderness circuits she's worked for two years. Combined with Osctra's Survival +3, the party has adequate wilderness competence even without a dedicated Ranger.

---

## Where Gwenwyn Shines

- Bringing downed allies back into the fight — Healing Word as a Bonus Action means she heals *and* acts on the same turn
- Opening fights against multiple enemies with Faerie Fire, granting party-wide Advantage on attack rolls
- Social-adjacent scenarios: she is the second-best talker in the party and the one most likely to notice when Cognodec's performance is being read
- Infiltration and locks: Thieves' Tools + Stealth +5 + Disguise Self makes her the quiet door-opener
- High-stakes moments: Lucky's three points and Bardic Inspiration's three dice mean she has more "emergency resources" than anyone else
- Situations that benefit from Speak with Animals (her rural knowledge of animals and what they communicate is genuine, not performed)

## Where Gwenwyn Struggles

- Sustained magical output: 2 spell slots per Long Rest is a hard ceiling; she burns through them fast if she tries to do too much
- Physical confrontation without Shillelagh active: her standard dagger is fine (+5, 1d4+3) but she is not built to be hit
- Perception: WIS +1, no proficiency, Passive Perception 11. She will not spot the hidden creature, the trap in the corridor, or the guard around the corner. This is the party's collective blind spot.
- Groups of enemies with high WIS saves or Elf-type resistance to Sleep: her Sleep slot can become near-useless in these matchups
- Long fights where she has exhausted Healing Word slots: once both slots are gone, she is using cantrips to heal (Bardic Inspiration as a partial substitute) and hoping no one drops

---

## Roleplaying Notes

Gwenwyn knows things because she has been paying attention for years. Her rural circuit built her a comprehensive mental map of roads, weather, animal patterns, innkeepers, and village rumours — and she has been noting the wrongness in that map for three months, carefully and without fully admitting to herself that it was serious.

She answers questions sideways and then completely. She never lies directly; she answers the question that is actually being asked, which is sometimes different from the question that was spoken. This can make her seem evasive until you realise she just told you exactly what you needed to know in the sentence that appeared to be about something else.

Her Lucky feat is a character trait as much as a mechanical one. She has exceptional luck, and she has noticed this, and she occasionally exploits it in ways that make superstitious people uncomfortable. She doesn't explain it; she doesn't try to. When something should have gone wrong and didn't, she moves on and thanks no one in particular.

The old herbalist woman who taught her to listen — the one who died two winters ago — is the reason she kept notes, the reason she reads the land as a communicative thing rather than a backdrop, and the reason she is unable to pass an animal in distress without stopping. She still starts sentences addressed to her, sometimes. The party might occasionally catch this, and she will not comment on it.

She considers herself a carrier of information, not a keeper of it. The right story, in the right place, at the right time, costs nothing and changes everything. She is starting to think that whatever is wrong with the valley and the wildlife is going to need its own story, and that she might be one of the people who has to tell it.
# Ix-Nambuth — Strategic Analysis

---

## Role

**Primary Tank / Frontline Controller / Party Buffer**

Ix-Nambuth is the party's armoured wall — the character enemies hit first and have the hardest time hitting at all. At AC 18 with a shield, she has the highest AC in the party by a meaningful margin, the hit points to absorb punishment, and the Orc resilience to survive being brought to zero HP once per Long Rest. Unlike a pure damage dealer, her value is not primarily in what she destroys: it is in the space she creates for everyone else to operate, the Bless concentration she sustains, and the Topple-prone enemies she leaves for Osctra to finish. She is the platform the party's offence runs on.

---

## Combat Priorities

### Bless Is the Default Concentration

Ix-Nambuth's Level 1 slots exist primarily to maintain Bless. This is not a dramatic choice — it is an expectation. **Bless** (Concentration, up to 1 minute, up to 3 creatures) adds +1d4 to all attack rolls and all saving throws for the duration. Applied to Osctra, herself, and either Gwenwyn or Cognodec, it elevates every offensive action the three of them take from that moment forward. The value compounds across rounds: every attack Osctra makes, every save Cognodec resists, every save Ix-Nambuth makes to maintain Bless itself — all benefit.

| Concentration | When |
|---|---|
| **Bless** | Default — cast in the first round of most fights; sustain it |
| **Searing Smite** | No Concentration required — it stacks with Bless |

Bless requires a Concentration save (CON +2) whenever she takes damage. Her Constitution is good but not exceptional. If she is being targeted heavily, prioritise positioning to reduce incoming hits rather than switching Concentration spells — losing Bless is more costly than positioning conservatively to sustain it.

### Weapon Selection

| Situation | Weapon | Why |
|---|---|---|
| Standard melee | **Quarterstaff 1H + Shield** | 1d6+3 Bludgeoning; Topple mastery; retains shield AC |
| Heavy single enemy | **Quarterstaff 2H** | 1d8+3 Bludgeoning; Topple mastery; drops shield — only when facing one dangerous enemy that must be pressured quickly |
| Ranged or closing enemy | **Javelin** | 1d6+3 Piercing; Slow mastery — reduces target's Speed by 10 ft on a hit; good for enemies trying to close or flee |

**On the two-handed option:** Unlike Party 1's Rixon (whose Duelling style made 1H better than 2H), Ix-Nambuth does not have a fighting style at Level 1. This means the 2H Quarterstaff (1d8 vs. 1d6) does produce meaningfully better damage, but at the cost of the +2 AC from the shield. In most Level 1 fights, AC 18 is the right trade over 1-point average damage increase — keep the shield. Consider 2H only when one specific dangerous enemy must go down this round and AC matters less than burst.

### Topple in Practice

On a hit, the target must make a CON save (DC 13 = 8 + PB + STR) or gain the Prone condition. Prone:
- Reduces the target's speed to 0 until it stands up (costs half its movement)
- Gives all **melee** attack rolls against it Advantage
- Gives the creature Disadvantage on its own attacks

When Ix-Nambuth Topples an enemy that Osctra is also engaged with, both of them attack that prone target with Advantage. When Ix-Nambuth Topples an enemy that Gwenwyn has hit with Faerie Fire (also granting Advantage), the frontliners are attacking with Advantage via two stacked sources — sources do not add more than Advantage, but it ensures the Advantage is maintained even if one source drops. Coordinate with Osctra explicitly: **call the target**, so both Topple weapons fire at the same creature.

---

## Spell Usage

**Bless (Concentration):** Cast it in round one. Choose three creatures, ideally Osctra, herself, and whoever is making the most attacks or most consequential saves. Sustain it. This is the correct use of one of her two Level 1 slots in most encounters. Against a single powerful enemy, Bless on three melee characters is exceptional; against a crowd, it still applies consistently because even spread enemies require the same attack rolls. The only situation where Bless is not the round-one default: when she needs Lay on Hands urgently (doesn't require a slot) or when an encounter is so brief that sustaining a Concentration spell for multiple rounds is impractical.

**Searing Smite (No Concentration):** Her second slot's best use in combat. Declare it as a Bonus Action immediately after a hit — it adds 1d6 Fire damage to the triggering attack and then deals 1d6 Fire at the start of the target's subsequent turns (CON save DC 12 to end). Because it is not Concentration, it stacks with Bless — she can be maintaining Bless with her Concentration and Searing Smite through a Bonus Action simultaneously. The ongoing Fire damage creates pressure: the target is incentivised to spend an action breaking the smite rather than attacking the party. Against a single strong enemy, this is her most efficient slot use if she is not yet concentrating on Bless.

**Lay on Hands (5 HP pool/LR):** A Bonus Action healing pool — not a spell, not a slot. She can spend any amount from the 5 HP pool in one use to restore Hit Points, or spend exactly 5 HP to remove the Poisoned condition. This is triage, not primary healing — at Level 1, 5 HP buys perhaps one more round of effective combat from a wounded ally. It is best used on herself (she does not have access to Healing Word) when she is at dangerously low HP but above 0. Gwenwyn is the party's primary healing resource; Lay on Hands is the emergency reserve when Gwenwyn is occupied or out of range.

---

## Key Traits in Play

**Adrenaline Rush (2/SR or LR):** Take the Dash action as a Bonus Action and gain 2 Temporary Hit Points. This is primarily a positioning tool — if an enemy is trying to flee, or the party needs her to cross a room before it collapses, or she needs to close to an enemy before it reaches the backline. The THP are incidental but real. She can use this twice before a Short Rest, making it the highest-frequency ability in her kit after her attacks.

**Relentless Endurance (1/LR):** When reduced to 0 HP but not killed outright, drop to 1 HP instead. This is a second life per Long Rest. In practical terms, it extends her effective HP by whatever damage would have killed her in that hit — at Level 1, often 5–15 HP. The party's primary healer (Gwenwyn) is on Bonus Action healing, which means she can bring someone back from 0 HP without sacrificing her action — but Relentless Endurance means Ix-Nambuth can briefly ignore one killing blow entirely. Against a single devastating attack, this is exceptional. Use: none — it triggers automatically.

**Weapon Mastery — Slow (Javelin):** On a hit, target's Speed is reduced by 10 ft until the start of Ix-Nambuth's next turn. This is a positioning and protection tool: if an enemy is charging the backline past Ix-Nambuth's position, a thrown Javelin at the start of combat (30/120 ft range) reduces their closing speed by 10 ft. Combined with Osctra at 35 ft movement and Ix-Nambuth herself at 30 ft, a Slowed enemy may not be able to close before one of the frontliners interposes. At the start of combat before melee is established, a Javelin throw is often the highest-value opening action.

**Musician Feat — Inspiring Song:** After a Short Rest in which she plays at least 10 minutes of music, any ally who heard the music and spent Hit Dice during the rest gains Heroic Inspiration. This is not a combat ability but a sustained campaign resource: the party is better-resourced going into every encounter after a Short Rest because of Ix-Nambuth. Heroic Inspiration (free Advantage on one D20 roll) is most valuable in moments of genuine consequence — an important save, a decisive attack. When the party takes Short Rests (which benefit Ix-Nambuth's Adrenaline Rush as well), she should always play.

**Darkvision 120 ft:** The longest Darkvision in the party — double the 60 ft range of Osctra and Cognodec. In dark environments, she is the party's eyes at range, seeing clearly where others would see only dimly (and where Gwenwyn would see nothing at all). She should lead in dark corridors, communicating what she sees to the party.

**Languages — Gnomish and Orcish:** She is the party's linguistic hub. She shares Gnomish with Cognodec (the only non-Common language both of them speak, enabling private conversations between the party's two most complex personalities) and Orcish with Osctra (the working language of his trading relationships, and a language he holds as more precise than Elvish for practical matters). She is the party's cultural translator in both directions.

**Athletics +5 / Acrobatics +3:** The highest Athletics in the party alongside Osctra (+5). She can climb, grapple, break down doors, hold a line physically, and generally apply force when needed. Acrobatics +3 is useful for resisting Topple from enemies — an enemy trying to knock her Prone faces her +3 Acrobatics on the contested roll.

---

## Where Ix-Nambuth Shines

- Absorbing punishment that would drop other party members in one or two hits, while Bless keeps the whole front rank effective
- Toppling dangerous enemies into the path of Osctra's follow-up — the coordination between their two Topple weapons is the party's most consistent frontline combo
- Short Rest recovery: Adrenaline Rush recharges, and her Musician feat means every Short Rest benefits the whole party with Heroic Inspiration
- Dark environments — her Darkvision 120 ft provides significantly more range than anyone else's
- Linguistic scenarios — shared Gnomish with Cognodec and Orcish with Osctra make her the party's social bridge in contexts where Common isn't sufficient
- Surviving what should kill her — Relentless Endurance turns one lethal hit per day into a dramatic near-death experience rather than an actual one

## Where Ix-Nambuth Struggles

- Stealth: Chain Mail imposes Disadvantage on all Stealth checks, permanently. She cannot participate in any silent approach and should not attempt one. She is the party member who stays back or creates a legitimate noise elsewhere while the others move quietly.
- Ranged combat without a Javelin: if she runs out of Javelins and cannot close, her options narrow. She carries six — this is a reasonable supply, but track them.
- Concentration breaks under sustained fire: Bless is exceptional but requires CON saves whenever she takes damage. Against enemies that focus on her specifically, Bless can fall before it pays off. If the DM starts targeting her for Bless specifically, she may need to position behind Osctra to share incoming fire.
- WIS save DC 12 and CHA +2 Spell Attack: her offensive spellcasting is noticeably weaker than Gwenwyn or Cognodec. Searing Smite's ongoing CON save (DC 12) is achievable but not overwhelming. She is not the party's magical damage dealer.

---

## Roleplaying Notes

Ix-Nambuth plays the viol. This is not a hobby; it is a position — the conviction that beauty is a serious undertaking and the world needs more of it, not less, and that someone being present to perform it is not decoration but necessity. The oath she holds came to her in a forest clearing without ceremony or explanation, and she has chosen to accept its weight because the power is real and the thing it asks of her is right, even if the theology is irregular.

She is quieter than people expect an Orc to be, and she lets the dissonance work for her. An Orc warrior who plays the viol, who speaks Gnomish because an old Gnomish musician corrected her technique, who carries a shield and a quarterstaff and a viol case: the ambiguity is entirely intentional. She does not explain herself. She shows up, she performs, she protects what she has sworn to protect, and she is consistently surprised when this isn't enough to communicate that she cares.

Her flaw — assuming that being present communicates what she intends — shapes her interactions with the party. She will arrive at the right moment and do the right thing and then not say the right words, and she will notice the confusion this creates a beat too late. She is working on this. She has been working on it for years.

Zib Tumbletock, the elderly Gnomish musician who trained her, is the reason she speaks Gnomish and the reason she plays the way she does. Meeting Cognodec — a Forest Gnome who communicates in the same language, from the same rough cultural territory — will produce in Ix-Nambuth a particular quality of attention that the party may eventually learn to read as warmth. It doesn't look like warmth at first.
# Osctra — Strategic Analysis

---

## Role

**Primary Melee Striker / Damage Sponge / Wilderness Expert**

Osctra is the party's primary combat engine — not the tankiest character (that is Ix-Nambuth's role), but the one who absorbs damage through raw Hit Points and Rage resistance while outputting consistent, escalating damage. His job is to be in the centre of the fight, hitting hard, and staying alive while Gwenwyn keeps him upright and Ix-Nambuth keeps enemies from fleeing or standing back up. He is also the party's wilderness specialist — Animal Handling, Nature, Survival, and decades of observational expertise give him a competence in outdoor environments that no other party member can match.

---

## Combat Priorities

### Rage: When and Whether

Rage is Osctra's defining ability. The question is not whether to use it — it is when.

**Use Rage when:**
- The fight is likely to last 3+ rounds (Rage only pays off with sustained engagement)
- He is taking, or expects to take, Bludgeoning, Piercing, or Slashing damage (Resistance to all three is the core benefit)
- He is fighting a single strong enemy or multiple melee attackers focused on him
- Ix-Nambuth has already cast Bless (the +1d4 to attack rolls stacks with Rage's +2 damage — both apply simultaneously)

**Hold Rage when:**
- The fight looks like it will be resolved in 1–2 rounds without it (wasting a charge)
- Gwenwyn needs to use Guidance on a skill check before the encounter escalates — Rage would interrupt leaving the conversation before combat
- He needs to speak or communicate diplomatically during the encounter (Rage ends if he tries to cast a spell or doesn't attack or take damage in a round, and roleplay situations mid-combat can trigger ambiguous states — agree with the DM on rules before this matters)

**Two uses per Long Rest; regain 1 on Short Rest.** Treat them as one per meaningful fight with a reserve. Do not blow both in the first encounter of the day.

### Weapon Selection

| Situation | Weapon | Why |
|---|---|---|
| Standard frontline | **Pitchfork/Trident (1H) + Barrel Lid Shield** | 1d8+3 (+2 Rage) Piercing; Topple mastery; retains +2 AC |
| Maximum pressure | **Pitchfork/Trident (2H)** | 1d10+3 (+2 Rage) Piercing; Topple; drops shield for +1 average damage — only worth it when shield isn't needed |
| Adjacent secondary attack | **Framing Hammers** | Nick mastery — the extra attack is part of the Attack action, freeing the Bonus Action for Rage; 1d4+3 (+2 Rage) Bludgeoning |

**The Nick mastery detail:** The Framing Hammers (Light property) would normally allow an off-hand attack as a Bonus Action. Nick mastery moves this extra attack into the Attack action itself, freeing the Bonus Action for Rage. This means Osctra can: Rage (BA), then Attack (1 Pitchfork hit) + Attack (1 Hammer hit via Nick). That is two weapon attacks in his first round while still activating Rage. This is a strong turn-one play.

However: this requires holding a Framing Hammer in one hand and relying on the Pitchfork, which conflicts with the shield setup. **Clarify with DM:** can he draw a hammer mid-action to execute Nick, or must it be pre-drawn? In general, he should default to Pitchfork + Shield unless the fight calls for maximum damage output.

### Topple in Practice

Same as Ix-Nambuth's Topple, applied from Osctra's Pitchfork. CON save DC 13 on a hit; failure = Prone. When both Ix-Nambuth and Osctra target the same creature with Topple, that creature makes CON saves twice (one per hit) and faces Advantage on all melee attack rolls against it if either Prone application succeeds. **Call the target explicitly in coordination with Ix-Nambuth.** The double Topple combination is the party's most reliable control mechanism.

---

## Key Traits in Play

**Rage — Resistance and Damage:**
- Resistance to Bludgeoning, Piercing, and Slashing damage: any physical hit deals half damage. Against enemies dealing these types (which is almost all melee and many ranged enemies), this doubles his effective HP while it is active.
- +2 damage on Strength-based attacks: every hit with the Pitchfork or Framing Hammer gains +2 damage beyond the STR +3 modifier.
- Advantage on Strength checks and saving throws: grappling, shoving, climbing, any physical contest, and saving throws against effects that move or restrain him. He is extraordinarily difficult to shift once engaged.

**Unarmored Defense:** AC = 10 + DEX (+1) + CON (+3) = **14** unarmored. With the Barrel Lid Shield: **16**. This is lower than Ix-Nambuth's 18 — Osctra takes more hits. This is intentional: he is the damage sponge who *should* be taking hits because Rage resistance means each one is halved. He is not trying to avoid damage; he is trying to absorb it efficiently.

**Tough Feat (+2 HP/level):** His 17 HP at Level 1 (already factored in) is the highest HP in the party. Combined with Rage resistance, he effectively has 34 HP against physical damage while Raging — the highest effective durability on the front line by a considerable margin.

**Speed 35 ft (Wood Elf):** He moves faster than every other party member. In pursuit situations, escape attempts, and positioning to intercept fleeing enemies, Osctra is the party member who covers ground. Combined with Rage's Advantage on STR checks (including Athletics for grappling), he can run down and physically restrain almost anything at this level.

**Druidcraft (cantrip, WIS):** A minor nature cantrip, not a combat tool. He uses it the way a farmer uses a weather vane: practical, unsentimental, occasionally stunning. Predict weather, make a seed sprout, describe what a berry will taste like, clean a tool, generate a pleasant scent. In character, he does this as a reflex — checking wind direction, testing soil composition — rather than as a mystical performance. He has never once used Druidcraft for dramatic effect in a social encounter. He probably could. He won't.

**Animal Handling +3 / Survival +3 / Nature +2:** These are the party's strongest wilderness skills. He reads displaced predators, assesses terrain, knows what is growing wrong and why, and can navigate in conditions that would disorient everyone else. His field journal is a record of months of careful observation — when the party is trying to understand the geographical pattern of the wildlife wrongness, Osctra's notes are primary evidence. Ask him before asking anyone else about what is happening in the natural world.

**Carpenter's Tools:** He can seal doors, pry open stuck mechanisms, reinforce camp structures, and assess the structural integrity of buildings. These are practical dungeoneering and wilderness camp skills that rarely make headlines but frequently matter. His Barrel Lid shield is itself a piece of craftsmanship he has improved and reinforced with his own hands.

**Trance (4-hour Long Rest):** He finishes a Long Rest in 4 hours of meditative trance, during which he retains consciousness. This means he can watch and rest simultaneously — he should often volunteer for watch duty at rest stops, and the party should build plans around his superior overnight vigilance, especially given the party's collective Perception gap.

**Fey Ancestry:** Advantage on saves to resist Charm. Osctra is not charmed easily. In encounters involving Charm-type effects, he is the most reliable to hold the line while others are affected.

---

## Out-of-Combat Role

Osctra's out-of-combat value is centred on two things: understanding the natural world and being quietly, unexpectedly right.

**Wilderness navigation and expertise:** In any outdoor environment, he reads the land with the precision of someone who has spent eighty years paying close attention to it. He assesses vegetation health, soil composition, water sources, animal signs, and weather shifts. The party will never starve in the wilderness as long as he is with them.

**The field journal:** He keeps meticulous notes on plant species, animal behaviour anomalies, and predator movement patterns. This is both character flavour and genuine campaign evidence. When the party is piecing together what is driving the wrongness, Osctra's journal provides months of data points that predate everyone else's observations. He should be sharing this information systematically with the party — it is the most complete record of the phenomenon's timeline.

**Silence as currency:** He communicates in brief sentences and long silences. In social encounters, this reads differently to different NPCs — some interpret it as menace, some as contempt, some (correctly) as a man who has very little patience for conversation that does not result in useful information. He should not lead negotiations; Cognodec and Gwenwyn handle that. He should be present, however, because his Animal Handling and Insight catches what is happening with the non-human elements of any situation, and because his Orcish connects him to Ix-Nambuth in ways that sometimes produce the relevant observation before anyone else has noticed what to look for.

---

## Where Osctra Shines

- Sustained melee combat while Raging: Rage resistance makes him extraordinarily difficult to put down, and his damage output with STR +3 + Rage +2 is consistently strong
- Against multiple melee opponents who focus on him: the more of them are hitting him, the more Rage's resistance multiplies his durability
- Topple coordination with Ix-Nambuth: the double Topple combo is the party's most reliable control mechanism
- Wilderness environments: nature expertise, survival, animal handling — these are his, uncontested
- Physical feats: Athletics +5, STR +3, and Advantage on STR checks while Raging mean virtually no physical obstacle stops him
- Long adventuring days: two Rages per Long Rest, one of which recovers on a Short Rest, gives him more combat uses per day than most of his abilities imply

## Where Osctra Struggles

- Magic: no spell resistance, no magic items, no meaningful INT or WIS saves for most spell effects. He is a good target for Hold Person, Sleep (though he is a Wood Elf — immune to Sleep), and any WIS or CHA effect. Spellcasters should be a priority target.
- Social encounters: CHA −1 and no social skill proficiencies. He should not speak when Cognodec can speak. His contempt for entitlement and authority can misfire on NPCs who are genuinely reasonable but happen to hold a title.
- Ranged combat: he has no ranged weapon. If he cannot close to melee, he cannot contribute directly. His Sickle and Pitchfork are theoretically throwable (20/60 ft) but do not have Topple at range and he loses his weapon.
- Stealth: STR-based character with no Stealth proficiency (Stealth +1, no Advantage). He is quiet enough not to be a liability, but he is not a scout.
- Dropping out of Rage early: Rage ends if he hasn't attacked or taken damage since his last turn. In encounters that pause or draw out — someone trying to talk during a fight, an enemy retreating and waiting — his Rage can drop at inopportune moments. Plan for this.

---

## Roleplaying Notes

Osctra has been growing things for eighty years. He has the patience of someone who has watched plants take seasons to produce what they promised, and the anger of someone who has watched a hunting party destroy in an afternoon what two centuries of work built. These two facts coexist in him without contradiction: long patience and short fury are both agricultural virtues. You wait as long as the crop requires. You move very quickly when you need to.

He is not a complicated person in the way Cognodec is complicated. His contempt for authority is not philosophical — it is practical experience. Titles and fine coats correlate, in his life, with people who take without giving back. He knows this is an overgeneralisation. He knows that Ix-Nambuth has a kind of authority that is real and earned. He is adjusting. The adjustment is visible and takes time, which is the most honest thing about him.

His ideal — *Growth: anything worth having takes time, patience, and the willingness to get your hands dirty* — is a worldview, not a motto. He assesses everything he encounters by this standard: does it tend, or does it take? Does it feed the system it belongs to, or does it exhaust it? His growing frustration with whatever is wrong in the valley is not just anger that his farm was destroyed. It is the anger of someone who has watched a system that took centuries to balance being disrupted by something that doesn't understand what it is doing. He wants to find it. He wants to stop it. And then he wants to rebuild his greenhouse.

He is keeping a meticulous field journal of every plant species he hasn't previously catalogued, which is everything outside his immediate valley region. There is no reason to let the whole enterprise go to waste.
