## 8. Notes & Backstory

### Appearance

Ix-Nambuth is six foot three of deliberate contradiction. She has the broad shoulders, grey-green skin, prominent lower canines, and general architectural scale of someone built to end arguments — and she carries a viol case on her back and a quarterstaff in her hand, which means she always looks simultaneously like she is going to perform and like she is going to hit something, and the ambiguity is entirely intentional.

Her hair is dark and kept in a single thick braid, wound with a strip of leather tooled with a pattern she carved herself one winter and has never explained. Her hands are large — noticeably so even on an Orc of her size — but anyone who watches her play notices the precision with which she holds the bow, and the contrast tends to produce a specific quality of silence in a room that she has found professionally useful. She wears two costumes for performing: one very plain, one very not. She alternates them with a strategic sense of occasion.

Her eyes are unexpectedly warm. This catches people off guard, and she has long since decided to let it.

### Personality Traits

- She is quieter than people expect. An Orc who plays the viol has learned to let the dissonance work for her before she opens her mouth.
- She notices small beautiful things with the exact, unsentimental attention of a craftsperson — a chord that resolves unexpectedly, the particular quality of late afternoon light, a bird doing something technically interesting. She does not comment on these observations. She just stops for a moment, and then continues.

### Ideal

**Presence.** A village where the fair has been cancelled, where the harvest supper went unplayed, where the fire went unlit — that is not a small thing. Joy is not decoration. She treats its absence with the same seriousness other people reserve for war.

### Bond

Zib Tumbletock — an elderly Gnomish musician who heard her humming one afternoon in a market, told her she had terrible technique and extraordinary instinct, and then spent four years correcting the former. Still alive, she believes. She tries not to worry about this with any regularity.

### Flaw

She assumes that being present communicates that she cares. She is consistently surprised when it doesn't, and consistently too late to say it another way.

### Backstory

Ix-Nambuth grew up in the kind of place that doesn't appear on careful maps — a borderland settlement, mixed enough that nobody quite belonged to anything, which meant everyone had to decide for themselves what they were. She decided early on that she was not going to be what people assumed when they looked at her, and she has been revising the specifics of that decision ever since.

She found her way into performance because music crossed borders that her appearance didn't. She was seventeen when she first played for coin — a drum, at a market, badly — and something in the response of the crowd told her that this was worth pursuing with more seriousness than she had applied to anything else. She pursued it with considerable seriousness.

Zib Tumbletock found her four years later, corrected her technique, gave her a second-hand viol, and introduced her to the idea that music was not a tool for crossing borders but a thing worth doing for its own sake, because beauty was a serious undertaking and the world required more of it, not less. She absorbed this completely. Zib is the reason she speaks Gnomish, and the reason she plays the way she does, and one of approximately three reasons she is not a completely different person.

She has been working a circuit of rural settlements for the past several years — festivals, harvest suppers, the winter gatherings that keep isolated communities from going entirely dark during the long months. She is known in these places. She is not always welcomed warmly at first, but she is always welcomed back.

**The Oath**

The oath did not come to her from a temple. She has never been inside a temple she found convincing.

It came to her three years ago in a forest clearing, on a night when she had stopped to sleep between villages and woken to find something watching her — not a creature, exactly. More like a quality of attention. Ancient, unhurried, and entirely certain about what it was doing.

She did not speak any vow she can recall. She woke the next morning understanding one had been made. She found this theologically irregular and has never fully resolved it, but the power is real, and the weight of what it asks of her is real, and on balance she has decided that whatever claimed her had reasonable taste.

**The Wrongness**

She first noticed it at a fair that wasn't. She arrived at a village on the expected date to find the green empty — no stalls, no fire, no music. Animals on the northern road, she was told. Attacking without cause or pattern. It wasn't safe.

She had heard similar things from the last two villages. And the one before that. Something was moving through the land and removing, one by one, exactly the things the oath had charged her to protect.

She is taking this personally.

**The Road**

She has heard, since then, of a Wood Elf farmer with a bounty on his head and a field journal full of observations about predators moving wrong. She considers this worth investigating. She is also fairly certain that somewhere on the same roads there is a young woman with a shawm who plays some of the same circuits she does, and who has probably noticed the same silences.

She is not, by nature, a person who travels in groups. She is reconsidering.
