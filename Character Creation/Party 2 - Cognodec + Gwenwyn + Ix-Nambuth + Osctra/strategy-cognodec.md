# Cognodec — Strategic Analysis

---

## Role

**Social Operative / Utility Caster / Ranged Support**

Cognodec is the party's information broker, social engine, and battlefield disruptor. He is not a damage dealer — his cantrips are reliable rather than spectacular — but he is the reason the party learns things other parties don't, navigates social encounters no one else can crack, and has options (pull an enemy into melee, summon a scout familiar, detect magic ritually) that never show up on a straightforward class audit. The key to playing him well is understanding that his greatest power is *off the battlefield*, and that in combat his job is to apply pressure from range, reposition enemies for the frontliners, and keep his one spell slot in reserve until it genuinely matters.

---

## Combat Priorities

### The One Spell Slot

Cognodec has a single Level 1 Warlock slot, which refreshes on a Short or Long Rest. This is dramatically different from every other caster in the party, who all have Long Rest slots. The implication: **his slot is not a daily resource, it is an encounter resource** — in a typical adventuring day with Short Rests, he can use it more freely than Gwenwyn can use her two. However, within a single fight, it is still one decision:

| Slot Choice | Use When |
|---|---|
| **Armor of Agathys** *(pre-combat)* | Enemies are likely to close on him; he wants the 5 THP buffer and the 5 Cold retaliatory damage |
| **Hellish Rebuke** *(reactive)* | He expects to stay at range; saves the slot for a Reaction after being hit — 2d10 Fire damage is exceptional value on a Reaction |

**The core principle:** Never enter a fight where he expects melee contact without deciding in advance which choice to make. If uncertain, Armor of Agathys is the safer default — it goes up before damage lands. Hellish Rebuke is higher-ceiling but requires him to be hit first, which is a worse position.

### Cantrip Selection

He has six cantrips. Most rounds he will choose between:

| Cantrip | When to Use |
|---|---|
| **Eldritch Blast** | Default ranged damage, 120 ft, 1d10 Force — consistent, reliable, no resource cost |
| **Sorcerous Burst** | When he wants burst potential: 1d8 (chosen type), chain on an 8 (up to +3 bonus d8s from CHA). Against a wounded enemy or when maximising one round of output matters |
| **Thorn Whip** | When pulling a fleeing, ranging, or awkwardly-positioned enemy 10 ft toward the frontliners is more valuable than raw damage. 30 ft melee spell attack — requires closing range relative to Eldritch Blast, but the repositioning value is frequently decisive |
| **Minor Illusion** | Out-of-combat pressure, misdirection, creating sounds across a room, distracting guards. Rarely the combat choice but don't overlook it in tactical creativity |

**Eldritch Blast vs. Sorcerous Burst:** Eldritch Blast averages 5.5 on a hit with 1d10; Sorcerous Burst averages 4.5 on a hit with 1d8, but with a ~12.5% chance of chaining into a second d8 and a small chance of further chains. At Level 1, they are close — favour Eldritch Blast for reliability, Sorcerous Burst when an enemy is low and you want to finish them this round.

**Thorn Whip as a tactical tool:** The pull triggers on any hit, which means it requires a melee spell attack at +5 rather than the ranged spell attack at +5 of Eldritch Blast. Against a creature Prone (from Ix-Nambuth or Osctra's Topple), melee attacks — including Thorn Whip — have Advantage. This synergy is worth noting: Topple someone Prone, then Thorn Whip with Advantage to drag them back into the frontliners' reach.

## Spell Usage

**Armor of Agathys:** The correct pre-combat self-buff when he expects to be targeted. 5 Temporary Hit Points act as a buffer; any creature that hits him while those THP persist takes 5 Cold damage automatically. Against enemies that attack in clusters or close to melee, this is better value than a reactive spell because it effectively taxes the first attack against him regardless of what Cognodec is doing on his own turns. Cast it before combat when possible — it doesn't require concentration and lasts 1 hour.

**Hellish Rebuke:** A Reaction, meaning it doesn't cost his action or movement. When he does get hit, this turns the attack into a 2d10 Fire damage counterpunch (DEX save DC 13 for half). Against enemies with mediocre DEX saves, this averages around 7 damage at no cost to his action economy. Reserve this if he genuinely expects to be at range and have Eldritch Blast/Sorcerous Burst handling his damage needs; use Armor of Agathys if he thinks he will be in the thick of it.

**Speak with Animals (2 free/LR; also Ritual):** This is the party's most important investigative tool for the central threat. Three party members have access; Cognodec should coordinate with Gwenwyn on use to avoid wasting both their free casts on the same conversation. Prioritise the ritual version for unhurried situations (camp, a quiet moment in town) and the free casts when encountering animals mid-travel. Any frightened or displaced beast is worth interrogating — they know more than they can articulate, and Cognodec knows how to read what they cannot say.

## Ritual Spells

These cost no spell slot. They cost 10 minutes of casting. Use them constantly.

**Find Familiar:** The single most powerful strategic investment Cognodec can make in any extended situation. An Owl familiar specifically has: Flyby (no opportunity attacks), exceptional Perception, silent flight, and 120 ft Darkvision. Use it to scout rooms before the party enters, monitor approaches while they sleep, observe guard patterns, and deliver touch spells (when Cognodec eventually gains any). The 10 GP incense cost is significant at Level 1 — factor it into resource planning, but do not delay casting it once funds allow. The familiar can communicate what it senses telepathically within 100 ft. This is the party's stealth scout, replacing what Mall-Lowick (not in this party) would do on foot.

**Detect Magic:** 10-minute ritual, Concentration for up to 10 minutes. Senses magical effects within 30 ft and allows identifying schools of magic on visible objects. Cast whenever the party enters an unfamiliar space and wants to audit it for enchantments, hidden magic items, or active spell effects. It requires Concentration while active — don't start it mid-fight.

## Key Traits in Play

**Gnomish Cunning:** Advantage on Intelligence, Wisdom, and Charisma saving throws. At Level 1, the most common save-based threats are enchantment (WIS), illusion (INT), and social manipulation (CHA). Cognodec resists all of these with Advantage, making him the party's most resilient mind against magical interference. When the party is walking into a situation that might involve Charm, Hold Person, Fear, or deception magic, Cognodec is the least likely to fall victim.

**Deception +5 / Persuasion +5:** These are the highest social skill modifiers in the party. Cognodec leads negotiations, talks his way past guards, establishes false identities, and manages first contact with NPCs the party needs something from. His Forgery Kit and Costumes are tools in the same toolkit — fake credentials, impersonated affiliations, assumed identities. The Charlatan background gives him Cover (fake identity documentation already in place) as a mechanical resource the DM can engage with.

**Nature +3 / Arcana +3 / Investigation +3:** He is the party's closest approximation of a scholar. None of these are exceptional by comparison to Gilda's Party 1 dominance, but they are respectable by Party 2 standards, and combined they give him competence across the intellectual skill range. He should be the default reader of inscriptions, assessor of arcane artefacts (until Detect Magic is active), and identifier of natural phenomena.

**Stealth +4 / Mage Hand:** He is a plausible scout, quieter than almost any frontliner. Mage Hand lets him manipulate objects (retrieve items from containers, open unlocked doors, pour vials) from 30 ft away without touching them — this is useful for handling suspected traps, retrieving objects from across a room, or interacting with a locked chest before Gwenwyn picks it.

**Thaumaturgy:** Performance and misdirection. Alter his eye appearance, triple the volume of his voice, cause tremors underfoot, make a door slam open or shut, create phantom sounds. In a social encounter, Thaumaturgy transforms his Forest Gnome Mystic performance into something genuinely unnerving. In a negotiation, a voice that fills the room and eyes that shift colour is worth two points of Intimidation. Use it whenever theatrical presence would help — and remember that his whole background is built on exactly this.

**Small size:** Cognodec is 3'4". He can move through the space of creatures one size larger. In combat, he can pass through human-sized enemies without stopping. He can hide behind medium-sized party members in a pinch. He occupies almost no space, can position unusually, and is generally not where enemies expect to find him.

## Where Cognodec Shines

- Social encounters — the party's most effective face by a meaningful margin
- Pre-combat intelligence: Speak with Animals, Detect Magic ritual, Find Familiar scouting
- Situations where deception, impersonation, or manufactured credibility solves a problem that force cannot
- Long adventuring days — his Short Rest slot recovery means he contributes magically even when others are dry
- Against mages and enchanters — Gnomish Cunning makes him frustratingly difficult to affect mentally
- Thorn Whip positioning when an enemy is running, ranging, or pulling away from the frontliners

## Where Cognodec Struggles

- Sustained direct damage: Eldritch Blast is reliable but not spectacular; without Agonising Blast he will never be a primary damage dealer
- If the familiar is killed or he has not yet cast Find Familiar: his scouting capacity drops dramatically
- Physical confrontation at melee range: AC 13 and 10 HP mean he cannot absorb hits. If enemies close on him and he has neither Armor of Agathys up nor Hellish Rebuke in reserve, he is in serious trouble
- Wisdom-based checks: WIS −1 across the board. He will not notice the thing that requires Perception. He should not be the one on watch.
- Common Sign Language: he speaks it; no one else in the party does. A useful tool with no current conversational partner.

## Roleplaying Notes

Cognodec has spent several decades being what people needed him to be, and has recently discovered — with some alarm — that the performance may have cured itself into something real. He is still working out which parts. The warlock power is real. The Book of Shadows is real. Whether *the Green Advocate* is real in the way he described it in performance, or in some other way entirely, he cannot yet say.

He is not dishonest with the party — or rather, he is honest in the way a craftsman is honest: he gives them what they need from him, which is accurate and true, and does not volunteer what they don't need yet. He will tell any of them who the patron is if they ask the right question. The unsettling part is that no one has asked the right question.

His relationship to the party is cautious goodwill. He notices things — the way Osctra assesses every living creature he encounters, the specific quality of Ix-Nambuth's silences, the fact that Gwenwyn answers questions sideways and gets them exactly right. These observations go into the same file as the animal reports: evidence of a picture he cannot yet see clearly. He considers himself a late arrival to a process that has been underway for longer than he knows, and he intends to be useful to it.

The Book grows heavier when something is wrong. He has stopped pretending not to notice.
