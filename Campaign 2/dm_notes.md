# DM Notes — Rixon Campaign

## Campaign Tone
- **Pulpy low fantasy** set inside a classic high-fantasy world
- Zoomed in on Rixon's adventuring life: contracts, jobs, the tavern, the road
- The wider world has its elven kingdoms and dragon hoards, but Rixon lives in the unglamorous margins
- Morally grey NPCs, practical problems, the occasional brush with the genuinely arcane
- Stakes are personal and immediate — rent, reputation, survival — not "save the world"

## DM Principles
1. **Roll only when it matters.** Only call for a roll when success and failure are both plausible and interesting. Auto-succeed the trivial, auto-fail the impossible, narrate the rest.
2. **DC before dice.** Always set the Difficulty Class before invoking the roller, to keep rulings honest.
3. **Show the work.** When a roll happens, display the DC, the roll, and the modifier transparently.
4. **Savage Attacker reminder.** Rixon rolls weapon damage twice per turn and takes the better result. Always roll this programmatically.
5. **Heroic Inspiration.** Rixon starts each adventuring day with Heroic Inspiration (Human: Resourceful). Track usage.

## Character Hooks & Threads
- **The arcane blacksmith.** Why did the apprenticeship fall through? Who was the smith? This is a dormant thread to pull on later — could connect to the Eldritch Knight awakening at level 3.
- **The militia.** Which city? Who did he serve with? Any enemies or allies from that time?
- **The demimonde.** Rixon sees himself as part of an underworld of mercenaries, fixers, and freelancers. Populate this world with recurring contacts, rivals, and patrons.
- **The gaming set.** The bluffing games are a social tool — a way into tavern back rooms, a way to size up strangers, a way to lose money.
- **Performance proficiency.** Rixon doesn't just fight — he performs. This should come up in social encounters. He enjoys inhabiting roles.

## Rixon's Weaknesses (to exploit lovingly)
- **DEX 6 / Initiative -2.** He is always slow to react. Ambushes are dangerous. He fumbles delicate tasks. He is loud in his chain mail.
- **11 HP.** Fragile at level 1. One good hit can put him in crisis. Shield (1/day) and Second Wind (2/day) are his safety nets — but they run out.
- **Chain mail stealth disadvantage.** He cannot sneak. Jobs requiring stealth need creative solutions.
- **No long-range bow.** Handaxes at 20/60 with +5 to hit, 1d6+3 damage (thrown weapons use strength) but only 60ft range.

## Mechanical Reminders
- Thrown melee weapons (handaxe) use STR for attack and damage.
- Dueling applies to longsword + shield but NOT to halberd (two-handed).
- Blade Ward (2024) is Concentration — can't stack with other Conc. spells (irrelevant for now, but matters at Eldritch Knight).
- Shield spell: Reaction, +5 AC, 1 free cast/Long Rest. At Eldritch Knight level, castable with spell slots too.
- Cleave (halberd mastery) is once per turn. Secondary target takes weapon die only (no Str mod).
