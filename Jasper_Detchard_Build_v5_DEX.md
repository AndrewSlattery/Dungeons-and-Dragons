# JASPER DETCHARD — The Surgical Duelist
## D&D 2024 Character Build (v6 — DEX Rapier Variant)
### Level 8 Human Fighter (Bravo)

---

## Part 1: The Build Process (Levels 1–8)

### 1. Baseline Choices

- **Class:** Fighter.
- **Species:** Human. Grants three traits: *Resourceful* (Heroic Inspiration after each Long Rest), *Skillful* (one free skill proficiency), and *Versatile* (a free Origin Feat).
- **Background:** Noble *(custom distribution — see note below)*. Grants proficiency in History and Persuasion; proficiency with a **Forgery Kit**; and the Skilled Origin Feat. Models his upper-class upbringing and public school education before his exile.

*Background note:* The Noble background normally distributes its ability score increases among Strength, Intelligence, and Charisma. With DM permission, this build redirects those points to **Dexterity (+2) and Intelligence (+1)** — reflecting a nobleman whose education emphasised fencing, languages, and analytical acuity over military horsemanship. The background flavour is identical; the mechanical distribution acknowledges that Detchard's talents ran a particular way. "I named myself. I chose who I am" applies to how he spent his formative years as well as everything since.

### 2. Starting Ability Scores (27-Point Buy)

| STR     |  13  |   5  |    —       |   13     |

*Design notes:* DEX and INT are the two pillars — Dexterity for the rapier (Finesse; the weapon naturally favours the precise touch of a trained duelist), Intelligence for the Bravo's signature features. DEX 17 is the highest base score, and this is deliberate: Defensive Duelist at level 4 bumps it to 18, matching the original v4's STR 18 and producing identical combat statistics by mid-heroic tier. STR 13 provides a proficient save and fuels Athletics; Sentinel's +1 takes it to 14 at level 8, sufficient for grappling and climbing without being the focus. CON 12 matches the original v4 — Detchard endures through stubbornness and the Tough feat, not robust constitution; the HP total is identical to v4 at 76. WIS 10 provides a neutral save baseline. CHA 8 is deliberate: he manipulates through cold analysis (Savvy's INT bonus to Deception, Intimidation, and Insight), not personal magnetism. He is "a crooked customer," not a charming one.

### 3. Origin Feats

- **From Human (Versatile):** Tough. Increases HP by 2 per level. Reflects "all scars and sinew" — a man who survives the unsurvivable.
- **From Noble:** Skilled. Grants proficiency in any combination of three skills or tools: **Gaming Set — Cards** (card-sharping; "a reputation for cheating at cards, fairly earned"), **Insight** (reading people), and **Perception** (a soldier's alertness).

### 4. Human Skillful Trait

One free skill proficiency: **Investigation**.

### 5. Level-by-Level Progression

**Level 1 (Fighter 1):**
- Skills (choose 2): **Athletics**, **Intimidation**.
- Fighting Style: **Dueling** (+2 damage when holding a melee weapon in one hand and no other weapons).
- Weapon Masteries (3): **Rapier** (Vex), **Dagger** (Nick), **Club** (Slow).

*Design note on weapon choices:* The rapier is the primary weapon — a disgraced Army officer with prizes for swordplay absolutely carries one, and Finesse means it uses DEX naturally. The dagger is the concealed backup; a man who is "always over-armed" keeps several. **Multiple daggers** replace the shortsword entirely: they are equally Light and Finesse, slot naturally into the Nick dual-wield configuration without requiring a separate weapon type, and directly represent the dossier's inventory of "belt knife, concealed knife at my back, boot knife." The **club** (Slow mastery) rounds out the starting loadout as a weighted cosh — a henchman's close-work tool, providing movement control before Tactical Master arrives at level 9. The longsword is gone. Every weapon with a to-hit roll is DEX-compatible except the club, which is used for its mastery property rather than its damage output.

*A note on Sap:* Without the longsword, Sap access is delayed until Tactical Master at level 9 — when the rapier itself can swap Vex for Sap on any individual attack. All Sap weapons in 2024 are non-Finesse (STR-based); with STR 13, drawing one would impose a −3 accuracy penalty versus the rapier. The cost of a clean DEX build is accepting that combat control pre-level 9 comes through Vex (Advantage on your own attacks) rather than Sap (Disadvantage on theirs). These are approximately equivalent in expected value, and the offensive framing is more characterful: Detchard neutralises threats by hitting first and hitting accurately, not by waiting to see what they do. The Club's Slow mastery provides a third control axis — not accuracy (Vex) or attack quality (Sap), but mobility: a Slowed target loses 10 ft of Speed, which combines with Sentinel's Halt to deny escape entirely.

**Level 2 (Fighter 2):**
- Gain **Action Surge** and **Tactical Mind** (expend a Second Wind use to add 1d10 to a failed ability check; if still failed, the use is not expended).

**Level 3 (Fighter 3):**
- Choose the **Bravo** subclass.
- **Savvy:** Choose **Deception** for the skill proficiency. You also learn two languages: **Thieves' Cant** and **French**. Thieves' Cant is the lingua franca of the demimonde — "adventurers, wanderers, gentleman thieves and gentleman thugs [...] a loose fraternity of rogues with a looser tangle of loyalties and rivalries"; French is the language of Antoinette, of Paris, of the Continental educated class he has inhabited since exile. You now add your Intelligence modifier (+2 at this level) to Deception, Intimidation, and Insight checks.
- **Calculated Violence:** When you use Second Wind, choose one creature you can see. You gain a bonus to damage rolls against that creature equal to your Intelligence modifier until the end of your next turn.

**Level 4 (Fighter 4):**
- Gain a 4th Weapon Mastery slot. Default: **Pistol** (Vex). A DEX-based ranged option — consistent with the "always over-armed" profile, and the Vex mastery extends the cascade to ranged attacks when closing isn't possible. *(Pistols are an optional Renaissance weapon from the DMG; their inclusion requires DM permission, already established alongside the custom background distribution.)* This slot can be rotated per Long Rest as circumstances demand; it is a utility slot, not a pillar.
- General Feat: **Defensive Duelist** (+1 Dexterity). **DEX is now 18** (+4 modifier).
  - *Parry:* When holding a Finesse weapon and hit by a melee attack, use your Reaction to add your Proficiency Bonus to your AC. This bonus persists against melee attacks until the start of your next turn. At this level: +2 (AC 18). Scales to +3 from level 5 (AC 19), +4 at level 9 (AC 20), and so on.
- Second Wind uses increase to **3 per Long Rest**.

*Design note on feat order:* In v4, Sentinel came first (level 4) and Defensive Duelist second (level 8). Here the order is reversed. Both orderings work narratively, but differently. The original read as a man whose default instinct is to protect others, who only later learns to protect himself. This version reads as a professional survivor who learns to parry first — years of looking after his own skin — and whose loyalty hardens into something mechanical and reliable only later. Both are true to the character. The DM and player should decide which arc fits the campaign.*

**Level 5 (Fighter 5):**
- Gain **Extra Attack** (two attacks per Attack action).
- Gain **Tactical Shift** (when you use Second Wind, you can also move up to half your Speed without provoking Opportunity Attacks).

**Level 6 (Fighter 6):**
- General Feat: **Skill Expert** (+1 Intelligence). **INT is now 16** (+3 modifier).
  - Gain proficiency in **Stealth**.
  - Gain **Expertise** in **Deception**.

**Level 7 (Fighter 7):**
- Gain **Cut and Run** (Bravo 7). When you use Action Surge, you also gain the benefits of Dash and Disengage, and your AC increases by your Intelligence modifier until the start of your next turn.

**Level 8 (Fighter 8):**
- General Feat: **Sentinel** (+1 Strength). **STR is now 14.**
  - *Guardian:* When a creature within 5 ft of you Disengages or hits a target other than you with an attack, you can make an Opportunity Attack against it.
  - *Halt:* When you hit a creature with an Opportunity Attack, its speed becomes 0 for the rest of the current turn.

### THE SURGICAL DUELIST: TACTICAL LOOPS

Detchard's combat identity is the **untouchable precision fighter**. He reads his opponent with every strike (Vex cascading Advantage), parries their counterattack (Defensive Duelist), and punishes them for turning their attention elsewhere (Sentinel). The rapier is the weapon of a man who won prizes for swordplay; the tactics are those of a man who fights to survive.

#### The Knife-Work: Nick Dual-Dagger (Bonus Action Free)

When the rapier isn't the right tool — close quarters, a grapple threatened, a situation that calls for something uglier — Detchard draws two daggers. Both are Light, both are Finesse, both hit at +7.

Nick makes the second dagger's strike part of the Attack action rather than a Bonus Action. Result: two attacks in the Attack action, Bonus Action fully free.

**Full survival turn:** Attack action (dagger + Nick dagger, Vex cascade on each hit), Bonus Action (Second Wind → 1d10+8 HP, Calculated Violence +3 damage, Tactical Shift 15 ft free movement). He attacks twice, heals, marks a target for +3 damage, and repositions — all in one turn, without sacrificing offensive output.

"I don't like to be unprepared." The man who carries three knives on his person at all times did not spend a weapon mastery slot on the dagger by accident.

#### The Core Loop: Rapier (Both Attacks, Vex Cascade)

Detchard's default. Rapier in one hand, nothing in the other — Dueling's +2 damage applies to every strike.

**First attack (rapier, Vex):** On hit, Vex grants Advantage on the next attack roll against this target.

**Second attack (rapier, with Advantage):** Strike with Advantage. On hit, Vex refreshes — Detchard now has Advantage on his first attack *next turn* as well.

**Net result:** Once the cascade starts, every second attack (and every first attack of the following round) has Advantage. This dramatically increases both hit rate and critical hit chance. Average damage per round is 2d8+12 (avg 21), but the Advantage on most attacks pushes effective DPR significantly higher when accounting for hit probability. "It felt as though I could read his mind, as though one intelligence choreographed us both in perfect harmony."

#### The Parry: Defensive Duelist (Reaction)

When hit by a melee attack, Detchard uses his Reaction to add +3 to his AC (AC 19), which persists against *all* melee attacks until the start of his next turn. This is the classic parry — the weapon that defines his swordsmanship also defines his defence. Available from **level 4** in this build, four levels earlier than v4.

The rapier is Finesse, so Parry is always available as long as it's in hand. At higher levels, as Proficiency Bonus scales, this becomes devastating: AC 20 at level 9, 21 at level 13, 22 at level 17.

"I dare say I looked intimidating, all scars and sinew."

#### The Protector: Sentinel (Reaction)

When a creature within 5 ft hits an ally or Disengages, Detchard makes an Opportunity Attack. On hit, the creature's speed becomes 0 — they aren't going anywhere.

This is the other half of the Reaction dilemma. Each round, Detchard must choose: protect himself (Parry) or protect his companion (Sentinel). That choice is the character's entire emotional arc in mechanical form. The man who claims "I am not a sentimental man" will, when it matters, choose Sentinel every time. "And with all that, I did not flee into the woods."

In this build, the order was reversed during play: Defensive Duelist came first (level 4), Sentinel last (level 8). He learned to parry before he learned to stay.

#### Burst Damage: Second Wind + Calculated Violence

Activate Second Wind as a Bonus Action. Choose your target. Until the end of your next turn, all hits deal +3 damage. Combined with the Vex cascade: 2d8+18 (avg 27) on the activation turn, with Advantage on most attacks. Tactical Shift lets you reposition up to 15 ft without Opportunity Attacks.

This is the execution sequence — Detchard decides someone needs to die, and they do.

#### Emergency Extraction: Action Surge + Cut and Run

Action Surge grants four rapier attacks in one turn (all benefiting from the Vex cascade after the first hit). Cut and Run simultaneously provides Dash, Disengage, and +3 AC (to 19, or **22** if Parry is also active). Attack four times, then walk away untouchable.

"Men like me skulk away into the darkness, or die in it." But Cut and Run works toward danger just as well as away from it. "And with all that, I did not flee into the woods."

### THE REACTION ECONOMY: DETCHARD'S DEFINING CHOICE

Most fighters at level 8 have little to do with their Reaction beyond the occasional Opportunity Attack. Detchard has three competing uses:

| Trigger | Reaction | Effect |
|:--------|:---------|:-------|
| Hit by a melee attack | **Parry** (Defensive Duelist) | AC becomes 19 against melee until next turn |
| Ally hit or enemy Disengages within 5 ft | **Guardian** (Sentinel) | Opportunity Attack; on hit, target's speed = 0 |
| Enemy leaves your reach | **Opportunity Attack** (standard) | On hit, target's speed = 0 (Sentinel's Halt) |

Only one Reaction per round. The choice defines the moment: self-preservation, or loyalty.

### SOCIAL & EXPLORATION PROFILE

Detchard's out-of-combat identity is the intelligent thug: he reads rooms through analysis, not intuition. His INT-boosted skills make him a formidable operative.

- **Deception +8 (Expertise + Savvy):** The master liar. Can throw a fight convincingly, maintain a cover identity, or bluff his way past guards. "I named myself. I chose who I am."
- **Insight +6 (Savvy):** Reads people clinically. Not empathy — pattern recognition. He doesn't feel what you feel; he knows what you're about to do. "It takes one to know one."
- **Intimidation +5 (Savvy):** Not charm — threat assessment. He knows exactly how to make someone understand their position. "All scars and sinew."
- **Investigation +6:** Pre-plans escape routes obsessively. Notices structural weaknesses, unlocked doors, alternative exits. Passive Investigation 16 means he's always mapping the room.
- **Stealth +7:** The mercenary's primary survival skill. He spent most of his life "in boarding school, the Army, and the more insalubrious back streets of half a dozen cities" — he knows how to move without being noticed. "Men like me skulk away into the darkness, or die in it." He has not died yet. At +7 against DC 10 routine patrol checks, Detchard passes without rolling.
- **Gaming Set — Cards (+7):** The hustler's game. "A reputation for cheating at cards (fairly earned)." DEX-based in 2024; at +7, detection of cheating by others (DC 10) and winning outright (DC 20) are both well within reach.
- **Forgery Kit (+6):** He forged Rassendyll's handwriting convincingly enough to deceive trained observers: "My hand bore a reasonable resemblance to Rassendyll's." INT-based; at +6, routine document forgeries succeed automatically. The tradecraft expression of his public-school classical education.
- **Perception +3 / Passive Perception 13:** A professional's baseline alertness. He won't miss the obvious, but he's no scout.
- **Tactical Mind:** If any ability check fails and the stakes matter, Detchard can burn a Second Wind use to add 1d10 to the roll. If it still fails, the use isn't spent. Combined with Heroic Inspiration, very few checks are truly beyond him when it counts.

### WEAKNESSES & VULNERABILITIES

- **Hit Points (76):** Identical to v4. CON 12 remains the price of investing elsewhere; the Tough feat is load-bearing. Detchard endures through stubbornness, not stamina.
- **DEX 18, not 20:** The rapier build delays DEX 20 to level 12 — the same delay as v4's STR 20. Attack and AC scaling are identical from level 12 onward, and after that the DEX variant pulls ahead: each ASI toward DEX 20 improves both to-hit and AC simultaneously.
- **CHA saves (−1):** Banishment and similar effects are dangerous. And when genuine sincerity is required — when the mask has to come off — he is mechanically at his weakest. Which is, of course, the character's entire emotional arc.
- **Low Persuasion (+2):** He cannot ask nicely. Every social approach is a lie, a threat, or a transaction. "Love is best avoided altogether; it is rarely anything but a bloody nuisance."
- **Athletics (+5 vs +7):** A meaningful drop from v4. STR 14 makes him a competent wrestler and climber, not an exceptional one. The original Detchard was formidably physical; this one is formidably nimble instead. Acrobatics is the compensating skill (DEX +4, untrained — +4 baseline without even spending a proficiency).
- **No Sap until level 9:** The longsword is gone, and so is pre-Tactical Master Sap access. This is not a hole in the build so much as a philosophical reorientation: Detchard controls combat by hitting accurately, not by degrading what the enemy can do. The Vex cascade provides comparable expected value to Sap; the absence is felt most in pure-defence scenarios where he'd rather not attack at all, which are rare.
- **Single Reaction per round:** Parry and Sentinel compete for the same resource. In multi-enemy encounters, Detchard is forced to choose between protecting himself and protecting allies — a meaningful cost.

### LOOKING AHEAD (Levels 9–12)

- **Level 9: Tactical Master** — the rapier can replace its mastery property with Push, Sap, or Slow for that attack. *Transformative and, in this build, particularly welcome:* Sap and Slow access arrive without any weapon-switching penalty. The Club mastery slot is now rotatable — the rapier handles Slow directly. A single turn could look like: first attack with Sap (Disadvantage on enemy's attack), second attack with Vex (granting Advantage next turn). Or: first with Slow (−10 ft speed), second with Vex. Mix and match, per-attack, every round. The longsword is not missed.
- **Level 9: Indomitable** — reroll a failed saving throw with a bonus equal to your Fighter level (+9). A critical patch for the CHA −1 save.
- **Level 10 (Bravo): Ready for Anything** — if your Initiative total is lower than your Intelligence score (16), replace it with 16. With DEX +4 already providing +4 initiative, Detchard rarely needs this floor — but it is unbreakable.
- **Level 11: Two Extra Attacks** — three attacks per Attack action. With Action Surge, that is *six* rapier strikes in one turn, all feeding the Vex cascade after the first hit. This is the level at which the cascade becomes genuinely self-sustaining: enough attacks in a single turn to build and exploit Advantage without prior setup.
- **Level 12:** **+2 DEX** (DEX 18 → 20). AC becomes **17 base** (studded leather 12 + DEX 5), rapier attack becomes **+8**. The original v4's breastplate AC never scaled beyond 16; studded leather has now surpassed it permanently. Every remaining ASI toward DEX 20 improved both simultaneously — something no amount of STR investment achieves.
- **Level 13: Studied Attacks** — if you miss a creature with an attack, you have Advantage on your next attack roll against it. This makes the Vex cascade self-healing: a miss generates its own Advantage, and the next hit re-establishes Vex. The rapier loop becomes nearly impossible to break.
- **Level 15 (Bravo): Exploit Error** — when a creature misses you with an attack, you gain +3 (INT modifier) to attack rolls against it until the end of your next turn. Defensive Duelist makes this productive: Parry raises AC to a threshold the attacker cannot clear, they miss, and Detchard gains +3 to punish them for trying. The parry becomes offensively generative rather than purely reactive.
