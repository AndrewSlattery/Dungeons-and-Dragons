# STOBOR

The ancestors of the Stobor (/ˈstoʊ.bɔɹ/) were animated objects given life by ancient magic—tomb guardians raised by priest-kings, dolls awakened by fey tricksters, or ritual figures shaped for arcane purposes. Over generations, this animating spark blossomed into a true life force, giving rise to a people in their own right who established communities across the multiverse.

Stobor are creatures of flesh and blood, but their physical characteristics are reminiscent of their crafted progenitors. Their skin might possess the smooth sheen of porcelain or the faint grain of carved wood, and their resting stillness can be unnervingly absolute. They live for roughly two centuries, wearing over time like beloved antiques rather than wrinkling and graying.

A Stobor chooses whether to embrace or adapt the ancestral magic of their crafted lineage. The three lineages are described below.

#### FAIENCE

The pristine elegance of glass, porcelain, and glazed ceramic calls to Stobor who have the faience lineage. Their features often possess an eerie painted perfection, and some bear faint crack-lines like repaired kintsugi or have eyes that catch the light like polished glass.

#### MARIONETTE

Stobor who have the marionette lineage carry the animating spark of articulated figures of wood, cord and cloth—puppets, mannequins, and jointed dolls built for theater or ritual. They might have visible joint-lines at their elbows and knees, faint wood-grain or embroidery in their skin, or fingers that seem just slightly too long and dexterous.

#### USHABTI

The warding essence of eternal vigilance flows through Stobor who have the ushabti lineage, whether tomb guardians carved from stone and clay or cast-tin soldiers. They tend toward solid or rigid builds, and some bear protective glyphs or ceremonial uniforms etched into their forms.

### STOBOR TRAITS

Creature Type: Humanoid

Size: Tiny (about 1–2 feet tall)

Speed: 30 feet

As a Stobor, you have these special traits.

Crafted Ancestry. Your innate magical lifeblood sustains you; you don't need to eat or drink. You have Resistance to Psychic damage, and you impose Disadvantage on checks made to identify you as more than an inanimate statue, doll, or mannequin.

Maker's Mark. You gain proficiency with one type of Artisan's Tools of your choice, and you have proficiency in the Arcana, History, or Investigation skill.

Rider's Perch. You can ride a willing Medium or larger creature, sharing its space. It acts as an independent mount. When your mount makes an attack roll or an ability check, you can use a Reaction to grant it Advantage on that roll. You can use this trait a number of times equal to your Proficiency Bonus, and you regain all expended uses when you finish a Long Rest.

Stobor Lineage. You are part of a lineage that grants you supernatural abilities, reflecting the material of your ancestors. Choose a lineage from the Stobor Lineages table. You gain the level 1 benefit of that lineage.

When you reach character levels 3 and 5, you learn a higher-level spell, as shown on the table. You always have that spell prepared. You can cast it once without a spell slot, and you regain the ability to cast it in that way when you finish a Long Rest. You can also cast the spell using any spell slots you have of the appropriate level.

Intelligence, Wisdom, or Charisma is your spellcasting ability for the spells you cast with this trait (choose the ability when you select the lineage).

## Appearance Notes

Diving into the physical appearance of a Stobor is one of the most rewarding parts of creating one. The golden rule of Stobor appearance is **Living Person First**. They are not literal constructs, toys, or dolls; they are true creatures of flesh and blood who look like standard humanoids (Humans, Elves, Dwarves, etc.), but scaled down to 1–2 feet tall. 

They carry the visual echo of their crafted progenitors as subtle, uncanny divergences—human enough to read as a person, divergent enough that something is unmistakably, quietly wrong.

### UNIVERSAL CHARACTERISTICS

When visualizing a Stobor, start with an ordinary person of your chosen base species, then apply the following modifiers:

**Scale and Weight:** Stobor stand between a newborn and a toddler in height but carry adult proportions, musculature, and presence. Because they are flesh and blood, they possess standard biological density. 

**Uncanny Stillness:** When a Stobor isn't moving, their resting stillness is chillingly absolute. They lack the natural micro-tremors, shifting weight, or visible breathing of other mortals, settling into a perfectly frozen posture.

**Antique Aging:** Stobor don't develop standard wrinkles. Instead, they wear over time like beloved antiques—pigmentation fades beautifully, and features smooth down to create a dignified patina rather than liver spots.

**Base Species Blending:** A Stobor's face reads clearly as their underlying biological species (e.g., a Stobor Elf has pointed ears and delicate features; a Stobor Dwarf has a broad nose and thick beard). The uncanniness comes from perfect facial symmetry and a lack of natural blemishes.

### THE FAIENCE LINEAGE
*The flesh-and-blood echo of glass, porcelain, and glazed ceramic.*

A Faience Stobor looks like a living person whose skin possesses an impossible, unnatural flawlessness. 
- **The Painted Figurine Mode:** The Stobor has smooth, poreless, waxy skin. Biological flush (like rosy cheeks or red lips) has hard, precise edges with no natural blending, looking almost like a birthmark shaped like perfect makeup. Healed wounds might leave fine, gold-colored scar tissue resembling *kintsugi*.
- **The Vessel Ware Mode:** The Stobor has bold, comprehensive birthmarks or magical tattoos that look exactly like blue-and-white porcelain patterns, sprawling across their living skin like continuous cartouches. 
- **The Glass Mode:** The Stobor's skin has a slight, unnatural translucence in direct sunlight. Their hair is incredibly fine and catches the light brilliantly, resembling spun optical fibers.

### THE MARIONETTE LINEAGE
*The flesh-and-blood echo of articulated figures, mannequins, and jointed dolls.*

A Marionette Stobor looks like a living person whose biology mimics mechanical articulation.
- **The Theatre Mode:** The Stobor has subtle, natural creases and pigmentation lines that circle their elbows, knees, wrists, and jawline, mimicking the look of carved joints. Their fingers are flesh and bone, but slightly too long, dexterous, and spidery.
- **The Ball-Jointed Mode:** The Stobor's eyes are biologically functional but unusually large, with intensely saturated, layered irises. The skin around their major joints has a rounded, swollen structure that mimics a ball-and-socket beneath the flesh.
- **The Manikin Mode:** The Stobor's facial features are remarkably soft and smoothed out, bordering on featureless. Their skin tone strongly resembles natural ash, birch, or maple hues, complete with faint, natural stretch-marks that uncannily resemble wood grain.

### THE USHABTI LINEAGE
*The flesh-and-blood echo of tomb guardians, cast-metal soldiers, and action figures.*

An Ushabti Stobor looks like a living, battle-ready person whose flesh carries the rigidity of the guardian.
- **The Funerary Guardian Mode:** The Stobor has a dense, matte complexion in hues of limestone buff, basalt gray, or clay red. They have geometric, protective scarification deeply etched into the flesh of their chest and arms.
- **The Toy Soldier Mode:** The Stobor has a distinctly flat, unshaded complexion. Their facial hair might grow incredibly dense and uniform, mimicking molded features. They tend to favor perfectly tailored, immaculate miniature military uniforms.
- **The Action Figure Mode:** The Stobor possesses idealized, almost perfectly sculpted musculature without organic variation. Their eyes have a clear, flat, piercing hue, and they often bear a single, perfectly clean-edged scar that seems to have always been there.

## Image Prompt Guidance

The key challenge in prompting Stobor images is the **uncanny valley of the acceptable**. If you use words like "doll," "puppet," "figurine," or "porcelain," the AI will ignore your request for a living creature and draw a literal toy. 

To force the AI to draw a Stobor correctly, use the **Living Person First** formula, describe their lineage traits as *skin conditions or tattoos*, and heavily use negative prompts.

### The Stobor Prompt Formula:
> `A photorealistic portrait of a [1-foot-tall] living, breathing [Base Species: e.g., human/elf/dwarf], [clothing description]. The person has [Stobor skin/eye texture described as a biological trait]. Highly detailed living flesh, intense eye contact, cinematic lighting.`
> **Negative Prompt:** `doll, toy, puppet, figurine, action figure, porcelain, wood, plastic, string, artificial, inanimate, statue`

### Example Prompts by Lineage:

- **Faience (Vessel Ware):** * *Prompt:* "A photorealistic portrait of a 2-foot-tall living, breathing human wizard wearing miniature scholarly robes. The human has a striking birthmark sprawling across their face and arms that looks exactly like a blue-and-white cobalt floral landscape. Living flesh, highly detailed." *(Negative: doll, toy, porcelain, ceramic, statue)*
- **Marionette (Theatre):** * *Prompt:* "A portrait of a tiny living elf rogue with long dexterous fingers. The elf has dark, symmetrical crease-lines on the skin around their elbows, jaw, and wrists that resemble articulated joints. Faint wood-grain textured tattoos on living skin." *(Negative: puppet, wood, strings, jointed doll, toy, artificial)*
- **Ushabti (Funerary Guardian):** * *Prompt:* "A dramatic portrait of a 1-foot-tall living dwarf paladin with a matte, stony-gray complexion. The dwarf has deep, geometric scarification resembling hieroglyphs carved into the flesh of their chest. Living breathing warrior." *(Negative: statue, ushabti, stone carving, figurine, miniature, toy)*
