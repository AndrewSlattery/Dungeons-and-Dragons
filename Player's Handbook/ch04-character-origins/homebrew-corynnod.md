# CORYNNOD

Corynnod carry within their biology a rare magical property: the ability to compress every physical dimension of their body to a fraction of its normal size. Whether this mutation entered their lineage through prolonged exposure to a spatial anomaly, the blood of a long-forgotten planar traveler, or a cataclysmic warping of reality that rewrote the rules of their ancestors' flesh, it has since bred true—a hereditary gift passed reliably from parent to child.

To an untrained eye, a Corynnod in their natural form is completely indistinguishable from any other humanoid. They breathe, sweat, bleed, and move like anyone else—perhaps with slightly more of it, a restless physical energy as though their body can feel the potential of compression humming just beneath the surface. When they shrink, they remain perfectly proportioned people of flesh and blood, scaled down to a matter of inches. There is nothing alien or constructed about the Tiny form; it is simply them, fully themselves, at a different magnitude.

Because Corynnod pass without remark in any crowd, they have rarely formed closed or exclusive communities. Instead, they scatter through cities and settlements of every kind, known only to those they trust with their secret. This produces a distinctive cultural pattern: Corynnod tend to maintain private networks of their own kind across wide geographic areas—extended families, old acquaintances, traveling merchants who know the right greeting—bonds invisible to the world outside. The knowledge that one can become mouse-sized and vanish into a wall gives Corynnod a particular psychological relationship to visibility, privacy, and the difference between what is seen and what is real. They are rarely the loudest person in a room, but they are often the most attentive.

### CORYNNOD TRAITS

**Creature Type:** Humanoid

**Size:** Medium (about 4–7 feet tall) or Small (about 2–4 feet tall), chosen when you select this species

**Speed:** 30 feet

As a Corynnod, you have these special traits.

**Scale Awareness.** Your shifting perspective gives you a keen eye for minute details. You gain proficiency in the Perception skill.

**Mutable Mass.** As a Magic action, you can magically compress to Tiny size (approximately 3 to 6 inches tall). You and everything you are wearing or carrying compress with you. Any item you drop, throw, or otherwise release from your grasp immediately returns to its normal size. You can remain Tiny for any duration, and you can revert to your normal size as a Bonus Action provided at least one unoccupied space exists within 5 feet of you. You can finish a Short or Long Rest while Tiny.

While Tiny, the following rules apply:

- **Compressed Stride.** Your Speed becomes 10 feet.
- **Narrow Passage.** You can move through openings as small as 1 inch wide without squeezing.
- **Creature Space.** You can end your movement in the space of any Medium or larger creature.
- **Unseen.** You have Advantage on Dexterity (Stealth) checks.
- **Weakened Frame.** You have Disadvantage on Strength checks and Strength saving throws.
- **Encumbered.** Your carrying capacity becomes 1 pound. Items that compressed with you do not count against this limit.
- **Limited Actions.** You can take the Dash, Disengage, Hide, Search, Study, and Utilize actions. You cannot make attack rolls, cast or concentrate on spells, or take Reactions.

You can use this trait to compress a number of times equal to your Proficiency Bonus, and you regain all expended uses when you finish a Long Rest. Reverting to your normal size does not expend a use.

**Spatial Expansion.** The sudden re-expansion of your body releases a burst of outward kinetic force. While Tiny from your *Mutable Mass* trait, you can end your movement in a creature's space (as described in that trait). If you revert to your normal size while still occupying that creature's space, it must make a Strength saving throw (DC 8 + your Strength modifier + your Proficiency Bonus).

On a failed save, the creature is pushed 5 feet into an adjacent unoccupied space of your choice and has the Prone condition. On a successful save, the creature is pushed 5 feet into an adjacent unoccupied space of your choice but is not knocked Prone.

If the creature is Huge or Gargantuan, or cannot be moved for any other reason, the creature is unaffected. In that case, you are instead moved to the nearest unoccupied space of your choice.

## Appearance Notes

A Corynnod looks, in every meaningful sense, like an ordinary humanoid of any stripe. The golden rule of Corynnod appearance is **Ordinary Person First**. Their mutation is internal; their surface is unremarkable.

That said, players who want to hint at the hidden power of their Corynnod have a few subtle avenues available.

### UNIVERSAL CHARACTERISTICS

When portraying a Corynnod, start with a completely ordinary member of their underlying species (Human, Elf, Dwarf, etc.) and consider the following optional flavour details:

**Restless Physicality.** A Corynnod in an idle moment is rarely fully still. They shift weight, tap fingers, or turn objects in their hands—not from nerves, but from a body that feels alive with latent potential. This is purely cosmetic and not a mechanical trait.

**Compression Tells.** Some Corynnod develop subtle physical tells that hint at their mutation, particularly around areas of the body under greatest stress during compression. These are optional and always minor:
- A very slight, momentary visual distortion (like heat shimmer) visible at the skin surface in the seconds before or after compression, if witnessed up close
- An unusual physical density for their apparent build—a Corynnod who looks slender but sits heavy in a chair

**Tiny Form.** When compressed, a Corynnod is indistinguishable from a doll-sized version of themselves. Their clothes fit. Their face is their face. Their voice, if they whisper, is their voice. There is nothing monstrous or abstract about the Tiny form—it is simply them, smaller.

## Image Prompt Guidance

Corynnod are significantly easier to prompt than Stobor because they look exactly like normal people. The challenge is not fighting AI assumptions but simply producing a high-quality humanoid portrait.

If you want to hint at the compression ability:

### The Corynnod Prompt Formula:
> `A photorealistic portrait of a [species: human/elf/dwarf/etc.], [clothing and personality description]. Cinematic lighting, highly detailed living flesh, intense eye contact.`
> **Optional add:** `Faint, faintly geometric faint markings on the collarbone and shoulders, like natural scar tissue in a pattern that is slightly too regular.`

### To depict the Tiny form:
> `A photorealistic macro portrait of a [1-inch-tall] living [species], [clothing], standing on [surface: a tabletop/a book/a stone floor]. Perfectly proportioned adult figure, photographed at extreme close range, dramatic macro lighting, photo-real living flesh.`
> **Negative Prompt:** `doll, toy, figurine, miniature, plastic, inanimate, ceramic, puppet`
