Word Domain

Speak Truth and Make All Listen

The Word Domain treats language as a divine force that shapes reality. Clerics who wield this magic are living heralds whose voices dictate the flow of battle, inspiring heroic hope in their allies while crushing enemies under absolute, undeniable commands.

Gods of this domain are patrons of truth, law, prophecy, and inspiration. They include strict arbiters of justice and divine messengers who deliver revelations to mortals. Their power is sought by diplomats, leaders, and peacekeepers who rely on unyielding mandates to forge alliances or uncover grand deceptions.

Level 3: Hope and Havoc

As a Magic action, you expend one use of your Channel Divinity to choose a number of creatures you can see within 60 feet of yourself, up to your Proficiency Bonus. For each chosen creature, apply one of the following effects:

  • Hope. The creature has Advantage on the next D20 Test it makes before the end of your next turn.
  • Havoc. The creature must succeed on a Wisdom saving throw against your spell save DC or have Disadvantage on the next D20 Test it makes before the end of your next turn.

Level 3: Divine Emissary

Your divine mandate natively empowers your voice, granting you the following benefits:

  • Blessed Preacher. You add your Wisdom modifier (minimum of +1) to any Charisma (Persuasion) or Charisma (Intimidation) check you make.
  • Unfettered Speech. As long as you do not have the Incapacitated condition, no effect (such as Silence) can prevent you from speaking or providing the Verbal components of your spells.

Level 6: Blessed Herald

You can extend your mandate to another. As a Magic action, you touch a willing creature, marking it as your emissary until you finish a Long Rest. While marked, the creature gains the benefits of your Divine Emissary feature, and you can both communicate telepathically with each other across any distance, provided you are on the same plane of existence.

Once you use this feature, you can't use it again until you finish a Long Rest.

Level 17: Word Perfect

Your mastery of divine speech grants you the following permanent benefits:

  • Echoes of Hope and Havoc. Whenever you cast a Cleric spell of level 1 or higher that has a Verbal component, you can immediately apply either the Hope or Havoc effect to one creature of your choice within 60 feet of yourself.
  • With One Voice. You can treat the target of your Blessed Herald feature as the point of origin for any Cleric spell you cast that has a Verbal component.
