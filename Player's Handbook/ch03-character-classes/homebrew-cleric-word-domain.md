### WORD DOMAIN

**Speak Truth and Make All Listen**

The Word Domain treats language as a divine force that shapes reality. Clerics who wield this magic are living heralds whose voices dictate the flow of battle, inspiring heroic hope in their allies while crushing enemies under absolute, undeniable commands.

Gods of this domain are patrons of truth, law, prophecy, and inspiration. They include strict arbiters of justice and divine messengers who deliver revelations to mortals. Their power is sought by diplomats, leaders, and peacekeepers who rely on unyielding mandates to forge alliances or uncover grand deceptions.

#### LEVEL 3: WORD DOMAIN SPELLS

Your connection to this divine domain ensures you always have certain spells ready. When you reach a Cleric level specified in the Word Domain Spells table, you thereafter always have the listed spells prepared.

| Cleric Level | Prepared Spells |
| :--- | :--- |
| 3 | *Command*, *Heroism*, *Suggestion*, *Zone of Truth* |
| 5 | *Sending*, *Tongues* |
| 7 | *Compulsion*, *Divination* |
| 9 | *Geas*, *Legend Lore* |

#### LEVEL 3: HOPE AND HAVOC

As a Magic Action, you present your Holy Symbol and expend one use of your Channel Divinity to issue divine promises of support or discord. Choose a number of creatures you can see within 60 feet of yourself, up to a number equal to your Proficiency Bonus. For each chosen creature, apply one of the following effects:

- **Hope.** The creature has Advantage on the next D20 Test it makes before the end of your next turn.
- **Havoc.** The creature must succeed on a Wisdom saving throw against your spell save DC or have Disadvantage on the next D20 Test it makes before the end of your next turn.

#### LEVEL 3: DIVINE EMISSARY

Your divine mandate empowers your voice. As a Magic action, you touch a creature and mark it as your deity's emissary until you take a Long Rest, granting it the following benefits:

- **Blessed Preacher.** The emissary adds your Wisdom modifier (minimum of +1) to any Charisma (Persuasion) or Charisma (Intimidation) check it makes.
- **Unfettered Tongue.** As long as the emissary is not Incapacitated, no effect (such as Silence) can prevent it from speaking or providing the Verbal components of its spells. 

Once you use this feature, you can't use it again until you finish a Long Rest.

#### LEVEL 6: BLESSED HERALD

Your connection to your chosen speaker deepens. While your Divine Emissary feature is active on a creature other than yourself, you gain the following benefits:

- **Common Tongue.** You and the emissary both know any language that either one of you knows, and (provided you are both on the same plane of existence) you and the emissary can communicate telepathically with each other, regardless of distance.
- **Warding Word.** When the emissary takes damage, you can take a Reaction to fulfil a promise of protection, reducing the damage taken by 1d8 plus your Wisdom modifier.

#### LEVEL 17: WORD PERFECT

As a Bonus Action, your word becomes infallible. This lasts for 1 minute or until you have the Incapacitated condition or die. While in this state, you gain the following benefits:

- **Echoes of Hope and Havoc.** Once per turn, when you cast a Cleric spell of level 1 or higher that has a Verbal component, you can immediately apply either the Hope or Havoc effect (from your Level 3 feature) to one creature of your choice within 60 feet of yourself.
- **With One Voice.** You can treat your Divine Emissary target as the point of origin for any Cleric spell you cast that has a Verbal component. 

Once you use this feature, you can't use it again until you finish a Long Rest, unless you expend a spell slot of level 5 or higher to restore your use of it.
